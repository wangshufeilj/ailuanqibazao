# 模块一：翻译与全文概要

## 英文翻译

> **Satellite Imagery Documents: The Once-Largest Iceberg in the World Breaks Apart in Weeks — Why?**

In the frigid waters approximately 250 kilometers west of South Georgia Island — at 52.75°S, 41.34°W — a glaciological saga spanning nearly four decades is now drawing to its close.

On January 14, true-color imagery at 250-meter resolution captured by the Fengyun-3D satellite revealed that the remnant core of A23a, once the world's largest iceberg, now covers a mere 506 square kilometers — less than one-eighth of its original extent. Just three weeks prior, the main body still measured 948 square kilometers.

Monitoring data show that three daughter icebergs have recently calved from A23a's southwestern flank, with a combined area of 251 square kilometers. Together with the surrounding debris field, the total ice-covered area now extends to 1,439 square kilometers — 51% larger than the main-body measurement recorded at the end of last year — presenting a pattern of "continuous fragmentation and ongoing dispersal."

Chief Expert Zheng Zhaojun of the International User Service Center at the National Satellite Meteorological Center (National Center for Space Weather), along with his team, has been continuously tracking A23a's drift trajectory since 2023 and its disintegration process throughout 2025 using the Medium Resolution Spectral Imager (MERSI) aboard the Fengyun-3 satellite. The latest monitoring data reveal a dramatic split at the start of this year: on January 8, the main body remained largely intact with only a few surface cracks; by January 9, it had visibly fractured into four sections; within the following days, the rate of breakup accelerated, and by January 14, multiple clear waterways had formed between the main body and the three major daughter icebergs, marking their complete separation.

Zheng Zhaojun explained that the rapid disintegration of A23a is closely linked to the cascading development of supraglacial melt ponds, ice lakes, and meltwater drainage systems. Satellite imagery clearly shows an abundance of blue meltwater pools and ice lakes distributed across the iceberg's surface. Along the edges of the ice body, meltwater causes the ice layers to tilt upward, forming natural-dam-like structures that temporarily obstruct meltwater drainage. However, as meltwater continues to accumulate, its weight generates enormous pressure at the margins, sufficient to open new fractures in the ice. Meltwater then percolates and erodes along these cracks — with some streams even cascading down the ice walls directly into the sea. This process continuously widens the fractures, acting as a wedge of water that relentlessly splits the ice apart. This "hydro-wedging" effect is identified as the key mechanism driving the rapid structural collapse of the iceberg.

The approximately 1,439 square kilometers of remnant ice and dispersed debris now constitute a significant navigational hazard for the coming months, harboring numerous hard-to-detect "ghost icebergs" that pose serious risks to shipping. Meanwhile, the large volumes of freshwater released by the melting ice will alter local ocean salinity, potentially affecting ocean circulation and marine ecosystems.

From 4,170 square kilometers when it calved from the Antarctic ice shelf in 1986 to a remnant main body of just 506 square kilometers today, A23a has traversed nearly its entire lifecycle over four decades. Its accelerating disintegration, together with the broader trend of accelerating thinning and retreat of Antarctic ice shelves observed in recent years, collectively points toward the impact of global warming — providing scientists with a case study for understanding ice sheet stability and sea-level rise mechanisms.

Zheng Zhaojun noted that the current period coincides with the Southern Hemisphere summer. Conditions in the iceberg's location are relatively clear, with elevated air and sea temperatures, seawater exceeding 3°C continuously eroding the ice body, and ocean currents pushing it northward into warmer waters. These combined factors are accelerating the disintegration. He predicts that A23a may completely disintegrate within a few weeks; even if a remnant persists, it is unlikely to meet the internationally recognized iceberg designation threshold of 20 square nautical miles (approximately 68.6 square kilometers).

**【English Summary】**

Once spanning 4,170 square kilometers and reigning as the world's largest iceberg, A23a calved from Antarctica's Filchner-Ronne Ice Shelf in 1986, lay grounded in the Weddell Sea for decades, and has drifted northward through the South Atlantic to meet its end. This report by China Meteorological News, underpinned by Fengyun-3D satellite remote sensing data and expert commentary from Chief Scientist Zheng Zhaojun and his team, documents in detail the iceberg's dramatic collapse in early January 2026 — when its main body shrank from 948 to 506 square kilometers in under three weeks.

The central scientific contribution of the piece lies in its elucidation of "hydro-wedging": surface meltwater pools accumulate on the iceberg's surface, seep into pre-existing cracks, and cascade down ice walls into the sea, relentlessly widening fractures in what amounts to a water-driven wedge effect. Compounding this are elevated summer temperatures, warming seawater above 3°C, and northward-pushing ocean currents. A23a is projected to dissolve entirely within weeks. The report situates this individual event within the broader context of Antarctica's accelerating ice-shelf retreat and anthropogenic climate change, offering both scientific documentation and a stark illustration of rising sea-level risks.

### T — 翻译重要词

**T1. sea level rise** *n.* [不可数/专有名词短语]

- **英文释义**：A long-term increase in the mean level of Earth's oceans, primarily driven by thermal expansion of seawater and the addition of meltwater from glaciers and ice sheets. *(NOAA / NASA)*
- **中文释义**：海平面上升
- **语域**：学术/新闻/政策/气候科学
- **常见搭配**：*sea level rise projections / contribute to sea level rise / accelerated sea level rise / sea level rise mitigation*
- **翻译难点**：
  - 中文"海平面上升"对应英文有两种写法：*sea level rise*（名词短语，更常用于标题/政策文件）与 *rise in sea level*（更偏叙述性）。
  - 注意区分 *sea level rise*（总体趋势）与 *coastal flooding*（海岸淹没，具体现象）。
- **例句**：Scientific models project that **sea level rise** could exceed one meter by the end of this century if current emission trends continue.
  - *科学模型预测，若当前排放趋势持续，本世纪末海平面上升幅度可能超过一米。*

---

**T2. hydrostatic pressure** /ˌhaɪ.drəˈstæt.ɪk ˈpreʃ.ɚ/ *n.* [不可数]

- **英文释义**：The pressure exerted by a fluid at rest due to the force of gravity; in glaciology, the pressure exerted by a column of water or ice. *(Physics/Glaciology)*
- **中文释义**：静水压力；液体静压力
- **语域**：物理/工程/地球科学专业术语
- **常见搭配**：*hydrostatic pressure differential / withstand hydrostatic pressure / hydrostatic equilibrium*
- **翻译难点**：*hydrostatic* 与 *hydrodynamic*（流体动力学的，涉及运动中的液体）易混淆；前者特指静止液体，后者指运动液体。
- **例句**：As the meltwater pool deepened, the **hydrostatic pressure** it exerted on the underlying ice became sufficient to propagate new fractures downward.
  - *随着融水池加深，其对下方冰体施加的静水压力足以向下扩展出新的裂缝。*

---

**T3. calving** /ˈkæv.ɪŋ/ *n.* [不可数] / *v.*（见 W 类 calve）

- **英文释义**：The process by which a piece of ice breaks off from the terminus of a glacier or the edge of an ice shelf to form a new iceberg. *(NSIDC)*
- **中文释义**：冰山崩解；冰体脱离（冰架或冰川末端）
- **语域**：冰川学/极地科学专业术语；科学新闻中常见
- **常见搭配**：*calving rate / calving event / calving front / calving glacier*
- **翻译难点**："calving"来自 *calf*（牛犊）的生育动作，引申为冰体"生出"子冰山。直译为"产犊"不可接受；规范译法为"冰山崩解"或"冰山脱离"。若强调过程，可译为"崩冰作用"。
- **例句**：The **calving** of the Thwaites Glacier has been accelerating at a rate that alarms scientists studying Antarctic ice stability.
  - *斯韦茨冰川的冰山崩解速率持续加快，令研究南极冰盖稳定性的科学家深感忧虑。*

**T4. drift** /drɪft/ *n.* [可数/不可数] / *v.*

- **英文释义**：(v.) To be carried slowly along by currents of water or air; (n.) A gradual movement caused by natural forces. *(Cambridge)*
- **中文释义**：漂移；漂流；（被水流/洋流）推动移动
- **语域**：通用；海洋学/气象学中有专业用法
- **同义词**：float, move, be carried along
- **翻译难点**：
  - "漂移"（drift）≠ "游动"（swim）≠ "航行"（navigate/sail）：drift 强调被动受力，无主动意志。
  - 新闻翻译中，"冰山漂移路径"= *iceberg drift trajectory*；"大陆漂移"= *continental drift*（地质学固定搭配）。
- **拓展**：
  - *snow drift*（雪堆；被风堆积的雪）；*drift net*（流刺网，渔业）；*drift apart*（渐行渐远，比喻）

- **例句**：The research team deployed a set of autonomous buoys to record the **drift** patterns of sea ice throughout the winter season.
  - *研究团队部署了一组自主浮标，记录整个冬季海冰的漂流规律。*

**T5. thermohaline circulation** /ˌθɜːr.moʊˈheɪ.laɪn ˌsɜːr.kjəˈleɪ.ʃən/ *n.* [不可数]

- **英文释义**：The large-scale ocean circulation driven by differences in temperature (*thermo*) and salinity (*haline*) that affect water density; also called the "global ocean conveyor belt." *(NOAA)*
- **中文释义**：热盐环流；温盐环流；大洋热盐环流（也称"全球海洋传送带"）
- **语域**：海洋学/气候科学专业术语
- **常见搭配**：*thermohaline circulation disruption / thermohaline conveyor belt / weakening of thermohaline circulation*
- **翻译难点**：此术语在中文专业文献中有标准译名"热盐环流"；在科普写作中也常见"洋流循环"，但后者过于笼统，需区分使用。
- **例句**：The influx of freshwater from melting ice sheets could disrupt **thermohaline circulation**, with far-reaching consequences for global climate patterns.
  - *来自融化冰盖的大量淡水涌入可能扰乱热盐环流，对全球气候模式产生深远影响。*

**T6. remote sensing** /rɪˈmoʊt ˈsens.ɪŋ/ *n.* [不可数]

- **英文释义**：The acquisition of information about an object or area from a distance, typically using satellite or aircraft-based sensors that detect and record reflected or emitted radiation. *(NASA / USGS)*
- **中文释义**：遥感；遥感技术
- **语域**：地理信息科学/航天/学术
- **常见搭配**：*satellite remote sensing / remote sensing data / remote sensing imagery / remote sensing techniques*
- **翻译难点**：汉语"遥感"已是标准技术术语，直接对应 *remote sensing*，无需直译"远程感知"。
- **例句**：The team relied on **remote sensing** data from multiple satellite platforms to reconstruct the iceberg's eight-year drift path.

**T7. spectral imager / spectral imaging** *n.*

- **英文释义**：A sensor or technique that captures images across multiple wavelengths of the electromagnetic spectrum, enabling identification of materials by their spectral signatures. *(NASA)*
- **中文释义**：光谱成像仪；光谱成像技术
- **语域**：遥感/航天/光学工程专业术语
- **翻译难点**：原文"中分辨率光谱成像仪（MERSI）"的英文对应为 *Medium Resolution Spectral Imager（MERSI）*，属专有名词缩写，翻译时需保留缩写并首次标注全称。
- **例句**：The **spectral imager** on board the satellite distinguishes between bare ice, snow cover, and meltwater pools by analyzing their different reflectance signatures.
  - *卫星搭载的光谱成像仪通过分析裸冰、积雪和融水池各自不同的反射特征，将三者加以区分。*

**T8. debris field** /ˈdeb.rɪ fiːld/ *n.* [可数]

- **英文释义**：An area covered with broken or scattered fragments of a larger object; in marine contexts, scattered pieces of ice, wreckage, or other material over a wide sea area.
- **中文释义**：碎片区；残骸散布区；（冰）碎冰散布区
- **语域**：军事/海洋/航空/新闻（跨领域通用）

**T9. ice sheet stability** *n.* [不可数]

- **英文释义**：The capacity of a large, continental ice sheet (such as those covering Antarctica or Greenland) to maintain its mass and structural integrity in the face of changing environmental conditions.
- **中文释义**：冰盖稳定性
- **语域**：冰川学/气候科学/政策报告
- **常见搭配**：*ice sheet stability and sea level rise / ice sheet stability threshold / long-term ice sheet stability*
- **翻译难点**：中文"冰盖"（*ice sheet*）≠"冰架"（*ice shelf*）≠"冰川"（*glacier*）≠"冰山"（*iceberg*）。四者层次关系：冰盖位于大陆上→延伸入海为冰架→冰架崩解出冰山→冰山最终融化。翻译需严格区分。
- **例句**：The collapse of floating ice shelves may compromise **ice sheet stability** on land, potentially releasing vast volumes of freshwater into the oceans.
  - *浮动冰架的崩塌可能影响陆地冰盖的稳定性，进而有可能将大量淡水释放入海洋。*

**T10. resolution** /ˌrez.əˈluː.ʃən/ *n.* [不可数/可数]

- **英文释义**：(In remote sensing) The level of detail captured in an image, determined by the size of the smallest feature that can be distinguished; often expressed in meters per pixel. *(NASA)*
- **中文释义**：分辨率（遥感/图像）；解像度
- **语域**：技术/遥感/摄影/学术；也有"决议/决心"的常见义
- **熟词僻义**：本词在外交/联合国语境中意为"决议"（如 *UN Security Council Resolution*）；在日常语境中意为"决心"（*New Year's resolution*）；在遥感语境中专指"分辨率"——三义差异极大，需结合上下文判断。
- **例句**：The 250-meter **resolution** of the Fengyun-3D sensor strikes a balance between spatial detail and the wide-area coverage needed to track large icebergs.

**T11. sentinel** /ˈsen.t̬ɪ.nəl/ *n.* [可数]

- **英文释义**：A soldier or guard whose job is to stand and keep watch; figuratively, something that monitors or watches for danger or change. *(LDOCE)*

**S1. ground** /ɡraʊnd/ *v.*（熟词僻义：搁浅；使停飞）

- **音标**：/ɡraʊnd/
- **词性**：*v.* [transitive/intransitive]
- **英文释义（僻义）**：(of a ship, iceberg, etc.) To become stuck on the seabed or in shallow water; to touch bottom and become unable to move freely. *(LDOCE)*
- **中文释义（僻义）**：搁浅；（冰山/船只）触底停住
- **语域**：航海/地球科学；另有"（飞机）停飞/（人）被禁止出行"之义（aviation/informal）

  - ✅ 熟义：*the ground*（地面）；*ground floor*（首层）；*ground rules*（基本规则）
  - ✅ 僻义1：*The iceberg grounded on the seabed.*（冰山在海底搁浅）

**S2. cascade** /kæˈskeɪd/ *n. / v.*（熟词僻义：逐级传导；连锁发展）

- **中文释义**：瀑布；倾泻；（比喻）逐级发展；连锁效应；级联
- **语域**：科学/工程/新闻/正式；"cascade effect"是重要学术词组
- **熟义 vs 僻义对比**：
  - ✅ 熟义：*a cascade of water*（倾泻的水）

**S4. wedge** /wedʒ/ *n. / v.*（熟词僻义：水楔效应；用作策略工具）

- **词性**：*n.* [可数] / *v.* [transitive]
- **英文释义**：(n.) A piece of wood, metal, etc. with a thick end tapering to a thin edge, used for splitting; (figurative) something that serves to split, separate, or divide; (v.) To force something into a narrow space. *(LDOCE)*
- **中文释义（本义）**：楔子；楔形物
- **中文释义（引申义）**：（水）劈；作为楔子（比喻手段）
- **语域**：通用；地质学/工程/政治写作中常见比喻用法

1.  **中文金句**：其当前的加速崩解，与近年来观测到的南极冰架整体加速变薄、退缩的趋势，共同指向全球变暖的影响，为科学家理解冰盖稳定性与海平面上升机制提供了案例。
    **英文翻译**：Its accelerating disintegration, together with the broader trend of accelerating thinning and retreat of Antarctic ice shelves observed in recent years, collectively points toward the impact of global warming — providing scientists with a case study for understanding ice sheet stability and sea-level rise mechanisms.

2.  **中文金句**：目前约1439平方公里的残存冰山和浮冰散布区，已成为未来数月航行安全的重要隐患，其中潜藏大量难以探测的“幽灵冰山”，将对航行构成严重威胁。
    **英文翻译**：The approximately 1,439 square kilometers of remnant ice and dispersed debris now constitute a significant navigational hazard for the coming months, harboring numerous hard-to-detect 'ghost icebergs' that pose serious risks to shipping.

3.  **中文金句**：A23a用四十年的历程，几乎走完了一座冰山从诞生到衰亡的主要生命旅程。
    **英文翻译**：Over its forty-year span, A23a has almost completed the primary lifecycle of an iceberg, from its genesis to its eventual demise.
