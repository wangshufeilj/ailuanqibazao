**Article 1: "Hunting the Flag": How Entomologists of Primorsky Krai Catch Ticks in the Botanical Garden**

Since mid-March, as soon as the air warms to +3–+5°C, entomologists in Primorsky Krai begin their hunt for ixodid ticks. They set out along fixed survey routes — Russky Island, the Botanical Garden, Nadezhdinsk District, and border territories. Their primary mission is to monitor population dynamics and species composition throughout the entire epidemic season. This enables forecasts for the following year regarding the density of ticks and their rodent hosts, and — crucially — helps identify which viruses they are likely to transmit.

A PrimaMedia correspondent joined the raid conducted by epidemiologists and entomologists from the Primorsky Center for Hygiene and Epidemiology on Tuesday, March 24. The route ran through a non-tourist zone of the Botanical Garden. Specialists carry light-colored flannel or waffle-weave fabric measuring 80×90 cm or 60×100 cm — pale shades are essential so parasites remain visible. If a tick needs to be sent to the laboratory, moistened gauze bandages are used.

"Our route in Vladivostok starts in the Botanical Garden — a three-kilometer zone — and then continues beyond its boundaries. We work from March through October or December," explained Yulia Nesterova, head of the epizootological research department at the Primorsky Center for Hygiene and Epidemiology.

The method employed is known as the "flag count." A specialist walks along the edge of a path, inspecting the cloth every 20–25 steps. Tick density at path margins is 10–14 times higher than five to ten meters away. Currently, the most active species are taiga ticks (*Ixodes persulcatus*) — the most abundant and most aggressive toward humans.

The specialist covers the first kilometer along the path, then the second beyond the garden boundaries, and the third on the return. This is repeated every ten days from March through late autumn — sometimes into December — with results recorded and compared to prior-year data and multi-year averages.

No ticks were found in the Botanical Garden on the day of the raid. "This indicates the zone is currently tick-free. Specialists will extend their search farther," Nesterova clarified.

Had ticks been found, they would have been identified to genus on-site and to species under a laboratory microscope. Individual specimens selected for pathogen testing are placed in moistened gauze, rolled up, and transported to the laboratory. "Our stationary route only counts population density. We collect select individuals for tick-borne infection pathogen testing to monitor whether a focal point of infection is being maintained here. But we do not disturb overall numbers. We work in areas with no human presence, so we release the ticks," the department head noted.

The tick population is projected to peak in May, with high numbers persisting through the end of July — a pattern derived from multi-year observations. Although temperatures remain cool, ticks have already become active. In the second ten-day period of March, Rospotrebnadzor collected one specimen. Two cases of tick attachment to humans have already been recorded — in Khasan and Partizansk districts, where temperatures reach +10°C and above.

Beyond tick-borne encephalitis, Primorsky Krai registers Lyme disease (tick-borne borreliosis), tick-borne rickettsiosis, human monocytic ehrlichiosis, and human granulocytic anaplasmosis. The infection rate in ticks has remained stable based on multi-year data.

As specialists explain, a tick does not choose its host at random. It perches on grass or a shrub at heights of 30 cm to 1.5 meters. Resting on its hind legs, it extends its front limbs — which bear its sensory organs. Ticks have no eyes; they detect hosts by scent, sensing carbon dioxide, moisture, and warmth. When a person or animal brushes past and nudges a blade of grass, the tick hooks onto clothing with its claws and begins crawling slowly upward, selecting an attachment site. It favors soft, well-vascularized areas: under the breasts, in the armpits, and the groin. In children, the scalp and area behind the ears are common targets.

A bite may go unnoticed. The first portion of a tick's saliva contains an anesthetic substance. Moreover, males may feed on blood multiple times with very brief attachment. Roughly half of people feel no bite at all. It is only after 8–12 hours, when active bloodsucking begins, that discomfort arises.

"Ticks will crawl onto you, but they can only bite after an hour. The longer the blood-feeding lasts, the greater the probability that an infected tick will transmit the pathogen," the specialist explained.

For protection, specialists advise wearing light-colored, closely fitted clothing when entering forested areas, conducting body checks every 30–40 minutes, and using repellents. A tick is best removed with tweezers using a rotating motion rather than pulling. If tweezers are unavailable, a tight thread loop placed beneath the tick's proboscis can work. The wound should be disinfected with an alcohol-based solution, and the tick itself submitted to a laboratory for testing. The most effective protection against tick-borne encephalitis, however, remains vaccination.

Notably, animals do not contract encephalitis — they can only serve as carriers. Ticks transmit pathogens both vertically (from female to offspring) and horizontally (during molting and metamorphosis). In a sense, ticks may even regulate populations of very small animals such as shrews: a female tick engorges to dozens of times her original size, and if several adult parasites attach to a small shrew, they may collectively drain it of blood, causing the animal's death.

**Article 2: Strawberry Abundance — How Much Does a Kilogram Cost in Vladivostok?**

Strawberries have arrived in abundance on the shelves of Primorsky Krai's stores and markets, with prices starting from 500 rubles per kilogram. According to Rosselkhoznadzor, from the beginning of the year through March 15, some 1,144 tonnes of the berry were imported from China through the region's phytosanitary checkpoints — 211 tonnes more than in the same period last year. The agency noted that all produce passed the required quarantine inspection and fully meets safety standards.

Prices vary by outlet. In the major retail chains Remi and Samberi, a kilogram costs 649 and 599 rubles respectively; packaged strawberries in Samberi are priced at 1,079 rubles per kilo. At the Vtoraya Rechka market near the bus station, the price is 550 rubles per kilo; at the Kutuzova market, loose berries can be found for 500 rubles — though they appear somewhat green — while riper, redder strawberries are packaged in two-kilogram clamshells for 1,100 rubles. On Lugovaya Street, an outdoor stall offers berries at 650–700 rubles per kilo. Near the Sportivnaya tram stop, where supplies are plentiful, prices run to 500 rubles per kilo or 750–800 rubles per box; deeper into the market rows, prices rise to 600 rubles per kilo and 1,000 rubles per box. For comparison, last year's average prices were similarly 500–650 rubles per kilo, and Korean-origin strawberries were also available alongside Chinese ones.

### W — 写作高频词

---

**W1. surveillance**
/sərˈveɪləns/ *n.*
**英文释义（LDOCE）**：The careful watching of a person, place, or situation especially by the police or authorities, often to prevent crime or monitor disease.
**中文释义**：监视；监控；（流行病学）监测
**语域**：正式 / 新闻 / 医学 / 法律
**同义词**：monitoring, observation, oversight, watch
**反义词**：neglect, inattention
**常见词组**：
- **disease/epidemiological surveillance** 疾病/流行病学监测
- **under surveillance** 处于监控之下
- **public health surveillance** 公共卫生监测
- **surveillance data/network** 监测数据/网络
- **conduct surveillance** 开展监测

**拓展**：不可数名词。词源为法语 *surveiller*（看守），由 *sur-*（over）+ *veiller*（watch）构成。写作中常与 *monitoring* 并用，但 *surveillance* 更强调系统性、持续性和官方性质；*monitoring* 则更通用。学术写作高频词，GRE/GMAT/学术论文必掌握。

**例句**：
**Continuous epidemiological surveillance** enables health authorities to detect early warning signals of emerging infectious diseases long before they escalate into full-blown outbreaks.
持续的**流行病学监测**使卫生部门能够在传染病演变为全面暴发之前，及早察觉预警信号。

---

**W2. proliferation**
/prəˌlɪfəˈreɪʃən/ *n.*
**英文释义（LDOCE）**：① A sudden increase in the amount or number of something. ② The very fast growth of new parts of a living thing, such as cells.
**中文释义**：① 激增；快速扩散 ② （生物学）增殖；细胞增生
**语域**：新闻 / 学术 / 政治 / 生物医学
**同义词**：surge, spread, multiplication, explosion (in numbers)
**反义词**：decline, reduction, contraction
**常见词组**：
- **nuclear non-proliferation** 核不扩散（NPT即《不扩散核武器条约》）
- **the proliferation of** ……的激增/扩散
- **cell proliferation** 细胞增殖
- **proliferation of tick populations** 蜱虫种群数量激增

**拓展**：作名词时为不可数（泛指现象）或可数（指具体事件）。词根来自拉丁语 *proles*（后代）。动词为 **proliferate** /prəˈlɪfəreɪt/，adj. 为 **proliferative**。注意 *proliferation* 在国际政治语境中几乎专指"核武器扩散"，行文时需区分语境。

**例句**：
The **proliferation** of invasive tick species into previously unaffected highland regions has prompted public health authorities to expand their field survey networks significantly.
侵入性蜱虫物种向此前未受影响的高地地区的**快速扩散**，促使公共卫生部门大幅扩展其野外调查网络。

---

**W3. endemic**
/enˈdemɪk/ *adj.*
**英文释义（Cambridge）**：An endemic disease or problem is regularly found in a particular place or among a particular group of people.
**中文释义**：地方性的；某地区特有的（疾病或问题）
**语域**：学术 / 医学 / 新闻
**同义词**：prevalent, ingrained, widespread
**辨析（三词区分）**：
| 词 | 含义 |
|---|---|
| **endemic** | 某地持续稳定存在 |
| **epidemic** | 某地某时骤然大规模爆发 |
| **pandemic** | 跨国/全球性蔓延 |

**常见词组**：
- **endemic disease / endemic area** 地方病/疫区
- **endemic to a region** 在某地区流行
- **endemic corruption** 根深蒂固的腐败（引申义）

**拓展**：词源自希腊语 *endemia*（en-"在内" + *demos*"人民/地方"）。在非医学语境中，*endemic* 也可形容社会问题（*endemic poverty*，根深蒂固的贫困），属熟词引申义，写作中频繁借用。

**例句**：
**Endemic** tick-borne encephalitis in the Russian Far East has driven decades of mandatory vaccination programs targeting forestry workers and outdoor laborers.
俄罗斯远东地区蜱传脑炎的**地方性流行**推动了数十年来针对林业工人和户外劳动者的强制疫苗接种计划。

---

**W4. vector**
/ˈvektər/ *n.*
**英文释义（LDOCE）**：An insect or animal that passes a disease from one person or organism to another.
**中文释义**：① （疾病）传播媒介；媒介生物 ② （数学/物理）向量；矢量
**语域**：医学/生物学（义①）；数理学科（义②）
**同义词**：carrier, transmitter, host（义①）
**常见词组**：
- **disease vector / tick vector** 疾病媒介/蜱虫媒介
- **vector-borne disease** 媒介传播疾病
- **vector control** 媒介控制（灭蚊、灭蜱等）
- **primary/secondary vector** 主要/次要传播媒介

**拓展**：可数名词。词源自拉丁语 *vector*（carrier，运载者）。**跨学科考点**：同一词根在医学中为"传播媒介"，在数学中为"向量"，在基因工程中为"载体（用于转入基因的质粒）"，备考时须依语境判断。

**例句**：
Rodents serve as the primary **vector** for maintaining tick-borne infections in natural ecosystems, as ticks feed on them during larval and nymph stages before seeking human hosts.
啮齿动物是维持自然生态系统中蜱传感染的主要**媒介**，因为蜱虫在幼虫和若虫阶段以其为食，之后才寻觅人类宿主。

---

**W5. transmission**
/trænsˈmɪʃən/ *n.*
**英文释义（LDOCE）**：The process of sending or passing something from one person, place, or thing to another; specifically, the passing of disease from one person/organism to another.
**中文释义**：① 传播；传染（疾病）② 传输（信号、数据）③ （汽车）变速箱/传动系统
**语域**：医学/学术（义①）；通信/技术（义②）；工程（义③）
**同义词**：spread, contagion, transfer（义①）
**词族**：*transmit* v.（传播；播出）→ *transmission* n. → *transmitter* n.（发射器；传播者）

**常见词组**：
- **transmission of disease** 疾病传播
- **vertical/horizontal transmission** 垂直/水平传播（医学专用，见原文）
- **airborne/tick-borne transmission** 空气/蜱虫传播
- **data transmission** 数据传输

**拓展**：原文出现"vertical transmission"（母婴/亲代传播）与"horizontal transmission"（同代个体间传播），是传染病学核心术语对。写作备考重点：区分 *transmission*（过程/方式）与 *infection*（结果状态）。

**例句**：
Understanding the dual mechanisms of **vertical and horizontal transmission** in ixodid ticks is essential for designing effective population-level control strategies against tick-borne pathogens.
理解蜱虫**垂直传播与水平传播**的双重机制，对于设计针对蜱传病原体的有效种群防控策略至关重要。

---

**W6. prevalence**
/ˈprevələns/ *n.*
**英文释义（Cambridge）**：The fact that something is very common or widespread; (medical) the proportion of a population found to have a condition.
**中文释义**：流行程度；普遍性；（流行病学）患病率
**语域**：学术 / 医学 / 统计
**同义词**：incidence（近义但有别）, frequency, commonness, ubiquity
**辨析（重要考点）**：
- **prevalence**：时间点上的存量——当下有多少人/多大比例处于患病状态
- **incidence**：时间段内的流量——新发病例率/新增感染数

**常见词组**：
- **disease prevalence / infection prevalence** 疾病/感染患病率
- **high/low prevalence** 高/低流行率
- **prevalence rate** 患病率
- **the prevalence of** ……的普遍程度

**拓展**：不可数名词（多数情况）。词源自拉丁语 *praevalere*（to prevail）。adj. 为 **prevalent** /ˈprevələnt/ （"流行的；普遍的"），亦为写作/阅读高频词。

**例句**：
The **prevalence** of tick-borne borreliosis in the region has remained remarkably stable over the past decade, indicating that existing surveillance and public health education programs have contained further spread.
该地区蜱传包氏病的**感染率**在过去十年间保持高度稳定，表明现有监测和公共卫生教育计划已有效遏制疾病的进一步扩散。

---

**W7. mitigate**
/ˈmɪtɪɡeɪt/ *v.*
**英文释义（LDOCE）**：To make a situation or the effects of something less harmful, serious, or painful.
**中文释义**：减轻；缓解；降低（危害或严重程度）
**语域**：正式 / 学术 / 法律 / 新闻
**同义词**：alleviate, lessen, reduce, diminish, cushion
**反义词**：aggravate, exacerbate, worsen, intensify
**词性变化**：*mitigation* /ˌmɪtɪˈɡeɪʃən/ n. 减轻；缓解。

**法律考点**：*mitigating circumstances/factors*（从轻情节）为法律英语核心词组，美国量刑程序（sentencing）中频繁出现；*mitigation of damages*（减损义务）是合同法/侵权法重要原则，要求受害方采取合理措施降低损失，否则无权就可避免部分的损失索赔。

**常见词组**：
- **mitigate the risk/impact of** 降低……的风险/影响
- **mitigate damage** 减轻损害
- **risk mitigation** 风险缓解措施
- **mitigating circumstances** 从轻情节（法律）

**例句**：
Public health officials emphasized that regular body checks and the use of **repellents** were the most practical measures available to **mitigate** the risk of tick attachment during the epidemic season.
公共卫生官员强调，定期检查身体和使用**驱虫剂**是流行季节内降低蜱虫叮咬风险最可行的措施。

---

**W8. sporadic**
/spəˈrædɪk/ *adj.*
**英文释义（LDOCE）**：Happening fairly often, but not regularly; not constant or systematic.
**中文释义**：零星的；不定期发生的；散发性的
**语域**：医学 / 学术 / 新闻 / 书面
**同义词**：intermittent, irregular, scattered, occasional
**反义词**：regular, systematic, continuous, persistent
**派生词**：*sporadically* adv. 不规律地；零星地
**词源**：中世纪拉丁语 *sporadicus*，源自希腊语 *sporaden*（"scattered in different places"），与 *spore*（孢子，散播之物）同源。

**医学专用义**：**sporadic disease/case**——散发病例，相对于 *epidemic*（流行）而言，指偶发的、非聚集性的病例，是流行病学重要术语。

**例句**：
Although the first **sporadic** cases of tick attachment were reported as early as mid-March, entomologists warned that peak infestation would not arrive until May.
尽管三月中旬已出现首批**零星**蜱虫叮咬病例，昆虫学家警告称，蜱虫数量峰值要到五月才会到来。

**R1. zoonosis / zoonotic disease**
*zoonosis*: /ˌzoʊəˈnoʊsɪs/ *n.*（复数：*zoonoses* /ˌzoʊəˈnoʊsiːz/）
**英文释义（Cambridge）**：A disease that can be passed from animals to humans or from humans to animals.
**中文释义**：人畜共患病；动物源性传染病
**语域**：医学 / 学术
**同义词**：zoonotic disease, animal-borne disease
**常见词组**：
- **vector-borne zoonosis** 媒介传播的人畜共患病
- **emerging zoonosis** 新兴人畜共患病
- **zoonotic pathogen** 动物源性病原体

**拓展**：可数名词，复数变化不规则（-sis → -ses），与 *diagnosis → diagnoses*、*thesis → theses* 同类，备考重点。词根来自希腊语 *zōion*（动物）+ *nosos*（疾病）。

**例句**：
The management of wildlife habitats near urban fringes requires careful assessment of potential **zoonotic** risks, as shrinking boundaries between human settlements and natural ecosystems accelerate pathogen spillover.
城市边缘野生动物栖息地的管理需要对潜在的**人畜共患病**风险进行仔细评估，因为人类聚居区与自然生态系统之间界限的缩小，正在加速病原体的跨物种传播。

**R2. quarantine**
/ˈkwɔːrəntiːn/ *n. / v.*
**英文释义（Cambridge）**：① (n.) A period of time when an animal or person that might have a disease is kept separate from others. ② (v.) To put a person, animal, or plant in quarantine.
**中文释义**：① 隔离检疫（期）② 对……实施隔离检疫；对（植物/产品）进行植物检疫
**语域**：医学 / 公共卫生 / 贸易/海关法规
**同义词**：isolation, containment（义①）
**常见词组**：
- **in quarantine** 处于隔离检疫中
- **quarantine period** 检疫期
- **phytosanitary quarantine** 植物检疫（原文中草莓进口情景）
- **quarantine inspection** 检验检疫
- **quarantine a person/animal/product** 对……隔离检疫

**拓展**：词源来自意大利语 *quarantina*（意为"四十天"），源于14世纪威尼斯对黑死病船只实施40天隔离的历史制度。可作名词或动词（*to quarantine sb/sth*）。

**例句**：
All strawberries imported from China are required to pass **phytosanitary quarantine** inspection at the border before being cleared for sale on the domestic market.
所有从中国进口的草莓，均须在边境通过**植物卫生检疫**检查，才能在国内市场上架销售。

**R3. phytosanitary**
/ˌfaɪtoʊˈsænɪtəri/ *adj.*
**英文释义**：Relating to the health of plants and the measures taken to prevent the spread of disease among them; used especially in the context of international trade regulations on agricultural produce.
**中文释义**：植物检疫的；植物卫生的
**语域**：贸易法规 / 农业 / 官方/书面
**常见词组**：
- **phytosanitary inspection/control** 植物检疫
- **phytosanitary certificate** 植物检疫证书（国际贸易必备单证）
- **phytosanitary measures** 植物检疫措施
- **phytosanitary checkpoint/post** 植物检疫检查站

**拓展**：词根 *phyto-*（植物，来自希腊语 *phyton*）+ *sanitary*（卫生的）。*phytosanitary certificate* 是《国际植物保护公约》（IPPC）框架下农产品出口的核心文件，WTO《实施卫生与植物卫生措施协定》（SPS Agreement）亦与此密切相关，法律/贸易备考必知。

**例句**：
Exporters must obtain a valid **phytosanitary certificate** for each consignment of fresh fruit, confirming that the produce has undergone inspection and meets the importing country's pest and disease requirements.
出口商必须为每批新鲜水果申领有效的**植物检疫证书**，以证明该批货物已经过检查，符合进口国的有害生物及疾病要求。

**R4. anesthetic (anaesthetic)**
*美式*: /ˌænəsˈθetɪk/ *n. / adj.*
**英文释义（LDOCE）**：A substance that causes a loss of feeling in your body, so you do not feel pain during an operation or procedure.
**中文释义**：麻醉剂；止痛剂；（形）麻醉性的
**语域**：医学
**辨析**：
- **general anesthetic**：全身麻醉剂（使人失去意识）
- **local anesthetic**：局部麻醉剂（仅麻醉局部）

**词族**：*anesthesia* /ˌænəsˈθiːʒə/ n. 麻醉（状态）；*anesthesiologist* n. 麻醉科医生；*anesthetic* adj. 麻醉的

**拓展**：原文关键信息：蜱虫首次注入的唾液中含有**麻醉物质**（*anesthetic substance*），使宿主感觉不到叮咬，这是其高度危险性的原因之一。英式拼写为 *anaesthetic*，美式为 *anesthetic*，两者均正确。

**例句**：
The initial secretion released by a feeding tick contains a natural **anesthetic** compound that numbs the bite site, allowing the parasite to attach and feed undetected for several hours.
吸血蜱虫释放的首次分泌物中含有一种天然**麻醉**成分，能使叮咬部位麻木，从而使寄生虫在数小时内悄然附着吸血而不被察觉。

**R5. engorged**
/ɪnˈɡɔːrdʒd/ *adj.*
**英文释义（Cambridge）**：(Of a body part) swollen with blood or other fluid; (of a tick or insect) having fed until fully swollen.
**中文释义**：（身体部位）肿胀的；（蜱虫、昆虫）吸饱血的；充血膨胀的
**语域**：医学 / 生物学
**词族**：*engorge* v. 使充血膨胀；大量吸食；*engorgement* n.
**同义词**：swollen, distended, bloated（均指膨胀）
**常见搭配**：
- **engorged tick** 吸饱血的蜱虫
- **become engorged** 充血胀大
- **fully engorged female** 完全吸饱血的雌虫

**拓展**：原文关键细节：雌性蜱虫吸血后体积可扩大数十倍（*increases in size dozens of times*），若多只成虫同时叮咬小型动物（如鼩鼱），可将其血液吸尽致死——这也是蜱虫能调控小型动物种群数量的机制。*engorged* 为医学/寄生虫学特定术语，普通词典释义较简略。

**例句**：
A fully **engorged** female tick can weigh dozens of times more than when unfed, having ingested enough blood to fuel the production of several thousand eggs.
一只完全**吸饱血的**雌性蜱虫，其体重可达空腹时的数十倍，所吸收的血液足以支撑其产下数千枚卵。

**T3. epidemiological vs. epizootological**

*epidemiological*: /ˌepɪˌdiːmiəˈlɒdʒɪkəl/ *adj.*
*epizootological*: /ˌepiˌzoʊətəˈlɒdʒɪkəl/ *adj.*

**英文释义**：
- **epidemiological**（流行病学的）：relating to the study of how diseases spread and affect human populations.
- **epizootological**（动物流行病学的）：relating to the study of how diseases spread among animal populations — the animal-science equivalent of epidemiology.

**中文释义**：
- **epidemiological**：流行病学的（研究疾病在人群中的分布与传播规律）
- **epizootological**：动物流行病学的（研究疾病在动物种群中的分布与传播规律，为兽医/野生动物科学专用词）

**语域**：学术 / 医学 / 兽医学 / 生态学

**重要对应关系**（翻译备考）：

| 英文 | 中文 | 适用对象 |
|---|---|---|
| epidemic | 流行病；（疾病的）流行 | 人 |
| epizootic | 动物流行病；（动物疾病的）流行 | 动物 |
| endemic | 地方病；地方性流行 | 人（稳定存在）|
| enzootic | 动物地方病；动物地方性流行 | 动物（稳定存在）|
| pandemic | 大流行（病）；全球性疫情 | 跨国/全球 |

**翻译提示**：原文 *отделение эпизоотологических исследований* 直译为"动物流行病学研究部门"，标准中文行政译名为**"流行病学研究部（所）"**或**"动物疫病研究室"**，实际翻译中无需字对字照译，应以目标语的习惯表达为准。

**拓展**：词根 *epi-*（在……之上/之中）+ *zoo-*（动物）+ *-ology*（学科）；对应 *epi-* + *demos-*（人民）+ *-ology* = epidemiology。两组词根逻辑完全平行，掌握一组即可类推另一组。

**例句**：
The **epizootological** monitoring program tracked pathogen presence in wild rodent populations throughout the year, providing critical baseline data for **epidemiological** risk assessments targeting nearby human settlements.
该**动物流行病学**监测项目全年追踪野生啮齿动物种群中的病原体分布，为针对附近人类聚居区的**流行病学**风险评估提供了关键基准数据。

**T4. molt / moult**
美式: *molt* /moʊlt/ *v. / n.*
英式: *moult*

**英文释义（Cambridge）**：(Of an animal) to lose feathers, skin, or hair as part of a natural process of renewal; the process of molting.
**中文释义**：（动物）蜕皮；换羽；褪毛；（名词）蜕皮/换羽过程
**语域**：生物学 / 动物学 / 学术

**拼写差异**：*molt*（美式）= *moult*（英式），两者含义完全相同。

**词族**：
- *molt* v. 蜕皮；换羽
- *molting* / *moulting* n. 蜕皮期；换羽过程
- *intermolt* adj. 蜕皮间隔期的

**原文关键语境**：原文指出蜱虫通过蜕皮（*линька*）和变态发育在不同生命阶段之间传播病原体——此即**水平传播**（horizontal transmission）的机制之一。蜱虫的生活史包括：卵 → 幼虫（larva）→ 若虫（nymph）→ 成虫（adult），每个阶段均需吸血并经历蜕皮。

**辨析**：
- *molt*（蜕皮）：节肢动物、爬行类、鸟类的自然周期行为
- *shed*（脱落）：更通用，也可指蛇蜕皮（*shed its skin*）

**例句**：
During each **molt**, immature ticks must take a blood meal from a suitable host before they can complete the transition to their next developmental stage, a process that creates multiple opportunities for pathogen acquisition and transmission.
在每次**蜕皮**过程中，未成熟蜱虫必须从适宜宿主处吸血，才能完成向下一个发育阶段的转变——这一过程创造了多次获取和传播病原体的机会。

**L1. go unnoticed**
**词性**：固定短语（动词短语）
**音标**：/ɡoʊ ʌnˈnoʊtɪst/
**英文释义（LDOCE）**：to happen or exist without anyone seeing it or realizing it is there; to pass without being detected or commented upon.
**中文释义**：未被察觉；被忽视；悄然发生
**语域**：书面 / 新闻 / 通用

**拓展**：这一表达属于"go + adj./past participle"结构（表示"进入某种状态"），类似结构：
- **go undetected** 未被检测出
- **go unreported** 未被报告
- **go unchallenged** 未受到质疑
- **go unpunished** 未受到惩处（法律/新闻常用）
- **go unheeded** （警告等）未被理会

以上均为写作高频地道表达，尤其适合学术/新闻写作。

**原文关联**："Укус можно не заметить" → "The bite **can go unnoticed**" = 叮咬可能不被察觉

**例句**：
The gradual increase in tick-borne disease reports across the region **went unnoticed** until a cluster of encephalitis cases in a single rural district triggered a formal epidemiological investigation.
该地区蜱传疾病报告数量的逐渐增加**未被察觉**，直到一个农村地区集中出现多例脑炎病例，才引发正式的流行病学调查。

**L2. set out (to do sth)**
**词性**：短语动词（phrasal verb）
**音标**：/set aʊt/
**英文释义（LDOCE）**：

- ② to start doing something with a particular aim in mind: *They set out to monitor tick populations along a fixed route.*
- ③ to explain ideas, facts, etc., in an organized way, especially in writing: *The report sets out the key findings.*

**中文释义**：
- ①出发；启程
- ②着手（做某事）；旨在（达成目标）
- ③（书面）阐述；列举（观点/事实）

**语域**：通用 / 书面 / 新闻

**辨析**：
- **set out to do** = *aim to do / intend to do*（目的性更强）

**原文关联**：研究人员"выходят на стационарные маршруты"→ "**set out** along fixed survey routes"

**例句**：

昆虫学家在黎明时分**出发**，决心在正午高温降低林缘蜱虫活动之前完成全程三公里的固定调查路线。

**L3. at random**
**词性**：介词短语（副词性）
**音标**：/æt ˈrændəm/

**语域**：通用 / 学术 / 统计 / 法律

**拓展**：
- **at random** vs. **randomly**：两者可互换，但 *at random* 更正式，多见于书面语；*randomly* 更口语化。

- **random check** 随机抽查（= spot check）

4. **《中华人民共和国传染病防治法》第二十一条 媒介生物监测义务** — 规定卫生行政部门及疾控机构对蜱虫等病媒生物开展监测的法定职责
5. **《突发公共卫生事件应急条例》蜱传疾病疫情报告机制** — 规范蜱传脑炎等乙类传染病的法定报告程序与应急响应机制
6. **Product Liability in Agricultural Imports — US UCC Article 2 (Goods) & FDA Food Safety Modernization Act (FSMA) Section 301** — 美国对进口农产品（如中国草莓）的食品安全强制检验要求及产品责任规则，适用于涉外贸易法律比较研究

## 4D. 金句积累

---

**金句一**

**原文（俄）**：
«Клещи будут заползать, но укусить могут только через час. Чем длительнее кровососание, тем больше вероятность, что если клещ инфицирован, он передаст возбудителя.»

**英译**：
"Ticks will crawl onto you, but they can only bite after an hour. The longer the blood-feeding lasts, the greater the probability that, if the tick is infected, it will transmit the pathogen."

**中译（书面优化版）**：
"蜱虫会爬上皮肤，但往往需要一个小时后才会开始叮咬。吸血时间越长，受感染蜱虫将病原体传递给宿主的概率就越大。"

**写作应用价值**：本句采用**"条件递进"结构**（the longer…, the greater…），即英语写作中高分常见的比较级并列句型（the + comparative, the + comparative），逻辑严密、表达精练，适用于科普写作、议论文逻辑展开以及风险沟通类文本。

---

**金句二**

**原文（俄）**：
«Примерно половина людей вообще не чувствуют укуса. Лишь спустя 8–12 часов, когда начинается активное кровососание, появляется дискомфорт.»

**英译**：
"Roughly half of all people feel no bite whatsoever. It is only after 8 to 12 hours — when active blood-feeding begins — that any discomfort arises."

**中译（书面优化版）**：
"大约有一半的人对叮咬毫无感觉。直至主动吸血开始后的8至12小时，才会出现不适感。"

**写作应用价值**：本句采用**倒装强调结构**（*It is only after…that…*），是英语学术写作和新闻写作中用于强调时间节点或条件的高级句式。原文数据（"约半数""8-12小时"）也示范了如何用精准量化数据增强说服力。

---

**金句三**

**原文（俄）**：
«В некотором роде клещ может даже регулировать численность очень мелких животных, например землероек.»

**英译**：
"In a sense, ticks may even function as regulators of populations of very small animals, such as shrews."

**中译（书面优化版）**：
"从某种意义上说，蜱虫甚至可以在一定程度上调控极小型动物（如鼩鼱）的种群数量。"

**写作应用价值**：本句展示了**科学论述中常用的"反直觉视角转换"手法**——将一般视为有害的生物（蜱虫）置于生态调控者的角色，以 *in a sense*（从某种意义上说）引入反直觉观点，既客观又有思想深度。适合用于议论文中展现多元视角（seeing both sides）、以及生态/科学类话题写作中拓展论点维度。
