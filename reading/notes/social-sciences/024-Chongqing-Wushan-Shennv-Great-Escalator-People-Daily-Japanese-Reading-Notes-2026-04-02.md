# 📋 模块一：翻译与全文概要

## 原文英译

**Chongqing's Giant Urban Escalator — Rising the Height of an 80-Story Building**

The administrative center of Wushan County in Chongqing is surrounded by mountains and characterized by steep terrain, forcing residents to navigate slopes and stairs on foot. The "Shennv Avenue" (Goddess Road), located on the city's central axis, features 1,136 steps connecting the upper and lower districts — a one-hour walk on foot. Those who avoid the stairs must take winding mountain roads, which are prone to traffic congestion. For years, local residents had longed for a more convenient means of vertical transit.

In February of this year, the "Shennv Grand Escalator" was installed alongside the stairway on Shennv Avenue and entered its trial-run phase. The escalator system stretches approximately 905 meters in total length and spans a vertical elevation difference of about 242 meters — equivalent to an 80-story residential building. It comprises 21 escalators, 8 elevators, and 4 moving walkways, connecting key urban nodes such as government offices, hospitals, schools, and docks. Over 50,000 residents benefit from the system, with travel time between the upper and lower districts reduced from nearly one hour to just 20 minutes. As of now, the cumulative number of citizens and tourists who have used the Shennv Grand Escalator has reached 1.32 million.

**Responding to Public Demand, Solving Mobility Challenges**

Resident Xiang Feng lives at the foot of the mountain near the riverbank, at the very bottom of the Shennv Avenue stairway. His child's school is midway up the mountain. Although the straight-line distance is barely 600 meters, climbing the stairs takes over 30 minutes.

A responsible official of Wushan County's Housing and Urban-Rural Development Committee recalls: "When the stairway was completed in 2002, we also conceived of installing motorized escalators — but at that time, neither the technology nor the funds were sufficient."

In recent years, as urban renewal has accelerated, the Shennv Grand Escalator project was placed back on the agenda.

Following principles of practicality and cost-effectiveness, Wushan County engaged specialized institutions for research and design, evaluated 12 different construction plans, and ultimately decided on the motorized escalator solution. Multiple rounds of public consultation were conducted, and it was reported that "everyone was tremendously supportive of building the grand escalator."

With public opinion firmly confirmed and careful planning and feasibility assessment completed, civil construction began in June 2024.

**Meticulous Design to Preserve the Historic Cityscape**

In order to preserve, to the greatest extent possible, the charm of this hilly city — often called a "mountain city" — and its atmosphere rich in local character, the design of the Shennv Grand Escalator incorporates a variety of ingenious features. For instance, in the middle and upper sections, escalators are connected by elevated overhead walkways, so as not to obstruct the operations of surrounding shops or pedestrian movement. In the lower riverfront section, the escalators are installed at ground level, simultaneously reducing costs and ensuring safety.

Construction faced formidable challenges: 40-degree slopes, soft karst terrain, limited workspace, and complex pipework — with much of the building material carried manually by workers. Pile foundations had to be driven 20–50 meters underground to ensure safety. To this end, the local housing and urban-rural development committee established a dedicated fund to properly resolve livelihood issues during construction, ensuring smooth project progress.

The Shennv Grand Escalator has not only solved residents' long-standing mobility difficulties, but also united the scattered public spaces of the city into a single "main artery." It occupies a vital position in the city's slow-transit system, with bus route integration taken into account, guiding residents toward greener commuting and helping to ease traffic congestion.

> 县政府**委托**一家独立工程公司在任何施工开始之前对喀斯特地层进行岩土勘察。

### L — 地道表达

#### ① **last-mile connectivity** *n. phr.*

**英文释义：** the final leg of a journey from a transportation hub to the final destination, often considered the most challenging and expensive part of transit networks to resolve (Urban Planning terminology)
**中文释义：** 最后一公里的连通性；末端接驳
**语域：** 城市规划 / 交通政策 / 商业物流 / 新闻

**拓展：**
- **last mile** 最早是电信/物流术语，指从主干网络/配送中心到终端用户的最后一段，因效率最低、成本最高而成为业界难题
- 现广泛用于城市交通规划：如何解决地铁站到居民家门口的"最后一公里"
- 类似表达：*first-and-last-mile problem*（首末端接驳问题）

**例句：**
> The escalator project directly tackles the city's most persistent **last-mile connectivity** challenge, bridging the gap between the riverfront transit hub and the hilltop residential and commercial districts.
> 扶梯项目直接破解了该城市最顽固的**最后一公里连通**难题，在滨江交通枢纽与山顶居住和商业区之间架起了桥梁。

---

#### ② **green commuting** *n. phr.*

**英文释义：** the practice of traveling to work or school using environmentally friendly means such as walking, cycling, or public transport, rather than private motor vehicles
**中文释义：** 绿色出行；低碳通勤
**语域：** 新闻 / 政策 / 环保

**近义表达：**
- *sustainable mobility* 可持续交通
- *low-carbon transport* 低碳交通
- *active travel* （英式）步行/骑行等主动出行方式
- *eco-friendly commute* 环保通勤

**例句：**
> By integrating seamlessly with local bus services, the escalator system actively promotes **green commuting** and is projected to reduce private vehicle use in the central district by an estimated 15 percent.
> 通过与本地公交服务的无缝衔接，扶梯系统积极引导**绿色出行**，预计将使中心城区私家车使用率降低约15%。

---

#### ③ **hairpin (bend/turn)** /ˈheərpɪn/ *n. / adj.*

**英文释义：** a very sharp curve in a road, especially on a steep hill (Cambridge)
**中文释义：** 急弯；（山路上的）U形弯道；发卡弯
**语域：** 非正式 / 新闻 / 日常 / 赛车

**常见词组：**
- *hairpin bend / hairpin turn / hairpin curve* 急转弯；发卡弯
- *hairpin road* 蜿蜒急弯山路
- *hairpin corner* 急弯路口（赛车术语）

**拓展：**
- 来源于 **hairpin**（发夹），因形状类似发夹的U形而得名
- 在描述山地道路时极为地道，比 *winding road* 或 *zigzag road* 更生动具体
- 可用于比喻"急转直下的形势"，如 *a hairpin turn in policy*（政策的急剧转向）

**例句：**
> Residents who wished to avoid the 1,136-step stairway previously had no choice but to take a long detour along **hairpin bends** prone to traffic snarls, a journey that could take well over an hour in peak hours.
> 不愿走1136级台阶的居民此前别无选择，只能绕行一段容易发生拥堵的**急弯山路**，高峰时段这段路程往往超过一个小时。

---

#### ④ **pile foundation** / **pile-driving** *n. phr.*

**英文释义：** a type of deep foundation in which long cylindrical columns (piles) are driven into the ground to transfer structural loads to deeper, more stable soil or rock layers (Engineering)
**中文释义：** 桩基础；打桩（施工工艺）
**语域：** 工程技术 / 建筑 / 新闻

**常见词组：**
- *drive/sink a pile* 打桩
- *pile foundation* 桩基础
- *pile-driving equipment* 打桩设备
- *bored pile* 钻孔桩；*driven pile* 打入桩

**拓展：**
- 在软弱地基（如喀斯特地层、软土地基）上建造重型结构时，桩基础是最常见的解决方案
- 深度达20-50米的桩基（如本文所述）属于深层桩基（deep pile foundation），工程难度和造价显著高于浅基础

**例句：**
> Given the soft and unpredictable nature of the **karst** subsoil, engineers specified **pile foundations** driven to depths of between 20 and 50 meters to ensure that the escalator structure would remain stable under all weather and load conditions.
> 鉴于**喀斯特**地层土质疏软且难以预测，工程师规定须将**桩基础**打至地下20至50米深处，以确保扶梯结构在各种气候和荷载条件下保持稳定。

---

#### ⑤ **slow-transit / slow traffic system** *n. phr.*

**英文释义：** an urban transportation concept emphasizing pedestrian walkways, cycling lanes, escalators, and other non-motorized infrastructure that prioritizes human-scale mobility over vehicle speed (Urban Planning)
**中文释义：** 慢行交通系统；步行与非机动交通体系
**语域：** 城市规划 / 政策 / 新闻

**近义表达：**
- *non-motorized transport (NMT)* 非机动化交通
- *active transport / active travel* 主动交通（强调步行、骑行等）
- *pedestrian-oriented design* 以行人为本的设计
- *walkability* 步行友好性

**拓展：**
- "慢行交通"是中国城市规划政策中的重要概念，强调在城市内部构建以步行、自行车、电动扶梯等为主的非机动交通网络，与"快行系统"（motorized traffic）相对
- 与 **Transit-Oriented Development (TOD)**（公交导向开发）理念密切相关

**例句：**
> Urban planners are increasingly recognizing that a well-designed **slow-transit system** — one that integrates escalators, cycle paths, and shaded walkways — can do more for quality of life in hilly cities than any number of additional vehicle lanes.
> 城市规划者越来越认识到，一套设计精良的**慢行交通系统**——将扶梯、自行车道与遮阴步行道整合在一起——对山地城市居民生活质量的提升，远比新增多少条机动车道都更为有效。
