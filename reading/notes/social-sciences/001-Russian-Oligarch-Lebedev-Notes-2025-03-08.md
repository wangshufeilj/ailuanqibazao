### 🔵 全文精读笔记 (Full-Text Intensive Reading)

#### 🔹 Headline & Intro
🔹 **Russian Oligarch Alexander Lebedev / Displays / His Czar Survival Skills.**
🔸 俄罗斯寡头亚历山大·列别杰夫 / 展示了 / 他的沙皇式生存技巧。

> 1. **Oligarch** [C] (R/T): a very rich and powerful person, especially a Russian businessman who became rich after the end of the Soviet Union. **寡头**。
>    *   *Example*: The country's economy is dominated by a few powerful **oligarchs**. (该国经济被少数几位强大的**寡头**所控制。)
> 2. **Czar** [C] (R/S): (also Tsar) the title of the former Russian emperors; or a person with great power in a particular area. **沙皇；大权在握者**。
>    *   *Example*: The government appointed a new energy **czar** to manage the crisis. (政府任命了一位新的能源**沙皇/主管**来应对危机。)
> 3. **Survival** [U/C] (W/R): the state of continuing to live or exist. **生存，幸存**。
>    *   *Example*: The small company's **survival** depends on securing new investment. (这家小公司的**生存**取决于能否获得新的投资。)

🔹 **Alexander Lebedev and other Russian oligarchs / are displaying / highly imaginative survival skills.**
🔸 亚历山大·列别杰夫和其他俄罗斯寡头 / 正在展示 / 极具想象力的生存技巧。

> 1. **Imaginative** (adj.) (W/L): containing new and interesting ideas; creative. **富有想象力的，创新的**。
>    *   *Example*: The architect presented an **imaginative** solution to the space problem. (建筑师针对空间问题提出了一个**富有想象力的**解决方案。)
> 2. **Display** (v.) (R/T): to show a feeling, quality, or ability. **表现，展示**。
>    *   *Example*: She **displayed** great courage during the difficult negotiations. (她在艰难的谈判中表现出了极大的勇气。)

#### 🔹 Paragraph 1
🔹 **Alexander Lebedev, a former-KGB-agent-turned-oligarch, / may have lost / almost two thirds of his multibillion-dollar fortune / during the recent economic crisis in Russia, / but his swagger / is / still intact.**
🔸 亚历山大·列别杰夫，这位由前克格勃特工变身的寡头，/ 可能已经失去了 / 他数十亿美元财富的近三分之二 / 在最近俄罗斯的经济危机期间，/ 但他的那份傲慢气势 / 依然 / 完好无损。

*   **注：KGB (克格勃)**：前苏联的国家安全委员会，是当时世界上最强大的情报机构之一。

> 1. **Intact** (adj.) (R/T): not damaged or destroyed; complete. **完好无损的**。
>    *   *Example*: Despite the rough transport, the delicate glassware remained **intact**. (尽管运输过程颠簸，精美的玻璃制品依然**完好无损**。)
> 2. **Swagger** [U] (S/L): a way of walking or behaving that shows you are very confident. **大摇大摆，傲慢自大的神态**。
>    *   *Example*: He entered the boardroom with a confident **swagger**. (他带着自信而**傲慢的神态**走进董事会会议室。)
> 3. **Fortune** [C] (W/R): a very large amount of money. **巨额财产，大笔钱**。
>    *   *Example*: He made a **fortune** in the real estate market. (他在房地产市场赚了一笔**巨款**。)
> 4. **Multibillion-dollar** (adj.) (W): involving several billion dollars. **数以十亿计美元的**。
>    *   *Example*: The two tech giants signed a **multibillion-dollar** merger agreement. (两家科技巨头签署了**数以十亿计美元的**合并协议。)

🔹 **"We / are talking / mostly about paper losses," / scoffs / the 50-year-old, silver-haired Lebedev, / clad in jeans and sneakers / during an early autumn interview at his headquarters, a 19th-century villa / perched / at a bend on the Moscow River.**
🔸 "我们 / 谈论的 / 主要是账面亏损，" / 嘲讽道 / 这位50岁、满头银发的列别杰夫，/ 他身穿牛仔裤和运动鞋 / 在初秋于其总部接受采访时，那是一座19世纪的别墅 / 坐落于 / 莫斯科河的一个拐弯处。

> 1. **Scoff** (v.) (R/L): to talk about something in a way that shows you think it is silly or empty. **嘲笑，蔑视**。
>    *   *Example*: Critics **scoffed** at the idea that the project would be finished on time. (批评者对该项目能按时完成的想法**嗤之以鼻**。)
> 2. **Clad** (adj.) (R/S): (formal) wearing a particular type of clothing. **身着……的**。
>    *   *Example*: The mountains were **clad** in a thick blanket of snow. (群山**覆盖**着厚厚的白雪。)
> 3. **Perched** (adj./v.) (R/L): to be in a position on top of something or on the edge of something. **位于高处的，坐落于**。
>    *   *Example*: The hotel is **perched** on a cliff overlooking the ocean. (酒店**坐落于**悬崖之上，俯瞰大海。)
> 4. **Paper loss** (n.) (R/T): a loss that has occurred but has not yet been realized through a transaction. **账面亏损**。
>    *   *Example*: The recent stock market dip resulted in significant **paper losses** for many investors. (最近的股市下跌导致许多投资者出现了严重的**账面亏损**。)

🔹 **Except for a barely profitable showing by his National Reserve Bank in the first half of this year, / Lebedev / acknowledges / that red ink / has soaked / the rest of his far-flung businesses — including two airlines, a five-star resort in Ukraine, Russian farmland, newspapers in Moscow and London, various construction and real estate projects and a few investments in telecommunications and oil extraction.**

> 3. **Profitable** (adj.) (W/R): resulting in or likely to result in a profit or a benefit. **盈利的，有益的**。
> 4. **Acknowledge** (v.) (W/T): to admit or accept that something is true or that a situation exists. **承认，认可**。
> 5. **Extraction** [U] (R/T): the process of removing something, especially by force. **开采，提取**。

#### 🔹 Paragraph 3
🔹 **Just how badly all of these ventures are faring, / Lebedev / won't say.**
🔸 这些投资项目的表现究竟有多糟糕，/ 列别杰夫 / 不愿透露。

🔸 但是 / 根据《福布斯》的数据 / 他的财富 / 已经缩水 / 从2008年初的37亿美元减少到今年的13亿美元。

*   **注：Forbes (福布斯)**：美国著名的商业杂志，以其发布的各种富豪榜单闻名。

> 1. **Shrink** (v.) (W/R): to become or to make something smaller in amount, size, or value. **缩水，减少** (过去式: shrank, 过去分词: shrunk/shrunken)。

🔹 **He / saved / his single largest investment, a 28 percent stake in Russia's state-controlled airline, Aeroflot, / with refinancing from his own bank, NRB.**
🔸 他 / 挽救了 / 他单一最大的投资，即俄罗斯国家控股的航空公司——俄罗斯航空（Aeroflot）28%的股份，/ 通过他自己的银行，即国家储备银行（NRB）的再融资。

*   **注：Aeroflot (俄罗斯航空)**：俄罗斯的国家航空公司。

> 1. **Stake** [C] (W/R/T): if you have a stake in a business, you have invested money in it. **股份，利害关系**。
> 2. **Refinancing** [U] (R/T): providing new financing for something, especially at a lower rate of interest. **再融资**。

🔹 **And this summer, / Lebedev / fired / the top managers of the National Reserve Corp., his 85 percent–owned holding company, / claiming / their expense accounts were bloated and that "they were behaving like state bureaucrats."**
🔸 而在今年夏天，/ 列别杰夫 / 解雇了 / 国家储备公司（NRC）的高层管理人员，这是他持有85%股份的控股公司，/ 声称 / 他们的报销账目臃肿，并且"他们的表现就像国家官僚一样"。

> 1. **Bloated** (adj.) (R/S/L): full of liquid, gas, or food; or (of an organization) having too many employees or too much cost. **臃肿的，膨胀的**。
> 2. **Bureaucrat** [C] (R/T): someone who works in an office and is part of a system of government or management. **官僚**。
> 3. **Holding company** [C] (R): a company that is formed to buy and own the shares of other companies, which it then controls. **控股公司**。

🔹 **NRC's 2008 annual report, which should have been ready by June, / hadn't been published / by October.**
🔸 国家储备公司（NRC）的2008年年度报告，原本应在6月前准备就绪，/ 直到10月 / 尚未发布。

#### 🔹 Paragraph 4
🔹 **Lebedev and his fellow oligarchs / have unquestionably been walloped / by the crisis:**
🔸 列别杰夫和他的寡头同行们 / 毫无疑问遭受了重创 / 受到这次危机的冲击：

> 1. **Unquestionably** (adv.) (W/L): used to emphasize that something is certainly true. **毫无疑问地，确定地**。
> 2. **Wallop** (v.) (R/S/L): (informal) to hit someone or something very hard; to defeat someone completely. **重击，重创**。

🔹 **According to Russian business magazine Finans, / the 25 richest oligarchs / collectively lost / more than $220 billion in 2008.**
🔸 根据俄罗斯商业杂志《金融》（Finans）的数据，/ 25位最富有的寡头 / 总共损失了 / 2008年超过2200亿美元。

> 1. **Collectively** (adv.) (W/T): as a group; together. **共同地，集体地**。

🔹 **Many / were forced / to beg the Kremlin to compel state banks to extend bailout loans to their empires, / spawning / widespread derision.**
🔸 许多人 / 被迫 / 恳求克里姆林宫强迫国有银行向他们的帝国发放救助贷款，/ 这引发了 / 广泛的嘲笑。

*   **注：Kremlin (克里姆林宫)**：俄罗斯联邦政府行政总部所在地，代指俄罗斯政府。

> 1. **Compel** (v.) (W/R/T): to force someone to do something. **强迫，迫使**。
> 2. **Bailout** [C] (R/T): an act of giving money to a company, foreign country, etc. that has very serious financial problems. **救助，融资救援**。
> 3. **Spawn** (v.) (R/L): to cause something to happen or be created. **产卵；引发，产生（大量）**。
> 4. **Derision** [U] (R/T): when you show that you think someone or something is stupid or silly. **嘲笑，嘲讽**。

🔹 **"The current joke / is / that oligarchs no longer really own their enterprises, they are only allowed to borrow them," / says / Anders Åslund, senior fellow at the Peterson Institute for International Economics in Washington.**
🔸 "现在的笑话 / 是 / 寡头们不再真正拥有他们的企业，他们只是被允许借用它们，" / 说道 / 安德斯·阿斯伦德，华盛顿彼得森国际经济研究所的高级研究员。

#### 🔹 Paragraph 5

🔸 尽管如此，/ 尽管损失巨大 / 很少有俄罗斯寡头 / 消失 / 从经济版图上——这得益于创造性的危机管理策略，这些策略像他们的商业利益一样多种多样。

> 1. **Landscape** [C] (R/S/L): a particular area of activity or the overall situation. **（活动）领域，版图，景象**。
> 2. **Varied** (adj.) (W/R): consisting of many different types of things or people. **各种各样的，变化的**。

🔹 **Case in point: / Aluminum and nickel king Oleg Deripaska / had accrued / a personal fortune of almost $50 billion by early 2008, making him the richest Russian.**
🔸 典型的例子是：/ 铝镍大王奥列格·德里帕斯卡 / 已经积累了 / 到2008年初近500亿美元的个人财富，使他成为俄罗斯首富。

> 1. **Case in point** (phrase) (W/L): a clear example of the problem, situation, etc. that you are discussing. **明证，典型的例子**。
> 2. **Accrue** (v.) (W/R/S): to gradually increase in amount over a period of time. **积累，增加**。

🔹 **But / as metal prices plummeted and his stock-market-backed bank loans soured / he / was soon / the most indebted Russian.**
🔸 但是 / 随着金属价格暴跌以及他以股票市场为担保的银行贷款恶化 / 他 / 很快成为 / 负债最重的俄罗斯人。

> 1. **Plummet** (v.) (W/R/L): to fall very quickly and suddenly. **暴跌，垂直落下**。
> 2. **Sour** (v.) (R/S/L): to stop being pleasant or friendly; (of a loan) to become bad or unlikely to be repaid. **变坏，恶化**。
> 3. **Indebted** (adj.) (W/R/T): owing money; or feeling grateful to someone. **负债的；感激的**。

🔹 **Nonetheless, / he / has successfully appealed / for state bank assistance on the grounds that his empire is too big to be allowed to fail.**
🔸 尽管如此，/ 他 / 成功申诉了 / 国家银行的援助，理由是他的帝国太大而不能倒闭。

> 1. **Appeal** (v.) (R/T): to make a serious or formal request, especially to the public or to a person in authority. **呼吁，申诉**。
> 2. **On the grounds that** (phrase) (W/L): for the reason that. **由于……理由**。

#### 🔹 Paragraph 6

🔸 米哈伊尔·弗里德曼，根据《福布斯》的数据，他去年的净资产为208亿美元，/ 已经恳求 / 他的债权人宽恕，同时对他自己的债务人却毫不留情。

> 3. **Debtor** [C] (R/T): a person, group, or organization that owes money. **债务人**。

🔸 这种先见之明 / 帮助推升了 / 普罗霍罗夫登上了《金融》杂志俄罗斯富豪榜的首位，其估计财富为149亿美元。

> 1. **Propel** (v.) (R/L): to move, drive, or push something forward. **推进，驱动**。

> 5. **Outlast** (v.) (R/L): to continue to exist or be effective for a longer time than something else. **比……长久，度过**。

🔸 "令人惊讶的是 / 考虑到其财务创伤的深度，这些寡头在多大程度上生存了下来，" / 说道 / 罗里·麦克法夸尔，高盛公司（Goldman, Sachs & Co.）驻莫斯科的一位经济学家。

#### 🔹 Paragraph 9 (Russian Survival?)
🔹 **Most Russian oligarchs / gained / their initial fortunes through the privatizations of state companies following the Soviet Union's dissolution in 1991.**
🔸 大多数俄罗斯寡头 / 获得 / 他们的第一桶金是通过1991年苏联解体后国有企业的私有化。

> 1. **Privatization** [U/C] (R/T): the sale of a business or industry that was owned and managed by the government. **私有化**。

🔹 **Large foreign investors / sought out / oligarchs as business partners who could act as political intermediaries with the Russian government.**
🔸 大型外国投资者 / 寻找 / 寡头作为商业伙伴，这些伙伴可以充当与俄罗斯政府沟通的政治中间人。

> 1. **Seek out** (v. phr.) (W/L): to look for someone or something, especially for a long time until you find them. **寻找，寻找并找到**。
> 2. **Intermediary** [C] (R/T): a person or organization that helps other people or organizations to make an agreement by being a means of communication between them. **中间人，调停者**。

🔹 **But / when the crisis hit, / the oligarchs / were overwhelmed / by huge debts racked up by borrowing heavily to expand their businesses.**
🔸 但是 / 当危机来临时，/ 寡头们 / 被击垮了 / 由于为了扩张业务而大举借债所积累的巨额债务。
