**Debts of Video Game Studio "Lesta Games" Exceed 11 Billion Rubles**

Enforcement proceedings have been initiated against the entities of video game studio "Lesta Games," with total debt recovery claims exceeding 11 billion rubles, according to data from Russia's Federal Bailiff Service (FSSP). The primary enforcement proceedings were initiated in favor of the state budget, which may complicate the further divestiture of assets currently under the management of the Federal Agency for State Property Management (Rosimushchestvo).

According to the FSSP database, the debt of one of the legal entities of "Lesta Games" (the developer of *World of Tanks*, *World of Warships*, etc.) amounts to 11.4 billion rubles. This relates to enforcement proceedings initiated on March 5, 2026 against LLC "Lesta Games Agency," on the grounds of "recovery in favor of the Russian federal budget." In addition, enforcement proceedings of 239.7 million rubles have been initiated against another legal entity of the studio, LLC "Lesta."

Originally, "Lesta" was the Russian subsidiary of the Belarusian studio Wargaming (*World of Tanks*, *World of Warships*, *WoT Blitz*) and was solely responsible for developing *World of Warships*. However, following the outbreak of hostilities in Ukraine and the subsequent imposition of sanctions, Wargaming announced its withdrawal from the Russian and Belarusian markets and divested from "Lesta." The latter passed into the ownership of Malik Khatazhayev (who is affiliated with an extremist organization banned in Russia, per *Kommersant* dated July 8, 2022). In September 2022, he transferred his stake to the Chinese company Lesta Hong Kong Limited, but reclaimed it in October 2024. In mid-July 2025, following litigation, three companies associated with "Lesta" (LLC "Lesta," LLC "Lesta Games Agency," LLC "Lesta Games Moscow") were transferred into state ownership under Rosimushchestvo, and Boris Dobrodeev — formerly CEO of VKontakte and the VK internet holding — was appointed general director. The company's assets were valued at 135 billion rubles according to materials from the Prosecutor General's Office (see *Kommersant*, June 3, 2025).

The press office of "Lesta Games" stated that "this event has no impact on the operation of our games or our plans." Two sources at major development studios offered one possible explanation for the scale of the recovery claims: it may represent an attempt to recoup funds allegedly underpaid during the nationalization of the studio.

For instance, in early autumn, one of Russia's largest video game festivals, "Red Expo," shut down; its organizers attributed the closure to the withdrawal of key sponsor "Lesta" (see *Kommersant*, September 12, 2025). In March of the current year, the Moscow Arbitration Court found evidence of fund transfers out of the studio's accounts after interim measures had been imposed pursuant to the Prosecutor General's claim, as reported by RBC. "Lesta" was able to demonstrate that the studio's former management had used festival sponsorship funding to channel money out of the company's accounts after those accounts had already been subject to restrictions — the company had managed to transfer an advance of 150 million rubles.

Anna Barabash, CEO of law firm Enterprise Legal Solutions, believes the debt recovery effort is being pursued now because doing so will become significantly more difficult after a change of beneficial owners. Furthermore, she noted, the existence of active enforcement proceedings will complicate the sale of "Lesta." The sum of 11.4 billion rubles is comparable to the company's annual revenue, and a lump-sum payment of this magnitude could lead to default and bankruptcy. At the same time, a source familiar with the matter noted that "Lesta" continues to actively recruit staff, which indirectly suggests there are no plans to dissolve the team in the medium term.

  - 区分：**comparable**（类似程度可比较）vs. **comparative**（通过比较得出的）

- **词性：** v.（及物）

- **常见句式：** *sth. complicates sth.*；*complicate the picture/matter/situation*

  - *complicating factor*（复杂化因素）

**24. sponsor / sponsorship** /ˈspɒnsər/

- **语域：** 商业/新闻/正式

**27. dissolve** /dɪˈzɒlv/

- **语域：** 法律/正式/商业

**28. precede** / **in the wake of** /ɪn ðə weɪk əv/
- **词性：** prep. phr.（习语）
- **英文释义：** As a result of, or following closely after a significant event. *(Longman)*
- **中文释义：** 在……之后；因……而引发
- **语域：** 书面/新闻/正式
- **⚠️ 引申义：** "In the wake of" 原义为船只驶过后留下的"尾迹"，引申为某重大事件发生之后随即出现的后续影响，语义上隐含"余波"或"直接后果"。
- **拓展：**
  - *in the aftermath of*（在……之后，强调破坏性后果）
  - *following*（较中性，仅表时间顺序）
- **例句：**
  > **In the wake of** the sanctions announcement, several major gaming companies accelerated their plans to **exit** the Russian market.
  > **在**制裁公告发布**之后**，数家主要游戏公司加快了退出俄罗斯市场的计划。

- **拓展：**

### L — 地道表达

---

**31. in the medium term** / **over the medium term**
- 见S类第26条，此处作为地道搭配单独强调
- **地道用法：** 英语中表示"中期内"，必须搭配介词 **in** 或 **over**，不可说 "in the medium-term period"（累赘），正确说法：*in the medium term*。
- **例句：**
  > **In the medium term**, the company's survival will depend on whether the state can negotiate a structured settlement rather than demanding immediate full repayment.
  > **就中期而言**，公司的生存将取决于国家能否协商出一个分期还款方案，而非要求立即全额偿还。

---

**32. at the brink of bankruptcy**（= on the brink of bankruptcy）
- **地道表达：** *push / teeter on the brink of bankruptcy/collapse/insolvency*
- **中文释义：** 处于破产边缘；濒临倒闭
- **语域：** 新闻/商业/书面
- **同义搭配：** *on the verge of, on the edge of, teetering on the precipice of*
- **例句：**
  > Industry experts warned that the combined **enforcement claims** could **push** the company **to the brink of** insolvency, forcing a rapid restructuring or dissolution.
  > 行业专家警告，累积的**追债金额**可能将公司**推至**破产**边缘**，迫使其迅速重组或解散。

---

**33. complicate the picture**
- **地道表达：** *complicate the picture / muddy the waters / cloud the issue*
- **中文释义：** 使局面更复杂；使局势更模糊
- **语域：** 新闻/正式/书面
- **例句：**
  > The discovery of additional undisclosed liabilities has **complicated the picture** further for potential investors considering an acquisition of the studio.
  > 新发现的未披露负债进一步**复杂化了**考虑收购该工作室的潜在投资者所面临的**局面**。

---

**34. aggressive hiring / aggressive recruitment**
- **地道表达（商业英语）：** *aggressive hiring drive / recruitment push*
- **中文释义：** 大力招聘；积极扩招（非"侵略性"，此处"aggressive"意为"积极进取的"）
- **⚠️ 易误译：** "aggressive"在商业语境中通常是**褒义**，意为"积极主动的、大规模推进的"，而非日常语境中的"侵略性的"，翻译时须注意语义语境转换。
- **语域：** 商业新闻/口语-正式过渡
- **例句：**
  > Despite ongoing legal turbulence, the company has maintained an **aggressive hiring** strategy, adding over two hundred engineers in the past quarter alone.
  > 尽管法律纠纷持续不断，该公司仍坚持**大力招聘**战略，仅上一季度就新增逾两百名工程师。

**35. shed light on / cast light on**
- **地道表达：** *shed light on sth.*（揭示；使（某事）变得清晰）
- **中文释义：** 阐明；揭示；使……得以理解
- **语域：** 书面/新闻/学术
- **同义：** *illuminate, clarify, elucidate*
- **例句：**
  > The court documents **shed light on** the complex web of **fund transfers** that occurred between the studio and its affiliated entities in the months before nationalization.
  > 法庭文件**揭示了**国有化前数月内，该工作室与其关联实体之间发生的复杂**资金流转**网络。

---

**36. to no avail** / **to little avail**
- **地道表达（书面/正式）：** *to no avail* = without success; despite efforts, the outcome was not achieved.
- **中文释义：** 徒劳无功；无济于事
- **语域：** 书面/正式
- **例句：**
  > The company **contested** the enforcement proceedings in court, but its arguments proved **to little avail**, as the judge upheld the state's claims in full.
  > 该公司在法庭上**对抗**强制执行程序，但其辩词**收效甚微**，法官全面支持了国家的诉求。

---

**37. leave (sb.) with no option but to**
- **地道表达：** *leave the company with no option but to file for bankruptcy*（让公司别无选择，只能申请破产）
- **中文释义：** 使……别无他法，只能……
- **语域：** 新闻/书面/正式
- **例句：**
  > A sudden demand for full repayment could **leave the company with no option but to** seek **creditor protection** under insolvency law.
  > 突然要求全额偿还可能使该公司**别无选择，只能**依据破产法申请**债权人保护**。

---

**38. lay off / downsize**
- **地道表达（商业语境）：** *lay off staff* = to dismiss employees due to financial difficulties (AmE / general)；*downsize* = to reduce the size of a company or workforce
- **中文释义：** 裁员；缩减规模
- **语域：** 商业/新闻
- **对应原文：** "распустить команду"（解散团队）
- **拓展：** *restructure, right-size*（"right-size"是"downsize"的委婉说法）
- **例句：**
  > The studio's decision to **continue hiring** rather than **lay off** staff sent a signal that its new management was committed to long-term development, not a managed wind-down.
  > 工作室决定**继续招聘**而非**裁员**，向外界传递出新管理层致力于长期发展而非有序收盘的信号。
