This article examines how Russian newsprint producers respond to the global digital shift and shrinking demand by adjusting strategies, seeking export markets, and diversifying products.

● 分段脉络 (Paragraph-level Logic):
1. 现状综述 (Introduction): 全球新闻纸需求大幅缩减，俄罗斯企业依靠出口暂时维持。
   Current State: Global newsprint demand is shrinking significantly; Russian firms survive via exports.

   Data Analysis: Capacity cuts and consumption decline in global and Russian markets.

- 企业端：产能利用率（Capacity utilization）的维持依赖于向中国出口包装用途的纸张。

🔹 **The newspaper / endures**

> *解析：此处标题双关，意指纸媒在数字化冲击下的顽强生存状态。*

🔻 **Будущее сегмента газетной бумаги во всем мире оказалось под вопросом в условиях серьезного сокращения потребления из-за перехода на электронные носители.**

> **Decline** /dɪˈklaɪn/ (noun): A continuous decrease in the number, value, or quality of something. (衰落，下降).

> **Pulp** /pʌlp/ (noun): A soft, wet mass of wood fibers used for making paper. (纸浆).

## 英文翻译

**Holding On: How Russia's Newsprint Producers Are Adapting to the Crisis**
*Kommersant Business Section, February 12, 2026*

The future of the newsprint segment globally has been called into question amid a severe contraction in consumption driven by the digital transition. Yet despite aggressive capacity shutdowns in Western countries, Russian producers have so far shown no intention of cutting output volumes, pinning their hopes on export markets. For now, some mills are even registering a buying frenzy triggered by shortages in certain countries—but they are already bracing for a market downturn.

**A Brief History**
According to the Center for Systemic Solutions (CSR), the global newsprint market has been declining at roughly 7% per year over the past decade, outpacing all other segments of the pulp and paper industry (PPI). Total capacity shrank by more than 4.6 million tonnes between 2020 and 2024. While the pace of decline has slowed in recent years, mill closures continue. The CSR estimates that 380,000 tonnes of capacity will be cut in 2025, followed by 100,000–200,000 tonnes annually in 2026–2027.

"The overarching trend is tied to the global move away from paper as an information medium. Many news outlets are going entirely digital. Advertising is increasingly migrating from print to online channels. As a result, demand for newsprint is inevitably and rapidly falling," analysts note.

According to Andrei Malakhov, commercial director of Prime Print Moscow, print-run volumes for Russian print media have declined more than threefold since 2012, and more than twofold since 2016, to 2.9 million copies.

Anton Potapov, head of market research and analytics at Taiga Group (which includes the Kondopoga Pulp and Paper Mill), says that the pace of decline in newsprint consumption on the Russian domestic market in 2025 exceeded that of European countries and North America. Domestic demand fell 17.8% year-on-year to 145,000 tonnes. Russian newsprint exports shrank by 13.3% to 705,000 tonnes.

According to Rosstat, output of newsprint in Russia fell 10.1% last year to 795,000 tonnes. By comparison, overall paper and board production rose 0.6%, output of corrugated cardboard boxes fell 0.4%, and paper and board labels fell 5.9%.

**The Newsprint Quartet**
Things were once very different. Newsprint led the field not only among graphic papers but across the entire PPI. Growing newspaper print runs and cheap book printing drove demand up by around 1 million tonnes every three years through the early 2000s, according to CSR estimates. Newsprint is the most affordable paper grade. Its key characteristics are low opacity and relatively low brightness, around 60%. The full range of newsprint is divided into several main grades: standard (used for newspapers, leaflets, and other short-lifespan products), improved high-brightness grades (more often used for advertising materials), and bulky paper (in demand for books, textbooks, and workbooks).

All newsprint in Russia is produced by four mills located in the Northwestern and Volga federal districts.

- The market leader with a share of roughly 60% is the Kondopoga Pulp and Paper Mill (capacity: approximately 500,000 tonnes).
- The remaining output is shared by Syktyvkar Forest Industrial Complex (SLIC), Solikamskbumprom, and JSC Volga (which operates the Balakhna Paper Mill).

In recent years, virtually all industry players have posted negative results against a backdrop of market contraction and falling demand. Volga's net profit fell 2.4 times in 2024 to 770 million rubles on revenue of 14.5 billion rubles. The Kondopoga Mill posted a loss of 720 million rubles on revenue of 28.8 billion rubles. Solikamskbumprom recorded a loss of 1.9 billion rubles on revenue of 16 billion rubles.

According to sources familiar with the matter, after Kondopoga came under Taiga Group's ownership, the mill secured new foreign contracts and returned to profitability in January 2026. At the same time, the difficult situation at Solikamskbumprom has been discussed on multiple occasions with creditor banks and relevant government agencies.

According to information obtained by Kommersant, a meeting was held at the Ministry of Industry and Trade in late 2025, at which Solikamskbumprom requested debt restructuring from December 2025 to end-2026/2027 and sought bank support to continue operations. Based on the minutes of the meeting reviewed by Kommersant, most creditors are willing to restructure the debt but are refusing to provide further financing, demanding that an investor be found. A source familiar with the situation says that Solikamskbumprom also recorded a substantial loss in 2025, and that disagreements among shareholders are compounding the difficulty of the enterprise's situation.

**Two Products Better Than One**
The fundamental problem facing the mills is that they were originally designed to produce a single product. The exception is SLIC. Historically oriented toward commodity paper grades—newsprint and offset—this mill has started producing office paper and developed a growth strategy that includes an accelerated transition to biodegradable packaging and industrial sanitary paper, demand for which is growing steadily. SLIC also made a bet on expanding pulp production capacity, which is in demand in the chemical and textile industries. Since 2019, Russian mills have been producing interliners—newsprint dyed to match the color of corrugated linerboard—which found a ready market in China as e-commerce parcel volumes surged.

According to Potapov, interliners, bulky printing grades, colored cover paper, and so on are produced on the same paper machines as the main product, meaning that diversification helps maintain acceptable equipment utilization rates even as newsprint output steadily falls. Taiga Group, he added, is today focused on finding new solutions in line with prevailing industry trends, as well as on realizing product and market synergies from managing its asset portfolio. Other newsprint producers declined to comment.

**The Last Bastion**
Foreign demand for newsprint remains for now, and Russian producers currently export the majority of their output. Globally, Russia has traditionally ranked third in newsprint production after Canada and Japan, with a roughly 10% share of world output. In export volumes, Russia ranks second, a distant second only to Canada. According to CSR data, Kondopoga exported 379,000 tonnes in 2024, Volga 110,000 tonnes, and SLIC and Solikamskbumprom approximately 100,000 tonnes each.

Among the world's main newsprint importers are European countries (37%), India (13%), the United States (12%), and China (7%). For Russian producers, following the 2022 cessation of deliveries to unfriendly countries, the key export destinations have been China, India, and Turkey. Russian suppliers hold a substantial share in those markets. As of 2023, Russia's share of China's newsprint imports exceeded 80%, and its share of total newsprint consumption in China stood at 31%.

In recent years, China has significantly reduced its newsprint imports from Canada, replacing them with Russian supplies. The CSR notes that the Chinese market has seen a trend toward reduced domestic newsprint production and a growing reliance on imports—though overall consumption continues to decline.

India shows a similar pattern: declining domestic production offset by imported supply. But Russia faces stiff competition there from Canada. Analysts cite logistics as the primary headwind, linked to the broader international environment. At the same time, India remains one of Russia's key markets, and future demand dynamics there, along with the competitive battle for market share against Canada, will substantially determine total demand for Russian-produced newsprint, CSR analysts conclude.

IndexBox agency notes that markets where demand growth is expected also remain. In particular, the Middle East and North Africa: newsprint consumption there may grow to 447,000 tonnes by 2035, with average annual growth of 2.1% from 2024 to 2035. In 2024, regional newsprint demand stood at 356,000 tonnes—still 66% below the 2013 record high.

**The Salvation of Scarcity**
Meanwhile, the global contraction in newsprint capacity over the past two years has helped support prices. The accelerated cutback in Canadian output has created a temporary shortage in importing countries. "Because the pace of capacity reduction is outrunning the pace of demand decline, Russian paper is riding high right now. Neighboring countries are scrambling to place orders in Russia, because they keep getting notices of production shutdowns from mills in Canada and Europe. This is creating a buying frenzy like in 2022, and prices are starting to rise," one market participant explains.

According to producers, prices for Russian newsprint surged sharply in 2024 to over 33,000 rubles per tonne, ending a prolonged period of relative stability.

The dynamics were driven by general price indexation in line with inflation indicators, according to CSR. Multiple cost increases are noted, including labor costs, the procurement of imported spare parts for equipment and machine clothing, chemicals, and other inputs.

At the same time, logistics cost increases are outpacing price gains, meaning that mills are not benefiting from higher selling prices, a source tells Kommersant. Moreover, as soon as falling demand catches up with supply, the market will swing to buyers and prices will fall sharply—especially if in the interim, print shops have built up strategic stockpiles.

**30. bracing for**
- **音标**：/ˈbreɪsɪŋ fɔːr/ v. phrase (brace for)
- **英文释义**：to prepare yourself for something difficult or unpleasant that is likely to happen
- **中文释义**：为…做好准备；绷紧神经应对；提前备战
- **语域**：新闻 / 书面 / 通用
- **同义词**：preparing for, girding for, steeling oneself for, gearing up for
- **常见词组**：*brace for impact / a downturn / bad news / a storm; brace oneself for*
- **拓展**：**brace** 原义为"支撑，固定"，引申为"使做好准备"。新闻英语中 *brace for* 是描述企业或市场预期的高频短语，比 *prepare for* 更富张力。常见于标题和导语。
- **例句**：Even as sales orders surge amid the current shortage, Russian newsprint producers are already **bracing for** a sharp market downturn once global supply and demand rebalance.
  尽管当前短缺期间订单激增，俄罗斯新闻纸生产商已经在**为**全球供需再平衡后市场急剧下行**绷紧弦**。

---

**31. corner the market**
- **音标**：/ˈkɔːrnər ðə ˈmɑːrkɪt/ idiom
- **英文释义**：to dominate or control a particular market, having much more supply or market share than competitors
- **中文释义**：垄断市场；独占市场份额；控制某一市场
- **语域**：商业 / 新闻 / 半正式
- **同义词**：monopolize, dominate the market, control the supply
- **拓展**：习语，含"将对手逼到角落"的隐喻。文中俄罗斯在中国进口市场超过80%份额，接近"cornering the market"状态，但不宜直接用此词，因含垄断负面含义；理解此语境有助于写作时精准择词。
- **例句**：Since Canadian producers pulled back, Russian suppliers have effectively **cornered the market** for newsprint in China, supplying more than four-fifths of all imports.
  自加拿大生产商退出以来，俄罗斯供应商已实际上**垄断**了中国新闻纸市场，供应了超过五分之四的进口量。

**32. window of opportunity**
- **音标**：/ˈwɪndoʊ əv ˌɒpərˈtjuːnɪti/ n. phrase
- **英文释义**：a period of time during which there is a chance to do something that will not be possible later
- **中文释义**：机遇窗口；机会窗口；有限的机会期
- **语域**：商业 / 外交 / 通用书面
- **同义词**：a brief opportunity, a short-lived chance, an opening
- **拓展**：固定搭配，极为常用。常见句型：*take advantage of / exploit / capitalize on / miss a window of opportunity*。在写作中，可与 *narrow*（缩小）、*close*（关闭）、*open up*（打开）搭配使用，形成动态描述。
- **例句**：The **window of opportunity** for Russian producers to lock in long-term contracts at favorable prices may be narrower than the current market euphoria suggests.
  俄罗斯生产商以有利价格锁定长期合同的**机遇窗口**，或许比当前市场乐观情绪所暗示的要短暂得多。

---

**33. catch up with**
- **音标**：/kætʃ ʌp wɪð/ phrasal verb
- **英文释义**：①to reach the same level as someone or something that was ahead; ②(informal) to have a negative effect on you after a period of time
- **中文释义**：①赶上；追平；②（负面结果）追上来；得到应有惩罚/报应
- **语域**：通用 / 商业 / 口语到书面均可
- **常见词组**：*demand catches up with supply; the past catches up with you; catch up on something（补上）*
- **拓展**：短语动词，注意两种不同含义：①中性/积极（追上）；②负面（恶果追上来）。文中语境"как только падающий спрос сравняется с предложением"即"once falling demand catches up with supply"，使用含义①。
- **例句**：Industry analysts warn that once demand decline **catches up with** the pace of capacity closures, prices will fall sharply and mills will be left with unsold inventory.
  行业分析人士警告，一旦需求下滑**追上**产能关闭的节奏，价格将急剧下跌，工厂将面临库存积压。

---

**34. tip the balance / swing to**
- **音标**：/tɪp ðə ˈbæləns/ idiom
- **英文释义**：to cause something to move in a particular direction or produce a particular result by adding a small but decisive factor
- **中文释义**：使天平倾斜；促成决定性转变；成为决定性因素
- **语域**：通用 / 书面 / 半正式
- **同义词**：swing the balance, be the deciding factor, turn the tide
- **拓展**：该表达源自物理天平（balance/scale）的意象，引申为"决定性的小因素打破均衡"。在商业和外交报道中均常见，适合用于分析价格、竞争、谈判等场景的转折点描述。
- **例句**：The loss of the Indian market to Canadian competitors could **tip the balance** against Russian producers and accelerate the industry's slide into overcapacity.
  失去印度市场给加拿大竞争对手，可能**成为决定性因素**，使俄罗斯生产商处于下风，并加速整个行业滑入产能过剩。

---

**35. on the brink of**
- **音标**：/ɒn ðə brɪŋk ɒv/ prepositional phrase
- **英文释义**：on the edge of a very unpleasant or difficult situation that is about to happen
- **中文释义**：处于…的边缘；濒临…；岌岌可危
- **语域**：书面 / 新闻 / 正式
- **同义词**：on the verge of, teetering on the edge of, at the precipice of
- **常见词组**：*on the brink of collapse / bankruptcy / war / ruin*
- **拓展**：比 *on the verge of* 语气更强烈，更常见于描述危机情境。与 *at the tipping point*（临界点）有区别：**brink** 侧重空间悬崖意象（"悬在边缘"），**tipping point** 侧重一旦超过便不可逆的临界状态。
- **例句**：Two of Russia's four newsprint producers are now **on the brink of** insolvency, as falling demand, rising costs, and limited export options converge to create a perfect storm.
  俄罗斯四家新闻纸生产商中已有两家**濒临**资不抵债，需求下滑、成本上升和出口渠道受限叠加，形成了完美风暴。

**36. mop up (demand)**
- **音标**：/mɒp ʌp/ phrasal verb
- **英文释义**：to deal with the remaining parts of something; (market) to absorb or capture residual demand or supply
- **中文释义**：收尾；吸纳剩余需求；抢占剩余市场
- **语域**：商业 / 军事 / 半正式
- **常见词组**：*mop up remaining demand, mop up after a crisis, mop up market share*
- **拓展**：字面义为"用拖把拖干净"，引申为"清理剩余部分"。商业语境中可描述一家企业在竞争对手退出后"吸收残余需求"，是较为形象地道的表达。
- **例句**：As Canadian mills continue to shut down, Russian and Asian producers are racing to **mop up** the remaining demand in the North American and European import markets.
  随着加拿大工厂持续关闭，俄罗斯和亚洲生产商正争相**吸收**北美和欧洲进口市场的剩余需求。

**37. squeeze (margins)**
- **音标**：/skwiːz/ v. / n.
- **英文释义**：（v.）to reduce or limit something forcefully; （n.）a period of reduction or difficulty, especially in profits
- **中文释义**：（v.）压缩；挤压（利润）；施压；（n.）利润收窄；紧缩期
- **语域**：商业 / 新闻 / 半正式
- **同义词**：compress, tighten, cut into, erode; (n.) crunch, pressure
- **常见词组**：*squeeze profit margins, a credit squeeze, squeeze out, feel the squeeze, a cost squeeze*
- **拓展**：规则动词。**squeeze** 在商业英语中是高度形象且地道的动词，常描述"夹击式压力"——成本上涨同时收入下滑。近义区分：**erode margins**（侵蚀利润，过程缓慢渐进）vs. **squeeze margins**（压缩利润，更强调主动外力的挤压）。
- **例句**：With logistics costs rising faster than selling prices, paper mills find their margins **squeezed** from both ends, leaving little room to invest in modernization.
  由于物流成本涨速快于销售价格，造纸厂利润空间被两头**挤压**，几乎没有余地投资现代化改造。

**38. structural**
- **音标**：/ˈstrʌktʃərəl/ adj.
- **英文释义**：relating to the way something is built or organized; (economics) relating to long-term, fundamental changes rather than temporary or cyclical ones
- **中文释义**：结构性的；深层次的；（与"周期性"相对）
- **语域**：学术 / 经济 / 正式
- **常见词组**：*structural decline / change / reform / shift / imbalance / problem*
- **拓展**：在经济分析中，区分 **structural**（结构性，长期根本性）vs. **cyclical**（周期性，随经济起伏）是关键分析框架。如"structural unemployment"（结构性失业）vs. "cyclical unemployment"（周期性失业）。文中"структурный кризис"即 *structural crisis*，意指需求下滑是不可逆的趋势，而非暂时现象。
- **例句**：The decline in newsprint demand is widely regarded as **structural** rather than cyclical, meaning that no eventual economic recovery will restore print media to its former relevance.
  新闻纸需求下滑被普遍视为**结构性**而非周期性的，意即任何经济复苏都无法使印刷媒体恢复昔日地位。

**39. creditor**
- **音标**：/ˈkredɪtər/ n.
- **英文释义**：a person or organization to whom money is owed
- **中文释义**：债权人；贷款方；应收款方
- **语域**：法律 / 财经 / 正式
- **同义词**：lender, claimant (legal), debenture holder
- **反义词**：debtor（债务人）
- **常见词组**：*major creditor, creditor bank, creditor committee, creditor negotiations, secured creditor（有担保债权人）, unsecured creditor（无担保债权人）*
- **拓展**：可数名词。法律语境中重要区分：**secured creditor**（有抵押担保）vs. **unsecured creditor**（无担保，破产时排在后面受偿）。中国法律：《企业破产法》第109条规定有担保债权人对担保财产享有优先受偿权。
- **例句**：The company's **creditors** have agreed in principle to extend repayment deadlines but are demanding that new equity investors be brought in before any additional credit is extended.
  该公司的**债权人**原则上同意延长还款期限，但要求在提供任何额外信贷之前，须先引入新的股权投资者。

**40. incumbent (players / producers)**
- **音标**：/ɪnˈkʌmbənt/ adj. / n.
- **英文释义**：（adj.）currently holding a position or role; (business) already established in a market; （n.）the current holder of a position; an established firm in a market
- **中文释义**：（adj.）现任的；在位的；（商业语境）原有的；既有市场参与者；（n.）现任者；原有企业
- **语域**：政治 / 商业 / 正式
- **常见词组**：*incumbent players, incumbent producers, incumbent firms, the incumbent government*
- **拓展**：在商业语境中，**incumbents**（既有企业）与 **new entrants / challengers**（新进入者）对立，是竞争分析的基础概念。原义"负有义务者"（it is incumbent on sb. to do sth.），引申为"在位者"。注意此义与"压倒性"（overwhelming）无关。
- **例句**：**Incumbent** newsprint producers face a structural dilemma: their heavy sunk costs in specialized machinery make it difficult to exit the market, even as returns continue to deteriorate.
  **既有**新闻纸生产商面临结构性两难困境：沉重的专用机械沉没成本使其难以退出市场，即便回报持续恶化。

## 4D. 金句积累

---

**金句一**

> *"Многие новостные издания полностью переходят в электронный формат. Реклама также все реже размещается в печатных изданиях в пользу продвижения в интернете. Таким образом, спрос на газету неизбежно и стремительно падает."*

**英文对照翻译：**
"Many news outlets are going entirely digital. Advertising is migrating away from print publications in favor of online promotion. As a result, demand for newsprint is falling inevitably and rapidly."

**中文翻译：**
"众多新闻媒体正全面转向数字化。广告投放也愈来愈少在印刷出版物上落脚，转而向互联网营销靠拢。由此，对新闻纸的需求不可避免且迅速地萎缩。"

**写作场景**：适用于描述传统媒体/印刷产业衰退，或数字化转型的结构性影响，可作论点支撑或段落引言，凸显"结构性不可逆"的论调。

---

**金句二**

> *"И так как скорость вывода мощностей опережает скорость падения спроса, сегодня российская бумага на коне."*

**英文对照翻译：**
"Because the pace of capacity removal is outrunning the pace of demand decline, Russian paper is riding high right now."

**中文翻译：**
"正因产能退出的速度超过了需求下滑的速度，俄罗斯纸张眼下可谓风头正劲。"

**写作场景**：经典的供需错位分析表达，适合用于说明"结构性稀缺制造短期红利"的经济逻辑，也可作为写作中"辩证分析"段落的精彩开篇，体现作者对市场节奏的敏锐把握。

---

**金句三**

> *"Как только падающий спрос сравняется с предложением — настанет рынок покупателя и цены резко упадут, особенно если за предыдущий период типографии создадут стратегические запасы."*

**英文对照翻译：**
"The moment falling demand catches up with supply, the market will swing to buyers and prices will fall sharply—especially if print shops have built strategic stockpiles during the preceding period."

**中文翻译：**
"一旦持续下滑的需求追上供应水平，市场将立即转向买方，价格将急剧下跌——尤其是若在此之前印刷厂已积累了战略库存，局面将更为严峻。"

**写作场景**：适用于分析价格周期、供需逆转或市场预警的段落，兼具分析的精确性与语言的画面感。"catches up with"与"swing to buyers"是标准商业英文写法，可直接模仿套用。
