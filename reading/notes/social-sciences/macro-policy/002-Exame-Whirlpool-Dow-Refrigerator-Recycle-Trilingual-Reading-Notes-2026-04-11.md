## 二、前情提要

```markdown
文章结构信息图：
1. 核心事件 (Core Event): 
   - 惠而浦 (Whirlpool) 与陶氏化学 (Dow) 达成战略合作。
   - 推出针对旧冰箱的免费回收计划，并为消费者提供购买新机的折扣。
2. 合作背景与动机 (Background & Motivation):
   - 解决大型家电回收难、运费高（可达 400 雷亚尔）的痛点。
   - 应对冰箱中含有的温室气体 (HFCs) 和聚氨酯 (Polyurethane) 等环境危害物质。
3. 运营模式 (Operational Model):
   - 覆盖巴西全境 5500 多个城市。
   - 联合巴西邮政 (Correios) 等合作伙伴进行物流调度。
   - 数字化预约：通过官网预约，上门回收超过 30 公斤的设备。
4. 经济与社会影响 (Economic & Social Impact):
   - 投资额：300 万雷亚尔。
   - 经济背景：巴西高利率 (Selic 14.75%) 导致信贷困难，免费回收可降低消费者负担。
   - 循环经济：将回收材料重新投入生产链。
5. 教育与未来展望 (Education & Outlook):
   - 联合 Akatu 研究所进行环保教育。
   - 推动行业可持续发展，提升能源效率。
```

## 三、逐句精读

**🔻 Agora, / descartar / uma geladeira velha / passa a ser / mais fácil — e sustentável.**  
**🔹 Now, / disposing of / an old refrigerator / becomes / easier — and more sustainable.**  
**🔸 现在，/ 处置 / 一台旧冰箱 / 变得 / 更加容易——也更加可持续。**

> **重点词汇解析**
>
> **Dispose of** [phrasal verb] 处置，处理 (To get rid of something, especially by throwing it away).
> * **语域**：正式/通用。
> * **常见搭配**：**Dispose of nuclear waste** (处置核废料)；**Dispose of assets** (处置资产)。
> * **辨析**：不同于 **Discard**，**Dispose of** 往往带有「按照程序处理」的含义。
>
> **Sustainable** [adj.] 可持续的 (Involving the use of natural products and energy in a way that does not harm the environment).
> * **语域**：商业/环境。
> * **名词形式**：**Sustainability** (可持续性)。
> * **反义词**：**Unsustainable** (不可持续的)。

***

**🔻 Essa é a proposta / da detentora / das marcas Brastemp, Consul e KitchenAid, / a fabricante de eletrodomésticos Whirlpool.**  
**🔹 This is the proposal / of the owner / of the brands Brastemp, Consul, and KitchenAid, / the home appliance manufacturer Whirlpool.**  
**🔸 这是 / 拥有 Brastemp、Consul 和 KitchenAid 品牌的 / 家电制造商惠而浦 (Whirlpool) / 提出的提议。**

> **背景注释**
> * **Whirlpool (惠而浦)**：全球领先的大型家用电器制造商，总部位于美国。在巴西市场，它拥有 Brastemp 和 Consul 等国民级品牌。
> * **KitchenAid (凯膳怡)**：惠而浦旗下的高端厨房家电品牌，以厨师机闻名。

> **重点词汇解析**
>
> **Manufacturer** [n. countable] 制造商 (A company that produces goods in large numbers).
> * **词性变化**：**Manufacture** (v. 制造)；**Manufacturing** (n. 制造业)。
> * **近义词**：**Producer**, **Maker**。
>
> **Home appliance** [n. phrase] 家用电器 (A device or machine, usually electrical, that is in your house and used for cleaning or cooking).
> * **分类**：分为 **White goods** (白色家电，如冰箱、洗衣机) 和 **Brown goods** (黑色/棕色家电，如电视、音响)。

***

**🔻 A companhia / acaba de firmar / uma parceria / com a Dow, / gigante de produtos químicos, / focada em acelerar / a logística reversa / de refrigeradores.**  
**🔹 The company / has just signed / a partnership / with Dow, / a chemical giant, / focused on accelerating / the reverse logistics / of refrigerators.**  
**🔸 该公司 / 刚刚 / 与化学巨头陶氏 (Dow) / 签署了合作协议，/ 致力于加速 / 冰箱的 / 逆向物流。**

> **背景注释**
> * **Dow (陶氏化学)**：美国跨国化学公司，全球最大的化学品生产商之一，提供塑料、化学品和农业产品。
> * **Reverse Logistics (逆向物流)**：指商品从消费者端退回商家或生产商的过程，通常用于维修、回收或废弃物处理。在 ESG 领域是循环经济的核心。

> **重点词汇解析**
>
> **Firmar (Sign)** [v.] 签署，建立 (To establish or make a formal agreement).
> * **语域**：商务/法律。
> * **常见搭配**：**Sign a contract** (签合同)；**Sign a treaty** (签署条约)。
>
> **Reverse logistics** [n. phrase] 逆向物流 (The process of planning, implementing, and controlling the efficient, cost-effective flow of raw materials, in-process inventory, finished goods and related information from the point of consumption to the point of origin).
> * **语域**：物流/商业。
> * **重要性**：在 **Circular Economy** (循环经济) 中，这是减少环境足迹的关键。

***

**🔻 E juntas / já investiram / R$ 3 milhões / no projeto, / que deve reverter / em descontos / para o consumidor.**  
**🔹 And together / they have already invested / R$ 3 million / in the project, / which should result / in discounts / for the consumer.**  
**🔸 双方 / 已在该项目上投资了 / 300 万雷亚尔，/ 这预计将转化为 / 给消费者的 / 折扣。**

> **重点词汇解析**
>
> **Invest** [v.] 投资 (To put money, effort, time, etc. into something to make a profit or get an advantage).
> * **动词变化**：过去式/过去分词 **invested**（规则变化）。
> * **常见搭配**：**Invest in something** (投资某事)。
> * **名词**：**Investment** (投资)；**Investor** (投资者)。
>
> **Reverter (Result in/Turn into)** [v.] 转化，导致 (To change into something else, or to have a particular result).
> * **语域**：正式。
> * **用法**：在文中指投资带来的直接收益或效果。

**🔻 O trabalho / ainda conta com / parceria do Instituto Akatu, / organização sem fins lucrativos / que trabalha / o consumo consciente / e os conceitos de reciclagem / e economia circular.**

> **背景注释**

> **重点词汇解析**
>
> **Non-profit organization** [n. phrase] 非营利组织 (An organization whose primary goal is to provide a service rather than make a profit).
> * **缩写**：**NGO** (Non-governmental organization) 或 **NPO**。
>
> **Conscious consumption** [n. phrase] 意识消费/理性消费 (Buying and using products that have a positive social, economic, and environmental impact).
> * **语域**：社会学/环境。
> * **同义词**：**Ethical consumerism** (道德消费主义)。

> * **语域**：新闻媒体。
> * **搭配**：**Exclusive interview** (独家专访)；**Exclusive rights** (独家代理权)。

**🔻 As políticas / de reciclagem e descarte / de resíduos atuais / consideram o lado do consumidor?**  
**🔹 Do current / recycling and waste disposal / policies / consider the consumer's side?**  
**🔸 现行的 / 废弃物回收与处置 / 政策 / 是否考虑到了消费者的一面？**

> **重点词汇解析**
>
> **Policy** [n. countable] 政策 (A set of ideas or a plan of what to do in particular situations that has been agreed to officially).
> * **复数**：**Policies**。
> * **注意**：不要与 **Police** (警察) 混淆。
>
> **Waste** [n. uncountable] 废弃物 (Materials that are no longer needed and are thrown away).
> * **类型**：**Industrial waste** (工业废料)；**Hazardous waste** (危险废物)。

***

**🔻 A economia circular / deveria / acrescentar tarefas e custos a ele?**

> **重点词汇解析**
>
> **Circular economy** [n. phrase] 循环经济 (An economic system based on the reuse and regeneration of materials or products, especially as a means of continuing production in a sustainable or environmentally friendly way).
> * **核心**：**Reduce, Reuse, Recycle** (3R 原则)。

**🔻 “O frete de um item desse porte / pode chegar a R$ 400, / um alto valor / por um item antigo, / que se revendido / não custaria nem R$ 50.”**  
**🔹 “The freight for an item of this size / can reach R$ 400, / a high value / for an old item / that, if resold, / would not even cost R$ 50.”**

>
> **Resell** [v.] 转售 (To sell something that you have bought).
> * **不规则变化**：**Resold** (过去式/过去分词)。

**🔻 Outro lado dessa iniciativa / são os materiais perigosos / envolvidos nos componentes / de um refrigerador.**

>

> * **语域**：技术/制造。

***

> * **缩写**：**GHG**。
> * **常见气体**：**CO2** (二氧化碳), **Methane** (甲烷), **HFCs** (氢氟碳化物)。

>

> * **名词**：**Schedule** (时间表)。
>
> **Removal** [n. uncountable/countable] 移走，清除 (The act of taking something or someone away from somewhere).
> * **动词**：**Remove** (移除)。

**🔸 该项目 / 将覆盖 / 全国 5500 多个市镇，/ 包括 / 巴西邮政 (Correios) 等战略合作伙伴。**

> **背景注释**
> * **Correios**：巴西国营邮政公司，拥有覆盖全巴西的物流网络，是此类全国性回收计划的关键。

> **重点词汇解析**

> * **语域**：行政/法律。
>
> **Strategic partner** [n. phrase] 战略合作伙伴 (A relationship between two commercial enterprises, usually formalized by one or more business contracts).

**🔻 Reis, da Whirlpool, / afirma que a iniciativa / foi desenvolvida / de forma a não repassar / o custo do programa / para os clientes.**

**🔸 惠而浦的 Reis 表示，/ 该倡议的开发 / 旨在不将 / 计划成本 / 转嫁给客户。**

> **重点词汇解析**
>
> **Pass on** [phrasal verb] 转嫁/传递 (To give something to someone, after someone else gave it to you; in business, to make someone else pay a cost).
> * **常见搭配**：**Pass on the cost to consumers** (将成本转嫁给消费者)。

**🔻 “Com a taxa Selic a 14,75% / e a maior dificuldade para acessar crédito, / se eu ainda repasso o custo / da logística reversa para o consumidor, / inviabilizo a compra.”**

## 模块一：翻译与全文概要

**全文英文翻译**（根据葡萄牙文原文整理的英文版；非杂志官方英稿，供对照。）

**Article Title:** "Old Refrigerator? Brastemp Owner and Dow Collect for Free and Offer Discount on New One"

**Full Text in English:**

Now, discarding an old refrigerator becomes easier and more sustainable. This is the proposal of the company that owns the brands Brastemp, Consul, and KitchenAid—appliance manufacturer Whirlpool. The company has just entered into a partnership with Dow, a chemical giant, focused on accelerating the reverse logistics of refrigerators. Together, they have already invested R$ 3 million in the project, which should result in discounts for consumers.

The work also counts on a partnership with Instituto Akatu, a nonprofit organization that works on conscious consumption and recycling and circular economy concepts with the population, especially young audiences. Douglas Reis, legal director, corporate affairs, and ESG at Whirlpool Latin America, stated in an exclusive interview with EXAME that the work began from a question: Do current recycling and waste disposal policies consider the consumer's perspective? Should the circular economy add tasks and costs to the consumer?

"When a cell phone breaks, it's easy to take it to a collection point. With a refrigerator, a large and heavy item, the situation becomes complicated," he explains. He states that in São Paulo, the disposal of this material often falls to the customer themselves. "The shipping of an item this size can reach R$ 400, a high value for an old item, which if resold wouldn't cost even R$ 50."

Another aspect of this initiative involves the dangerous materials in refrigerator components. Edilson Machado, marketing director for Dow's polyurethane business, explains that white goods, which include freezers and refrigerators, contain greenhouse gases (often with some impact on the ozone layer), such as HFCs and hydrocarbons.

"Dow enters the project with material recovery and technical complexity, as it involves non-simple chemical processes," he says. The polyurethanes, a type of plastic used in the insulation of cold chambers in freezers and refrigerators, are also an issue for the chemical industry. "We are the only manufacturer of this type of foam in Brazil and want to reduce the impact it can cause on the environment," he explains.

**How Will It Work?**

In practice, customers with items to dispose of can enter the Whirlpool website and schedule the removal of materials weighing over 30 kilograms directly from their homes. The project will cover all over 5,500 municipalities across the country, including strategic partners to carry out the removal and distribution of items to recycling centers, such as the Post Office.

For the consumer, the process will be 100% free, which was a concern for the companies. Reis from Whirlpool states that the initiative was developed so as not to pass the program's cost on to customers, given that the economic situation already strains consumer purchasing power. "The consumer generally needs a financing source to acquire durable goods like refrigerators. With the Selic rate at 14.75% and greater difficulty accessing credit, if I still pass on the reverse logistics cost to the consumer, I make the purchase impossible," he explains.

In total, the program has already required R$ 3 million in investments, divided equally between the companies. "Reverse logistics requires significant costs, but when we unite with partners like Dow, I can reduce expenses and reach other consumers," Reis says. Machado from Dow explains that the program involves other strategic partnerships due to Brazil's structural challenges. "Reverse logistics here is already complex, but we still have regions with very different cultures and knowledge about recyclability that varies greatly," he says.

The program is still under development, but the expectation is that it will begin operating with the union of the companies in the coming months. The expectation is to include a "trade-in" mechanism, in which the old item can be returned to the manufacturer, and as an incentive, the customer receives significant discounts.

After collection, refrigerators and other heavy equipment will be sent to circular economy centers and waste will be reinserted into the production chain.

**History of Circular Economy**

The program did not emerge overnight but from the union of two existing actions. Whirlpool has had a project since 2023 that facilitates the conscious disposal of electronics, even those from other brands. The work counts on the partnership of Circular Brain, which ensures traceability and environmental compliance during waste management. By 2025, the project had already processed over 111 thousand tons of electronic and electric waste—in other words, around 2.5 million products were sent for recycling.

At Dow, the Reuse project already works on refrigerator recycling. This is a project created with Panasonic, Ecoassist, and the Brazilian Association for Electronic and Home Appliance Recycling, in addition to Instituto Akatu, also a partner in this initiative. Customers who request the collection of their used refrigerators receive up to 20% discounts.

**Environmental Education**

Lucio Vicente, director of Instituto Akatu, explains that for the organization, every product is an instrument with potential to guide society about sustainability, recycling, and reverse logistics. The organization's work involves an environmental consciousness program that uses its own pedagogical methodology to mobilize school communities in various regions of the country.

"Our role is to bring knowledge on this topic, a challenge that goes beyond Dow and Whirlpool communities. The work involves bringing to people greater participation and affective connection with responsibility, which is shared in this matter," Vicente says.

For him, one of the connection points with the public is understanding that trading an obsolete device for a new one is also an energy efficiency action, and based on consumer demand, encouraging more actions in the electronic appliance market. "We can drive the market to respond more quickly to customers' sustainable demands. The consumer begins to include environmental issues in the product choice process, which brings the issue to the companies' priority," he says.

Instituto Akatu has already reached 9,000 schools with environmental and sustainable education programs.

Edilson Machado from Dow hopes that the movement can inspire other companies, partners or competitors, to accelerate their sustainability journey in processes. "We believe that all populations increasingly value sustainability, so we accept the risk of investing in such a high-cost project. We hope that in a few years we will already consider concepts like this as natural," he explains.

- **例句** The **win-win** partnership between Whirlpool and Dow benefits consumers through affordability, companies through cost reduction, and the environment through better waste management. 惠而浦与陶氏的双赢合作让消费者因可负担性受益，让企业因成本降低受益，让环境因更好的废物管理受益。

**6. BOTTOM LINE** /ˈbɑːtəm laɪn/ (n.)

- **英文释义** The essential fact or most important point; the final profit or loss in a financial statement
- **中文释义** 底线；关键要点；最终结果；净利润
- **语域标注** 商务/非正式用语
- **同义词** Key point; essential fact; net result | (无明确反义词)
- **拓展内容**
  - 名词短语：通常作 "the bottom line is..."（关键是……）或 "bottom-line results"（最终成果）
  - 搭配：bottom-line profit (净利润)；bottom-line impact (最终影响)；improve the bottom line (改善最终结果)
  - Native context: 商业讨论中很常见，特别是在财务与战略讨论中；可指财务或战略的最终结果
  - 注意：两个单词，中间有空格
- **例句** The **bottom line** is that this initiative allows companies to fulfill their environmental responsibilities while maintaining profitability through operational efficiency. 关键是这项倡议允许公司履行环保责任，同时通过运营效率维持盈利能力。

### 3C. 金句积累

**1. 企业责任与消费者利益平衡**

**原文英文：**  
"The consumer generally needs a financing source to acquire durable goods like refrigerators. With the Selic rate at 14.75% and greater difficulty accessing credit, if I still pass on the reverse logistics cost to the consumer, I make the purchase impossible."

**中文翻译：**  
「消费者通常需要融资来购买冰箱等耐用品。在基准利率达到 14.75% 且信贷难以获得的情况下，如果我还将反向物流成本转嫁给消费者，就会使购买成为不可能。」

**应用场景：** 讨论企业社会责任与商业可行性的平衡；论述成本转嫁对消费者的影响
