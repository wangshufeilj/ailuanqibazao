# 3. 全文逐句精读解析 | Sentence-by-Sentence Intensive Analysis

🔹 **Headline: US F-35 / damaged / by suspected Iranian fire / makes / emergency landing, / sources say.**
🔸 **标题：据消息人士透露，一架 / 被疑似伊朗火力 / 击伤的 / 美国F-35战斗机 / 进行了 / 紧急降落。**

> 1. **suspected** [səˈspektɪd] *adj.* believed to be true, but without proof. **疑似的；被怀疑的**。
>    - [Usage] **Suspected** is often used in news to avoid definitive legal or political liability before proof is found. **"suspected"常用于新闻报道中，在证据确凿前避免承担法律或政治责任**。
> 2. **emergency landing** [iˈmɜːrdʒənsi ˈlændɪŋ] *n. [C]* a landing made by an aircraft in response to a crisis. **紧急降落**。
>    - [Collocation] **make/force an emergency landing** (进行/被迫紧急降落)。
> 3. **fire** [ˈfaɪər] *n. [U]* the shooting of guns or missiles. **炮火；火力**。
>    - [Antonym] **ceasefire** (停火)。

🔹 **Para 1: A US F-35 fighter jet / made / an emergency landing / at US air base / in the Middle East / after it was struck / by what is believed to be / Iranian fire, / according to / two sources familiar with the matter.**
🔸 **第一段：据两位知情人士透露，一架美国F-35战斗机 / 在被据信是 / 伊朗的火力 / 击中后，/ 在中东的 / 美国空军基地 / 进行了 / 紧急降落。**

> 1. **fighter jet** [ˈfaɪtər dʒet] *n. [C]* a fast military aircraft designed for attacking other aircraft. **战斗机**。
>    - [Note] **jet** refers to the propulsion system (喷气式)。
> 2. **strike** [straɪk] *v. (struck, struck/stricken)* to hit or attack someone or something forcefully. **击中；打击**。
>    - [Synonym] **hit, impact**.
> 3. **familiar with the matter** [fəˈmɪliər wɪð ðə ˈmætər] *adj. phrase* having knowledge about a specific situation. **知情；熟悉情况**。
>    - [IELTS Writing] This is a standard journalistic phrase for anonymous sourcing. **这是新闻报道中引用匿名消息源的标准用语**。
> 4. **air base** [ˈer beɪs] *n. [C]* a center where military aircraft take off and land. **空军基地**。

*   **[注释] F-35 (Lightning II):** 由美国**洛克希德·马丁 (Lockheed Martin)** 公司开发的第五代单座单发隐形战斗机。

🔹 **Para 2: Capt. Tim Hawkins, / a spokesperson / for US Central Command, / said / the fifth-generation stealth jet / was "flying a combat mission / over Iran" / when it was forced / to make an emergency landing.**
🔸 **第二段：美国中央司令部 / 发言人 / 蒂姆·霍金斯上尉 / 表示，/ 这架第五代隐形战斗机 / 在"伊朗上空 / 执行战斗任务"时 / 被迫 / 进行紧急降落。**

> 1. **spokesperson** [ˈspoʊkspɜːrsn] *n. [C]* a person who speaks on behalf of a group or organization. **发言人**。
>    - [Synonym] **spokesman / spokeswoman**.
> 2. **stealth** [stelθ] *adj.* designed to be difficult to detect by radar or other systems. **隐形的；秘密的**。
>    - [Derivative] **stealthy** (adj. 鬼鬼祟祟的)。
> 3. **combat mission** [ˈkɑːmbæt ˈmɪʃn] *n. [C]* a specific task assigned to a military unit in a war. **战斗任务**。
>    - [Collocation] **assign/execute a mission** (指派/执行任务)。
> 4. **force** [fɔːrs] *v. (forced, forced)* to make someone do something they do not want to do. **强迫；迫使**。
>    - [Pattern] **be forced to do sth.** (被迫做某事)。

*   **[注释] US Central Command (CENTCOM):** **美国中央司令部**，负责美国在中东、北非和中亚地区的军事行动。

🔹 **Para 3: "The aircraft / landed / safely, / and the pilot / is in stable condition," / Hawkins added. / "This incident / is under investigation."**
🔸 **第三段：霍金斯补充道："飞机 / 安全 / 降落，/ 飞行员 / 状态稳定。" / "这一事件 / 正在调查中。"**

> 1. **stable** [ˈsteɪbl] *adj.* not likely to change or fail; firmly established. **稳定的**。
>    - [Antonym] **unstable** (不稳定的)。
>    - [Usage] In medical terms, it means the patient's life is not in immediate danger. **在医学语境下，指病人没有生命危险**。
> 2. **incident** [ˈɪnsɪdənt] *n. [C]* an event or occurrence, especially one that is unusual or unpleasant. **事件；事故**。
>    - [Distinction] **Incident** usually refers to a single event (often negative), while **event** is more general. **Incident通常指不愉快的小插曲或事故，而event更通用**。
> 3. **under investigation** [ˈʌndər ɪnˌvestɪˈɡeɪʃn] *prep. phrase* being officially examined. **正在调查中**。
>    - [Collocation] **launch/conduct an investigation** (发起/进行调查)。

---

🔹 **Para 4: The incident / would be / the first time / Iran has hit / a US aircraft / in the war / started in late February.**
🔸 **第四段：这次事件 / 将是 / 自2月下旬 / 战争开始以来，/ 伊朗首次击中 / 美国飞机。**

> 1. **late** [leɪt] *adj.* near the end of a period of time. **晚期的；末期的**。
>    - [Usage] **late February** (二月下旬)；**early February** (二月初)；**mid-February** (二月中旬)。
> 2. **hit** [hɪt] *v. (hit, hit)* to strike with a weapon or missile. **击中**。

> 1. **conflict** [ˈkɑːnflɪkt] *n. [C/U]* a serious disagreement or argument; a long period of fighting. **冲突；战争**。
>    - [Usage] Often used as a synonym for "war" in diplomatic or news contexts. **在外交或新闻语境中常作为"战争"的同义词**。
> 2. **upwards of** [ˈʌpwərdz əv] *prep. phrase* more than; in excess of. **多于；……以上**。
>    - [Synonym] **more than, over**.
>    - [IELTS Writing] Useful for describing data trends. **在雅思小作文描述数据趋势时非常有用**。

🔹 **Para 5: The emergency landing / comes / as senior US officials / continue to claim / widespread success / in its campaign / against Iran.**
🔸 **第五段：此次紧急降落 / 发生之际，/ 美国高级官员 / 仍继续声称 / 在针对伊朗的 / 军事行动中 / 取得了广泛的成功。**

> 1. **senior** [ˈsiːniər] *adj.* high in rank or status. **高级的；资深的**。
>    - [Antonym] **junior** (初级的)。
> 2. **claim** [kleɪm] *v.* to state that something is the case, typically without providing proof. **声称；主张**。
> 3. **widespread** [ˈwaɪdspred] *adj.* found or distributed over a large area or number of people. **广泛的；普遍的**。
>    - [Synonym] **extensive, pervasive**.
> 4. **campaign** [kæmˈpeɪn] *n. [C]* a series of military operations intended to achieve a particular objective. **（军事）行动；战役**。
>    - [Note] Also used in politics: **election campaign** (竞选活动)。

🔹 **Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth / said / Thursday morning / that the US / is "winning decisively" / and that / Iran's air defenses / have been "flattened."**
🔸 **国防部长皮特·海格塞斯 / 周四上午 / 表示，/ 美国正"取得决定性胜利"，/ 并且 / 伊朗的防空系统 / 已被"彻底摧毁"。**

> 1. **Defense Secretary** [dɪˈfens ˈsekrəteri] *n.* the head of the Department of Defense. **国防部长**。
>    - [Note] In the UK, the equivalent is **Defense Secretary** or **Secretary of State for Defence**.
> 2. **decisively** [dɪˈsaɪsɪvli] *adv.* in a way that settles an issue convincingly. **决定性地；果断地**。
>    - [Root] **decide** (v. 决定) -> **decision** (n. 决定) -> **decisive** (adj. 决定性的)。
> 3. **air defense** [er dɪˈfens] *n. [U]* measures taken to protect an area against attack by aircraft or missiles. **防空（系统/设施）**。
> 4. **flattened** [ˈflætnd] *v. (past participle)* to destroy or knock down something completely. **彻底摧毁；夷为平地**。
>    - [Usage] Literally means "to make flat". In military context, it refers to total destruction. **原意是"弄平"，在军事语境下指完全摧毁**。

*   **[注释] Pete Hegseth:** 此处指2026年设定下的美国国防部长。
