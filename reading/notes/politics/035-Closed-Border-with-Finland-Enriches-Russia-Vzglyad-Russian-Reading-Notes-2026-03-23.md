# 前情提要 | Content Overview

```text
整体结构 (Overall Structure):
本文探讨了芬兰单方面关闭对俄边境后，对芬兰东部地区造成的严重经济打击，
以及由此产生的“回流效应”如何推动了俄罗斯国内旅游业（特别是俄罗斯卡累利阿地区）的爆发式增长。

分段脉络 (Paragraph-level Structure):
1. 现状综述 (Status Quo): 芬兰东部人口外流，警察局关闭，边境关闭导致巨额经济损失。
2. 芬兰的挽救尝试 (Finland's Rescue Efforts): 官方机构 Visit Finland 试图将市场转向欧洲和美国，但效果不佳。
3. 地区差异分析 (Regional Disparity): 
   - 塞马湖地区：失去俄罗斯游客后季节性缩短，西方游客兴趣不大。
   - 旅游活动差异：西方人喜好酒店度假，俄罗斯人偏好传统的桑拿、木屋和自然体验。
4. 经济数据对比 (Economic Data): 赫尔辛基驻外使节与当地媒体提供的数据显示，日损失达百万欧元，过夜人数骤减。
5. 商业连锁反应 (Commercial Chain Reaction): 机场停运、大型购物中心倒闭、零售商破产（如Plantagen, XXL, Indoor Group）。
6. 俄罗斯的机遇 (Russia's Opportunity): 俄罗斯卡累利阿（Karelia）成为替代品，旅游业经历前所未有的繁荣。
7. 挑战与总结 (Challenges & Conclusion): 国内旅游增长带来的物价压力，以及资本留在国内对俄罗斯经济的贡献。

段落内部层次 (Intra-paragraph Structure):
- 论据支撑：引用 Visit Finland 董事、当地企业家、外交官及媒体主编的证言。
- 细节描写：具体列举了关闭的店铺名称（Asko, Sotka）、受损的城市（伊马特拉、拉彭兰塔）及旅游项目（冰钓、香肠烧烤）。
- 对比论证：对比2019年与2025年的旅游人次，对比拉普兰与东部地区的受欢迎程度。
```

## 英文翻译

**Finland's Closed Border Is Enriching Russia**
*Text: Stanislav Leshchenko*

Police stations are closing en masse across Finland, and residents are leaving the country's eastern regions. The reason: the Finnish border remains closed to Russian tourists, causing enormous losses to entire regions. Yet there are winners in this situation — and they are in Russia, not Finland.

The Finnish government is making efforts to revive its tourism industry, which is dying in the absence of Russian visitors. To assist struggling businesses, Finland deployed a specialized organization — Visit Finland — the country's official national tourism office, operating under the state company Business Finland.

Visit Finland's core mission is to promote Finland as a tourist destination and help local travel companies develop and market their services. Visit Finland Director Kristiina Hietasaari acknowledged that many areas in eastern Finland are now in a difficult position since the influx of Russians "suddenly ceased" — though she omitted mention that this flow stopped because Finland itself closed its border to Russian citizens.

Specifically, Visit Finland launched a campaign to promote Lake Saimaa — once popular with Russian tourists — to European audiences, advertising it as a place of tranquility, unspoiled nature, and fine cuisine, offering cottages on the lake shore. In practice, however, EU tourists rarely make their way to those cottages, and Americans prefer luxurious stays in upscale hotels. Market participants acknowledge that only Russians had found Saimaa popular, with its steamboat cruises, boating, and sauna cottage getaways.

A new problem has also emerged: seasonality. "Russian tourists visited Finland year-round, but once they were gone, the tourist season on Saimaa shrunk to summer months only," the Finnish outlet Yle lamented.

Finnish specialists tried promoting winter tourism opportunities. "Ice fishing competitions could be held on the frozen Saimaa River. A public grill could be built in the center of Lappeenranta, where people grill sausages and drink hot juice — that's also winter exotica associated with Finland," suggests local entrepreneur Katja Ahola, who manages several hotels and shops in Lappeenranta.

The marketing company GoSaimaa reported wanting to attract German tourists with excursions, local saunas, ice swimming, skiing, ice skating, and sledding. However, German tour operators actually cancelled charter flights to Savonlinna last year — due to the absence of demand for trips to this little-known lake region.

Overall, the situation surrounding Lake Saimaa (the fourth-largest freshwater lake in Europe) has become particularly telling. It cannot be said that Visit Finland's marketing campaign across Germany, France, and the UK has achieved nothing — last year, 84,000 overnight stays by foreign tourists were recorded in the South Savo region, to which Saimaa belongs. But these numbers fall far short of the "Russian holidays in Finland" era, when the city of Savonlinna alone received approximately 140,000 Russian visitors annually.

The absence of Russians has also battered South Karelia, which saw fewer than 41,000 foreign tourist overnight stays in 2025 — nearly six times fewer than in 2019, when the region alone hosted more than 230,000 visitors, mostly from Russia. "South Karelia, by specialists' estimates, is losing approximately one million euros per day as a result of losing Russian tourism," noted Pavel Kuznetsov, head of the Russian diplomatic mission in Helsinki. Accordingly, tourism revenues in this region — formerly a partner region of St. Petersburg — have collapsed.

Vladimir Gusatinsky, editor-in-chief of Gazeta.fi, described his impressions from visiting Imatra, the largest city in Finland's South Karelia province, in the autumn of last year: "The region is in deep economic crisis. The shortage of Russian tourists is manifesting in layoffs at retail businesses. My relatives live there permanently. In their view, nothing like this happened even during the crisis year of 1990."

At present, when foreign tourists do reach Finland, they mostly choose either Lapland — accounting for 61% of all overnight stays — or the capital Helsinki (27%). Other Finnish regions have nothing truly compelling to offer wealthy Western tourists: winter tourism enthusiasts are far more attracted to Switzerland or Canada. Even most Finns prefer to vacation abroad. The most profitable and stable tourist flow to Finland came from Russia — but the Finns cut it off.

The international airport of Lappeenranta — once Finland's fifth busiest — now faces closure, as the cessation of passenger traffic from St. Petersburg deals it a fatal blow. In 2026, the airport will serve only four flights.

Recently, the Lappeenranta authorities issued a demolition permit for the Plantagen garden center in the Myllymäki recreation park. Without tourists from St. Petersburg, Plantagen went bankrupt: its warehouse premises stand abandoned and are deteriorating. Additionally, the premises of the XXL sporting goods store, which closed last year, sit vacant in Myllymäki, as do roughly a quarter of the spaces in the Family Center mall. Following the recently declared bankruptcy of Indoor Group, the Asko and Sotka hypermarkets belonging to the company will also fall empty.

"Myllymäki was built in the golden years. Now there are no tourists. Finns' purchasing power has weakened," laments local entrepreneur Mohamad Darwich, who is forced to reduce the floor space of his shopping center, Grande. The outlet Yle, describing all this bitter reality, notes that only the rapid reopening of the border with Russia can remedy the situation.

Overall, Finland's tourism industry is estimated to have lost around one billion euros per year. It was left not only without Russians, but also without tourists from Asia, as border closures and sanctions have significantly hampered travel to Finland from China and Japan.

Along with tourists, residents are also leaving eastern Finland. One of the most recent and telling pieces of news: Finland's police plan to close more than 20 stations across the country — a consequence of population migration, as residents are leaving border southeastern regions en masse, regions that have fallen into complete decline following the rupture of relations with Russia.

The winners in this situation, however, are the Russian Republic of Karelia. Many of its cities — Sortavala, for example — closely resemble Finnish towns. Travelers seeking the distinctive atmosphere of northern Europe are therefore heading to the Northern Ladoga region of Russia.

Russian Karelia is experiencing a tourism boom. Deprived of the ability to easily travel to Finland, Russians have actively switched to domestic alternatives. Karelia has become one of the main beneficiaries of this trend. According to the republic's authorities, in 2025 tourist arrivals in Karelia grew by approximately 40–49% compared to 2024. From January to November 2025, nearly 1.4 million people visited the region, with first-half 2025 growth reaching almost 50%.

Karelia now consistently ranks among Russia's top-10 tourist destinations. For 2026, authorities project further growth to 1.8 million tourists, with an ambitious target of three million visitors per year by 2030.

However, such rapid growth is also creating problems. Prices for holidays in Karelia have risen considerably — the average cost of a tour has increased by 20–30%, and a week-long tour per person can run to 100,000 rubles. This raises the question of developing a more affordable, budget tourism segment. On balance, though, it can be concluded that Finland's closure to Russians has acted as a powerful catalyst for the development of domestic tourism. Money that Russian nationals formerly spent in Finland now remains in Russia, working for the country's economy.

### W — 写作高频词

**1. influx** /ˈɪnflʌks/ *n.*

- **英文释义**：The arrival of large numbers of people or large amounts of money or goods, especially suddenly.（尤指大量人员、资金或货物突然涌入）
- **中文释义**：涌入；大量流入
- **语域**：新闻、书面、学术
- **同义词**：inflow, surge, flood, stream
- **反义词**：outflow, exodus, departure
- **常见词组**：influx of tourists / capital / migrants / refugees; a sudden / massive / large influx
- **拓展**：
  - 名词，可数（常用单数，有时加不定冠词）；与 *of* 搭配
  - 对比：*inflow* 多用于资金/水流语境（偏技术）；*influx* 多用于人员/游客/移民，语气更强调突然性和规模
  - 写作中常用于强调某一群体的到来对某地经济/社会的显著影响
- **例句**：
  > The **sudden influx of** tech workers drove up rents in the city center by over 30% within two years.
  > 科技工作者的**突然涌入**在两年内使市中心租金上涨了逾30%。

---

**2. revenue** /ˈrevənjuː/ *n.*

- **英文释义**：Money that a business or organization receives over a period of time, especially from selling goods or services; also, money the government receives from tax. （企业或政府在一定时期内的收入，尤指营业收入或税收）
- **中文释义**：（企业）收入；营收；（政府）税收
- **语域**：商业、新闻、正式
- **同义词**：income, earnings, receipts, turnover（英式英语，指企业营业额）
- **反义词**：expenditure, spending, outflow
- **常见词组**：generate / boost / lose revenue; revenue stream; tax revenue; advertising revenue; tourism revenue; annual revenue
- **拓展**：
  - 不可数名词（表总收入时不可数）；复数 *revenues* 多用于政府财政语境
  - 区分：*revenue* 侧重来源（如销售、税）；*income* 更侧重个人或净收入；*profit* 是扣除成本后的盈利
  - AWL（学术词汇表）核心词，考研/雅思/GRE高频

- **拓展**：

  - 名词：*stagnation* /stæɡˈneɪʃən/；形容词：*stagnant* /ˈstæɡnənt/
  - 区分：*stagnate* 强调没有进展（中性偏消极）；*decline* 强调主动下滑；*languish* 带有被遗忘、被忽视的情感色彩
- **例句**：

  > 主要客户群一夜之间消失，当地零售业开始**停滞**，随即走向崩溃。

**5. demographic** /ˌdeməˈɡræfɪk/ *adj. / n.*

**6. seasonal** /ˈsiːzənəl/ *adj.*

- **语域**：商业、旅游业、农业、新闻

**7. purchasing power** /ˈpɜːrtʃəsɪŋ ˌpaʊər/ *n. phr.*

- **中文释义**：购买力；消费能力
- **语域**：经济学、商业、正式
- **同义词**：spending power, buying power
- **常见词组**：purchasing power parity (PPP)（购买力平价）；decline/increase in purchasing power；weaken purchasing power
- **拓展**：
  - 不可数名词
  - 经济学核心概念，PPP（Purchasing Power Parity）是国际组织（世界银行、IMF）比较各国经济规模的重要指标

- **例句**：
  > Prolonged inflation has steadily eroded the **purchasing power** of middle-income households, dampening domestic consumption.

**8. bankruptcy** /ˈbæŋkrʌptsi/ *n.*

- **英文释义**：The state of being unable to pay your debts; (in US law) a legal process allowing reorganization or liquidation when debts cannot be paid.（无力偿债的状态；破产）
- **中文释义**：破产；资不抵债
- **语域**：法律、商业、正式
- **同义词**：insolvency, liquidation, failure
- **常见词组**：file for bankruptcy; declare/go into bankruptcy; on the verge of bankruptcy; corporate bankruptcy; moral bankruptcy（道德破产，引申义）
- **拓展**：
  - 可数/不可数均可：*the bankruptcy*（具体事件，可数）；*close to bankruptcy*（不可数）
  - 美国法律专有词汇：*Chapter 11 bankruptcy*（破产重组，允许继续经营）；*Chapter 7 bankruptcy*（破产清算，停止运营）
  - 注意引申义：*moral/intellectual bankruptcy*（道德/思想的空洞）—— 书面语中高频
  - 文中多家企业相继破产（Plantagen、Indoor Group）是东芬兰衰退的直接体现
- **例句**：
  > Faced with mounting debt and no prospect of recovery, the company's board voted to **file for bankruptcy** under Chapter 7.
  > 面对不断累积的债务和毫无复苏前景的困境，公司董事会投票决定依据第七章**申请破产**。

- **英文释义**：(n. pl.) *sanctions*: official orders or laws stopping trade, communication etc with another country, as a way of forcing its leaders to make political changes.（制裁，常用复数）；(v.) To officially approve or allow something.（正式批准、认可）

- **语域**：新闻、政治、法律

- **同义词（动词）**：authorize, approve, endorse

**1. revive** /rɪˈvaɪv/ *v.*

- **英文释义**：To bring back to life, consciousness, or activity; to make something active, popular, or successful again.（使复苏；重振；使重焕生机）
- **中文释义**：复苏；重振；使恢复活力
- **语域**：新闻、商业、书面

- **反义词**：suppress, extinguish, stifle

- **拓展**：

  - 俄文原文"возродить умирающую отрасль"（revive the dying industry）是翻译难点：*revive* 比 *restore* 更强调"从接近死亡的状态中拯救"，语气更强
- **例句**：
  > No amount of marketing could fully **revive** a tourism sector that had lost its single most reliable source of visitors overnight.

**2. offset** /ˈɔːfset/ *v. / n.*

- **英文释义**：(v.) To counterbalance the effect of something; to compensate for.（抵消；补偿）(n.) Something that compensates for something else.（抵消物；补偿）
- **中文释义**：抵消；弥补；（名词）抵消额；补偿
- **语域**：商业、经济、新闻
- **同义词**：counterbalance, compensate, neutralize, make up for
- **常见词组**：offset losses/costs; carbon offset（碳补偿/碳抵消）; offset the decline in X with Y
- **拓展**：
  - 动词：offset → offset → offset（不规则变化，三态同形）
  - 文中隐含逻辑：卡累利阿旅游收入的增长 *offsets* 了芬兰因失去俄罗斯游客而产生的损失（只是地理位置不同）
  - 碳排放领域的 "carbon offset" 是当代高频词组，考试和写作中均须掌握
- **例句**：
  > The surge in domestic travel **offset** — and ultimately exceeded — the revenue that Russian tourists had previously spent abroad.

**3. rupture** /ˈrʌptʃər/ *n. / v.*

- **英文释义**：A sudden or violent break in relations, communications, or structures; to break or burst.（决裂；破裂；断绝）

- **语域**：新闻、外交、医学（身体义）
- **同义词**：break, split, fracture, severing（of ties）
- **常见词组**：rupture in relations / ties / trade; rupture of diplomatic relations; suffer a rupture（医学：疝气/裂伤）
- **拓展**：
  - 可数/不可数均可
  - 原文"разрыва отношений с Россией"（after the rupture of relations with Russia）是外交语境翻译的经典句式：**after the rupture of / breakdown in [diplomatic] relations with...**
  - 区分：*rupture*（突然决裂，带有戏剧性）；*breakdown*（逐渐崩溃）；*severance*（正式切断）
- **例句**：
  > The **rupture** in bilateral relations plunged border communities into an economic tailspin from which they have yet to recover.

**4. plunge** /plʌndʒ/ *v. / n.*

**6. lament** /ləˈment/ *v. / n.*
