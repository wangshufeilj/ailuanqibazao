🔸 **Cockroaches will be watching us.**
🔻 **蟑螂将会监视我们。**

---

🔹 **В войнах нового поколения живая природа и микроэлектроника сольются воедино...**
🔸 **In new-generation wars, living nature and microelectronics will merge into one...**
🔻 **在新一代战争中，自然界与微电子学将合二为一……**

> **merge** = 融合；**microelectronics** = 微电子学；整句奠定全文的技术-军事框架。

---

🔹 **...позволив вывести на поле боя насекомых-разведчиков...**
🔸 **...allowing for the deployment of insect scouts on the battlefield...**
🔻 **……从而将昆虫侦察兵投放到战场上……**

> **deployment** 在军事语境里是“部署”，不是一般意义上的“放置”。

---

🔹 **Современное поле боя всё больше зависит от технологий...**
🔸 **The modern battlefield increasingly depends on technology...**
🔻 **现代战场越来越依赖技术……**

> 文中用无人机、卫星、热像仪串起“高技术战争”的常见图景，再转入“黑暗地带”。

---

🔹 **Но есть среда, где этот набор резко теряет эффективность – тоннели, бункеры и городские коммуникации.**
🔸 **But there is an environment where this toolkit sharply loses its effectiveness – tunnels, bunkers, and urban utilities.**
🔻 **但有一种环境会让这套装备的效力骤降——隧道、地堡和城市管网。**

> 这是全文最关键的转折：常规技术在地下空间失灵，成为生物侦察需求的现实来源。

🔸 **Therefore, the military is seeking solutions that can work specifically in such "dark zones."**
🔻 **因此，军方正在寻求能在这些“黑暗地带”工作的解决方案。**

> **seek solutions** 是政策/安全报道高频搭配，语气比 simple "want" 更正式。

---

🔹 **На стыке биологии и высоких технологий формируется... биологические дроны.**
🔸 **At the intersection of biology and high technology... biological drones.**
🔻 **在生物学和高科技的交汇点上，正在形成生物无人机这一领域。**

> **intersection** 有“交汇点”的抽象意味，适合学术和评论写作。

🔹 **Именно НАТО стало катализатором превращения этой антиутопии в чертежи и устройства.**
🔸 **It was NATO that became the catalyst for turning this dystopia into blueprints and devices.**
🔻 **正是北约成为将这一反乌托邦构想变成蓝图和设备的催化剂。**

> **catalyst** 这里是比喻义，强调“推动者”；整句带明显评论色彩。

🔻 **开发合同由 Swarm Biotactics 财团获得……**

> **consortium** = 财团 / 联合体；凸显项目是跨公司、跨国协作。

🔻 **……向该项目投入了超过1500万欧元。**

> 资金规模说明项目并非概念玩具，而是已进入较成熟的工程化阶段。

🔻 **昆虫背上固定着一个微型“背包”……**

# 模块一：翻译与全文概要

## 英文翻译

**Cockroaches Will Be Watching Us**

*In next-generation warfare, living nature and microelectronics will merge into one, enabling insect scouts to be deployed on the battlefield — capable of penetrating places no conventional machine can reach.*

The modern battlefield is increasingly dependent on technology: drones observe from above, satellites provide terrain mapping, and thermal imagers help locate targets. Yet there is an environment where this toolkit loses effectiveness sharply — tunnels, bunkers, and urban infrastructure. There, communication is weaker, navigation is impaired, and there is virtually no "transparency" for surveillance. That is why the military is seeking solutions that can operate precisely in such "dark zones": small, inconspicuous, and able to penetrate where standard-sized equipment cannot.

Hence the interest in devices that camouflage themselves as familiar elements of the urban environment — they are harder to detect and easier to deploy for reconnaissance in enclosed spaces. No wonder engineers are increasingly turning to the natural world. At the intersection of biology and high technology, a field is emerging that until recently seemed like the stuff of dystopian fiction — biological drones.

NATO itself became the catalyst for transforming this dystopia into blueprints and prototypes. Analysis of combat operations in Ukraine by European military strategists showed that conventional drones, for all their successes, are often blind and helpless when the target is hidden deep underground, behind concrete bunker slabs, or in the rubble of urban debris.

The response to this challenge was a NATO-initiated program to develop ultra-miniature biological drones. The development contract was awarded to the Swarm Biotactics consortium — a joint American-German venture backed by heavyweights of the defense industry. The venture fund of the German industrial conglomerate Rheinmetall and the American investment group In-Q-Tel (known for working directly in the interests of the U.S. intelligence community) invested more than 15 million euros in the project at the prototype stage.

The technology being developed in laboratories in Kassel, Germany, and San Francisco, USA, balances on the edge of science fiction and rigorous neurophysiology. Externally, the biodrone resembles an ordinary Madagascar hissing cockroach — this species was chosen for a reason: it is large enough (up to 7–8 cm) to carry a payload, and remarkably hardy. A microscopic "backpack" weighing up to 10–15 grams is attached to the insect's back, housing an electronic brain, battery, and sensors.

Electrodes are implanted into the cockroach's body, connected to its nervous system and brain — a direct hookup to the insect's biological wiring. Weak electrical impulses sent from the control station intercept control over the cockroach's motor functions. The insect is not pulled by strings; rather, signals are sent straight to its "steering wheel" — that is roughly how to describe the technology developed in collaboration with neural-interface specialists from the Technical University of Darmstadt. The impulses cause the insect to turn left, right, or accelerate, while the cockroach itself believes it is moving of its own free will.

A single spy cockroach is already a threat. But the real revolution lies not in the hardware itself, but in the software: it transforms a single device into a controllable system, and at scale — into a tool of mass deployment. The developers are betting on swarm interaction. Radio-wave transmitters mounted on the insects' backs allow them to exchange data with each other, forming a self-organizing network.

In such a scenario, not one cyborg is sent into a basement or bunker, but several dozen. They spread through ventilation ducts, crevices, and corners, relaying each other's signals. If one "scout" is destroyed or cannot proceed, the others work around the obstacle and transmit a terrain map to the ground station. Such a network can cover vast areas of underground fortifications where modern radar systems are powerless.

The company stresses that scaling production of this technology does not require building new factories. "We scale through reproduction, not through factories," Swarm Biotactics' management boasts, hinting that cockroaches are the ideal consumable — one that nature has already learned to produce in batches of millions.

Notably, the biodrone race has long ceased to be one-sided. While German engineers are teaching cockroaches to crawl through bunkers, Russia has placed its bet on airspace. The Neiry group of companies has presented technology for controlling pigeons.

Externally, the PJN-1 pigeon biodrone is distinguishable from an ordinary pigeon only by a thin wire protruding from its head and a neat backpack with solar panels on its back. Electrodes implanted in the bird's brain are connected to a stimulator. The operator loads a flight mission into the bird's controller, and the stimulator sends impulses, making the pigeon "want" to fly in the desired direction.

The range and autonomy of such a drone far exceeds the capabilities of a conventional quadcopter. As the developers claim, ravens can be used for carrying heavier payloads, seagulls for coastal surveillance, and albatrosses can patrol ocean waters for weeks.

Naturally, such experiments have drawn fierce criticism from bioethicists. Duke University Professor Nita Farahani called the practice of turning animals into controllable devices "repulsive," comparing it to treating a living being as a commodity. However, this does not appear to be stopping the developers. Swarm Biotactics emphasizes that the process is painless for insects, and that their wellbeing is a prerequisite for effective operation. The Russian startup Neiry even compares controlling a pigeon to horse riding: the animal's free will is restricted, but its fundamental rights to life are not violated.

According to Defence Blog, Swarm Biotactics systems have already undergone field trials in operational environments in Europe and the United States and have been delivered to "paying NATO customers," including Bundeswehr structures. The existence of a fully functional prototype, a stable funding channel, and military interest indicates that serial production of biodrones is a matter not of years, but of months.

The emergence of such systems signals the formation of a completely new battlefield — micro-warfare, in which the very concepts of "front line" and "rear" are definitively erased. Traditional defensive measures, proven effective against drones, are powerless here. You cannot shoot down a cockroach crawling through the ventilation shaft of a command post with a machine gun. Electronic warfare systems that jam control signals for quadcopters prove useless against a swarm of cyborgs: even if contact with the "center" is lost, the insect will continue moving autonomously, obeying its last command or simply the inertia of a living organism.

In the wars of the next generation, living nature and microelectronics will merge into one, enabling insects to be deployed on the battlefield. And if today it is a cockroach or a pigeon, then tomorrow, with the miniaturization of electronics, a fly or a mosquito could become the spy.

### **W — 写作高频词（续）**

---

**9. catalyst** /ˈkætəlɪst/ *n.*（可数）

- **英文释义**：①（化学）A substance that makes a chemical reaction happen more quickly without being changed itself. ②（引申义）Something or someone that causes an important change or event to happen.（朗文）
- **中文释义**：①催化剂 ②（引申）促进变化的人/事物；导火索
- **语域**：新闻/学术/书面（引申义极高频）
- **同义词**：trigger, impetus, driving force, stimulus
- **拓展**：
  - 核心搭配：**act as a catalyst for sth**（成为某事的催化剂）；**catalyst for change/reform/growth**
  - 词性变化：*catalytic* (adj.)，*catalysis* (n.)，*catalyze* (v.)
  - 词源：1900年，源自 *catalysis*（化学催化）+ *analyst* 的构词模式
  - ⚠️ 考点：引申义在新闻和学术写作中极为常见，需牢记 **catalyst for** 这一固定搭配
- **例句**：
  - The battlefield lessons learned in Ukraine have **acted as a catalyst for** a new wave of military investment in biological reconnaissance technology.
  - 乌克兰战场上汲取的教训已**成为**新一轮生物侦察技术军事投资的**催化剂**。

---

**10. consortium** /kənˈsɔːrtiəm/ *n.*（可数，复数 consortia 或 consortiums）

- **英文释义**：A group of companies or organizations who are working together to achieve a particular goal.（朗文）
- **中文释义**：财团；联合体；（企业/机构）联合集团
- **语域**：商业/法律/新闻/正式
- **同义词**：syndicate, alliance, joint venture（合资企业）, coalition
- **拓展**：
  - 词源：拉丁语 *consortium*（合伙关系），来自 *consors*（共有人）
  - 常见搭配：**a consortium of companies/banks/universities**；**a joint consortium**；**form/lead a consortium**
  - ⚠️ 复数形式：*consortia*（正式/学术）或 *consortiums*（更口语化），两者均可接受
  - 商法重点词：在公司法/合同法中常指联合竞标体或联合融资方
- **例句**：
  - A **consortium** of European defense firms secured the contract to supply the next-generation drone communication system to NATO member states.
  - 一个欧洲国防企业**联合体**获得合同，为北约成员国供应新一代无人机通信系统。

### **R — 阅读高频词**

---

**11. neural** /ˈnʊrəl/ *adj.*

- **英文释义**：Relating to a nerve or the nervous system.（朗文，技术性用词）
- **中文释义**：神经（系统）的；与神经相关的
- **语域**：技术/医学/学术
- **同义词**：neurological（更宽泛，指神经学的）
- **拓展**：
  - 常见搭配：**neural network**（神经网络，AI领域高频词）；**neural circuit**（神经回路）；**neural activity**（神经活动）；**neural interface**（神经接口）；**neural implant**（神经植入体）
  - 词族：*neuron* (n. 神经元)，*neurological* (adj. 神经学的)，*neurophysiology* (n. 神经生理学)，*neuroethics* (n. 神经伦理学)
  - 注意区分：*neural* 特指"与神经相关的"（技术性），*neurological* 偏指"神经系统疾病/功能"（临床性）
- **例句**：
  - The **neural interface** technology used in biodrones sends electrical pulses directly into the insect's **neural** pathways, overriding its natural motor responses.
  - 生物无人机所使用的**神经接口**技术将电脉冲直接发送至昆虫的**神经**通路，覆盖其自然运动响应。

**12. swarm** /swɔːrm/ *n.（可数）/ v.*

- **英文释义**：（n.）① A large group of insects, especially bees, moving together. ② A large number of people or things moving together. （v.）① (of insects) to move in a swarm. ② To move somewhere in large numbers.（剑桥）
- **中文释义**：（n.）①（昆虫）群；（人/物）大批涌动 （v.）①成群移动 ②（人群）蜂拥而入
- **语域**：通用/学术/新闻；军事语境中 **swarm technology/drone swarm** 为高频术语
- **拓展**：
  - 关键搭配：**a swarm of bees/locusts**（一群蜂/蝗虫）；**drone swarm**（无人机集群）；**swarm intelligence**（群体智能，AI/军事术语）；**swarm with sth**（到处是…，如 *The place was swarming with tourists*）
  - ⚠️ 注意动词用法：**swarm with** 表示"某地充满/挤满某物"（固定搭配）
  - 本文中 *Swarm Biotactics* 以此命名，暗含"集群作战战术"之意
- **例句**：
  - Military planners are increasingly concerned about the threat posed by **drone swarms** programmed to **swarm** enemy command structures simultaneously.
  - 军事规划者对被编程为同时**蜂拥袭击**敌方指挥结构的**无人机集群**所构成的威胁日益警惕。

**13. subterranean** /ˌsʌbtəˈreɪniən/ *adj.*

- **英文释义**：Beneath the surface of the Earth.（朗文，通常作定语）
- **中文释义**：地下的；地表以下的
- **语域**：书面/学术/新闻（正式）
- **同义词**：underground, subsurface, subsoil
- **拓展**：
  - 词源：拉丁语 *subterraneus*，*sub*（在…下方）+ *terra*（土地）
  - 常见搭配：**subterranean passage/tunnel/cavern/network**（地下通道/隧道/洞穴/网络）；**subterranean fortifications**（地下工事）
  - 引申义（书面）：隐秘的、秘密进行的（如 *subterranean political currents*，地下政治暗流）
  - 区分：*subterranean* 比 *underground* 更正式、更具书面色彩
- **例句**：
  - Modern radar systems remain largely ineffective against targets concealed within **subterranean** bunkers reinforced with multiple concrete layers.
  - 面对藏匿于多层混凝土加固的**地下**掩体中的目标，现代雷达系统在很大程度上依然无能为力。

**14. implant** /ɪmˈplænt/ *v.* / /ˈɪmplænt/ *n.*（可数）

- **英文释义**：（v.）To put something into a person's body by means of a surgical operation; to fix an idea, feeling, or attitude firmly in someone's mind. （n.）Something that has been implanted into a person's body.（朗文）
- **中文释义**：（v.）①将…植入（体内）；②（将想法/情感）灌输、根植 （n.）植入物
- **语域**：医学/技术（动词）；通用/书面（引申义）
- **同义词（v.）**：embed, insert, graft（移植）; instill（灌输，引申义）
- **拓展**：
  - 注意重音变化：**动词** /ɪmˈplænt/，**名词** /ˈɪmplænt/（英语中此类名/动重音对立现象常见，另见 *record*, *permit*, *protest*）
  - 常见搭配：**implant electrodes/chips/devices**（植入电极/芯片/装置）；**implant ideas in sb's mind**（在某人脑中植入想法）；**dental/breast/cochlear implant**（牙种植体/乳房假体/人工耳蜗）
  - 法律/医学语境：implant 在医疗器械监管法规中为核心词汇
- **例句**：
  - The research team **implanted** micro-electrodes into the motor cortex of the subject to enable precise, real-time movement control.
  - 研究团队将微电极**植入**受试对象的运动皮层，以实现精确的实时运动控制。

**15. prototype** /ˈproʊtətaɪp/ *n.*（可数）

- **英文释义**：①The first form that a new design of a car, machine, etc. has; a model used to test the design before it is produced. ②Someone or something that is one of the first and most typical examples of a group or situation.（朗文）
- **中文释义**：①（产品/机器）原型；样机 ②（最早的）典型；雏形
- **语域**：技术/新闻/商业/学术
- **同义词**：model, template, archetype（原型/典型，偏抽象义）
- **拓展**：
  - 词源：希腊语 *prōtotypon*，*prōto-*（最初的）+ *typos*（印记、模型）
  - 常见搭配：**a working prototype**（能够运行的原型）；**develop/build/test a prototype**；**prototype of/for sth**
  - ⚠️ 区分：*prototype*（首个实物模型）vs. *model*（泛指模型，不强调"首个"）vs. *template*（模板，偏抽象）
- **例句**：
  - The company confirmed that a **working prototype** of the biodrone had already been successfully tested in enclosed urban environments.
  - 该公司证实，生物无人机的**可运行原型机**已在封闭城市环境中成功完成测试。

**16. relay** /rɪˈleɪ/ *v.* / /ˈriːleɪ/ *n.*（可数）

- **英文释义**：（v.）①To receive and pass on information, news, etc.; to send radio or TV signals from one point to another. （n.）①A relay race. ②An electrical device that responds to small currents to control larger ones.（朗文/剑桥）
- **中文释义**：（v.）①传递（信息/信号）；中继转发 ②转告 （n.）①接力赛 ②中继（器）；继电器
- **语域**：通用/技术/新闻
- **拓展**：
  - 注意重音：**动词** /rɪˈleɪ/，**名词** /ˈriːleɪ/
  - 常见搭配：**relay a message/signal/information**（传递信息/信号）；**relay station**（中继站）；**relay race**（接力赛）
  - 军事/通信语境：**relay a signal through a network**（通过网络中继信号）
  - ⚠️ 同形词区分：*relay* (v. 转发) vs. *re-lay* (v. 重新铺设，如 re-lay a carpet)
- **例句**：
  - Each insect in the swarm functioned as a node in a mesh network, **relaying** reconnaissance data back to the ground station in real time.
  - 集群中的每只昆虫都充当网状网络中的一个节点，将侦察数据实时**中继回传**至地面站。

**17. replenish** /rɪˈplɛnɪʃ/ *v.*

- **英文释义**：To make something full again by adding a new supply; to bring something back to its former level.（朗文）
- **中文释义**：补充；重新填满；恢复（至原有水平）
- **语域**：书面/新闻/正式
- **同义词**：refill, restock, restore, top up (非正式)
- **拓展**：
  - 常见搭配：**replenish supplies/stocks/reserves**（补充供给/库存/储备）；**replenish one's energy**（补充精力）
  - 词源：古法语 *repleniss-*，*re-*（再次）+ *plenir*（填满），与 *plenty* 同源
  - 写作亮点词：比 *refill* 更正式，尤适用于学术/新闻写作
- **例句**：
  - Unlike conventional weapon systems that require industrial supply chains, biodrone programs can **replenish** their biological components simply through natural reproduction.
  - 与依赖工业供应链的传统武器系统不同，生物无人机项目仅需通过自然繁殖即可**补充**其生物组件。

**18. miniaturization** /ˌmɪniətʃərɪˈzeɪʃən/ *n.*（不可数）

- **英文释义**：The process of making something very small using modern technology; the trend toward making electronic components progressively smaller.（剑桥）
- **中文释义**：微型化；小型化
- **语域**：技术/学术/新闻
- **拓展**：
  - 词性变化：*miniaturize* (v.)，*miniature* (adj./n.，微型的/缩小版)
  - 常见搭配：**the miniaturization of electronics/components/devices**；**advances in miniaturization**（小型化技术进步）
  - ⚠️ 翻译注意：中文"微型化"与"小型化"在不同语境下均可，前者强调极致缩小（如纳米级），后者更通用
  - 剑桥例句：*"Since 1980 there has been major progress in miniaturization, nanotechnology, materials science."*
- **例句**：
  - The rapid **miniaturization** of microprocessors means that tomorrow's spy devices could be embedded in organisms as tiny as a mosquito.
  - 微处理器的迅速**微型化**意味着，明天的间谍装置可能会被嵌入蚊子这样微小的生物体中。

**19. bioethics** /ˌbaɪoʊˈɛθɪks/ *n.*（不可数）

- **英文释义**：The study of the ethical, social, legal, and philosophical issues arising from advances in biology and medicine, such as genetic engineering and experimental treatments.（剑桥）
- **中文释义**：生命伦理学；生物伦理学
- **语域**：学术/新闻/政策
- **拓展**：
  - 词性变化：*bioethicist* (n. 生命伦理学家)，*bioethical* (adj.)
  - 子领域词：*neuroethics*（神经伦理学）= 神经技术领域的伦理问题；*research ethics*（研究伦理）
  - 常见搭配：**bioethics committee/panel**（生命伦理委员会）；**raise bioethical questions**（引发生命伦理问题）；**bioethics of military use**（军事应用的生命伦理）
  - ⚠️ 翻译注意：*bioethics* 通译"生命伦理学"（含义宽泛）；*neuroethics* 译为"神经伦理学"（仅限神经技术）；两者需准确区分
- **例句**：
  - The field of **bioethics** has struggled to keep pace with the speed at which brain-computer interface technologies are being developed and commercialized.
  - **生命伦理学**领域一直难以跟上脑机接口技术被开发和商业化的速度。

**20. commodity** /kəˈmɑːdɪti/ *n.*（可数，复数 commodities）

- **英文释义**：①A product that is bought and sold, especially a raw material or primary agricultural product. ②A useful or valuable thing, quality, or person.（朗文）
- **中文释义**：①商品；货品（尤指大宗商品/初级产品）②（引申）有价值的东西；稀缺资源
- **语域**：经济/商业/法律/书面（引申义）
- **同义词**：product, good(s)；（引申义）asset, resource
- **拓展**：
  - 重要经济/商法词汇：**commodity market**（大宗商品市场）；**commodity prices**（商品价格）；**soft commodities**（软性商品，如农产品）；**hard commodities**（硬性商品，如金属/能源）
  - 引申义（写作亮点）：*Time is a precious commodity.*（时间是宝贵的财富）—— 此类用法极具表达力
  - 本文中被用于强调生物伦理批评：将生命体视为 *commodity*（商品），具有强烈道德谴责色彩
  - ⚠️ 考点：区分 *commodity*（可交易商品，偏经济）vs. *good(s)*（更宽泛，指任何商品）vs. *product*（成品/产品）
- **例句**：
  - Critics argue that treating sentient animals as an interchangeable **commodity** for military use crosses a fundamental ethical red line.
  - 批评人士认为，将有感知的动物当作可替换的军事用途**消耗品**，跨越了一条基本的伦理红线。

**21. intercept** /ˌɪntərˈsɛpt/ *v.* / /ˈɪntərsɛpt/ *n.*

- **英文释义**：（v.）To stop, catch, or take control of someone or something before they are able to reach the place they are going to; to redirect or block a signal, message, or moving object before it reaches its intended destination.（朗文）
- **中文释义**：（v.）拦截；截获；截断（通信/信号）（n.）拦截（行动）
- **语域**：军事/法律/通信/新闻
- **同义词**：capture, block, seize, tap（截听）
- **拓展**：
  - 常见搭配：**intercept a signal/message/call**（截获信号/信息/电话）；**intercept a missile/aircraft**（拦截导弹/飞机）；**intercept enemy communications**（截获敌方通信）
  - 法律语境：**wiretapping and signal interception**（窃听与信号截获）涉及隐私权相关法律问题
  - 注意重音：**动词** /ˌɪntərˈsɛpt/，**名词** /ˈɪntərsɛpt/
  - 本文用法精妙：*impulses intercept control over the cockroach's motor functions*（脉冲截取了对蟑螂运动机能的控制权）——比 *take over* 更精准
- **例句**：
  - Electronic warfare systems designed to **intercept** conventional drone control signals are largely ineffective against biodrones operating on biological feedback loops.
  - 专为**拦截**传统无人机控制信号而设计的电子战系统，对依赖生物反馈回路运行的生物无人机基本失效。

**22. scalable** /ˈskeɪləbəl/ *adj.*

- **英文释义**：Able to be changed in size or scale; (of a system, network, or process) able to handle increasing amounts of work or be expanded readily.（朗文）
- **中文释义**：可扩展的；可伸缩的；（规模上）可升级的
- **语域**：科技/商业/新闻
- **拓展**：
  - 词性变化：*scalability* (n., 不可数，可扩展性)，*scale* (v./n.)
  - 常见搭配：**a scalable solution/system/model/platform**（可扩展的解决方案/系统/模式/平台）；**highly scalable**（高度可扩展的）
  - 商业/科技写作核心词：本文中作者以 *scalability through reproduction* 揭示生物武器的独特优势
  - ⚠️ 区分：*scalable* 强调"规模可调节"，*flexible* 强调"灵活可变"，*sustainable* 强调"可持续性"
- **例句**：
  - The **scalable** nature of insect-based biodrone programs — relying on natural reproduction rather than industrial manufacturing — represents a genuinely disruptive paradigm shift.
  - 基于昆虫的生物无人机项目依赖自然繁殖而非工业生产，其**可扩展性**代表了一种真正颠覆性的范式转变。

**23. repulsive** /rɪˈpʌlsɪv/ *adj.*

- **英文释义**：①Extremely unpleasant, in a way that almost makes you feel sick. ②（技术性）Relating to repulsive forces that push objects away from each other.（朗文）
- **中文释义**：①令人厌恶的；令人作呕的 ②（物理）排斥性的（排斥力）
- **语域**：通用/新闻（义①）；物理学（义②）
- **同义词（义①）**：disgusting, revolting, abhorrent, repugnant
- **词性变化**：*repulse* (v. 击退；使厌恶)，*repulsion* (n. 厌恶；排斥力)，*repulsively* (adv.)，*repulsiveness* (n., 不可数)
- **拓展**：
  - 本文原文（英译）中法拉哈尼教授用 "repulsive" 描述将动物改造为可控工具的行为，语气极强
  - 区分近义词强度：*unpleasant*（不愉快）< *disgusting*（令人恶心）≈ *repulsive*（令人厌恶）< *abhorrent*（令人憎恶）
- **例句**：
  - Several bioethicists described the use of living animals as disposable surveillance hardware as **repulsive**, arguing it reduces sentient creatures to mere instruments.
  - 数位生命伦理学家将把活体动物用作一次性监控硬件的行为描述为"**令人厌恶的**"，认为此举将有感知的生命降格为纯粹的工具。

### **S — 熟词僻义 / 引申义**

---

**24. transparent** /trænsˈpærənt/ *adj.*

- **英文释义**：①Easy to see through（透明的）. ②（比喻义）Open and honest, without hidden motives. ③（本文语境）Used in the metaphorical sense of "visible/legible to surveillance" — a space that is not *transparent* means surveillance cannot penetrate it.（朗文/OED）
- **中文释义**：①透明的 ②（行为/机构）公开透明的 ③（本文军事语境引申义）可被侦察/感知到的；"透明性"（战场可见性概念）
- **语域**：通用（义①）；政治/新闻（义②）；军事专业用语（义③）
- **⚠️ 僻义重点**：本文中俄语原文"нет «прозрачности» для наблюдения"（没有观测上的"透明性"）被翻译为英文 *no "transparency" for surveillance*。**transparency** 在现代军事理论中有专业含义，指战场上敌我态势对侦察方的"可见程度"——某区域对侦察"透明"意味着该区域完全暴露于监控之下；反之则为"暗区（dark zone）"。

**27. blind** /blaɪnd/ *adj. / v.*

- **英文释义**：
  - ①（adj.常规义）Unable to see.（无法看见的；失明的）
  - ②（adj. 引申义）Unable to notice or understand something; not informed about something.（对…无法察觉；无知觉的）（朗文）
  - ③（adj. 军事/情报引申义）Unable to detect, observe or perceive a target — "blind to a threat" means lacking sensor coverage of it.（对目标无侦测能力的）
  - ④（v.）To make someone unable to see, permanently or temporarily; to prevent from understanding.（使失明；蒙蔽）
- **中文释义**：①失明的 ②视而不见的；蒙在鼓里的 ③（军事引申）无感知能力的；侦察盲区 ④（动词）使看不见；蒙蔽
- **⚠️ 熟词僻义重点**：本文中 *drones are often **blind** and helpless when the target is hidden underground*（当目标藏于地下时，无人机往往**盲目**而无能为力）用的是引申义③——设备对目标**没有探测能力**，形成"侦察盲区"，而非物理视力问题。这是军事/情报写作中的高频隐喻。
- **语域**：通用（义①②）；军事/新闻（义③）；书面（义④）
- **同义词（义②③）**：oblivious (to), unaware (of), unable to detect
- **拓展**：
  - 重要搭配：**turn a blind eye to sth**（对…视而不见，睁一只眼闭一只眼）；**blind spot**（盲点；知识/侦测的死角）；**blind faith**（盲信）；**fly blind**（盲飞，喻义：在无信息的情况下决策）
  - 词性变化：*blindly* (adv.)，*blindness* (n., 不可数)，*blinder* (n. 眼罩)
  - ⚠️ 固定搭配考点：**turn a blind eye** 是写作金句，尤其适合描述监管失职、国际社会对某问题的漠视
- **例句（义③）**：
  - Even the most sophisticated aerial surveillance systems are effectively **blind** once targets descend below a few meters of reinforced concrete.
  - 即便是最先进的空中侦察系统，一旦目标下降至几米厚的钢筋混凝土之下，也实际上陷入**完全失盲**状态。
- **例句（固定搭配）**：
  - International regulators have largely **turned a blind eye to** the rapid militarization of biological research, citing jurisdictional ambiguity.
  - 国际监管机构以管辖权模糊为由，基本上对生物研究的快速军事化**视而不见**。

**28. dystopia** /dɪsˈtoʊpiə/ *n.*（可数，通常单数）

- **英文释义**：An imaginary place where life is extremely difficult and a lot of unfair or immoral things happen. （朗文）OPP *utopia*（乌托邦）
- **中文释义**：反乌托邦；（虚构的）噩梦式社会；极权苦境
- **语域**：文学/学术/新闻（越来越常见于非文学语境）
- **同义词/反义词**：反义词 *utopia*（理想社会）；近义词 *anti-utopia*，*nightmare scenario*
- **拓展**：
  - 词源：1900年代，*dys-*（希腊语"坏的"）+ *utopia*（乌托邦，托马斯·莫尔1516年造词）；词根 *dys-* 同见于 *dysfunction*（功能失调）、*dyslexia*（阅读障碍）

  - 词性变化：*dystopian* (adj.)，*dystopist* (n. 反乌托邦作家，较罕见)
- **例句**：
  - What once seemed like a **dystopian** science fiction scenario — insects controlled by machines to spy on human beings — is now being actively funded and field-tested.

**29. paradigm shift** /ˈpærədaɪm ʃɪft/ *n.*（可数）

- **英文释义**：An important change in which the usual way of thinking or doing something is replaced by another way of thinking or doing something.（朗文）；A complete change in attitude towards the economy or a particular industry, caused by an event or discovery that has changed your way of thinking.（朗文商业词典）
- **中文释义**：范式转变；（思维/行业）革命性转变；颠覆性转型
- **语域**：学术/商业/新闻（高频，有时被滥用为流行词）
- **拓展**：
  - 词源：哲学/科学史术语，由托马斯·库恩（Thomas Kuhn）在1962年《科学革命的结构》（*The Structure of Scientific Revolutions*）中提出，原指科学体系的"范式转变"

  - 常见搭配：**a major/fundamental paradigm shift**；**represent/constitute a paradigm shift**；**paradigm shift in thinking/strategy/warfare**
  - 相关词：*paradigm* (n. 范式，典型模式)
- **例句**：
  - The ability to **scale military capability through biological reproduction** rather than industrial manufacturing constitutes a genuine **paradigm shift** in the economics of warfare.
  - 通过生物繁殖而非工业制造来**扩展军事能力**，构成了战争经济学上真正意义的**范式转变**。

**30. mesh** /mɛʃ/ *n.（可数/不可数）/ v.*

- **英文释义**：
  - （n.）①A material made of threads or wires woven together with holes between them; a net-like structure. ②（技术）A network of interconnected nodes in which each is connected to several others — **mesh network**.（剑桥）
  - （v.）①To fit or work together well. ②To become entangled in a mesh.（朗文）
- **中文释义**：（n.）①网眼；网状物；格网 ②（技术）网状网络，每节点与多节点相互连通 （v.）①（齿轮/计划等）咬合；匹配运作 ②缠住
- **⚠️ 熟词僻义**：本文关键技术术语 **mesh network**（网状网络/自组织网络）——区别于传统的"星形网络"（所有节点连接单一中心），网状网络中每个节点与多个邻近节点直接相连，具有极强的抗毁性。即使部分节点失联，信息仍可绕路传输。这是本文生物无人机集群的核心技术原理。
- **语域**：通用（n.义①）；技术/通信（n.义②）；书面（v.）
- **同义词（v.）**：interlock, align, dovetail (into), synchronize
- **拓展**：
  - 核心搭配：**mesh network**（网状网络）；**mesh with sth**（与某物协调配合）；**a fine/wire mesh**（细网/铁丝网）；**self-organizing mesh**（自组织网状网络）
  - 剑桥例句：*"The low-power wireless technology can be embedded into a wide variety of devices to be part of a **self-organizing mesh network**."*
  - 动词变形：mesh / meshed / meshed（规则变化）；meshing (现在分词)
- **例句（技术义）**：
  - Unlike traditional hub-and-spoke communication architectures, a **mesh network** of insect biodrones can reroute signals autonomously when individual nodes are lost.
  - 与传统的中心辐射型通信架构不同，昆虫生物无人机构成的**网状网络**可在单个节点失联时自主重新路由信号。
- **例句（v.义）**：
  - The biological and electronic components of the biodrone must **mesh** seamlessly to ensure stable, real-time signal transmission.
  - 生物无人机的生物组件与电子组件必须**无缝咬合**，以确保稳定的实时信号传输。

**31. on the edge of** *固定短语*

- **英文释义**：At or near the point of transition between two very different states; at the boundary of two concepts.（剑桥，引申）
- **中文释义**：处于…的边缘；处于…与…之间的临界点
- **语域**：通用/新闻/书面
- **原文用法**：*"The technology balances **on the edge of** science fiction and rigorous neurophysiology."*（这项技术游走于科幻与严格神经生理学的**边缘**之间。）
- **拓展**：
  - 近似表达：**on the brink of**（临界点，多用于灾难性事件前）；**at the intersection of**（在…的交叉点上，更学术）；**teetering between**（摇摆于…之间）
  - 区分：*on the edge of* 强调临界状态的戏剧感；*on the verge of* 更常搭配即将发生的动作（如 *on the verge of collapse*）
  - 搭配：**on the edge of reality and fiction / science and ethics / war and peace**
- **例句**：
  - The laboratory's research **on the edge of** neuroscience and weapons engineering has attracted intense ethical scrutiny from academic institutions worldwide.
  - 该实验室游走于神经科学与武器工程**边缘**的研究，吸引了全球学术机构的强烈伦理审查。

**32. make a bet on / bet on** *动词短语*

- **英文释义**：To commit resources or strategy to a particular approach, believing it will succeed; to place confidence in.（朗文，引申）
- **中文释义**：押注于；将赌注押在…上；战略性选择并押宝某一方向
- **语域**：新闻/商业/口语化书面
- **原文用法**：*"The developers are **betting on** swarm interaction."*（开发商**押注于**集群协作。）
- **拓展**：
  - 这是新闻写作中极为地道的商业/战略隐喻表达，比 *rely on* 或 *focus on* 更生动，含有"风险性选择"的隐含语义
  - 常见搭配：**bet on a technology/strategy/candidate**；**double down on a bet**（加倍押注）；**a risky bet**（冒险的押注）
  - 正式替换表达（写作中可与之互换）：*place their stake in*, *stake their strategy on*, *invest confidence in*
- **例句**：

**33. (there is) no such thing as** *惯用句式*

- **英文释义**：Used to say that something does not exist or is not possible; a rhetorical structure denying the existence of an assumed concept.（朗文）
- **中文释义**：（世上）不存在…这回事；…根本不存在
- **语域**：口语/新闻/学术（修辞性否定，极具说服力）
- **原文映射**：文章结尾逻辑相当于 *"there is no such thing as a safe rear area"*（"不存在所谓安全的后方"），即在微战争时代，前线/后方的传统概念彻底消失。
- **拓展**：
  - 这是英语中极高频的**修辞性否定句式**，尤适合论述文写作中推翻一个假定前提
  - 变体：*there is no longer such a thing as…*（…这回事已不复存在）；*there is no such thing as a free lunch*（天下没有免费的午餐，著名谚语）
  - 类似表达：*X is a myth*（X是一个神话/谎言）；*the notion of X is obsolete*（X的概念已过时）
- **例句**：
  - In an age of biodrone swarms, **there is no such thing as** a secure command bunker — the very infrastructure designed for protection becomes a vulnerability.
  - 在生物无人机集群时代，**根本不存在**所谓安全的指挥掩体——那些专为保护而设计的基础设施本身反而成了弱点。

**34. scale through** *动词短语（商业/科技地道表达）*

- **英文释义**：To grow or expand a system, process, or production model by leveraging inherent multiplicative properties rather than linear resource addition; distinct from *scale up* (增大规模) in that it implies organic or structural multiplication.（商业新闻用法）
- **中文释义**：通过（内在的扩增机制）实现规模化；以非线性方式扩张
- **语域**：科技/商业/新闻（当代流行表达）
- **原文用法**：*"We scale through **reproduction**, not through factories."*（"我们通过**繁殖**来实现规模化，而不是通过工厂。"）——这是本文最具颠覆性的一句话，精炼地概括了生物武器系统与传统武器的根本差异。
- **拓展**：
  - 区分：*scale up*（扩大规模，通用，多指投入更多资源）vs. *scale through X*（借助X这一内在机制实现扩张，更精准）
  - 当代商业写作高频词：*This model scales through network effects.*（该模式通过网络效应实现规模化。）
  - 相关词组：*scalability*（可扩展性）；*at scale*（在规模化阶段）；*scale-resistant*（难以规模化的）
- **例句**：
  - Unlike drone manufacturers who must invest in new production lines, a biodrone program can theoretically **scale through** natural insect reproduction alone.
  - 与必须投资新生产线的无人机制造商不同，生物无人机项目理论上仅需依靠昆虫的自然繁殖就能实现**规模化扩张**。

- **例句**：

  - 自主生物无人机系统的扩散将不可避免地**侵蚀**国际人道法所依赖的平民空间与军事空间之间的界限。

---

**37. render** /ˈrɛndər/ *v.*

- **英文释义**：①To cause something or someone to be in a particular state; ②To provide or give something; ③（技术）To process and display visual data.（朗文）
- **中文释义**：①使（某物/人）处于某种状态 ②提供；给予 ③（计算机）渲染
- **⚠️ 熟词僻义**：义①在学术与新闻写作中极常见，但中国学生往往只记住义②（提供）。**render sth + adj./n.** = "使某物变得/成为…" 是高频书面句型，相当于 *make*，但更正式有力。
- **语域**：书面/正式/法律/学术
- **同义词（义①）**：make, cause to become, leave (in a state)
- **拓展**：
  - 核心句型：**render sth + adjective**（使某物变得…）：*render sb helpless/useless/obsolete/ineffective*
  - 法律语境（义②）：**render a verdict/judgment/decision**（作出裁决/判决/决定）——法律英语高频词
  - 常见搭配：**render assistance**（提供援助）；**render services**（提供服务）；**render sb speechless**（使人无言以对）
- **例句（义①）**：
  - The swarm's decentralized architecture **renders** traditional signal-jamming countermeasures largely **ineffective**, since each insect can navigate autonomously without a central controller.
  - 集群的去中心化架构使传统信号干扰反制手段**基本失效**，因为每只昆虫无需中央控制器即可自主导航。
- **例句（法律义）**：
  - The arbitral tribunal is expected to **render its decision** on the dual-use technology export dispute within the next sixty days.
  - 仲裁庭预计将在未来六十天内就这起两用技术出口争议**作出裁决**。

**38. harness** /ˈhɑːrnɪs/ *v. / n.*

- **英文释义**：（v.）①To control and use the natural force or power of something; ②（具体义）To put a harness on a horse.（朗文）（n.）Equipment used to control a horse; a set of straps.
- **中文释义**：（v.）①驾驭；利用（自然力量/技术/潜力）②为马套具 （n.）马具；安全带
- **⚠️ 熟词僻义**：义①是学术/科技写作中的核心高频词，但很多中国学生只知道"马具"的具体义。**harness + 抽象名词** = 有效利用/驾驭某种力量或潜力，是科技与政策写作的重要表达。
- **语域**：学术/科技/新闻（义①）；通用（义②/n.）
- **同义词（义①）**：utilize, exploit (中性义), channel, leverage
- **拓展**：
  - 核心搭配：**harness the power of/energy/potential/technology**；**harness nature/biology/AI**
  - 本文关联：biodrone程序本质上是在 **harnessing** nature's biology for military ends（将自然生物学驾驭用于军事目的）
- **例句**：
  - Military engineers are racing to **harness** the navigational instincts of migratory birds, potentially creating a class of biodrone that requires no electronic guidance at all.
  - 军事工程师正竞相**驾驭**候鸟的导航本能，有望打造出完全无需电子导引的生物无人机。

## 4D. 金句积累

这一组金句可以看作前面词汇与表达的“总回扣”：前半部分强调规模化、侦察与反制，后半部分则把全文的技术融合命题压缩成一句可直接引用的判断。

**金句一**

> **"We scale through reproduction, not through factories."**
> **"我们通过繁殖来实现规模化，而不是通过工厂。"**

*— Swarm Biotactics 管理层*

**写作价值分析**：
This sentence encapsulates the fundamental disruption biodrones pose to traditional weapons economics: the supply chain is biological, not industrial.
这句话精炼地道出了生物无人机对传统武器经济学的颠覆本质：供应链是生物性的，而非工业性的。

它可直接用于**技术颠覆、军事经济学或将自然武器化之伦理**等议题的写作。

**金句二**

Highly effective in argumentative writing as a **reductio ad absurdum** — demonstrating through an extreme example why an entire category of defense strategy needs rethinking.
在议论文写作中以**归谬法**极具说服力——通过一个极端案例说明整个防御战略范畴为何需要重新思考。

**金句三**
