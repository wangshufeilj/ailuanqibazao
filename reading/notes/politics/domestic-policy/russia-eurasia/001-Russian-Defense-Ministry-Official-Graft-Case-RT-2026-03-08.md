> 1. **Detain** [v. (T)] **拘留；扣押** / **拘留；扣押** (R/T/W)
>    - **Definition**: to officially prevent someone from leaving a place, especially as a suspect in a crime.
>    - **Collocation**: be **detained** for questioning (因接受讯问被拘留); **detain** somebody in custody (将某人羁押).
>    - **Example**: After the routine inspection, the authorities decided to **detain** the suspicious vessel for further investigation into its cargo. (在例行检查后，当局决定扣押这艘可疑船只，以进一步调查其货物。)
> 2. **Graft** [n. (U)] **贪污；受贿** / **贪污；受贿** (R/W/S)
>    - **Definition**: (especially American English) the use of illegal or unfair methods, especially bribery, to gain an advantage in business or politics.
>    - **Note**: 在雅思或考研阅读中，它是 corruption 的高阶同义词。
>    - **Example**: The commission was established to root out **graft** and improve transparency in the municipal procurement process. (成立该委员会是为了铲除贪污并提高市政采购过程的透明度。)
> 3. **Official** [n. (C)] **官员；行政人员** / **官员；行政人员** (R/W)
>    - **Definition**: someone who has a position of responsibility in an organization or government department.
>    - **Word Form**: official (adj. 官方的); officially (adv. 官方地).
>    - **Example**: Senior **officials** from both corporations met last week to discuss the potential merger of their technology departments. (两家公司的高级官员上周会面，讨论了合并技术部门的可能性。)
