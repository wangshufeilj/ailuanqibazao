# Analytical Reading: Trump—Conqueror of Iran or Adrenaline-Fueled Politician?

*   **来源 (Source):** 《莫斯科共青团员报》(Moskovsky Komsomolets / МК) - 俄罗斯发行量最大的报纸之一，以深度报道和独家评论见长。
*   **题目 (Title):** Трамп — победитель Ирана или политик на адреналине? Что выдает его мимика (Trump—Conqueror of Iran or Adrenaline-Fueled Politician? What His Facial Expressions Reveal)

### 全文精读解析 | Detailed Sentence-by-Sentence Analysis

🔻 **Трамп — победитель Ирана или политик на адреналине? Что выдает его мимика.**
🔹 **Trump / — / [the] winner / of Iran / or / [a] politician / on adrenaline? / What / [his] facial expressions / reveal.**
🔸 **特朗普——是伊朗的征服者，还是一个处于亢奋状态的政客？他的面部表情揭示了什么。**

> 1. **Adrenaline** (n.): /əˈdrenəlɪn/ 肾上腺素。在政治语境中常指“刺激、高度亢奋的状态”。
> 2. **Facial expression** (n. phr.): 面部表情。其中 **Facial** 为名词 **Face** 的形容词形式。
> 3. **Reveal** (v.): /rɪˈviːl/ 揭露，展现。雅思写作高频词，用于描述研究结果或证据显示出的真相。

---

🔻 **Профайлер Максим Коваленко разобрал жесты и интонации президента США.**
🔹 **Profiler / Maksim Kovalenko / analyzed / [the] gestures / and / intonations / of / the US President.**
🔸 **犯罪心理画像师马克西姆·科瓦连科分析了美国总统的手势与语调。**

> 1. **Profiler** (n.): /ˈproʊfaɪlər/ 犯罪心理画像师。指通过分析犯罪行为、言行举止来推断人心理特征的专家。
> 2. **Gesture** (n.): /ˈdʒestʃər/ 手势，姿态。
> 3. **Intonation** (n.): /ˌɪntəˈneɪʃn/ 语调，抑扬顿挫。

---

🔻 **В последние дни президент США Дональд Трамп почти ежедневно повторяет одну и ту же формулу: «Иран побежден».**
🔹 **In recent days, / US President / Donald Trump / has been repeating / almost daily / the same formula: / "Iran is defeated."**
🔸 **最近几天，美国总统唐纳德·特朗普几乎每天都在重复同样的一句话：“伊朗已被击败”。**

> 1. **Formula** (n.): /ˈfɔːrmjələ/ 公式，准则。在此处引申为“刻板的话术”或“成套的言论”。
> 2. **Defeat** (v.): /dɪˈfiːt/ 击败，战胜。在雅思阅读中常出现于战争、竞赛类题材。
> 3. **Donald Trump**: 唐纳德·特朗普，美国第45任及第47任总统，以极具个人风格的沟通方式著称。

---

🔻 **Чем чаще звучит эта фраза, тем больше вопросов она вызывает — и у политиков, и у психологов.**
🔹 **The more often / this phrase / is heard, / the more questions / it raises / — / both among / politicians / and / psychologists.**
🔸 **这句话听起来越频繁，引起的疑问就越多——无论是在政治家还是心理学家中。**

> 1. **The more... the more...**: 愈……愈……。经典语法结构，非常适合雅思写作表达逻辑关系。
> 2. **Raise a question**: 提出/引起问题。雅思写作地道搭配。
> 3. **Psychologist** (n.): /saɪˈkɑːlədʒɪst/ 心理学家。注意开头字母 p 不发音。

---

🔻 **Повод для новой дискуссии дал военкор Александр Долгачев, который со ссылкой на анонимного психиатра заявил, что поведение Трампа напоминает «классическую маниакальную фазу».**
🔹 **The impetus / for a new discussion / was provided / by war correspondent / Aleksandr Dolgachev, / who, / citing / an anonymous psychiatrist, / stated / that / Trump’s behavior / resembles / a "classic manic phase."**
🔸 **战地记者亚历山大·多尔加切夫引发了新一轮讨论，他援引一位匿名精神科医生的话称，特朗普的行为让人想起“经典的躁狂相”。**

> 1. **Impetus** (n.): /ˈɪmpɪtəs/ 动力，促进。雅思高阶词汇，常用于“引发某种讨论或变革”。
> 2. **Manic phase** (n. phr.): 躁狂相。心理学专业术语，指双相情感障碍中情绪极度高涨、精力过旺的阶段。
> 3. **War correspondent**: 战地记者。

---

🔻 **Разница, по словам собеседника, лишь в том, что в данном случае речь идет не о фантазиях пациента, а о человеке, реально принимающем решения, от которых зависит судьба мира.**
🔹 **The difference, / according to the speaker, / lies only in the fact / that / in this case / we are talking / not about / the fantasies / of a patient, / but about / a person / who actually / makes decisions / on which / the fate of the world / depends.**
🔸 **据这位知情人士所说，区别仅在于，在这种情况下，我们谈论的不是病人的幻想，而是一个真正做出决定、关系到世界命运的人。**

> 1. **Lie in**: 在于。雅思写作中解释原因的核心短语。
> 2. **Fantasy** (n.): /ˈfæntəsi/ 幻想，白日梦。
> 3. **Fate** (n.): /feɪt/ 命运。

---

🔻 **На этом фоне психолог и профайлер Максим Коваленко решил разобрать поведение американского лидера с профессиональной точки зрения.**
🔹 **Against this background, / psychologist and profiler / Maksim Kovalenko / decided / to analyze / the behavior / of the American leader / from a professional perspective.**
🔸 **在此背景下，心理学家兼画像师马克西姆·科瓦连科决定从专业角度分析这位美国领导人的行为。**

> 1. **Against this background**: 在此背景下。写作中常用的承上启下连接词组。
> 2. **Perspective** (n.): /pərˈspektɪv/ 视角，观点。雅思写作中“From the perspective of...”是高频句式。

---

🔻 **Эксперт неожиданно оказался наблюдателем событий почти изнутри: после круиза по Персидскому заливу он несколько дней не мог вылететь из Дубая в Москву из-за обострения ситуации вокруг Ирана.**
🔹 **The expert / unexpectedly / found himself / as an observer / of events / almost from the inside: / after a cruise / in the Persian Gulf, / he / was unable / to fly out / of Dubai / to Moscow / for several days / due to / the escalation / of the situation / around Iran.**
🔸 **这位专家意外地成为了近距离观察者：在波斯湾游轮旅行后，由于伊朗周边局势恶化，他在迪拜滞留数日无法飞回莫斯科。**

> 1. **Escalation** (n.): /ˌeskəˈleɪʃn/ 升级，恶化。常指冲突或紧张局势加剧。
> 2. **Persian Gulf**: 波斯湾。位于西亚，是世界重要的地缘政治中心和石油产区。
> 3. **Dubai**: 迪拜。阿拉伯联合酋长国人口最多的城市，中东地区的金融中心。

---

🔻 **По словам Коваленко, именно тогда он внимательно посмотрел последние выступления Трампа — и обратил внимание на характерную мимику, жесты и манеру речи.**
🔹 **According to Kovalenko, / it was then / that / he / closely watched / Trump’s recent speeches / — / and / noticed / the characteristic / facial expressions, / gestures / and / manner of speech.**
🔸 **据科瓦连科称，正是在那时，他仔细观看了特朗普最近的演讲，并注意到了其特征鲜明的表情、动作和言语方式。**

> 1. **Characteristic** (adj.): /ˌkærəktəˈrɪstɪk/ 特有的，典型的。
> 2. **Manner of speech** (n. phr.): 言谈举止，说话方式。

---

🔻 **Профайлер отмечает: когда человек регулярно повторяет одну и ту же формулу о победе, это может говорить не столько о фактах, сколько о внутренней необходимости закрепить собственную версию происходящего.**
🔹 **The profiler / notes: / when / a person / regularly repeats / the same formula / about victory, / it / may indicate / not so much / facts / as / an internal need / to reinforce / one's own version / of events.**
🔸 **画像师指出：当一个人定期重复同样的胜利话术时，这可能说明的不是事实，而是一种内在需求，即巩固其对所发生事件的个人解读。**

> 1. **Not so much... as...**: 与其说是……不如说是……。高级比较句式。
> 2. **Reinforce** (v.): /ˌriːɪnˈfɔːrs/ 加强，巩固。
> 3. **Version** (n.): /ˈvɜːrʒn/ 版本，（对某事物的）描述。

---

🔻 **В языке тела это проявляется в демонстративных жестах, расширенной амплитуде движений и повышенной эмоциональной интонации.**
🔹 **In body language, / this / manifests / in / demonstrative gestures, / expanded amplitude / of movements / and / heightened / emotional intonation.**
🔸 **在肢体语言中，这表现为夸张的手势、动作幅度加大以及情绪激昂的语调。**

> 1. **Manifest** (v.): /ˈmænɪfest/ 表现，显现。常用于描述症状或现象的出现。
> 2. **Amplitude** (n.): /ˈæmplɪtuːd/ 振幅，广度。此处指动作的“幅度”。
> 3. **Heightened** (adj.): /ˈhaɪtnd/ 提高的，加强的。常用于描述情绪或感官的增强。

🔻 **Но главный вопрос, который сейчас обсуждают эксперты, звучит иначе: насколько самостоятельны решения Трампа.**

🔸 **但专家们目前讨论的核心问题并非如此，而是：特朗普的决策在多大程度上是独立的。**

> 1. **Independent** (adj.): /ˌɪndɪˈpendənt/ 独立的，不受影响的。
> 2. **Currently** (adv.): /ˈkɜːrəntli/ 目前，现在。雅思写作中替换 "now" 的常用词。

---

🔻 **«В последнее время информационное пространство переполнено диаметрально противоположными оценками психического состояния 47-го президента США».**
🔹 **"Recently, / the information space / has been filled / with / diametrically / opposite / assessments / of / the mental state / of the 47th US President."**
🔸 **“近期，信息空间充斥着对美国第47任总统精神状态截然相反的评估。”**

> 1. **Diametrically opposite** (adv. + adj. phr.): 截然相反。极其地道的表达方式，形容观点完全不同。
> 2. **Assessment** (n.): /əˈsesmənt/ 评估，估价。
> 3. **The 47th US President**: 指唐纳德·特朗普（根据文章设定的未来时间线2026年，指其第二个任期）。

---

🔻 **«От утверждений о "классическом пациенте психиатрии" до обвинений в симуляции и следовании "теории безумца"».**
🔹 **"From / assertions / about / a 'classic psychiatric patient' / to / accusations / of / faking / and / following / the 'madman theory'."**
🔸 **“从‘经典的心理疾病患者’论调，到指责其装疯卖傻并遵循‘疯子理论’。”**

> 1. **Assertion** (n.): /əˈsɜːrʃn/ 断言，声明。
> 2. **Madman theory** (n. phr.): 疯子理论。这是一种外交政策理论，源于尼克松时期。其核心是通过表现得不可预测甚至“疯狂”，让敌手因恐惧其做出极端行为而选择退让。

---

🔻 **«Как практикующий психолог, профайлер с многолетним стажем, я считаю важным отбросить эмоциональные ярлыки и провести трезвый, профессиональный анализ...»**
🔹 **"As / a practicing psychologist, / a profiler / with years of experience, / I consider it / important / to discard / emotional labels / and / conduct / a sober, / professional analysis..."**
🔸 **“作为一名执业心理学家、拥有多年经验的画像师，我认为摒弃情绪化的标签并进行冷静、专业的分析至关重要……”**

> 1. **Discard** (v.): /dɪˈskɑːrd/ 丢弃，摒弃。
> 2. **Sober** (adj.): /ˈsoʊbər/ 清醒的，冷静的。不仅指不醉酒，更多指态度克制。
> 3. **Practicing** (adj.): 在职的，执业的（如律师、医生）。

🔻 **«...основанный на доступных данных: мимике, жестикуляции, публичных высказываниях и контексте событий», — говорит Коваленко.**
🔹 **"...based on / available data: / facial expressions, / gestures, / public statements / and / the context of events," / — / says / Kovalenko.**
🔸 **“……这种分析基于现有数据：面部表情、手势、公开陈述以及事件背景，”科瓦连科说。**

> 1. **Available** (adj.): /əˈveɪləbl/ 可获得的。
> 2. **Public statement** (n. phr.): 公开声明。
> 3. **Context** (n.): /ˈkɑːntekst/ 背景，语境。

🔻 **Его предположение будет касаться трех ключевых аспектов: наличие тяжелой психиатрии (шизофрении), состояние аффекта (мания) и суверенность личности.**
🔹 **His hypothesis / will concern / three / key aspects: / the presence / of / severe mental illness / (schizophrenia), / a state of affect / (mania), / and / the sovereignty of the individual.**
🔸 **他的设想将涉及三个关键方面：是否存在严重精神疾病（精神分裂症）、情感状态（躁狂）以及人格的自主权。**

> 1. **Hypothesis** (n.): /haɪˈpɑːθəsɪs/ 假设，假说。学术词汇。
> 2. **Schizophrenia** (n.): /ˌskɪtsəˈfriːniə/ 精神分裂症。
> 3. **Sovereignty** (n.): /ˈsɑːvrənti/ 主权，自主权。

🔻 **— Как вы думаете, реальна ли версия о том, что у Трампа манифестировала поздняя шизофрения?**
🔹 **— Do / you / think / the version / that / late-onset schizophrenia / has manifested / in Trump / is realistic?**
🔸 **——你认为关于特朗普表现出晚发型精神分裂症的说法是现实的吗？**

> 1. **Late-onset** (adj.): 晚发的（指疾病）。
> 2. **Realistic** (adj.): /ˌriːəˈlɪstɪk/ 现实的，可能的。

---

🔻 **— Первый и самый простой вопрос — может ли 79-летний Трамп страдать шизофренией? Теоретически да.**
🔹 **— The first / and / simplest question / is / — / can / 79-year-old Trump / suffer from / schizophrenia? / Theoretically, / yes.**
🔸 **——第一个也是最简单的问题是——79岁的特朗普可能患有精神分裂症吗？理论上是可以的。**

> 1. **Theoretically** (adv.): /ˌθiːəˈretɪkli/ 理论上地。
> 2. **Suffer from**: 患有（疾病），受……之苦。

---

🔻 **Современная психиатрия признает возможность как поздней (после 40 лет), так и очень поздней (после 60 лет) манифестации шизофрении.**
🔹 **Modern psychiatry / recognizes / the possibility / of / both / late / (after age 40) / and / very late / (after age 60) / manifestation / of schizophrenia.**
🔸 **现代精神病学承认晚发型（40岁后）和极晚发型（60岁后）精神分裂症发作的可能性。**

> 1. **Psychiatry** (n.): /saɪˈkaɪətri/ 精神病学。
> 2. **Recognize** (v.): /ˈrekəɡnaɪz/ 承认，识别。

---

🔻 **Более того, существуют задокументированные клинические случаи дебюта заболевания в 70 лет.**
🔹 **Moreover, / there are / documented / clinical cases / of / the disease's onset / at age 70.**
🔸 **此外，还有记录在案的70岁发病的临床案例。**

> 1. **Documented** (adj.): /ˈdɑːkjumentɪd/ 有据可查的，记录在案的。
> 2. **Onset** (n.): /ˈɑːnset/ （尤指不快的事物）开始，发作。

---

🔻 **Однако, говоря о Трампе, мы должны учитывать историчность наблюдения.**
🔹 **However, / when speaking / about Trump, / we / must consider / the historicity / of observation.**
🔸 **然而，说到特朗普，我们必须考虑观察的历史连续性。**

> 1. **Historicity** (n.): /ˌhɪstəˈrɪsəti/ 历史真实性，历史性。

## 英译全文

**Trump — Victor Over Iran or an Adrenaline-Driven Politician? What His Facial Expressions Reveal**
*Profiler Maxim Kovalenko dissects the gestures and intonations of the U.S. president*

In recent days, U.S. President Donald Trump has been repeating virtually the same formula every day: "Iran has been defeated." The more often this phrase is uttered, the more questions it raises — among politicians and psychologists alike.

The latest wave of discussion was triggered by war correspondent Alexander Dolgachev, who, citing an anonymous psychiatrist, claimed that Trump's behavior resembles "a classic manic phase." The key distinction, according to his source, is that this is not a patient's fantasy, but a man who actually makes decisions on which the fate of the world depends.

Against this backdrop, psychologist and profiler Maxim Kovalenko decided to examine the American leader's behavior from a professional standpoint. The expert found himself an almost inside observer of events: after a cruise in the Persian Gulf, he was stranded in Dubai for several days, unable to fly to Moscow due to the escalating situation around Iran.

According to Kovalenko, it was during that period that he carefully studied Trump's latest public appearances — and took note of characteristic facial expressions, gestures, and manner of speech.

The profiler notes: when a person regularly repeats the same formula of victory, this may speak less to facts than to an inner need to cement one's own version of events. In body language, this manifests in demonstrative gestures, an enlarged amplitude of movement, and heightened emotional intonation.

But the central question being debated among experts is different: how autonomous are Trump's decisions?

"Recently, the information space has been flooded with diametrically opposite assessments of the mental state of the 47th U.S. president — ranging from claims of 'a classic psychiatric patient' to accusations of simulation and adherence to the 'madman theory.' As a practicing psychologist and profiler with many years of experience, I consider it important to set aside emotional labels and conduct a sober, professional analysis based on available data: facial expressions, gestures, public statements, and the context of events," says Kovalenko.

His assessment will address three key aspects: the presence of severe psychiatric illness (schizophrenia), a state of affect (mania), and the sovereignty of personality (external or internal influences on behavior).

**— Do you think the possibility of late-onset schizophrenia in Trump is realistic? And can this even occur at nearly 80?**

— The first and simplest question: could 79-year-old Trump suffer from schizophrenia? Theoretically, yes. Modern psychiatry acknowledges the possibility of both late-onset (after age 40) and very-late-onset (after age 60) schizophrenia. Moreover, there are documented clinical cases of the disease manifesting at age 70.

However, when speaking of Trump, we must consider the historical record of observation. Over decades of public life — from *The Apprentice* to presidential campaigns — we do not observe the classic picture: a progressive emotional-volitional deficit, breakdown of social ties, gross impairment of critical thinking along schizophrenic lines. What we observe instead is a stable, hypertrophied, but cohesive personality structure.

My verdict: no. The diagnosis of "schizophrenia" (especially with onset at age 80) is an attempt to offer a simple explanation for a complex political phenomenon. This is not about Trump.

**— Could he be suffering from mania, as has already been reported?**

— The question of mania is more complex. The high-profile claim by correspondent Dolgachev, citing an anonymous psychiatrist, paints a picture of "a classic acute-ward patient" with delusions of grandeur and a "spring episode." Here it is important to distinguish between clinical presentation and personality type. I would characterize Trump as a person with hyperthymic accentuation. This means: elevated tone, sociability, impulsivity. His plans may change in a flash; he relies on intuition rather than rigid algorithms. He is dominant and craves combat. This is not a delusion of grandeur — it is "a boyish desire to win." His handshakes — whether with Vladimir Putin or other leaders — demonstrate, in my observation, not so much manic aggression as an attempt to establish equal-footing yet competitive contact, sometimes tinged with "boyish bravado." His facial expressions (squinting, pursing the lips) are established behavioral patterns, not spontaneous affective reactions.

My verdict: he has no clinical mania. What is taken for mania is a hyperthymic personality type, amplified by years of success and the absence of "brakes" in the form of criticism from his inner circle. This is not illness — it is character.

**— Perhaps the "madman" image serves him as a winning strategy?**

— One of the most interesting observations is Trump's ability to "play the fool." I see in this a subtle strategic move. Periodic slips of the tongue (confusing Albania with Cambodia, or Azerbaijan with Armenia) or absurd statements (a "war on paracetamol") may be part of an image designed to put competitors off guard and lead them to underestimate their opponent. This is not cognitive decline (dementia) — from which, incidentally, his father suffered, as has also been reported. This is a tool: Nixon's "madman theory," brought to perfection. Trump creates the image of an unpredictable person with whom direct confrontation is dangerous.

**— In your view, what influences his behavior?**

— The most unsettling questions concern the sovereignty of his decisions. The anonymous psychiatrist in the controversial publications described Melania Trump as a "more psychopathic" figure with influence over her husband. However, other specialists paint a different picture: Trump is "stubborn as a bull," possessing "absolute self-confidence," and ruthlessly expels "parasitic figures" from his circle. He is an ambitious dominant, and it is unlikely that anyone can truly manipulate him against his will. More likely, he himself chooses whom to listen to.

Pharmacological influence: the only documented risk worth noting relates to his COVID-19 treatment. The drug dexamethasone (a steroid) was part of the treatment regimen and can indeed cause feelings of euphoria, surges of energy, a sense of invincibility, and even paranoia. This is an important nuance: during his illness, his "strange" behavior may have been amplified by pharmacology. However, this is a temporary factor, not a permanent condition.

Some experts note that Trump's presidential authority is not absolute. He faces determined sabotage from the "deep state," which forces him to wage a constant struggle for control even over his own foreign policy instruments. This is not a mental disorder — it is a political reality, which, however, may constitute a powerful chronic stressor.

To summarize this psychological portrait, I must disappoint sensation-seekers: in my view, Donald Trump is fully competent. He does not suffer from schizophrenia and is not in a state of clinical mania. His behavior is the quintessence of a hyperthymic, dominant, and narcissistic personality accentuation, refined over years of showbusiness and high politics.

His facial expressions and gestures are controlled instruments behind which stands a calculating mind, not a chaotic psyche. His "eccentricities" are a conscious strategy (the "madman syndrome") or established habits. External influence on the president is minimal: he himself tends to dominate his environment rather than submit to it. The pharmacological factor (dexamethasone) may have come into play episodically, but does not define his personality. Trump cannot be called an "idiot" or a "madman," as his opponents sometimes do. He is a player who always wants to win. And so far, based on behavioral analysis, he remains fully in control of his game — however insane it may appear from the outside.

Whether the U.S. president acts on the basis of his own strategy, emotional impulse, or under the influence of a more complex system of political advice and pressure remains a matter of debate.

In any case, every new public gesture of the White House's occupant is today scrutinized almost under a microscope. For what lies behind the words of "victory" may determine not only the fate of the Middle East conflict, but global political stability as well.

**① profiler** /ˈproʊfaɪlər/ *n.*

- **英文释义：** A specialist who constructs a behavioral or psychological profile of a person, typically used in criminal investigation, intelligence work, or personality analysis.
  **中文释义：** 画像师；行为分析专家；通过分析行为、表情、语言等特征推断人格特质的专业人员。
- **语域：** 专业/心理学/新闻
- **同义词：** behavioral analyst, criminal profiler, psychological analyst
- **拓展：** 词根来自 *profile*（n./v. 轮廓、画像、描述特征），名词加后缀 *-er* 构成施动者名词。注意区分：
  - *profiler*（画像师，专业人士）
  - *profile*（n. 侧面像；人物简介；v. 描述特征）
  - "high-profile" 是常见合成形容词，意为"引人注目的"
  - "keep a low profile" 是固定表达，意为"保持低调"
- **例句：** The forensic **profiler** constructed a detailed behavioral portrait of the suspect based solely on the pattern of his communications.
  **法医画像师仅凭嫌疑人的通讯模式，就构建出了一幅详细的行为画像。**

**② quintessence** /kwɪnˈtesns/ *n.*

- **英文释义：** The most perfect or typical example of a quality or class; the purest or most essential part of something. *(Longman)*
  **中文释义：** 精华；典型；最完美的体现；某类事物最纯粹的本质。
- **语域：** 书面/正式/学术
- **同义词：** epitome, embodiment, essence, archetype
- **反义词：** caricature, distortion
- **拓展：** 来自拉丁语 *quinta essentia*（第五元素），古代哲学中指以太（ether），是超越土水火气的第五种元素，引申为"精华"。形容词形式：*quintessential*（adj. 典型的、精华的），副词：*quintessentially*。常见搭配：*the quintessence of elegance / courage / modern diplomacy*。
- **例句：** The statesman's address was considered the **quintessence** of measured rhetoric — compelling without being inflammatory.
  **这位政治家的讲话被认为是克制修辞的极致范本——有说服力却不煽情激进。**

**③ accentuation** /ækˌsentʃuˈeɪʃən/ *n.*

- **英文释义（psychiatry/psychology）：** In personality psychology, a pronounced but non-pathological intensification of certain character traits, making a person more vulnerable to specific psychological stressors. Derived from Leonhard's typology.
  **中文释义（心理学术语）：** 人格强调；指某些人格特质的非病理性强化，使人对特定类型的心理应激更为敏感。源于苏/俄心理学家列昂哈德的人格分类体系。
- **语域：** 学术/心理学/精神病学
- **同义词：** personality accentuation, character emphasis, temperament amplification
- **拓展：** 动词：*accentuate*（v. 强调、突出）；形容词：*accentuated*。在普通语境中，*accentuate* 常指"强调某特征"，如 *The lighting accentuated her cheekbones.* 注意该词在心理学语境中有专门技术含义，不同于日常"强调"义。
- **例句：** Researchers argue that **hyperthymic accentuation**, far from being a disorder, often correlates with exceptional drive and leadership capacity in public figures.
  **研究者认为，高情感气质型人格强调远非病理状态，在公众人物中往往与卓越的行动力和领导力相关联。**

**④ impulsive** /ɪmˈpʌlsɪv/ *adj.*

- **英文释义：** Doing things or tending to do things suddenly, without thinking carefully about whether they are sensible. *(Longman)*
  **中文释义：** 冲动的；凭一时冲动行事的；缺乏深思熟虑的。
- **语域：** 通用/心理学/新闻
- **同义词：** rash, impetuous, spontaneous, hasty
- **反义词：** deliberate, cautious, calculated, measured
- **拓展：** 副词：*impulsively*；名词：*impulsiveness*（不可数）；*impulse*（n. 冲动；推力）。注意区分：*impulsive*（行为模式，性格特征）vs. *spontaneous*（自然发生的，不含负面评价）vs. *rash*（莽撞的，更强调鲁莽危险性）。搭配：*impulsive decision / behavior / reaction / purchase*。
- **例句：** Her **impulsive** response to the journalist's question cost her weeks of diplomatic damage control.
  **她对记者提问的冲动回应让她随后数周都在进行外交层面的损控工作。**

**⑤ grandiose** /ˈɡrændiˌoʊs/ *adj.*

- **英文释义：** Grandiose plans or ideas seem very impressive but are not practical. *(Longman)*
  **中文释义：** 夸大的；宏大却不切实际的；（尤指心理学）夸大妄想的。
- **语域：** 书面/心理学/政治评论（多含贬义）
- **同义词：** overblown, inflated, bombastic, pretentious
- **反义词：** modest, understated, realistic
- **拓展：** 名词：*grandiosity*（夸大感；自大；在精神病学中为躁狂症核心症状之一）。固定搭配：*grandiose delusions*（夸大妄想，精神病学术语），*grandiose scheme/plan/vision*。注意词源来自拉丁语 *grandis*（宏大），与 *grand* 同源，但 *grandiose* 语义中含有"不切实际"或"过度自大"的贬义色彩。
- **例句：** The politician's **grandiose** promises of overnight economic transformation were met with skepticism by independent analysts.
  **这位政客关于经济一夜腾飞的宏大承诺遭到了独立分析人士的质疑。**

**⑥ demeanor** /dɪˈmiːnər/ *n.*（美式）/ **demeanour**（英式）

- **英文释义：** The way someone behaves, dresses, speaks, etc. that shows what their character is like. *(Longman)*
  **中文释义：** 举止；风度；仪态；外在言行所体现的性格特征。
- **语域：** 书面/正式/法律/新闻（法律语境中尤为常见：jury assessing demeanor）
- **同义词：** bearing, comportment, manner, conduct, behavior
- **拓展：** 不可数名词，单数。注意与 *manner*（方式；礼貌）区分：*demeanor* 侧重整体外在气质与态度，*manner* 更聚焦于行事方式或礼节。常见搭配：*composed demeanor, hostile demeanor, professional demeanor, public/private demeanor*。法律英语中，陪审团被允许观察证人的 *demeanor*（庭上表现）以判断可信度。
- **例句：** Despite the mounting pressure of the cross-examination, the witness maintained a calm, measured **demeanor** that impressed the jury.
  **尽管交叉质询的压力与日俱增，证人始终保持着沉稳克制的举止，给陪审团留下了深刻印象。**

**⑦ sovereignty** /ˈsɒvrənti/ *n.*（英式）/ /ˈsɑːvrənti/（美式）

- **英文释义：** ① Complete power to govern a country; ② the freedom and power to make one's own decisions without being controlled by anyone else. *(Longman)*
  **中文释义：** ① 主权；最高统治权；② 决策的独立自主性；不受他人控制的能力。
- **语域：** 正式/政治/法律/外交
- **同义词：** autonomy, supreme authority, self-determination, independence
- **拓展：** 形容词：*sovereign*（adj. 主权的；最高权力的；n. 君主）；动词无直接对应，常以 *exercise sovereignty, assert sovereignty, territorial sovereignty* 搭配出现。国际法核心概念之一：*state sovereignty*（国家主权），*sovereignty over territory*（对领土的主权）。文章中科瓦连科以"суверенность личности"（人格主权/决策独立性）指特朗普是否真正自主做决定。
- **例句：** The question of whether a sitting president retains genuine **sovereignty** over foreign policy — or merely ratifies decisions shaped by entrenched bureaucratic interests — has long vexed political scientists.
  **一位在任总统究竟是否真正拥有外交政策上的自主权，还是不过在批准由根深蒂固的官僚利益塑造的决定，这一问题长期困扰着政治学家。**

**⑧ stressor** /ˈstresər/ *n.*

- **英文释义：** A stimulus or event that causes stress, especially a psychological or environmental factor that disrupts normal physiological or psychological equilibrium.
  **中文释义：** 应激源；压力源；导致心理或生理应激反应的刺激因素。
- **语域：** 学术/心理学/医学
- **同义词：** stress trigger, pressure factor, stressor agent
- **拓展：** 源自 *stress*（n. 压力；v. 施加压力）+ 拉丁语施动者后缀 *-or*。区分：*stressor*（客观的压力来源）vs. *stress*（主体的压力感受）。常见搭配：*chronic stressor, acute stressor, environmental stressor, psychological stressor, occupational stressor*。
- **例句：** Chronic political opposition from within one's own administration can function as a persistent **stressor**, gradually eroding decision-making clarity.
  **来自本届政府内部的持续政治阻力可能成为一种长期应激源，逐渐侵蚀决策判断力。**

**① mania** /ˈmeɪniə/ *n.*（可数/不可数）

- **英文释义：**
  - ① *(medical, uncountable)* A serious mental illness characterized by abnormally elevated mood, energy, racing thoughts, and impaired judgment. *(Longman)*
  - ② *(countable)* A very strong desire or enthusiasm for something, especially one shared widely. *(Longman)*

  **中文释义：**
  - ① （医学）躁狂症；以情绪异常高涨、精力过剩、思维奔逸为特征的严重精神疾病。
  - ② 狂热；痴迷；席卷大众的强烈热情。

- **语域：** 医学（义①）；通俗/新闻（义②）
- **同义词（义②）：** craze, obsession, frenzy, fever
- **拓展：**
  - 词源：来自希腊语 *mainesthai*（精神失常），同根词：*maniac*（狂人）、*manic*（adj. 躁狂的）、*megalomania*（夸大妄想症）、*kleptomania*（偷窃癖）。
  - 注意区分：*mania*（临床诊断术语，不可随意用于描述他人）vs. *enthusiasm*（热情，中性）vs. *obsession*（执念，通常指有害的念头）。
  - 重要延伸：*bipolar disorder*（双相情感障碍）的核心症状是 *manic episode*（躁狂发作）与 *depressive episode*（抑郁发作）交替出现。
  - 本文涉及的"戈德华特规则"（Goldwater Rule）指出：未经当面诊查，精神科医生不得对公众人物作出精神病学诊断，此为美国精神病学协会（APA）伦理准则。

- **例句（义①）：** Physicians warned that **manic episodes** in elderly patients are often misattributed to stress or cognitive decline rather than recognized as treatable psychiatric conditions.
  **医生警告称，老年患者的躁狂发作往往被误归因为压力或认知衰退，而非被识别为可治疗的精神科病症。**

- **例句（义②）：** The sudden **mania for** cryptocurrency investment swept through office workers and retirees alike, fueled by social media hype and fear of missing out.
  **在社交媒体炒作和错过恐惧症的双重驱动下，一股加密货币投资狂热迅速席卷了上班族和退休人员群体。**

**② schizophrenia** /ˌskɪtsəˈfriːniə/ *n.*（不可数）

- **英文释义：** A serious mental illness in which someone's thoughts and feelings are not based on what is really happening around them. *(Longman)*
  **中文释义：** 精神分裂症；患者思维与情感脱离现实的严重精神疾病。
- **语域：** 医学/精神病学；书面
- **同义词：** psychosis（广义精神病）
- **拓展：**
  - 词源：希腊语 *schizein*（分裂）+ *phren*（心智），字面意思为"心智分裂"，但现代医学已不用"人格分裂"（split personality）描述此病，应注意与 *dissociative identity disorder*（解离性身份障碍）区分。
  - 延伸用法：文章最后一例句（"the Bush Administration's schizophrenia over Moscow"）体现了该词的引申义：**自相矛盾的政策立场**，属于新闻/政治评论语域的隐喻性用法，考试写作中需谨慎使用。
  - 分类：*paranoid schizophrenia*（偏执型）、*disorganized schizophrenia*（瓦解型）、*late-onset schizophrenia*（晚发性，文中核心概念）、*very-late-onset schizophrenia*（极晚发性，60岁后）。

- **例句：** The documentary challenged popular misconceptions by showing that people living with **schizophrenia** are far more likely to be victims of violence than perpetrators.
  **这部纪录片通过展示精神分裂症患者更可能是暴力受害者而非施害者，挑战了大众的错误认知。**

**③ dementia** /dɪˈmenʃə/ *n.*（不可数）

- **英文释义：** An illness that affects the brain and memory, and makes you gradually lose the ability to think and behave normally. *(Longman)*
  **中文释义：** 痴呆症；认知功能进行性衰退，影响记忆、思维和正常行为能力的脑部疾病。
- **语域：** 医学/学术/新闻
- **同义词：** cognitive decline, neurocognitive disorder
- **拓展：**
  - 区分：*dementia*（痴呆症，总称）、*Alzheimer's disease*（阿尔茨海默病，最常见类型）、*cognitive impairment*（认知损害，程度较轻）。

  - 与法律相关：痴呆症患者的民事行为能力认定在中美法律中均有专门规定（见4C模块）。

- **例句：** Early-stage **dementia** can be difficult to distinguish from normal age-related memory lapses, which is why standardized cognitive screening tools are essential in clinical settings.
  **早期痴呆症难以与正常的年龄相关记忆衰退相区分，这正是标准化认知筛查工具在临床环境中不可或缺的原因。**

**④ affect** /ˈæfekt/ *n.*（心理学专业名词，不可数/可数）

- **英文释义（psychology）：** The external expression of emotion, including facial expressions, tone of voice, and body language; the observable component of emotional state. Distinguished from *mood* (internal, sustained emotional state).
  **中文释义（心理学）：** 情感表现；情绪的外在表达，包括面部表情、语调和肢体语言；可观察到的情绪状态分量。
- **语域：** 学术/精神病学/心理学（此为名词专业义，与动词 *affect* 完全不同）
- **同义词：** emotional expression, affective display
- **拓展：**
  - ⚠️ **熟词僻义**：*affect* 作为**名词**在精神病学中是专有术语，与日常动词"影响"(*affect* /əˈfekt/) 拼写相同但读音略异（名词重音在前：/ˈæfekt/），意义截然不同。
  - 相关术语：*flat affect*（情感平淡，表情缺乏变化，精神分裂症症状之一）；*blunted affect*（情感钝化）；*labile affect*（情感不稳）；*appropriate/inappropriate affect*（情感反应适当/不适当）。
  - 文章中"состояние аффекта"（state of affect）对应英文：*affective state*，科瓦连科以此指情绪性驱动行为模式。

- **例句：** The psychiatrist noted that the patient's **flat affect** — a near-total absence of visible emotional response — was one of the most diagnostically significant features of the presentation.
  **精神科医生指出，患者的**情感平淡**——几乎完全缺乏可见的情绪反应——是此次就诊中最具诊断意义的特征之一。**

**⑤ narcissism** /ˈnɑːrsɪsɪzəm/ *n.*（不可数）

- **英文释义：** Excessive interest in or admiration of oneself and one's physical appearance; in psychology, a personality disorder characterized by an exaggerated sense of self-importance and lack of empathy. *(Longman)*
  **中文释义：** 自恋；过分关注自身形象和能力；（心理学）以夸大自我重要性和缺乏共情能力为特征的人格障碍。
- **语域：** 心理学/学术；亦见于日常批评语境（含贬义）
- **同义词：** self-centeredness, egotism, vanity
- **反义词：** altruism, empathy
- **拓展：**
  - 词源：希腊神话人物纳西索斯（Narcissus），因迷恋水中自己的倒影而憔悴致死，后被变为水仙花。
  - 区分：*narcissism*（性格特征，日常义）vs. *Narcissistic Personality Disorder (NPD)*（自恋型人格障碍，临床诊断术语）。本文中科瓦连科以 *narcissistic accentuation*（自恋型人格强调）描述特朗普，有意区别于 NPD 这一疾病诊断。

- **英文释义：**
  - *in any case*: Whatever has happened or whatever may happen; used to add a remark that strengthens or justifies a preceding statement. *(Longman)*
  - *in any event*: Whatever happens; used to emphasize that something will be true or will happen regardless of other circumstances. *(Cambridge)*

  **中文释义：**
  - **无论如何**；**不管怎样**；用于强调后续陈述不因其他情况而改变，常作为论证收尾的逻辑锚点；在前文已提出某立场后，以此短语进一步确认或加强该立场的确定性。

- **语域：** 通用；书面/口语均适用；学术写作中用于论证层次过渡
- **同义词：** regardless, nevertheless, at any rate, be that as it may, whatever the case may be
- **拓展：**
  - 朗文词典将 *in any case* 标注为"BUT"（转折）类连接词，说明其核心语用功能是**在承认其他可能性的前提下，仍坚持某一陈述为真**。
  - 区分（三个近义但语用侧重不同的短语）：
    - ***in any case***（无论如何）：最常用，强调不管之前情况如何，后文陈述均成立；
    - ***in any event***（无论如何）：比 *in any case* 更正式，多用于书面语和法律文本；
    - ***at any rate***（无论如何；至少）：口语色彩稍强，也可表"至少"（= *at least*）；
    - ***nevertheless / nonetheless***（尽管如此）：强调转折，含"虽然如此但……"的让步义，语义上比 *in any case* 更具对比张力。
  - 写作技巧：*in any case* 用于段末收尾时，能有效强化论证的终结感（finality），是政论文和学术评论中常见的**段落锚定词**（paragraph anchor）。

- **例句：** The proposed amendment may face procedural challenges, but **in any event**, the underlying policy objective enjoys broad cross-party support and is unlikely to be abandoned.
  **拟议修正案或许面临程序性障碍，但**无论如何**，其背后的政策目标享有广泛的跨党派支持，不太可能被放弃。**
