**How Iran's Air Defense Managed to Hit the American F-35**

*March 20, 2026 | World Affairs | Boris Dzherelievsky*

The American fifth-generation stealth aircraft F-35, as it turns out, appears quite clearly on the screens of Iran's air defense systems. Moreover, Iranian air defense crews managed to hit the fighter. Which surface-to-air missile system accomplished this—and why did both the F-35 and its pilot nevertheless survive?

An event has taken place in the Middle East that has a chance to enter the history of combat aviation: a fifth-generation aircraft has been damaged by air defense fire for the first time. At least, the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) has claimed that it struck a U.S. Air Force F-35 stealth fighter in Iranian airspace using air defense systems. Video footage of the strike on the American aircraft, recorded by the electro-optical targeting system of a surface-to-air missile complex (SAM), has been posted online.

The American side acknowledged the incident. CNN cited a statement from U.S. Central Command that the F-35 made an emergency landing at a U.S. air base in the Middle East, though "the aircraft landed safely and the pilot is in stable condition." Citing its own sources, the network confirms that the F-35 came under fire from Iran.

Several sources cast doubt on whether the pilot managed to fly back to base and land the aircraft. In support of this version, it is noted that since the night of March 19, Flightradar24 has been tracking intensive flights by a Boeing MH-47G Chinook search-and-rescue helicopter over the eastern Al-Jubail region in Saudi Arabia. However, this activity could be linked not to the search for a downed pilot and aircraft wreckage, but to training exercises for search-and-rescue personnel recently rushed to the Middle East who need to familiarize themselves with local conditions in a compressed timeframe.

Which SAM system specifically damaged the American fighter? The IRGC itself remains silent on the matter, but several media outlets name the Iranian-made mobile SAM system Majid as a contender for the title of "F-35 killer." As evidence, they point to the fact that the hit was recorded by the electro-optical targeting system of this complex. This version is maintained, in particular, by Military Watch Magazine.

The Majid, also known as the AD-08, is a mobile short-range system: it is mounted on a military pickup truck and can carry up to four missiles with a range of 700 to 8,000 meters and an altitude engagement envelope of 20 to 6,000 meters.

The Majid uses a multi-channel electro-optical/infrared station capable of detecting a target at a range of up to 15 km. The ability to detect and track adversaries without resorting to radar ensures high survivability for the system.

To elaborate: an electro-optical system on a SAM, operating in the infrared spectrum and receiving the thermal radiation of objects, can detect targets under any optical visibility conditions and at any level of radio interference—all while maintaining high covertness of combat operations. On some SAM systems, the control module is made detachable and remote-deployable, allowing for remote combat management to maximize crew safety.

During the twelve-day war of 2025, Iran's air defenses suffered serious losses, primarily from anti-radiation missiles, and the appropriate lessons were drawn. Today, radars are used only as a last resort and for brief periods; all other target detection and tracking work in Iran is carried out by electro-optical/infrared systems.

However, the footage provided by the Iranians is not in itself proof that the fighter was hit specifically by the Majid system. Moreover, it is a short-range system designed primarily to combat UAVs. One cannot fully trust either the United States, which claims the F-35 landed safely, or Iran, which claims it was shot down.

"Iran's air defense forces have quite a few SAM systems that use electro-optical guidance that could have hit the F-35," says Colonel (ret.) Yuri Knutov, director of the Air Defense Museum, speaking to Vzglyad. "These include our Pantsir, Tor, Buk M2, as well as a whole range of Iran's own developments. For example, the Mersad-16 is a more powerful system than the Majid. The AD-08 is a very light system designed to engage other targets, though one cannot rule out that it was indeed this SAM system."

At the same time, the expert doubts that the strike on the F-35 was carried out by the Russian S-300 system, which Iran also possesses. "After a powerful S-300 missile detonated, the aircraft would unlikely have been able to escape," Knutov says. "Most likely, the F-35 was hit by either a low-yield munition or it managed to evade the detonation and thereby sustained damage that still allowed it to reach the base. In any case, what matters is the very fact that it was hit, most likely through an electro-optical station. Although for radar it is also hard to call it invisible. Our SAM systems at the Hmeimim base in Syria could see these aircraft and were tracking them."

For the American-Israeli coalition, Iran's abandonment of radar systems—which radically reduced their ability to detect and suppress air defenses—came as an extremely unpleasant surprise. Furthermore, electro-optical systems operating in the visible and infrared spectrum cannot be neutralized by electronic warfare systems, and their operation is not registered by the onboard sensors that warn of radar illumination.

Yet the F-35 cannot be considered entirely defenseless against such a threat. The fighter is also equipped with the AN/AAQ-37 Distributed Aperture System (DAS), consisting of several optical sensors mounted on the fuselage capable of providing 360-degree coverage and projecting the image onto the pilot's helmet-mounted display.

This system can detect an attacking missile even with a passive guidance system, and the onboard computer can—without pilot input—activate automatic evasive maneuvers or begin deploying infrared flares. Apparently, the AN/AAQ-37 system saved the pilot by enabling a maneuver that reduced damage to the F-35, allowing it to reach the base (if it indeed made it).

This was not the first attempted engagement of American-Israeli coalition aircraft by Iran's air defenses. The Times of Israel reported on March 14 that an Israeli Air Force aircraft was attacked by Iranian air defense but managed to evade the missile. The type of aircraft was not reported, but judging by the fact that it detected the incoming missile and executed a counter-missile maneuver, it was likely an F-35.

It is strange, however, that the Israelis either failed to warn their allies about the emerging threat, or the Americans ignored the warning. This is all the more noteworthy given that Military Watch states that "the first-ever successful air defense strike against a fifth-generation aircraft is highly significant, as it may lead to a review of their operational doctrine across several theaters of war. And this event could influence the decisions of more than 20 countries around the world that have ordered the F-35."

As if by irony, just hours before the F-35 was hit, U.S. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth declared that Iran's air defenses had been "leveled to the ground"—yet within hours it became clear that the reports of their complete destruction had been somewhat exaggerated. Moreover, as it turned out, the latest American fighter was very much within reach of Iranian SAM systems with quite conventional characteristics, whose electro-optical systems could discern, fix, and track the "invisible" aircraft.

The tactics of Iranian air defense crews also pose a threat to the U.S.-Israeli coalition's air forces. Iran's mobile air defense systems "can appear virtually anywhere and give pilots very little time to react. These systems can be concealed virtually anywhere, and even after the complete destruction of fixed air defense assets, they will continue to operate effectively for a very long time," notes The War Zone.

The F-35's combat record remains modest, but it would be rash to underestimate the aircraft. Israel, in particular, has employed it to strike not only Iran but also Syria, including when Syrian air defense systems were still fully operational. Nevertheless, the very fact that a fifth-generation stealth fighter was hit in Iranian airspace demonstrates that it is no "invisible" aircraft in reality. And the American-Israeli coalition still has a long way to go before achieving full air superiority over Iran.

**W1. stealth** /stelθ/ *n.（不可数）*

**英文释义：** The quality of doing something quietly, slowly, and secretly; in military contexts, technology that makes aircraft difficult or impossible to detect by radar.
**中文释义：** 悄然行动；隐蔽性；（军事）隐身技术

**语域：** 军事/书面语/新闻

**同义词：** concealment, covertness, invisibility（军事语境）
**反义词：** conspicuousness, visibility, openness
**常见词组：** stealth technology, stealth bomber/fighter, by stealth, stealth mode

**拓展：**
- 名词（不可数）；形容词：*stealthy*；副词：*stealthily*
- 词源：古英语 *stælth*，与 *steal*（偷窃）同根，核心语义是"不被察觉地行动"
- "by stealth" = 偷偷摸摸地、秘密地，书面语常用
- 军事语境中 stealth aircraft 的核心不是光学隐身，而是**雷达截面积（RCS，Radar Cross Section）**极小，难以被雷达探测
- 区分：**stealth**（名词，技术/性质）vs. **stealthy**（形容词，描述行为方式）

**例句：**
> Decades of investment in **stealth technology** have not rendered any aircraft completely immune to detection, as passive **stealth** measures remain powerless against infrared tracking systems.
> 数十年对**隐身技术**的投入，并未使任何飞机对探测完全免疫，因为被动**隐身**手段在红外追踪系统面前依然无能为力。

**语域：** 学术/书面/正式

**同义词：** consequence, ramification, inference, significance
**反义词：** irrelevance, explicitness（针对"暗示"义）
**常见词组：** have profound/far-reaching implications for; by implication; the implication is that; implications for policy/security

**拓展：**
- 复数形式 *implications* 在学术与新闻写作中极为常见

**T1. neutralize** /ˈnuːtrəlaɪz/ *v.*

**英文释义：** ①To make something ineffective or harmless; to counteract the effect of something. ②(Military, euphemistic) To kill or put out of action; to disable an enemy position or personnel.
**中文释义：** ①消除（威胁）；使中和；使无效 ②（军事委婉语）歼灭；消灭；摧毁（阵地）

**同义词：** counteract, nullify, offset（一般义）；eliminate, destroy, suppress, kill（军事委婉义）
**反义词：** activate, enable, reinforce
**常见词组：** neutralize a threat/position/target; electronic warfare neutralizes; neutralize and eliminate

**拓展：**
- 规则动词：neutralize → neutralized → neutralized
- 名词：*neutralization*；形容词：*neutral*
- **翻译难点**：在军事/政治语境中，"neutralize" 往往是"kill/destroy"的委婉替代词（euphemism），译成中文时需根据语境判断是译"消除""中和"还是"歼灭""摧毁"

**T2. degrade** /dɪˈɡreɪd/ *v.*

**英文释义：**
① To reduce the quality, effectiveness, or capability of something; to cause deterioration.
② (Military) To reduce an enemy's operational capability without necessarily destroying it—a key concept in U.S. DoD targeting doctrine.
③ To lower someone in rank, dignity, or status; to treat someone with disrespect.

**中文释义：** ①降低（能力/质量）；削弱 ②（军事）消耗、压低（作战能力） ③降级；贬低；羞辱

**语域：** 军事/技术/正式/新闻

**同义词：** diminish, erode, impair, undermine, weaken（能力义）；demean, humiliate（尊严义）
**反义词：** enhance, upgrade, improve, strengthen
**常见词组：** degrade capabilities/performance, degrade the enemy, signal degradation, environmental degradation

**拓展：**
- 规则动词：degrade → degraded → degraded
- 名词：*degradation* /ˌdeɡrəˈdeɪʃən/（降级；退化；环境退化；羞辱）
- 形容词：*degraded*（被削弱的；退化的）；*degrading*（有辱人格的）
- **军事重要术语**：美国国防部（DoD）作战目标效果分级为：**Suppress → Neutralize → Degrade → Destroy**（压制→使丧失战斗力→削弱→摧毁），层层递进，*degrade* 表示使敌方能力降低但未彻底摧毁
- 剑桥词典例证："He said he believes the enemy's military capabilities have been **badly degraded**."
- 区分：**degrade**（使逐步削弱）vs. **destroy**（彻底摧毁）vs. **neutralize**（使暂时丧失功能）

**例句①（军事义）：**
> Sustained airstrikes over three weeks significantly **degraded** Iran's long-range radar network, but failed to **degrade** its mobile short-range systems, which quickly dispersed and resumed operations.
> 持续三周的空袭显著**削弱**了伊朗的远程雷达网络，但未能**削弱**其机动短程系统——后者迅速分散并恢复了作战运作。

**例句②（贬低义）：**
> Commentators warned that portraying the adversary's technology as primitive was both **degrading** to serious analysis and dangerous to operational planning.
> 评论人士警告，将对手的技术描述为原始落后既是对严肃分析的**贬低**，也对作战规划构成危险。

**S2. level** /ˈlevəl/ *v. / adj. / n.*

**常见义（熟）：** 水平的；级别；程度
**僻义/引申义：**
① *level sth to the ground* = 将某物夷为平地，彻底摧毁（文中赫格塞斯称伊朗防空被"сровняли с землей"，英文对应 "leveled to the ground"）

**例句②（提出指控）：**

**S3. fix** /fɪks/ *v. / n.*

**常见义（熟）：** 修理；固定；确定
**僻义/引申义：**
① (Military/Navigation) *a fix* = 定位；锁定目标位置（名词）—— 文中"фиксируют"（固定/锁定）对应英文 *fix on a target*
② *fix sb's position* = 精确确定……的位置（军事定位）

**语域：** ①军事/航海/正式；③④口语

**语域：** ①军事/体育（引申）；②商业；③政治/历史

**英文释义（僻义①）：** To take in and deal with something damaging without being destroyed; to sustain an impact and remain functional.
**中文释义（僻义①）：** （军事/比喻）承受打击；消化损伤；（受损后）仍保持运作

**拓展：**
- 规则动词：absorb → absorbed → absorbed
- 名词：*absorption*；形容词：*absorbent*（吸水的）；*absorbing*（引人入胜的）
- 军事语境：*The vehicle was designed to absorb a mine blast and protect the crew.* = 车辆被设计为能承受地雷爆炸并保护乘员

**例句：**
> The aircraft's composite airframe was designed to **absorb** a certain degree of blast damage and remain structurally intact, a design philosophy that may have been critical in allowing the damaged F-35 to remain airborne.

**词性：** 动词短语（新闻/正式/书面）

**英文释义：**
① (LDOCE, literal/military) To be shot at by enemy weapons: *"Our patrol came under fire from rooftop gunmen."*
② (LDOCE, figurative/media) To be severely criticized: *"Rail chiefs came under fire after raising train fares."*

**中文释义：** ①遭到炮火/枪击；受到实弹攻击 ②遭受批评；受到抨击（媒体/政治语境）

- 剑桥词典对比喻义的中文翻译：**遭受批评**（简体）/ **遭受批評**（繁体）

**L4. contested airspace** *(noun phrase)*

**音标：** /kənˈtestɪd ˈerˌspeɪs/
**词性：** 名词短语（军事/战略/正式）

**英文释义：** Airspace over or near an area where opposing forces are actively competing for air superiority, and where aircraft face a significant threat from enemy air defense systems or hostile fighter aircraft.
**中文释义：** 争议/争夺空域；敌我交火空域；高威胁空域

**L5. it would be rash to** *(sentence structure)*

**音标：** /ɪt wʊd biː ræʃ tə/
**词性：** 句型结构（正式/书面/新闻/学术）

**英文释义：** (LDOCE) A formal sentence pattern meaning it would be unwise, hasty, or imprudent to do something; *rash* = if you are rash, you do things too quickly, without thinking carefully about whether they are sensible or not.
**中文释义：** 轻率地……是不明智的；贸然……是草率的（正式委婉否定句型）

**语域：** 正式/书面/学术/新闻评论

**同义词句型：**
- *It would be premature to…*（下……结论还为时过早）
- *It would be foolish/unwise to…*（……是不明智的）
- *One should be cautious about…*（应对……保持谨慎）
- *There are grounds for caution in…*（在……方面有谨慎的理由）

**拓展：**
- *rash* (adj.) 朗文词典：*"if you are rash, you do things too quickly, without thinking carefully"*；词源来自古英语，与 *raze*（铲平）同有快速、不顾后果之意
- 剑桥词典将 *rash* 列入"not taking enough care"同义词组：*careless, sloppy, slapdash, thoughtless*
- **考试写作**：此句型可替代平淡的 "we should not…"，大幅提升书面语档次
- 变体：*"It would be equally rash to…"*（同样轻率的是……）——用于在承认一个观点的同时否定另一个极端

**例句：**
> **It would be rash to** conclude from a single engagement that stealth technology has been rendered obsolete; equally, **it would be rash to** dismiss this incident as a mere anomaly with no implications for future air operations.
> **轻率地**从一次交战中得出隐身技术已经过时的结论是不明智的；同样，**贸然**将这一事件视为对未来空中作战毫无影响的偶然异常也是草率的。

**音标：** /kʌm æz ən ʌnˈplezənt sərˈpraɪz/

**L7. reports of X's death have been greatly exaggerated** *(allusion/set phrase)*

**音标：** /rɪˈpɔːrts əv … dɛθ həv bɪn ˈɡreɪtli ɪɡˈzædʒəreɪtɪd/
**词性：** 典故句型（文学典故/新闻/书面）

**英文释义：** A paraphrase of a famous quotation attributed to Mark Twain (*"Reports of my death have been greatly exaggerated"*); used to suggest that something which was claimed to be finished, defeated, or destroyed has in fact survived and remains effective.
**中文释义：** 关于……死亡/消亡/被摧毁的报道言过其实（马克·吐温名句活用）；某事物远未消亡

**语域：** 新闻/书面/评论/讽刺

**同义词表达：** *X is far from dead/finished/defeated; premature reports of X's demise; the death of X has been overstated*

**拓展：**
- 原句出处：马克·吐温（Mark Twain）于1897年收到其去世的误报后所作回应，原话为 *"The reports of my death are greatly exaggerated."*
- 文章原文即使用了这一典故结构：*"reports of their complete destruction had been somewhat exaggerated"*（关于伊朗防空被彻底摧毁的报道有所夸大）
- 此句型在英文新闻评论中极为高频，可套用于任何"过早宣布某事物消亡"的语境：*"Reports of the death of traditional media have been greatly exaggerated."*
- 适合写作中用于反驳过于绝对的论断，语气既严肃又带有讽刺意味

**例句：**
> To borrow from Mark Twain: **reports of Iran's air defense network's destruction had been greatly exaggerated** — a lesson in the dangers of accepting battle-damage assessments at face value.
> 套用马克·吐温的话说：**关于伊朗防空网络被摧毁的报道言过其实**——这是一个关于轻信战损评估的危险的教训。

**L8. all but** *(adv. phrase)*

**音标：** /ɔːl bʌt/
**词性：** 副词短语（正式/书面/新闻）

**英文释义：** Almost completely; very nearly; used to describe something that is so close to a certain state that it is effectively in that state, though not technically/officially so.
**中文释义：** 几乎；差不多；实际上已经……（强调程度极高，仅一步之遥）

**语域：** 正式/书面/文学/新闻

**同义词：** almost, nearly, virtually, practically, as good as
**反义词：** far from, hardly, scarcely

**拓展：**
- **重要区分**：*all but* + 形容词/动词 = "几乎"（nearly, virtually）；*all but* + 名词 = "除……之外全部"（all except）——两种用法语义截然不同！
  - *"The debate is **all but** over."* = 辩论几乎已经结束。（nearly）
  - *"**All but** two soldiers survived."* = 除两人外全部士兵生还。（all except）
- 文中对应俄语 "фактически" 或 "почти"，英文最佳对应即 *all but*
- 朗文词典标注此用法为书面语，尤见于新闻和文学文体

**例句①（"几乎"义）：**
> The shift to passive electro-optical guidance has **all but** eliminated the effectiveness of conventional anti-radiation missile attacks, which rely on detecting radar emissions that these systems simply do not produce.
> 向被动式光电制导的转变**几乎**消除了传统反辐射导弹攻击的有效性，而后者依赖探测这些系统根本不产生的雷达辐射。

**例句②（"除……之外全部"义）：**
> **All but** the most mobile air defense units were destroyed in the first wave of strikes; those that survived did so precisely because they had dispersed before the attack commenced.
> **除最具机动性的防空单位外**，其余均在第一波打击中被摧毁；那些得以幸存的单位之所以生还，恰恰是因为它们在攻击开始前已完成疏散。

→ 《中华人民共和国对外贸易法》（2022年修订）及《军品出口管理条例》（2022年修订）构成中国军品/两用物项出口的基本法律框架，与本文武器扩散议题相关。

④ **"Federal Acquisition Regulation FAR Part 49 termination for convenience defense contracts"**
（美国法）联邦采购条例中的合同终止条款
→ FAR Part 49规定政府可以"为便利"单方面终止国防合同，对F-35等长期采购项目的合同稳定性具有重要法律含义，尤其在盟国因安全评估调整而欲退出采购协议时。

⑤ **"《中华人民共和国出口管制法》两用物项 无人机技术 军民两用管控"**
（中国法）出口管制法两用物项管控
→ 《出口管制法》（2020年施行）第二条明确将军事用途两用物项纳入管控范围，2023年商务部针对无人机相关零部件出口实施的管制措施是该法的最新适用案例，与本文涉及的无人机/光电技术扩散问题直接相关。

⑥ **"Law of Armed Conflict LOAC targeting proportionality civilian infrastructure"**
（国际人道法）武装冲突法中的比例原则与平民基础设施保护
→ 《日内瓦公约附加议定书I》第51-52条确立了打击军事目标时的区分原则（distinction）与相称性原则（proportionality）。本文提及伊朗机动防空系统隐匿于民用建筑附近，直接涉及打击合法性的法律评估问题。

## 4D. 金句积累

**Quotation Accumulation / 金句积累**

---

**金句一**

**原文（英译版）：**
> "These systems can be concealed virtually anywhere, and even after the complete destruction of fixed air defense assets, they will continue to operate effectively for a very long time."

**中文译文：**
> "这些系统几乎可以隐藏在任何地方，即便固定防空资产遭到彻底摧毁，它们仍将在相当长的时间内持续有效运作。"

**写作用途：** 适用于论述**非对称战争韧性**、**机动战术的持久性**、**技术优势的局限性**等主题。可改写为通用句式：*"Even after the complete elimination of [X], [mobile/distributed alternatives] will continue to [function] for a very long time."*

---

**金句二**

**原文（英译版）：**
> "It would be rash to underestimate the aircraft. Nevertheless, the very fact that a fifth-generation stealth fighter was hit in Iranian airspace demonstrates that it is no 'invisible' aircraft in reality."

**中文译文：**
> "轻视这款飞机是轻率的。然而，一架第五代隐身战机在伊朗领空被击中这一事实本身，就已证明它在现实中并非'隐形'飞机。"

**写作用途：** 适用于**辩证论证结构**（先退一步承认，再转折强调核心论点）；"it would be rash to + 动词" 句型可直接套用于任何议论文中对极端观点的反驳；"the very fact that…demonstrates that…"是高级学术/新闻写作的标志性句式。

---

**金句三**

**原文（英译版）：**
> "The American-Israeli coalition still has a long way to go before achieving full air superiority over Iran."

**中文译文：**
> "美以联军在实现对伊朗的完全制空权方面，仍有很长的路要走。"

**写作用途：** 经典**收尾句式**，简洁有力，以"还有很长的路要走"（*have a long way to go*）作为论点的开放式结论，暗示问题的复杂性与长期性。可套用于任何尚未解决的战略/政策/技术问题的结尾，语气客观而不失分量。改写模板：*"[Subject] still has a long way to go before [achieving the stated goal]."*
