## 模块一：翻译与全文概要

### 英文翻译

**Recently Updated Headline: Israel Continues Killing Al Jazeera Journalists Without Deterrence — Last Among Them: Washah**

Israeli targeting operations against journalists in Gaza have escalated to unprecedented levels, with the assassination of Al Jazeera correspondent Mohammed Washah, which highlights an approach aimed at silencing Palestinian media and preventing the true picture of the conflict from being conveyed.

Washah's assassination represents a new episode in a series of precise and deliberate Israeli targeting operations, confirming that Palestinian journalists in Gaza have become a strategic objective within the confrontation, not merely accidental victims of war.

An Israeli drone targeted Washah's vehicle on Wednesday evening as he drove down Al-Rashid Street near the Al-Nablusi intersection west of Gaza City.

**Al Jazeera's Condemnation**

Al Jazeera media network confirmed in an official statement that Washah's assassination represents an "abhorrent crime" and constitutes a continuation of systematic targeting of journalists. The operation was not a random act but a deliberate crime intended to intimidate media professionals and prevent them from fulfilling their mission of conveying truth.

Al Jazeera's statement added that Washah's assassination is a flagrant violation of international laws and conventions, and a continuation of the policy of targeting journalists, noting that the assassination comes in the context of a dangerous escalation aimed at media workers to prevent the true picture of what is happening in Gaza from being conveyed.

The media network held Israeli occupation forces "fully responsible for the crime that falls within a pattern of systematic targeting of our journalists."

**Wael Al-Dahdouh's Testimony**

Wael Al-Dahdouh, director of Al Jazeera's office in Gaza, described his colleague Mohammed Washah's martyrdom as a recurring tragedy that has continued for over two years. He added that the journalism sector has lost more than 262 journalists and that the journalism community has paid an extremely heavy price.

Al-Dahdouh confirmed to Al Jazeera that Israel is immune from punishment, which allows it to continue systematic killing operations. The international community often suffices with timid statements and symbolic positions without taking practical measures to stop this bloody series of events.

- **常见词组**：impede progress (阻碍进展); impede justice (妨碍司法); impeded (形容词/过去分词); impeding (进行式)
- **拓展内容**：Impedance (名词，物理学术语); impediment (名词，障碍)。动词变化：impedes (第三人称单数), impeded (过去式), impeding (进行式)。
- **例句**：Restrictions on international media access and movement **impede** the ability of independent journalists to report on allegations of mass atrocities and human rights violations.

- **例句中文**：证据表明官方调查可能被用来**隐瞒**而非澄清记者死亡的情况，引发了关于事件肇事者身份的怀疑。

**5. retaliation** /rɪˌtæliˈeɪʃən/

- **例句中文**：政府警告称，任何针对平民的进一步袭击都可能引发与挑衅相称的**报复**，令人担忧冲突范围扩大。

#### S - 熟词僻义 / 引申义

**1. deliberate** /dɪˈlɪbərət/ (形容词); /dɪˈlɪbəreɪt/ (动词)

- **词性**：形容词; 动词 (transitive verb)
- **英文释义**：(形容词) Deliberately considered; done consciously and intentionally; not accidental or impulsive. (动词) To think carefully about something; to consider or discuss thoroughly.
- **中文释义**：(形容词) 蓄意的；故意的；经过深思熟虑的；(动词) 仔细考虑；深思；商讨
- **语域标注**：正式、法律、新闻、学术
- **同义词**：intentional, premeditated, planned (形容词); consider, ponder, reflect (动词)
- **常见词组**：deliberate attack (蓄意攻击); deliberate violation (蓄意违反); deliberate killing (故意杀害); deliberate on sth (对…进行深思熟虑)
- **拓展内容**：作形容词时发音为 /dɪˈlɪbərət/，作动词时发音为 /dɪˈlɪbəreɪt/。Deliberately (副词)，Deliberation (名词，深思熟虑；考虑)。常见搭配：deliberate murder/homicide (蓄意杀人); deliberate indifference (故意漠视)。注意区分：deliberate (蓄意) vs. accidental (意外) vs. negligent (过失)。
- **例句**：The investigator argued that the attack was deliberate and not a case of mistaken identity, presenting evidence that the journalists were specifically identified and targeted with precision.
- **例句中文**：调查员辩称这次袭击是蓄意的，而不是身份错误事件，提供了证据表明这些记者是被特别识别并精准打击的。

**2. persecution** /ˌpɜːrsɪˈkjuːʃən/

- **语域标注**：正式、法律、人权、政治评论
- **同义词**：harassment, oppression, torment, discrimination, victimization; **反义词**：protection, tolerance, acceptance
- **常见词组**：religious persecution (宗教迫害); political persecution (政治迫害); persecution of journalists (对记者的迫害); persecution complex (被害妄想); face/suffer persecution (遭受迫害)
- **拓展内容**：Persecute (动词，迫害); persecuted (形容词/过去分词)；persecutor (迫害者)；persecutory (形容词，迫害性的)。在人权和法律语境中，persecution 指系统性的、针对特定群体的压制和伤害。Related: **prosecute** (起诉，与 **persecute** 易混)。
- **例句**：The persecution of independent media outlets has intensified with journalists facing threats of imprisonment, financial ruin, and physical violence for reporting on government abuses.
- **例句中文**：对独立媒体的迫害升级，记者因报道政府滥权而面临监禁、经济破产和人身暴力的威胁。

#### L - 地道表达

**1. on the ground** /ɒn ðə ɡraʊnd/

- **表达含义**：Actually happening or present in reality; referring to real conditions or actual events as opposed to theoretical or official descriptions.
- **中文表达**：实地；现场；第一线；真实情况
- **语域标注**：新闻、报道、正式、政治评论
- **搭配用法**：what's happening on the ground (实际发生的情况); situation on the ground (现场情况); verify on the ground (实地核查); on-the-ground reality (地面现实)
- **拓展内容**：该短语常用于新闻报道，指记者或观察者在事件发生地直接获得的信息，相对官方声明或理论推测更具可信度。Synonyms: in reality, actually, practically。
- **例句**：International journalists have been largely prevented from documenting conditions on the ground in Gaza, severely limiting independent verification of humanitarian claims and military operations.
- **例句中文**：国际记者在很大程度上被阻止记录加沙现场的情况，严重限制了对人道主义声明和军事行动的独立核查。
