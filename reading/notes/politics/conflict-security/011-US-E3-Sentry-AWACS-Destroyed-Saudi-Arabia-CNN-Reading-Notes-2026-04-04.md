# 模块一：翻译与全文概要

## 英文翻译

**The Destruction of a US Aircraft Carrying a Key Radar Could Hamper Detection of Iranian Threats, Analysts Say**  
*By Brad Lendon, Isaac Yee and Thomas Bordeaux, CNN — March 30, 2026*

The destruction of a US Air Force E-3 Sentry aircraft in an Iranian attack on an air base in Saudi Arabia could impair the United States' ability to detect incoming Iranian threats at range, according to analysts.

Striking images of the downed aircraft, geolocated by CNN, show its tail section torn away and its distinctive rotating radar dome — a critical component of the Airborne Warning and Control System, or AWACS — lying on the ground at Prince Sultan Air Base.

The loss of the AWACS is "a serious blow to (US) surveillance capabilities," said CNN military analyst Cedric Leighton, a retired US Air Force colonel who has flown aboard the type. "It can potentially affect (US) ability to control combat aircraft and guide them toward their targets or protect them from engagements with hostile aircraft and missile systems," he said.

CNN contacted US Central Command for comment on the destruction of the aircraft.

The AWACS enables airborne surveillance of up to 310,000 square kilometers of battlespace, from ground level to the stratosphere, and has been a vital component of US combat forces for decades. The fleet of 17 E-3s, and America's experience operating them over the years, is viewed by analysts as a major advantage Washington holds.

Images of the destroyed aircraft began appearing on social media over the weekend. CNN geolocated the images at the central Saudi air base by comparing them with a satellite image from March 11 showing the E-3 in the same spot that appears in the recent photos.

CNN had previously reported that an attack on the air base left at least 10 US military personnel wounded, with no deaths reported. A US Air Force tanker aircraft was also damaged, according to sources.

The E-3 itself is a powerful airborne command post, as well as a surveillance platform. It can track around 600 targets simultaneously — other aircraft, missiles, large drones, and even tanks on the battlefield. Personnel aboard the E-3 can transmit that information in real time to theater commanders, ships at sea, or back to the Pentagon.

Meanwhile, controllers aboard the AWACS can vector interceptor fighters toward incoming threats or dispatch attack aircraft to support ground troops under fire.

A report this month from the Center for a New American Security rates the AWACS as the "quarterback" of the battlefield, "providing with agility critical situational awareness and real-time coordination that turns individual sorties into a dominant force." The report describes the AWACS as "an indispensable asset for US military operations today and for the foreseeable future."

Peter Layton, a former Royal Australian Air Force officer and researcher at the Griffith Asia Institute, said airborne radars exponentially increase threat detection time. In the current conflict, an E-3 could detect an incoming Iranian Shahed drone launched from 320 km away some 85 minutes before a ground-based radar, he said. Because they are mobile, AWACS can rapidly move to new crisis zones and are a harder target for adversaries than fixed ground-based radars.

**An Exposed Aircraft**

Analysts questioned Monday how the US allowed the E-3 to become vulnerable to an Iranian attack. "Extraordinary measures are often taken to protect it from hostile enemy fire while in flight. It sometimes receives fighter escorts and is never allowed to fly over hostile territory to keep it safe," said Leighton. He described the loss of the E-3 on the ground as "a serious failure in our force protection efforts."

Leighton also said the attack may indicate Iran is receiving assistance targeting key US assets. "Russia most likely gave Iran geographic coordinates and satellite imagery that provided the precise location," he said.

Kelly Grieco, a researcher at the Stimson Center, wrote on X that the attack shows Iran is being selective in going after a limited number of high-value targets with the forces at its disposal. She noted attacks on radar and satellite communications infrastructure at other US bases in the region since the war began. "Iran is going after the radars that detect threats, the tankers that keep fighters airborne, and the AWACS that direct the battle. That is a counter-air defense campaign. Tailored to what Iran can actually do. And the damage is real," she wrote.

**An Aging Asset**

Analysts also noted the size and age of the US E-3 fleet and the strain that Middle East operations are placing on it. The E-3 is a scarce resource: only 17 were in the fleet at the start of the year, according to FlightGlobal.com's 2026 World Air Forces directory — fewer than the number of B-2 bombers (20).

And they are old. The first aircraft entered Air Force service in 1978, and the US fleet has shrunk from 32 aircraft in 2015. The four-engine jets, based on the commercial Boeing 707 fuselage, carry a flight crew of four, plus 13 to 19 mission specialists, a number that can vary by specific assignment, according to the Air Force.

The aircraft cost approximately $270 million in fiscal year 1998 dollars, about $540 million today. Besides the United States, Saudi Arabia, France, and Chile operate the E-3, and NATO operates its own joint force of 14.

The US Air Force has been evaluating replacements for the aging fleet, but the Pentagon has yet to settle on a platform, though some prototypes are in development.

The US Navy operates a similar but much smaller airborne early warning and control aircraft — the E-2 Hawkeye — which can launch from aircraft carriers and is used to surveil the battlespace of carrier strike groups. But the Hawkeye is not a straightforward replacement for the Sentry. Being smaller, it carries fewer crew to monitor the battlespace; being a turboprop rather than a jet, it cannot fly as high as the Sentry, meaning its radar cannot cover as much territory.

### 飞行器/装备

**E-3 Sentry（E-3「哨兵」预警机）**  
由波音公司在 707/320 商用机身基础上改装的机载预警与控制系统（AWACS）载机。首架原型机于 1972 年首飞，1977 年进入美国空军服役（美空军正式换装始于 1978 年）。机背标志性的直径 9.1 米旋转雷达天线罩为其核心组件，可同时追踪约 600 个目标，覆盖从地面到平流层的 310,000 平方公里战场空间。四台普惠 TF33-PW-100A 涡扇发动机提供动力，飞行机组 4 人，任务专家 13 至 19 人。除美国外，沙特阿拉伯、法国、智利及北约亦有列装。

**E-2 Hawkeye（E-2「鹰眼」预警机）**  
美国海军舰载预警机，可从航母弹射起飞，与 E-3 相比机身更小、采用涡桨发动机、飞行高度较低，雷达覆盖范围和机载人员均少于 E-3，不能直接替代后者。

**Shahed 无人机（沙赫德无人机）**  
伊朗自主研发的无人作战飞行器及巡飞弹系列，由伊朗沙赫德航空工业公司研发生产。其中 Shahed-136 型射程约 2,500 公里，最大飞行速度 185 公里/小时，弹头重量约 50 公斤，因低廉造价与大规模集群使用而受到广泛关注。

**Prince Sultan Air Base（苏丹亲王空军基地）**  
位于沙特阿拉伯中部 Al Kharj 地区，距首都利雅得约 100 公里东南方向，是美国空军在中东地区的重要前进基地，隶属于美国空军中央司令部管辖。

# 模块四：素材与语料库积累

## 4A. 重点词汇解析

### W — 写作高频词

**1. surveillance**

- **音标**：/sɜːrˈveɪləns/（美式）
- **词性**：n.
- **英文释义**：Close observation, especially of a suspected spy or criminal, or the careful watching of a place, person, or situation, especially by the police or military.
- **中文释义**：监视；监控；侦察
- **语域**：正式 / 新闻 / 军事 / 法律
- **同义词**：monitoring, observation, reconnaissance, oversight
- **反义词**：neglect, inattention
- **常见词组**：under surveillance（处于监视中）；surveillance system（监控系统）；aerial surveillance（空中侦察）；mass surveillance（大规模监控）；surveillance drone（侦察无人机）
- **拓展**：不可数名词。源自法语 *surveiller*（监视）。在军事语境中与 reconnaissance（侦察）有所区别：surveillance 强调持续性监视，reconnaissance 更强调主动搜集情报的行动。常与 camera, network, satellite, radar 等词搭配。
- **例句**：The military satellite provided **continuous aerial surveillance** of the border region, enabling commanders to track troop movements in real time.
  - 军用卫星对边境地区实施持续空中监视，使指挥官能够实时追踪部队动向。

---

**2. capability**

- **音标**：/ˌkeɪpəˈbɪlɪti/（美式）
- **词性**：n.
- **英文释义**：The ability to do something, especially something that could lead to a particular advantage; the quality or state of being capable.
- **中文释义**：能力；实力；性能；作战能力
- **语域**：正式 / 新闻 / 军事 / 外交
- **同义词**：capacity, ability, competence, power, potential
- **反义词**：incapability, inability, limitation
- **常见词组**：military capability（军事实力）；nuclear capability（核能力）；surveillance capability（侦察能力）；within one's capabilities（在某人能力范围内）；stretch one's capabilities（发挥潜力至极限）
- **拓展**：可数名词，复数形式 capabilities 在军事和政策语境中极为常见，专指国家或军队的综合能力组合。注意与 *capacity*（容量、能力）的区别：capability 更侧重于「具备某种特定能力」，尤其指潜在的或尚未完全发挥的能力。
- **例句**：The destruction of the radar plane severely degraded Washington's **early-warning capabilities** in the region, leaving a critical gap in its air defense network.
  - 雷达飞机被摧毁严重削弱了华盛顿在该地区的预警能力，在其防空网络中留下了关键缺口。

---

**3. indispensable**

- **音标**：/ˌɪndɪˈspensəbl/（美式）
- **词性**：adj.
- **英文释义**：Too important to be without; absolutely necessary.
- **中文释义**：不可或缺的；必不可少的
- **语域**：正式 / 书面 / 新闻 / 学术
- **同义词**：essential, vital, necessary, irreplaceable, crucial
- **反义词**：dispensable, optional, expendable, replaceable
- **常见词组**：indispensable to / for（对……不可或缺）；an indispensable asset（不可或缺的资产）；an indispensable role（不可替代的角色）
- **拓展**：形容词，无比较级。名词形式：indispensability（不可或缺性）。副词：indispensably。在新闻和政策报告中常用于强调某一资产、人员或机制的战略地位，带有较强的正式书面色彩。
- **例句**：Air-to-air refueling tankers have become **indispensable** to modern power projection, allowing strike aircraft to operate far beyond their unrefueled range.
  - 空中加油机已成为现代力量投送不可或缺的组成部分，使攻击机的作战半径得以远超其未加油时的航程。

---

**4. degrade**

- **音标**：/dɪˈɡreɪd/（美式）
- **词性**：v.
- **英文释义**：（军事语境）To reduce the effectiveness or capability of something, especially a weapons system or military force; to damage to the point of reduced functional value.
- **中文释义**：（军事）降低……的效能；削弱；使（能力）退化
- **语域**：军事 / 技术 / 正式
- **同义词**：diminish, impair, weaken, undermine, erode
- **反义词**：enhance, improve, upgrade, strengthen
- **常见词组**：degrade capabilities（削弱能力）；degrade the enemy's air defenses（削弱敌方防空力量）；signal degradation（信号衰减）
- **拓展**：不规则变化：degrade / degraded / degraded。名词：degradation（降级；退化；削弱）。注意该词在军事语境中为半技术词汇，与日常用法（贬低某人）有语义区别。在环境科学中亦常用（environmental degradation，环境退化）。
- **例句**：Prolonged drone attrition gradually **degraded** the adversary's integrated air defense network, making it increasingly vulnerable to manned aircraft strikes.
  - 持续的无人机消耗战逐渐削弱了对手的综合防空网络，使其对有人驾驶飞机的打击愈加脆弱。

---

**5. asset**

- **音标**：/ˈæset/（美式）
- **词性**：n.
- **英文释义**：A useful or valuable thing, person, or quality; in military/financial contexts, any resource of strategic or monetary value.
- **中文释义**：资产；有价值的人/物；（军事）战略资源

- **同义词**：target, pursue, go for, hunt down, attack, seek
- **常见词组**：go after high-value targets（锁定高价值目标）；go after a contract（争夺合同）；go after the truth（追求真相）
- **拓展**：这是一个高频口语化短语动词，在新闻写作中亦常见，比正式的「target」或「attack」更口语化、更生动。文中 Kelly Grieco 的 X 帖子连用三次「Iran is going after...」，形成排比修辞，强化了「定点猎杀」的意象，是地道的英文新闻/社媒写作风格。
- **例句**：The prosecution decided to **go after** the company's executives directly rather than pursuing a broader corporate settlement that would have shielded individuals from liability.
  - 检察机关决定直接追诉公司高管，而非寻求更宏观的公司和解协议——后者将使个人免于承担责任。

---

**21. a far cry from**

- **音标**：/ə fɑːr kraɪ frɒm/（美式）
- **词性**：idiom（习语）
- **英文释义**：Very different from something; a long way from reaching a particular standard or condition.
- **中文释义**：与……相差甚远；远非……
- **语域**：口语 / 新闻 / 书面
- **同义词**：very different from, nothing like, poles apart from
- **例句**：The E-2 Hawkeye's coverage and crew capacity is **a far cry from** that of the E-3 Sentry, making it a supplement rather than a true replacement for the larger aircraft.
  - E-2「鹰眼」预警机在覆盖范围和机载人员上与 E-3「哨兵」相差甚远，使其只能作为补充，而非大型飞机的真正替代。

---

**22. at the expense of**

- **音标**：/æt ðə ɪkˈspens əv/（美式）
- **词性**：prep. phr.
- **英文释义**：With the loss of, or to the detriment of something or someone else; resulting in harm or disadvantage to.
- **中文释义**：以……为代价；损害……的利益
- **语域**：正式 / 书面 / 新闻
- **同义词**：at the cost of, to the detriment of, sacrificing
- **例句**：Maintaining a high tempo of combat operations has come **at the expense of** routine maintenance cycles, accelerating wear on an already aging aircraft fleet.
  - 维持高强度作战节奏是以牺牲例行维护周期为代价的，加速了本已老化的飞机机队的磨损。

---

**23. in the foreseeable future**

- **音标**：/ɪn ðə fɔːrˈsiːəbl ˈfjuːtʃər/（美式）
- **词性**：adv. phr.
- **英文释义**：Within the period of time that one can reasonably anticipate or predict; the near or medium-term future.
- **中文释义**：在可预见的未来；短中期内
- **语域**：正式 / 商业 / 新闻 / 学术
- **同义词**：in the near future, for the foreseeable future, in coming years
- **拓展**：注意英文中通常说「for the foreseeable future」（持续状态）或「in the foreseeable future」（某事发生的时间范围），两者细微差别在于是否强调持续性。这是新闻和报告写作中表达时间展望的高频惯用语。
- **例句**：Analysts agree that ground-based air defense systems will remain essential **in the foreseeable future**, even as directed-energy weapons move closer to operational deployment.
  - 分析人士一致认为，即便定向能武器正逐步接近实战部署，地基防空系统在可预见的未来仍将不可或缺。

---

**24. at a premium**

- **音标**：/æt ə ˈpriːmiəm/（美式）
- **词性**：prep. phr.（习语）
- **英文释义**：If something is at a premium, it is difficult to obtain or in short supply, and therefore more valuable; above the usual price.
- **中文释义**：供不应求；稀缺而珍贵；高价
- **语域**：商业 / 新闻 / 正式
- **同义词**：in short supply, scarce, hard to come by, highly valued
- **例句**：With only 17 aircraft remaining in the fleet, every E-3 Sentry sortie is **at a premium**, and the loss of even one airframe represents a disproportionate blow to US airborne surveillance capacity.
  - 机队中仅剩 17 架飞机，每次 E-3「哨兵」出击都弥足珍贵，哪怕损失一架机体，对美国机载侦察能力的打击都是不成比例的。

## 4D. 金句积累

**金句一**

> *"Iran is going after the radars that detect threats, the tankers that keep fighters airborne, and the AWACS that direct the battle. That is a counter-air defense campaign. Tailored to what Iran can actually do. And the damage is real."*  
> — Kelly Grieco, Stimson Center

**中文翻译**：  
「伊朗正在锁定那些探测威胁的雷达、那些让战斗机持续滞空的加油机，以及那些指挥战斗的预警机。这就是一场反防空作战。量伊朗之力而为之。而且破坏是实实在在的。」

**积累价值**：三个排比结构（「the radars that.../ the tankers that.../ the AWACS that...」）构成递进式列举，节奏铿锵，是英语时评写作中以简短句群取代复合长句的典范。「Tailored to what Iran can actually do」是独立断句，起强调停顿效果，适用于写作中强调「量力而为的战略克制」这一主题。

---

**金句二**

> *"(The AWACS is) providing with agility critical situational awareness and real-time coordination that turns individual sorties into a dominant force."*  
> — Center for a New American Security report

**中文翻译**：  
「（预警机）以高度灵活的方式提供关键的态势感知与实时协调，将一次次单独的出击架次整合为一支主导性力量。」

**积累价值**：这句话精炼地概括了「系统整合」与「力量倍增」的军事概念，其核心句型「turns [individual units] into a [dominant/collective force]」可迁移至政治、商业、社会等多类议论文写作场景，用于论述协调整合的价值。

---

**金句三**

> *"Extraordinary measures are often taken to protect it from hostile enemy fire while in flight. It sometimes receives fighter escorts and is never allowed to fly over hostile territory to keep it safe."*  
> — Cedric Leighton, CNN Military Analyst

**中文翻译**：  
「在飞行过程中，通常会采取特殊措施保护它免遭敌方炮火攻击。它有时会获得战斗机护航，且从不被允许飞越敌对领土以确保安全。」

**积累价值**：这两句简短的陈述句形成强烈的隐性反讽：正因如此完善的飞行保护机制存在，地面被毁才显得格外讽刺和触目惊心——「force protection failure」的结论由此呼之欲出。是英语新闻写作中以铺垫反衬强化核心论点的典型技法，适合借鉴用于议论文中「欲扬先抑」或「以背景衬托结论」的写作手法。
