> [!TIP] **重点词汇解析**
> 1. **Rule on** [v. phr.]: to make an official decision about something. (就……做出裁决/判定)

>    - **考点**: unpaid leave (无薪假); outstanding (未偿付的，同义词).
> 4. **Claim** [n.]: a formal request for something, such as money, that you believe you have a legal right to. (索赔，申索)
>    - **用法**: file a claim (提出索赔); insurance claim (保险索赔).

# 模块四：素材与语料库积累

## 4A. 重点词汇解析

---

### W — 写作高频词

---

**1. claim** /kleɪm/ *n. / v.*

- **英文释义**：*(n.)* A formal demand for something you believe you have a right to; a legal assertion that you are owed something. *(v.)* To state that something is true, or to demand something as your right.
- **中文释义**：（名词）索赔；主张；权利请求；（动词）声称；主张；索取
- **语域**：法律 / 新闻 / 正式书面
- **同义词**：assertion, demand, entitlement (n.); allege, assert, contend (v.)
- **反义词**：denial, disclaimer (n.)
- **常见词组**：make a claim, file a claim, settle a claim, reject a claim, insurance claim, legal claim, claim damages
- **拓展**：
  - *claim* 作可数名词，常与 "bring/make/file/settle/reject" 搭配。
  - 在法律语境中，"claimant"（原告/索赔人）是与之对应的重要派生词。
  - *claim* 后接 *that* 从句表示"声称"，如 *He claimed that he had worked throughout.*
  - 区分：**claim** vs. **allegation**：前者是正式法律请求，后者强调"尚未证实的指控"。
- **例句**：
  > She **filed a claim** against her former employer for unpaid overtime wages.
  > 她就未付加班工资向前雇主提出了索赔请求。

---

**2. ruling** /ˈruːlɪŋ/ *n. / adj.*

- **英文释义**：*(n.)* An official decision made by a judge or court. *(adj.)* Having the most power or influence in a situation.
- **中文释义**：（名词）裁决；判决；裁定；（形容词）执政的；起支配作用的
- **语域**：法律 / 新闻 / 正式
- **同义词**：decision, judgment, verdict, determination (n.)
- **常见词组**：deliver a ruling, issue a ruling, landmark ruling, court ruling, preliminary ruling
- **拓展**：
  - *ruling* 作名词，可数，常与 "deliver/issue/hand down/overturn" 搭配。
  - 区分：**ruling**（法官就具体事项作出的程序性或实体性裁定）vs. **verdict**（陪审团作出的有罪/无罪裁决）vs. **judgment**（法院就诉讼作出的最终判决）。
- **例句**：
  > The appellate court is expected to **deliver a ruling** on the constitutional question next month.
  > 上诉法院预计将于下月就这一宪法问题作出裁定。

---

**3. dispute** /dɪˈspjuːt/ *n. / v.*

- **英文释义**：*(n.)* A serious disagreement or argument, especially an official one. *(v.)* To question whether something is true or correct; to argue against it.
- **中文释义**：（名词）争议；纠纷；争端；（动词）对……提出异议；争辩
- **语域**：法律 / 新闻 / 正式
- **同义词**：disagreement, controversy, conflict (n.); contest, challenge, refute (v.)
- **反义词**：agreement, consensus (n.); concede, accept (v.)
- **常见词组**：in dispute, settle a dispute, labor dispute, commercial dispute, dispute a claim, beyond dispute
- **拓展**：
  - *dispute* 作名词，可数。动词形式：*disputed / disputing*，规则变化。
  - "at the centre of the dispute"（争议的核心）是新闻写作中的高频固定搭配。
- **例句**：
  > The ownership of the intellectual property rights remains **in dispute** between the two companies.
  > 两家公司之间的知识产权归属问题仍存在争议。

---

**4. testimony** /ˈtestɪmoʊni/ *n.*

- **英文释义**：A formal statement made by a witness, especially in a court of law, affirming that something is true.
- **中文释义**：证词；证言；（法庭上的）口头陈述
- **语域**：法律 / 正式
- **同义词**：evidence, deposition, statement, attestation
- **常见词组**：give testimony, expert testimony, sworn testimony, conflicting testimony, testimony under oath
- **拓展**：
  - 不可数名词，但在法律语境中有时可作可数用法表示"一项证词"。
  - 区分：**testimony**（口头陈词）vs. **affidavit**（书面宣誓证词）vs. **deposition**（庭外录取的宣誓证词）。
  - 动词形式：*testify*（出庭作证）；名词派生：*testifier*（证人）。
- **例句**：
  > The witness's **sworn testimony** contradicted the documentary evidence presented by the prosecution.
  > 证人的宣誓证词与检方提交的书证相互矛盾。

---

**5. sanction** /ˈsæŋkʃən/ *v. / n.*

- **英文释义**：*(v.)* To officially approve or allow something. *(n.)* Official permission or approval; or a penalty imposed for breaking a law or rule.
- **中文释义**：（动词）批准；认可；制裁；（名词）制裁；批准；认可
- **语域**：法律 / 正式 / 外交
- **同义词**：authorize, ratify, endorse (v.); penalty, punishment, approval (n.)
- **拓展**：
  - ⚠️ **一词多义警示**：*sanction* 既可表示"批准/认可"（正面义），也可表示"制裁/惩罚"（负面义），须根据语境判断。文章中 "sanctioned by the court" 意为"经法院批准"。
  - 区分两种语义：*The plan was sanctioned by the board.*（获批准）vs. *Economic sanctions were imposed.*（经济制裁）
- **例句**：
  > The merger was **sanctioned by** the regulatory authority after a thorough antitrust review.
  > 该合并案在经过彻底反垄断审查后，获得了监管机构的批准。

---

**6. liabilities** /ˌlaɪəˈbɪlɪtiz/ *n. (pl.)*

- **英文释义**：The amount of money that a person or organization owes; legal or financial obligations.
- **中文释义**：负债；债务；法律责任；应付款项
- **语域**：法律 / 财务 / 正式
- **同义词**：debts, obligations, financial obligations
- **反义词**：assets
- **常见词组**：outstanding liabilities, contingent liabilities, total liabilities, limited liability, assume liability
- **拓展**：
  - 单数形式 *liability* 也可指"法律责任"（可数或不可数）。
  - **contingent liabilities**（或有负债）指仅在特定事件发生时才产生的潜在债务，为会计与法律中的专业术语。
  - 重要词组：**limited liability company (LLC)**（有限责任公司）。
- **例句**：
  > The auditors discovered that the company's **contingent liabilities** far exceeded what had been disclosed in the financial statements.
  > 审计人员发现，该公司的或有负债远超财务报表中披露的数额。

---

**7. rehabilitation** /ˌriːhəˌbɪlɪˈteɪʃən/ *n.*

- **英文释义**：The process of restoring something or someone to a former condition; in legal/financial contexts, the process of restructuring an organization to restore its financial viability.
- **中文释义**：（财务/法律）重整；复原；（人的）康复；恢复名誉
- **语域**：法律 / 医学 / 正式
- **常见词组**：financial rehabilitation, corporate rehabilitation, rehabilitation plan, rehabilitation process
- **拓展**：
  - 动词：*rehabilitate*；形容词：*rehabilitated*。
  - 在破产法中，*rehabilitation* 指企业通过债务重组而非清算来恢复偿债能力，与 *liquidation*（清算）相对。
- **例句**：
  > The court-appointed administrator developed a **rehabilitation plan** to restore the insolvent firm's financial stability without resorting to liquidation.
  > 法院指定的管理人制定了一份重整计划，旨在不采取清算手段的情况下恢复该资不抵债企业的财务稳定性。

---

**8. creditor** /ˈkredɪtər/ *n.*

- **英文释义**：A person or company to whom money is owed.
- **中文释义**：债权人；贷方
- **语域**：法律 / 财务 / 正式
- **同义词**：lender, claimant (in insolvency contexts)
- **反义词**：debtor（债务人）
- **常见词组**：secured creditor, unsecured creditor, creditors' meeting, majority of creditors, creditor's rights
- **拓展**：
  - 区分：**secured creditor**（有担保债权人，享有抵押物优先受偿权）vs. **unsecured creditor**（无担保债权人，在清算时排名靠后）。
  - 文中提到债务偿还方案由债权人"一致批准"（unanimously approved by creditors），具有重要法律意义。
- **例句**：
  > Under the restructuring proposal, **unsecured creditors** would receive only thirty cents on the dollar.
  > 根据重整方案，无担保债权人每一美元债权仅可收回三十美分。

---

**9. defendant** /dɪˈfendənt/ *n.*

- **英文释义**：The person or organization against whom a legal action is brought in a court of law.
- **中文释义**：被告
- **语域**：法律
- **同义词**：respondent (in appellate or administrative proceedings)
- **反义词**：plaintiff / claimant（原告）
- **常见词组**：name as the defendant, the defendant's counsel, represent the defendant
- **拓展**：
  - 在英联邦民事诉讼体系中，原告可称为 *claimant* 或 *plaintiff*，被告称为 *defendant*。
  - 在上诉案件中，被上诉方可称为 *respondent*。
- **例句**：
  > The **defendant** was represented by a team of three attorneys at the commercial court hearing.
  > 被告在商业法庭审理中由三名律师组成的团队代理出庭。

---

**10. affidavit** /ˌæfɪˈdeɪvɪt/ *n.*

- **英文释义**：A written statement that someone makes under oath, which can be used as evidence in court.
- **中文释义**：宣誓书；书面证词；誓章
- **语域**：法律
- **常见词组**：file an affidavit, sworn affidavit, affidavit in support, supporting affidavit
- **拓展**：
  - 复数：*affidavits*。
  - 区分：**affidavit**（庭外书面宣誓证词，用于书面程序）vs. **testimony**（当庭口头证词）vs. **deposition**（庭外录取的口头宣誓证词，通常有录音记录）。
- **例句**：
  > The plaintiff **filed an affidavit** setting out the timeline of events in support of her application for an injunction.
  > 原告提交了一份宣誓书，详述事件时间线，以支持其申请禁令的请求。

---

**11. proposal** /prəˈpoʊzəl/ *n.*

- **英文释义**：A formal plan or suggestion, especially one put to a creditor or court in insolvency proceedings.
- **中文释义**：（破产法中的）债务偿还方案；提案；建议
- **语域**：法律 / 商务 / 正式
- **同义词**：plan, scheme, offer (in legal contexts)
- **常见词组**：debt repayment proposal, make a proposal, accept/reject a proposal, table a proposal, proposal process
- **拓展**：
  - 可数名词。
  - 在破产与资不抵债法语境中，*proposal*（方案）是避免清算的关键机制，须由多数债权人通过并经法院批准方可生效。
- **例句**：
  > The company's **debt repayment proposal** was endorsed by seventy-eight percent of its creditors at the statutory meeting.
  > 该公司的债务偿还方案在法定会议上获得了78%债权人的认可。

---

**12. documentary proof** /ˌdɒkjəˈmentri pruːf/ *n. (phrase)*

- **英文释义**：Evidence in the form of documents, such as contracts, emails, invoices, or records.
- **中文释义**：书证；文件证明；文件佐证
- **语域**：法律
- **同义词**：documentary evidence, written evidence, paper trail
- **常见词组**：provide documentary proof, lack documentary proof, supported by documentation
- **拓展**：
  - 法律诉讼中的核心概念，口头陈述若无文件佐证，证明力通常较弱。
  - 相关表达："paper trail"（书面记录痕迹）在商业和法律语境中指一系列能证明事件经过的文件。
- **例句**：
  > Without **documentary proof** of the agreement, the court could not enforce the alleged verbal contract.
  > 由于缺乏书面协议证明，法院无法强制执行被声称存在的口头合同。

**13. normalisation committee** /ˌnɔːrməlaɪˈzeɪʃən kəˈmɪti/ *n. (phrase)*

- **英文释义**：A temporary body appointed by FIFA to oversee and manage a national football association, typically when the association faces governance crises, financial irregularities, or statutory violations.
- **中文释义**：（FIFA）正常化委员会；规范化委员会
- **语域**：体育管理 / 国际组织 / 正式
- **拓展**：
  - FIFA依据其章程（Statutes）有权对发生严重违规的成员协会暂停资格或任命正常化委员会临时接管，以恢复协会的正常治理秩序，最终目标是组织新的民主选举。
  - *normalisation* 为英联邦拼写；美式英语为 *normalization*。
- **例句**：
  > A **normalisation committee** was installed to restructure the national football federation following widespread governance failures and financial mismanagement.
  > 在国家足球联合会出现普遍治理失范和财务管理不善后，一个正常化委员会被设立以推动整改重组。

**14. trustee** /trʌˈstiː/ *n.*

- **英文释义**：A person or organization that holds and administers property or funds on behalf of another; in insolvency, a court-appointed official who manages the debtor's assets and oversees debt repayment.
- **中文释义**：受托人；信托管理人；（破产程序中的）受托人
- **语域**：法律 / 财务 / 正式
- **同义词**：administrator, custodian (in certain contexts)
- **常见词组**：appoint a trustee, trustee in bankruptcy, sole trustee, trustee's mandate
- **拓展**：
  - 可数名词。派生词：*trusteeship*（受托人职位/信托管理状态）。
  - 区分：**trustee**（信托/破产中持有管理权的人）vs. **executor**（遗嘱执行人）vs. **administrator**（无遗嘱时管理遗产的人）。
- **例句**：
  > The court-appointed **trustee** was given authority to review all creditor claims and distribute the remaining assets accordingly.
  > 法院指定的受托人被授权审查所有债权人的索赔并据此分配剩余资产。

**15. chartered financial analyst (CFA)** /ˈtʃɑːrtərd faɪˈnænʃəl ˈænəlɪst/ *n. (phrase)*

- **英文释义**：A professional designation awarded by the CFA Institute, indicating mastery of investment analysis and portfolio management; candidates must pass three levels of rigorous examinations, accumulate 4,000 hours of relevant professional experience, and hold a bachelor's degree or equivalent.
- **中文释义**：特许金融分析师；CFA资格持有人
- **语域**：金融 / 专业资质 / 正式
- **拓展**：
  - CFA Institute（特许金融分析师协会）总部位于美国弗吉尼亚州夏洛茨维尔，成立于1947年，是CFA资格的唯一授予机构[ref:1,2]。
  - 持有人称 *CFA charterholder*；
  - 三个考试等级分别侧重：Level I（投资工具与职业道德）→ Level II（资产估值）→ Level III（投资组合管理）。
  - 区分：**CFA**（投资/金融分析）vs. **CPA**（Certified Public Accountant，注册会计师，侧重审计与会计）vs. **CFP**（Certified Financial Planner，注册理财规划师）。
- **例句**：
  > The fund manager's **CFA** designation gave institutional investors confidence in her ability to conduct rigorous credit risk analysis.
  > 这位基金经理所持有的CFA资格，增强了机构投资者对其进行严格信用风险分析能力的信心。

**16. cross-examine** /ˌkrɒs ɪɡˈzæmɪn/ *v.*

- **英文释义**：To question a witness in a court of law about statements they have already made, typically by the opposing party's lawyer, in order to test or challenge the truth of their evidence[ref:11].
- **中文释义**：（法庭上）对（证人）进行盘问；质询；反询问
- **语域**：法律
- **同义词**：interrogate, question, probe
- **常见词组**：cross-examine a witness, be cross-examined by counsel, subject to cross-examination
- **拓展**：
  - 名词：*cross-examination*（交叉询问；盘问）；对应词：*examination-in-chief*（主询问，即本方律师对本方证人的询问）/ *direct examination*（美式）。
  - 区分三种庭审询问：**direct examination**（主询问）→ **cross-examination**（交叉询问/盘问）→ **re-examination/redirect**（再询问）。
- **例句**：
  > Defence counsel **cross-examined** the expert witness for nearly three hours, attempting to discredit her forensic findings.
  > 辩护律师对专家证人进行了近三小时的盘问，试图质疑其法证鉴定结论。

**17. insolvency** /ɪnˈsɒlvənsi/ *n.*

- **英文释义**：The state of not having enough money to pay debts or obligations; the legal condition in which a person or organization cannot pay its debts as they fall due[ref:21].
- **中文释义**：资不抵债（状态）；破产；偿债能力不足
- **语域**：法律 / 财务 / 正式
- **同义词**：bankruptcy, financial failure
- **反义词**：solvency（偿债能力）
- **常见词组**：insolvency proceedings, declare insolvency, near insolvency, Bankruptcy and Insolvency Act, corporate insolvency
- **拓展**：
  - 可数/不可数兼用，作"一种状态"时多为不可数[ref:22]。
  - 形容词：*insolvent*；动词短语：*be declared insolvent*。
  - 区分：**insolvency**（技术层面的偿债能力不足）vs. **bankruptcy**（经法律程序正式认定的破产状态，在美国指在法院申请的正式程序，如Chapter 7/11）。
  - 文中"Bankruptcy and Insolvency Act"为特立尼达和多巴哥2007年破产与资不抵债法，规定了债务偿还方案（proposal）等替代清算机制[ref:50,51]。
- **例句**：
  > The company filed for **insolvency** protection after its creditors refused to extend the repayment deadline.
  > 在债权人拒绝延长还款期限后，该公司申请了破产保护。

**18. disallow** /ˌdɪsəˈlaʊ/ *v.*

- **英文释义**：To officially refuse to accept or permit something, especially a claim, expense, or piece of evidence, on the grounds that it does not meet required standards or conditions.
- **中文释义**：（正式）不承认；拒绝接受（索赔/支出/证据等）；否决
- **语域**：法律 / 正式
- **同义词**：reject, refuse, overrule, deny
- **反义词**：allow, accept, approve
- **常见词组**：disallow a claim, disallow expenses, disallow evidence
- **拓展**：
  - 规则动词：*disallowed / disallowing*。
  - 名词：*disallowance*（不认可；否决）。
  - 在破产/诉讼语境中，受托人或法院"disallow a claim"意指正式否决某一债权人或当事方的权利请求，具有重要法律效果。
- **例句**：
  > The tax authority **disallowed** the deduction on the grounds that the expenses were not wholly incurred for business purposes.
  > 税务机关以该支出并非完全用于商业目的为由，否认了该扣除申请。

**19. mandate** /ˈmændeɪt/ *n. / v.*

- **⚠️ 熟词僻义**：（参见词条20详细解析）*reserve* 在法律语境中特指法官"暂不宣判、待日裁定"，与日常"预订/保留"用法形成鲜明反差，是阅读司法报道时的高频陷阱词。
- **补充例句**：
  > The tribunal **reserved its award** to allow the arbitrators time to study the voluminous expert reports submitted by both parties.
  > 仲裁庭保留裁决，以便仲裁员有时间研究双方提交的大量专家报告。

**40. turn on (sth)** /tɜːrn ɒn/ *v. (phrasal)*

- **英文释义（僻义）**：*(formal/legal)* To depend on a particular fact or point; to hinge on.
- **中文释义（僻义）**：（正式/法律）取决于；关键在于；（案件/论点）以……为核心
- **语域**：法律 / 正式 / 书面
- **⚠️ 熟词僻义**：*turn on* 通常理解为"打开（电器）"或"攻击"，但在正式书面和法律语境中表示"取决于某一关键事实"，是高度地道的法律英语表达，也是英文写作中替代"depend on"的高级选择。
- **常见搭配**：The case turns on...; the ruling will turn on...; the outcome turns on...
- **例句**：
  > The entire appeal **turns on** the admissibility of a single piece of electronic evidence that was obtained without a valid warrant.
  > 整个上诉案件的关键取决于一份在没有有效搜查令的情况下获取的电子证据是否具有可采性。

**41. position** /pəˈzɪʃən/ *n.*

- **英文释义（僻义）**：*(legal/formal)* A party's stated stance, argument, or legal contention in a dispute; not merely a physical location or job title.
- **中文释义（僻义）**：（法律/正式）（一方的）立场；主张；论点
- **语域**：法律 / 正式
- **⚠️ 熟词僻义**：*position* 在法律语境中频繁出现为"立场/主张"义（"the trustee's position", "Miller rejected that position"），而非日常的"位置"或"职位"。
- **常见词组**：take the position that, maintain a position, reject a position, the defendant's position
- **例句**：
  > Counsel for the respondent maintained the **position** that the oral agreement was unenforceable in the absence of written consideration.
  > 被申请人的律师坚持认为，在缺乏书面对价的情况下，口头协议不具有可执行性。

**42. bring (a claim/action)** /brɪŋ/ *v.*

- **英文释义（僻义）**：*(legal)* To initiate or commence legal proceedings; "bring a claim/action/lawsuit" means to file a formal legal action against another party.
- **中文释义（僻义）**：（法律）提起（诉讼/索赔）；对……提起诉讼
- **语域**：法律
- **⚠️ 熟词僻义**：*bring* 日常意为"带来"，但搭配 *claim/action/lawsuit/proceedings* 时，专指"提起/发起"法律行动。不规则变化：bring/brought/brought。
- **常见词组**：bring a claim, bring an action, bring proceedings, bring a case before the court
- **例句**：
  > The shareholders voted to **bring a derivative action** against the former directors for breach of fiduciary duty.
  > 股东们投票决定就前任董事违反信义义务提起派生诉讼。

### L — 地道表达

**43. at the centre of the dispute** *(→已见词条31，以下补充地道变体)*

**hinge on** /hɪndʒ ɒn/ *v. (phrasal)*
- **英文释义**：To depend entirely on a particular factor; to turn on a key point.
- **中文释义**：取决于；（成败）完全依赖于
- **语域**：新闻 / 正式 / 书面
- **例句**：
  > Whether the contract is enforceable **hinges on** the question of whether there was a genuine meeting of minds at the time of signing.
  > 合同是否可执行的关键，取决于签约时双方是否达成了真正的合意。

**44. in the absence of** /ɪn ðə ˈæbsəns əv/ *(phrase)*

- **英文释义**：When something is not present or has not been provided; lacking something.
- **中文释义**：在缺乏……的情况下；由于没有……；在没有……佐证/依据的前提下
- **语域**：法律 / 正式 / 书面
- **拓展**：这是法律文书和学术写作中极高频的结构，优雅地表达"条件缺失"，是替代"without"的正式书面表达。
- **常见搭配**：in the absence of evidence, in the absence of documentation, in the absence of a written agreement
- **例句**：
  > **In the absence of** a formal termination letter, the court held that the employment relationship remained in force until the date of the hearing.
  > 由于缺乏正式的解雇函，法院裁定雇佣关系在庭审之日前持续有效。

**45. bear out** /beər aʊt/ *v. (phrasal)*

- **英文释义**：To show or prove that something is true; to confirm or support a claim or assertion.
- **中文释义**：证实；证明……属实；支持（某一说法）
- **语域**：书面 / 正式 / 新闻
- **同义词**：corroborate, substantiate, confirm, support
- **拓展**：*bear out* 强调"以证据/事实来证实某主张"，是正式书面英语中替换 "prove" 的地道说法。不规则：bear/bore/borne。
- **例句**：
  > The financial records submitted at trial fully **bore out** the claimant's assertion that invoices had been submitted but never paid.
  > 庭审中提交的财务记录充分证实了原告所主张的：发票已按时提交，但款项从未被支付。

**46. lay out (a case/plan)** /leɪ aʊt/ *v. (phrasal)*

- **英文释义**：To present or explain something clearly and in detail, especially the facts or arguments in a case.
- **中文释义**：详细陈述；逐一阐明；（清楚地）提出（案情/计划/论点）
- **语域**：法律 / 书面 / 正式
- **同义词**：set out, outline, present, articulate
- **拓展**：在法律和学术写作中，*lay out* 和 *set out* 均表示有条理地呈现内容，可互换。
- **例句**：
  > The skeleton argument **laid out** the claimant's case in twelve numbered paragraphs, each addressing a separate legal ground.
  > 诉讼骨架陈述书以十二个编号段落逐条陈述了原告的立案理由，每段对应一项独立的法律依据。

**47. fall within** /fɔːl ˈwɪðɪn/ *v. (phrasal)*

- **英文释义**：To be included in a particular category, scope, or range of responsibility.
- **中文释义**：属于（某职责/范围/类别）之内；包含在……范围内
- **语域**：法律 / 正式 / 学术
- **同义词**：come within, be within the scope of, fall under
- **拓展**：这是法律和行政文件中划定职责范围、权限边界时的核心表达，常见于合同条款解释和职责分工争议。
- **例句**：
  > The tribunal concluded that managing investor relations clearly **fell within** the scope of the applicant's duties under the employment contract.
  > 仲裁庭裁定，管理投资者关系明确属于申请人在劳动合同项下职责范围之内。

**48. hold off (until verification)** *(→已见词条35)* **/ give the benefit of the doubt**

**give the benefit of the doubt** /ɡɪv ðə ˈbenɪfɪt əv ðə daʊt/ *(idiom)*
- **英文释义**：To accept that someone is telling the truth or has good intentions, even though there is some uncertainty.
- **中文释义**：作有利于当事人的推定；姑且相信；不作苛责
- **语域**：口语 / 非正式（引申用于法律讨论）
- **拓展**：此习语源于法律中的"疑点利益归于当事人"原则，现已广泛用于日常语境。
- **例句**：
  > The arbitrator decided to **give the claimant the benefit of the doubt** and accepted his explanation for the missing documentation.
  > 仲裁员决定对申请人作有利推定，接受了他对文件缺失的解释。

**49. on the basis that** /ɒn ðə ˈbeɪsɪs ðæt/ *(phrase)*

- **英文释义**：For the reason that; because of the fact that; used to explain the grounds or justification for an action or decision.
- **中文释义**：以……为由；基于……的理由；在……的基础上
- **语域**：法律 / 正式 / 书面
- **翻译提示**：直译"在……的基础上"虽不错，但中文法律文书更地道的译法是"以……为由"或"理由是……"。
- **常见搭配**：on the basis that he failed to perform; disallowed on the basis that; accepted on the basis that
- **例句**：
  > The application was refused **on the basis that** the applicant did not meet the minimum residency requirements stipulated in the relevant regulations.
  > 该申请以申请人未满足相关法规规定的最低居住要求为由遭到拒绝。

---

**50. amounts to (ongoing work)** /əˈmaʊnts tuː/ *v. (phrasal)*

- **英文释义**：To be equivalent to; to constitute; to add up to a particular quality, status, or legal standard — especially when determining whether actions qualify as something.
- **中文释义**：构成；相当于；等同于
- **语域**：法律 / 正式 / 书面
- **⚠️ 地道提示**：*amount to* 不仅仅指数量累加，更重要的法律用法是"（行为性质上）构成/等同于某事"，如 "whether such communications amounted to ongoing work"（此类通讯是否构成持续履职）。
- **常见搭配**：amount to a breach, amount to fraud, amount to acceptance, amount to evidence of
- **例句**：
  > The court had to determine whether the director's continued presence in the office after the notice period **amounted to** acceptance of the new employment terms.
  > 法院需要判断，该董事在通知期结束后继续在岗的行为是否构成对新雇用条款的接受。

## 4D. 金句积累

**金句一**

> *"My position was to hold off until I could verify."*
> — Robert Hadad, TTFA Normalisation Committee Chairman

**中文翻译**：
"我的态度是暂缓处理，直到我能够加以核实为止。"

**积累价值**：这句话简洁有力，是庭审直接证词的典型风格，体现了商业和法律决策中"审慎核实"原则（due diligence）的语言表达。*hold off until* 结构和 *verify* 用词精准地道，适合运用在论述"谨慎决策""核实求证"等场景的写作中。

**金句二**

> *"The debt proposal was designed to provide a stay of legal proceedings while allowing the association to continue its operations and work toward financial rehabilitation."*

**中文翻译**：
"该债务偿还方案旨在通过暂缓诉讼程序，使该协会能够维持正常运营，同时推进财务重整工作。"

**积累价值**：此句完美呈现了破产重整机制的核心逻辑，结构清晰，兼顾法律精度与可读性。*stay of legal proceedings*、*financial rehabilitation*、*work toward* 等组合均为法律与新闻写作的高频表达，适合描述企业危机管理和合规背景。

**金句三**

> *"Justice Seepersad indicated that his ruling on Miller's claim would turn largely on whether the evidence supports his assertion that he continued to perform his contractual duties after March 2020."*

**中文翻译**：
"西珀萨德法官表示，其就米勒索赔作出的裁决，将在很大程度上取决于相关证据能否支持其持续履行合同职责至2020年3月之后这一主张。"

**积累价值**：此句精准浓缩了整个案件的核心争点，语法结构严谨，*turn largely on*、*assertion that*、*contractual duties* 等法律新闻语汇密度高，适合作为阐述法律争议焦点的写作范式。"turn on"在法律/正式语境中取代"depend on"是高分写作的地道选择。
