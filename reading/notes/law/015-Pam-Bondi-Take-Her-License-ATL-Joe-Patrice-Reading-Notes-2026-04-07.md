# Pam Bondi May Be Out Of A Job… But That's Even More Reason To Take Her License

**来源**：Above the Law (ATL)  
**作者**：Joe Patrice（纽约大学法学院毕业，曾任 Big Law 律师；2013 年起任 ATL 高级编辑，播客 *Thinking Like A Lawyer* 共同主持人；关注美国司法与法律职业文化，文风犀利、常带讽刺。）

---

### 文章结构前情提要

1. [引言段]：指出潘·邦迪（Pam Bondi）在特朗普政府中因“忠诚度”问题遭遇挫折，类比首席大法官罗伯茨。
2. [过渡段]：讽刺性地提到邦迪在 ATL 举办的“司法崩坏”锦标赛（bracket challenge）中表现强劲。
3. [业绩评估]：对比邦迪自称的政绩（低犯罪率）与实际操作（将资源从重罪转移到陈年移民案件）。
4. [机构影响]：论述邦迪的行为如何损害了司法部的公信力并导致严重的行政人员流失问题。
5. [终极对决]：介绍另一位特朗普法律团队成员 Todd Blanche，并将两人列为“年度最差表现者”决赛对手。
6. [结尾]：呼吁读者投票，并提供作者的背景及联系方式。

### 逐句精读解析

**🔹/Pam Bondi/ found out/ the hard way/ that/ Trump's obsession with loyalty/ is a one-way street./**  
**🔸潘·邦迪通过惨痛的教训发现，特朗普对忠诚的痴迷是一条单行道。**

背景注释：Pam Bondi 是前佛罗里达州总检察长，后在特朗普政府中担任代理司法部长。

> **find out the hard way** 🔹通过亲身惨痛教训学到（常指不听劝告后的自食其果）。
> **obsession** 🔹[U/C] **痴迷，执念** (noun)。*English definition: a state in which someone thinks about something too much.* 语域：通用。可数名词，常与 with 连用。拓展：形容词为 obsessive (痴迷的)，动词为 obsess。考点：常用于描述心理上的病态关注。近义词：fixation, preoccupation。
> **one-way street** 🔹**单行道；单方面的关系** (noun phrase)。*English definition: a situation in which only one person or group of people benefits.* 语域：非正式/比喻。拓展：在这里指特朗普要求下属绝对忠诚，但他本人并不回馈这种忠诚。反义词：two-way street (互惠互利的关系)。

**🔹/Like Chief Justice John Roberts/ before her,/ she learned/ that/ no amount of/ selling out your principles/ or/ mortgaging your dignity/ will ever be enough/ for Trump./**  
**🔸就像在她之前的首席大法官约翰·罗伯茨一样，她领悟到，无论如何背弃原则或抵押尊严，对特朗普来说永远都不够。**

背景注释：John Roberts 是美国最高法院第17任首席大法官，常被特朗普指责不够忠诚。

> **no amount of** 🔹**再多也（不）……** (phrase)。*English definition: used to say that something will have no effect.* 语域：书面/口语。用法要点：后面接不可数名词，表示即使有再多的量也无法改变结果。例如：No amount of money can buy happiness.
> **sell out** 🔹**背叛，出卖（原则/灵魂）** (verb phrase)。*English definition: to behave in a way that is not loyal to your beliefs.* 语域：非正式/贬义。动词不规则变化：sell-sold-sold。拓展：名词形式为 sellout (背叛者)。近义词：betray, compromise。
> **mortgage** 🔹**抵押（本义指房屋抵押，此处比喻透支、牺牲）** (verb)。*English definition: (figuratively) to put something at risk to gain a temporary advantage.* 语域：引申义。名词可数，动词常用于比喻牺牲长远利益（如尊严、未来）。词组搭配：mortgage one's future (牺牲未来)。

**🔹/But/ we have/ some good news/ for the departing Attorney General!/**  
**🔸但对于这位即将离任的司法部长，我们倒是有一些好消息！**

> **departing** 🔹**即将离任的，离开的** (adjective)。*English definition: leaving a place or a job.* 语域：正式。派生词：depart (动词，离开)，departure (名词，离职/出发)。考点：在职场英语中常用于描述 outgoing (卸任的) 官员。
> **Attorney General** 🔹**司法部长；总检察长** (noun)。*English definition: the top legal officer in some governments.* 语域：法律/政府。复数形式特殊：Attorneys General。拓展：在美国，AG 是司法部（DOJ）的首脑。

**🔹/She's still on track/ for a potential victory/ in our bracket challenge./**  
**🔸她仍有望在我们举办的锦标赛对阵图中获得最终胜利。**

背景注释：bracket challenge 原指美国“疯狂三月”篮球赛的对阵预测图，此处 ATL 借此讽刺性地对亲特朗普的法律人物进行“最差表现”排名。

> **on track** 🔹**有望成功；在正确轨道上** (phrase)。*English definition: making progress and likely to succeed.* 语域：通用。拓展：反义词为 off track。常与 for 连用。
> **potential** 🔹**潜在的；可能的** (adjective)。*English definition: possible when the necessary conditions exist.* 语域：正式/通用。派生词：potentiality (潜能)。近义词：prospective, probable。

**🔹/Which is important news/ as/ she moves on/ to the "much needed and important new job/ in the private sector"/ that/ she absolutely does not have./**  
**🔸这是个重要的消息，因为她即将转向“那份急需且重要的私企新工作”——尽管她压根儿就还没找着。**

> **private sector** 🔹**私营部门；私企** (noun)。*English definition: the part of the economy not under direct government control.* 语域：经济/职场。拓展：与之对应的是 public sector (公共部门)。在法律圈，指从政府公职跳槽到高薪律所（Big Law）。
> **move on** 🔹**（换工作等）继续前进；迈向新阶段** (verb phrase)。*English definition: to leave your job to do something different.* 语域：通用。

**🔹/And/ she absolutely crushed/ Alina Habba/ in our Final Four matchup:/**  
**🔸而且在我们的“四强赛”中，她彻底击败了阿丽娜·哈巴：**

背景注释：Alina Habba 是特朗普的高级法律顾问之一，常在媒体曝光。

> **crush** 🔹**彻底击败，碾压** (verb)。*English definition: to defeat someone completely.* 语域：非正式/新闻。拓展：名词形式指“暗恋”。用法要点：作“击败”讲时，语气强于 defeat，强调力量悬殊。
> **matchup** 🔹**比赛；对决** (noun)。*English definition: a competition or game between two people or teams.* 语域：体育/通用。

**🔹/Bondi issued a statement/ attempting/ to slather lipstick on her shitcanning,/ she touted the fact/ that/ murders in this country/ are at an historic low./**  
**🔸邦迪发表了一份声明，试图掩盖她被开除的狼狈局面；她吹嘘说这个国家的谋杀率正处于历史最低水平。**

> **slather lipstick on (a pig)** 🔹**遮丑；粉饰太平** (idiom)。*English definition: to give something unpleasant a superficial or deceptive makeover.* 语域：极非正式/讽刺。原意是“给猪涂口红”，这里形容将解雇包装成光荣离职。
> **shitcanning** 🔹**开除，解雇** (noun/gerund)。*English definition: (vulgar) the act of firing someone.* 语域：俚语/粗俗。源自 shitcan (动词，扔进垃圾桶/开除)。
> **tout** 🔹**吹嘘；极力赞扬** (verb)。*English definition: to advertise or praise something, especially as a way of encouraging people to like it.* 语域：新闻评论。考点：常用于政治人物夸耀政绩。

**🔹/This is true,/ but/ has also been true/ of pretty much every year/ since the 90s./**  
**🔸这确实是事实，但自90年代以来，几乎每一年都是如此。**

> **pretty much** 🔹**几乎；差不多** (adverb phrase)。*English definition: almost or very nearly.* 语域：口语。比 almost 语气更随意。

**🔹/She did not,/ for example,/ mention/ that/ she's diverted the Department of Justice's efforts/ from prosecuting/ 23,000 drug, terrorism, and other crimes/ to chase down roofers/ who messed up/ their immigration paperwork/ 20 years earlier./**  
**🔸例如，她并未提及，她将司法部的精力从起诉2.3万起毒品、恐怖主义及其他犯罪活动中转移，转而用于追捕那些在20年前弄错移民文件的屋顶修缮工。**

> **divert** 🔹**转移（资金、注意力、精力等）** (verb)。*English definition: to cause something or someone to change direction.* 语域：正式。搭配：divert A from B to C (将A从B转向C)。
> **chase down** 🔹**追捕；搜寻** (verb phrase)。*English definition: to find someone or something by looking or asking many people.* 语域：通用。
> **mess up** 🔹**搞砸；弄错** (verb phrase)。*English definition: to do something badly or make a mistake.* 语域：非正式。

**🔹/Along the way,/ she convinced/ the federal judiciary/ that/ the Department of Justice/ could not be trusted/ and/ created/ a staggering staffing problem./**  
**🔸在此过程中，她让联邦司法系统确信司法部已不再值得信任，并制造了惊人的人才流失问题。**

> **judiciary** 🔹**司法系统；司法部（全体法官）** (noun)。*English definition: the part of a country's government that is responsible for its legal system.* 语域：法律/正式。不可数名词。
> **staggering** 🔹**令人震惊的；难以置信的** (adjective)。*English definition: deeply shocking; astonishing.* 语域：通用/新闻。
> **staffing problem** 🔹**人员配备问题；员工短缺** (noun phrase)。*English definition: issues related to finding or keeping enough employees.* 语域：职场/行政。

**🔹/One hell of an accomplishment./**  
**🔸这可真是个了不起的“成就”。**

> **one hell of a** 🔹**极其……（表示程度极深，褒贬皆可，此处为反讽）** (phrase)。*English definition: used to emphasize that something is very good, very large, or very special.* 语域：非正式。

**🔹/On the other side of the bracket/ is the last standing/ of Trump's Killer Bs./**  
**🔸锦标赛对阵图的另一边，是特朗普“杀手B计划”中硕果仅存的一员。**

背景注释：Killer Bs 是作者起的绰号，指姓氏以 B 开头的邦迪、布兰奇、博夫三名亲特朗普法律人。

> **last standing** 🔹**最后留下的；屹立不倒的** (phrase)。*English definition: the final person or thing remaining after others have failed or departed.* 语域：文学/修辞。

**🔹/Bondi, Blanche, and Bove/ rolled into/ the Justice Department/ and/ destroyed/ a couple centuries worth of/ credibility./**  
**🔸邦迪、布兰奇和博夫涌入司法部，摧毁了两个世纪以来建立的公信力。**

> **roll into** 🔹**涌入；大量进入** (verb phrase)。*English definition: to arrive at a place in a casual or forceful way.* 语域：非正式。
> **worth of** 🔹**价值……的；足足……的** (phrase)。*English definition: an amount of something that lasts for a particular period.* 语域：通用。例如：Two hours' worth of work.

**🔹/Blanche will take over/ for Bondi/ in the short-term,/ but/ even his personal efforts/ to run interference for Jeffrey Epstein/ and/ publicly attack the rule of law/ did not prove sufficient/ to give him/ the inside track/ to the top job./**  
**🔸布兰奇将在短期内接替邦迪，但即使他曾亲自为杰弗里·爱泼斯坦遮掩，并公开抨击法治，这些努力仍不足以让他在这份最高职位的竞争中占据先机。**

背景注释：Todd Blanche 是特朗普的辩护律师。Jeffrey Epstein 是臭名昭著的性犯罪者。

> **run interference for** 🔹**（比喻）为……阻挡干扰；暗中保护** (idiom)。*English definition: to help someone by dealing with problems for them.* 语域：源自美式足球。拓展：在法律政治语境中，指利用权力阻碍对某人的调查。
> **the inside track** 🔹**捷径；领先地位** (noun phrase)。*English definition: an advantage that someone has because they have special knowledge or connections.* 语域：非正式。搭配：have/get the inside track on something.
> **sufficient** 🔹**足够的；充分的** (adjective)。*English definition: enough for a particular purpose.* 语域：正式。反义词：insufficient。

**🔹/Blanche recently confessed/ that/ senior Trump administration officials/ expect/ to be indicted/ after the next election./**  
**🔸布兰奇最近坦承，特朗普政府的高级官员们预计在下次选举后会被起诉。**

> **indict** 🔹**控告；起诉** (verb)。*English definition: (of a grand jury) to formally accuse of or charge with a serious crime.* 语域：法律专用。注意发音：/ɪnˈdaɪt/ (c不发音)。拓展：indictment (名词，起诉书)。考点：法律英语高频词。

**🔹/Most people/ would take that fear/ and,/ you know,/ stop committing acts/ that/ would get them indicted./**  
**🔸大多数人面对这种恐惧，会——你知道的——停止那些会导致自己被起诉的行为。**

> **commit** 🔹**犯（罪）；干（坏事）** (verb)。*English definition: to do something illegal or something that is considered wrong.* 语域：正式。词组搭配：commit a crime, commit suicide。

**🔹/Blanche, however,/ is willing/ to put his foot on the gas./**  
**🔸然而，布兰奇却甘愿“加大油门”（变本加厉）。**

> **put one's foot on the gas** 🔹**踩油门；加速；加大力度** (idiom)。*English definition: to speed up or increase the intensity of an effort.* 语域：非正式/比喻。近义词：accelerate.

**🔹/Which is a commitment/ since/ this administration/ is driving oil/ to $200 a barrel!/**  
**🔸这可真是个了不起的承诺，毕竟这届政府正把油价推向每桶200美元！**

> **commitment** 🔹**承诺；奉献；投入** (noun)。*English definition: a promise or firm decision to do something.* 语域：通用。可数/不可数。
> **barrel** 🔹**（原油单位）桶** (noun)。*English definition: a unit of measure for oil.* 语域：能源/商业。

**🔹/So/ the final matchup/ is Bondi v. Blanche./ VOTE HERE./**  
**🔸所以，最终的决赛是在邦迪和布兰奇之间展开。请在此投票。**

> **v.** 🔹**对抗（versus的缩写）** (preposition)。*English definition: against (used in legal cases and sports).* 语域：法律/体育。法律文书中读作 "and" 或 "against"。
