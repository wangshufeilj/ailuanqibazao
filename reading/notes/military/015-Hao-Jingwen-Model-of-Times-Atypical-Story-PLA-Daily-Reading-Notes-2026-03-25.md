# **精读笔记：一个“时代楷模”的非典型故事**
**来源**：解放军报 (PLA Daily) | **日期**：2019-01-17 | **作者**：范江怀 (Fan Jianghuai, 资深军事记者)

### 🟢 前情提要 | Introduction & Structure

```markdown
# 文章结构剖析 | Article Structure Analysis

## 1. 整体架构 (Overall Structure)
本文通过对“时代楷模”郝井文的深度特写，打破了传统英雄人物“高大全”的刻板印象，展现了一位既有“霸气”又接地气、既重视传统血性又拥抱前沿科技的现代指挥官形象。
The article provides a deep profile of Hao Jingwen, breaking the "larger-than-life" stereotype of heroes to show a modern commander who balances "toughness" with approachability and "traditional spirit" with "cutting-edge technology."

## 2. 段落分布 (Paragraph Distribution)
- **引言 (Intro):** 揭示郝井文的双重特质：天上的“雷霆之势”与地面的“平易近人”。
  Reveals Hao's dual nature: "thunderous momentum" in the air and "approachability" on the ground.
- **第一部分 (Part 1):** 练兵备战的极致务实。从国际比赛到叙利亚战事启发，强调实战化训练，杜绝“花拳绣腿”。
  Extreme pragmatism in training. Highlights realism and survival skills inspired by international events.
- **第二部分 (Part 2):** 领导艺术的“硬核”与“温情”。传承红色基因，既有拼命劲头，又对战友充满信任与关怀。
  Leadership: "Hardcore" spirit mixed with "warmth." Inheritance of military heritage and trust in subordinates.
- **第三部分 (Part 3):** 竞技与实战的底层逻辑。不仅追求第一，更追求通往第一的“门道”和科学规律。
  The logic of excellence. Seeking the "knack" and scientific laws behind winning.
- **第四部分 (Part 4):** 瞄准未来的“智胜”。拥抱AI、大数据等“外脑”，打造智能化空中劲旅。
  Future-oriented "smart victory." Embracing AI, Big Data, and "external brains."

## 3. 内部逻辑 (Internal Logic)
文章采用“由表及里、由事及理”的逻辑。先描述外在表现（飞行动作、生活细节），再引申出治军理念（实战化、规律探索、智能化），最后升华为对未来战争的深邃思考。
The logic moves from surface to essence. It starts with behaviors, leads to management philosophies, and culminates in reflections on future warfare.
```

### 🟦 逐句精读与深度解析 | Sentence-by-Sentence Analysis

#### **标题与导语 | Title & Lead**

🔹 **An "Atypical" Story / of a "Model of the Times" / ——Reflections and Enlightenment / Brought to Us / by Hao Jingwen, / Commander of an Air Force Aviation Brigade / Preparing for War.**
🔸 **一个“时代楷模”的/非典型/故事/——/空军/航空兵/某旅/旅长/郝井文/备战/打仗/给/我们/带来的/思考/和/启示/。**

> 💡 **重点词汇解析**
>
> 1. **Atypical** [eɪˈtɪpɪkl] *adj.* Not representative of a type, group, or class. (非典型的，反常的)
>    - [拓展] 同义词: unconventional, unusual. 反义词: typical. 在写作中用于描述打破常规的人或事。
> 2. **Enlightenment** [ɪnˈlaɪtnmənt] *n.* The action of enlightening or the state of being enlightened. (启示，觉悟)
>    - [拓展] 动词形式: enlighten (启发)。常用于学术或深度报道，表达获得深刻见解。
> 3. **Brigade** [brɪˈɡeɪd] *n.* A subdivision of an army, typically consisting of a small number of battalions. (旅，大部队)
>    - [拓展] 军事术语：Army (军) -> Division (师) -> Brigade (旅) -> Regiment (团) -> Battalion (营) -> Company (连)。

#### **第一部分：平凡中的不平凡 | Section 1: Extraordinary in the Ordinary**

🔹 **Looking up / at the sky, / Commander Hao Jingwen, / a "Model of the Times," / pilots / advanced fighter jets, / bringing us / a thunderous momentum / that commands / deep respect.**
🔸 **仰望/天空/，“时代楷模”/郝井文/旅长/驾驭/先进的/战机/，带给我们的是/雷霆之势/，令人/敬慕/。**

> 💡 **重点词汇解析**
>
> 1. **Pilot** [ˈpaɪlət] *v.* To operate the flying controls of an aircraft. (驾驶，飞行；作为动词使用)
>    - [拓展] 过去式/过去分词: piloted. 名词 pilot 意为“飞行员”。
> 2. **Thunderous** [ˈθʌndərəs] *adj.* Very loud; resembling thunder. (雷鸣般的，声势浩大的)
>    - [拓展] 常用搭配: thunderous applause (雷鸣般的掌声)。文中指代战机的威慑力。
> 3. **Momentum** [məˈmentəm] *n. [U]* The impetus gained by a moving object. (势头，动力，冲力)
>    - [拓展] 物理语义为“动量”。引申义为“发展的势头”。

🔹 **When he / returns to the ground / and we look at / this commander / at eye level, / we see / another kind of landscape.**
🔸 **当他/回到/地面/，我们/用平视的目光/端详/这位/旅长/时，看到的是/另外/一道/风景/。**

> 💡 **重点词汇解析**
>
> 1. **At eye level** *phrase* At the same height as one's eyes. (平视，处于同一高度)
>    - [拓展] 在翻译“平视”或“平等交流”时非常地道。
> 2. **Landscape** [ˈlændskeɪp] *n.* All the visible features of an area of countryside or land. (风景，景色；此处引申为“风貌、特质”)
>    - [拓展] 写作中常用 a political landscape (政治格局) 或 a cultural landscape (文化景观)。

🔹 **The time / spent with Commander Hao / was not long, / but he / did not feel / unapproachable.**
🔸 **与/郝旅长/接触的/时间/并不长/，但他/并没有/高不可攀/之感/。**

> 💡 **重点词汇解析**
>
> 1. **Unapproachable** [ˌʌnəˈprəʊtʃəbl] *adj.* Not welcoming or friendly and therefore difficult to talk to. (高不可攀的，冷漠的)
>    - [拓展] 词根 approach (接近)。形容人物性格时指“难以接近”。

🔹 **Wearing / a flight suit, / Commander Hao / is a combatant / full of "dominance"; / changing into / casual clothes, / he is / as simple as / a next-door neighbor.**
🔸 **穿上/飞行服/，郝旅长/就是一名/“霸气”十足的/战斗员/；换上一身/便服/，郝旅长/朴素得/就像/隔壁的/邻居/。**

> 💡 **重点词汇解析**
>
> 1. **Combatant** [ˈkɒmbətənt] *n.* A person or nation engaged in fighting during a war. (战斗员，参战者)
>    - [拓展] 动词 combat (战斗)。雅思写作中涉及战争或冲突时常用。
> 2. **Dominance** [ˈdɒmɪnəns] *n. [U]* Power and influence over others. (霸气，统治地位，优势)
>    - [拓展] 形容词 dominant (占优势的)。此处用引号表示人物的一种强大气场。
> 3. **Casual clothes** *phrase* Clothing suitable for everyday use. (便服，休闲装)
>    - [拓展] 对应 formal clothes (正装)。

🔹 **When interviewing him, / the reporter / often thought of / a sentence / spoken by Chairman Mao: / "It is not difficult / for a person / to do a good deed; / what is difficult / is to do / good deeds / for a lifetime."**
🔸 **记者/采访/他的时候/，常常/想起/毛主席/说过的一句话/：/做/一件/好事/并不难/，难的是/一辈子/都/做好事/。**

> 💡 **重点词汇解析**
>
> 1. **Deed** [diːd] *n.* An action that is performed intentionally or consciously. (行为，事迹)
>    - [拓展] 常用搭配: noble deeds (高尚的行为)。
> 2. **Lifetime** [ˈlaɪftaɪm] *n.* The duration of a person's life. (一辈子，终生)
>    - [拓展] 常作定语，如 a lifetime achievement (终身成就)。

🔹 **The "good deeds" / Commander Hao does / are, of course, / matters / related to fighting wars.**
🔸 **郝旅长/做的/“好事”/，当然是/与/打仗/有关的/事/。**

> 💡 **重点词汇解析**
>
> 1. **Related to** *phrase* Connected and having a relationship to. (与……相关的)
>    - [拓展] 雅思写作高频短语。同义词: associated with, linked to.

🔹 **If Commander Hao's / flying skills / are beyond the reach / of ordinary people, / many things / he does / are / what anyone / can achieve.**
🔸 **如果说/，郝旅长的/飞行技术/是/常人无法企及/的话/，他做的/很多事情/却是/常人/都能够/做到的/。**

> 💡 **重点词汇解析**
>
> 1. **Beyond the reach of** *phrase* Outside the power of someone to achieve. (无法企及，够不着)
>    - [拓展] 反义词: within the reach of (在……能力范围内)。
> 2. **Achieve** [əˈtʃiːv] *v.* Successfully bring about or reach by effort, skill, or courage. (达到，完成，实现)
>    - [拓展] 名词 achievement (成就)。

🔹 **Is leading troops / into battle / profound? / Of course / it is.**
🔸 **带兵打仗/深不深奥/？当然/深奥/。**

> 💡 **重点词汇解析**
>
> 1. **Profound** [prəˈfaʊnd] *adj.* (Of a state, quality, or emotion) very great or intense; showing great knowledge or insight. (深奥的，深刻的)
>    - [拓展] 常用于形容思想、理论或影响。

🔹 **Military work / is both / an occupation / and a career.**
🔸 **军事工作/既是/一个职业/，更是一项/事业/。**

> 💡 **重点词汇解析**
>
> 1. **Occupation** [ˌɒkjuˈpeɪʃn] *n.* A job or profession. (职业)
>    - [拓展] 指具体的谋生工作。
> 2. **Career** [kəˈrɪə(r)] *n.* An occupation undertaken for a significant period of a person's life and with opportunities for progress. (事业，生涯)
>    - [拓展] 强调长期发展和追求。

🔹 **A pilot's career / is in the sky, / but to become / an "aerial ace," / one must / be down-to-earth / and perform / seemingly simple / small tasks / well on the ground.**
🔸 **飞行员的/事业/在天上/，但/要想/成为/“空中王牌”/，又必须/脚踏实地/，在地面上/把/一件件/看似简单的小事/做好/。**

> 💡 **重点词汇解析**
>
> 1. **Aerial ace** *n.* A military aviator credited with shooting down several enemy aircraft. (空中王牌)
>    - [注] **Ace** 原指纸牌中的“尖子”，军事中指王牌飞行员。
> 2. **Down-to-earth** [ˌdaʊn tu ˈɜːθ] *adj.* Practical, reasonable, and friendly. (脚踏实地的，务实的)
>    - [拓展] 地道的形容词，雅思口语描述人物性格的高分词汇。
> 3. **Seemingly** [ˈsiːmɪŋli] *adv.* So as to give the impression of having a certain quality; apparently. (看似，表面上)

🔹 **Standing / by the runway, / watching / the moment / Hao Jingwen / piloted the fighter jet / into the sky, / it could be described / as a "thunderous strike" / or a "sword drawn from its scabbard."**
🔸 **站在/跑道边/，看着/郝井文/驾驶着/战机/飞向/空中的/那一刻/，真可谓/“雷霆出击”/“利剑出鞘”/。**

> 💡 **重点词汇解析**
>
> 1. **Runway** [ˈrʌnweɪ] *n.* A strip of hard ground on which aircraft take off and land. (跑道)
> 2. **Scabbard** [ˈskæbəd] *n.* A sheath for the blade of a sword or dagger. (剑鞘)
>    - [拓展] **Sword drawn from its scabbard** (利剑出鞘) 是典型的中国军事文学英译表达。

🔹 **In his / 25-year / flying career, / Commander Hao / has done / some "remarkable" / "big things," / yet he / has done / more / ordinary "small things."**
🔸 **在/长达25年的/飞行生涯中/，郝旅长/做了/一些/“可圈可点”的/“大事”/，然而/做得更多的是/一些/平凡的/“小事”/。**

> 💡 **重点词汇解析**
>
> 1. **Remarkable** [rɪˈmɑːkəbl] *adj.* Worthy of attention; striking. (卓越的，可圈可点的)
>    - [拓展] 同义词: extraordinary, noteworthy.

🔹 **Consistently performing / ordinary / small and easy tasks / to the point of excellence / for a long time / may result / in being "outstanding" / and "extraordinary."**
🔸 **把/平凡的/小事、易事/长时间/坚持/做好/，甚至/做到极致/，其结果/可能就会/“出类拔萃”/“不同凡响”/。**

> 💡 **重点词汇解析**
>
> 1. **Excellence** [ˈeksələns] *n. [U]* The quality of being outstanding or extremely good. (卓越，极致)
> 2. **Outstanding** [aʊtˈstændɪŋ] *adj.* Exceptionally good. (出类拔萃的)
> 3. **Extraordinary** [ɪkˈstrɔːdnri] *adj.* Very unusual or remarkable. (不同凡响的)

🔹 **This / is perhaps / the reason / why / Commander Hao / could become / a "Model of the Times," / and it is also / the reflection / and enlightenment / he brings to us.**
🔸 **这/恐怕/才是/郝旅长/能/成为/“时代楷模”的/原因/，也是/他/带给/我们的/思考/和/启示/。**

#### **第二部分：练兵备战的极致务实 | Section 2: Extreme Pragmatism in Combat Readiness**

🔹 **Prepare for war / with the worst-case / scenarios / in mind, / and strive / for the best.**
🔸 **练兵/备战/要/做/最坏的/打算/，往/最好/去/努力/。**

> 💡 **重点词汇解析**
>
> 1. **Worst-case scenario** *phrase* The most unpleasant or serious thing that could happen in a situation. (最坏的情况)
>    - [拓展] 雅思阅读/写作中讨论风险管理时的必备表达。
> 2. **Strive for** [straɪv] *v.* Make great efforts to achieve or obtain something. (奋斗，力争)
>    - [拓展] 不规则变化: strive-strove-striven.

🔹 **After returning / to the barracks / from leading the team / in the "Aviadarts" / international military competition, / Chief of Staff / Zhao Demin / noticed / that Commander Hao Jingwen / became increasingly / "restless."**
🔸 **带队/参加/“航空飞镖”/国际军事比赛/后/回到/营区/，参谋长/赵德民/发现/旅长/郝井文/越发/“坐不住了”/。**

> 💡 **重点词汇解析**
>
> 1. **Barracks** [ˈbærəks] *n.* A building or group of buildings used to house soldiers. (营房，军营)
> 2. **Aviadarts** [ˌeɪviəˈdɑːts] *n.* An international flight competition for military pilots. (航空飞镖)
>    - [注] **Aviadarts** 是俄罗斯发起的国际军事竞赛项目。
> 3. **Restless** [ˈrestləs] *adj.* Unable to rest or relax as a result of anxiety or boredom. (坐立不安的，不安分的)

🔹 **Logically, / one should / be happy / after achieving / excellent results / in the competition.**
🔸 **按理/，在/比赛中/取得/优异成绩/，应该/高兴/才是/。**

🔹 **Why / was he / unhappy?**
🔸 **为啥/不高兴/？**

🔹 **Zhao Demin / said / that Commander Hao / received / a significant "stimulus" / on the field.**
🔸 **赵德民/说/，郝旅长/在/赛场上/受到了/挺大的/“刺激”/。**

> 💡 **重点词汇解析**
>
> 1. **Stimulus** [ˈstɪmjələs] *n.* A thing that rouses activity or energy in someone or something; a spur or incentive. (刺激，刺激物)
>    - [拓展] 复数形式: stimuli. 文中指郝在目睹外军表现后受到的触动。

🔹 **In this / international military competition, / Hao Jingwen / saw / foreign pilots / who had transitioned / directly / from the battlefield / to the competition ground.**
🔸 **在/这次/国际军事比赛中/，郝井文/看到/从/战场/直接/转战/赛场/的/外军/飞行员/。**

🔹 **They / performed / narrow-lane attacks, / high-angle / continuous maneuvers, / and landings / without deploying / drag parachutes, / which were / highly valuable / for actual combat.**
🔸 **（他们）飞出的/窄航线攻击/、大角度/连续机动/和/降落/不放/减速伞/等动作/，实战/含金量/颇高/。**

> 💡 **重点词汇解析**
>
> 1. **Maneuver** [məˈnuːvə(r)] *n./v.* A movement or series of moves requiring skill and care. (机动，演习；策略)
>    - [拓展] 军事中特指战术动作。
> 2. **Drag parachute** *n.* A parachute used to slow down an aircraft after landing. (减速伞)
>    - [注] 降落不放减速伞意味着更短的降落时间和更高的风险，体现了实战性。

🔹 **Hao Jingwen, / deeply "stimulated," / immediately / adjusted / the training plan / after returning.**
🔸 **受到/深深/“刺激”的/郝井文/归建后/，马上/调整了/训练计划/。**

🔹 **In attack training, / he required / pilots / to pull up / at boundary conditions, / greatly compressing / attack time / to continuously / enhance / battlefield survival rates / and increase / damage effects.**
🔸 **在/攻击训练中/，他/要求/飞行员/在/边界条件/拉起/，大大/压缩/攻击时间/，不断/提升/战场生存率/，增强/毁伤效果/。**

> 💡 **重点词汇解析**
>
> 1. **Compress** [kəmˈpres] *v.* Squeeze or press together; force into less space. (压缩)
> 2. **Lethality** (Damage effects) [liːˈθæləti] *n.* The capacity to cause death or serious harm. (杀伤力，毁伤效果)

🔹 **We shout / "actual combat training" / every day, / but what exactly / is "actual combatization"?**
🔸 **我们/天天/喊/实战化训练/，什么/才是/“实战化”/？**

🔹 **The flying unit / where Hao Jingwen / is stationed / has not / fought a war / for decades, / but this / does not prevent them / from / constantly / approaching / and entering / actual combat / during the process / of "going out and bringing in" / exchanges.**
🔸 **郝井文/所在/的/飞行部队/，有/几十年/没有/打过仗了/，但这/并不妨碍/在/“走出去、请进来”/的/交流过程中/，不断/去/接近/实战/、走进/实战/。**

> 💡 **重点词汇解析**
>
> 1. **Exchanges** [ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒɪz] *n.* An act of giving one thing and receiving another (especially information or ideas). (交流)
> 2. **Approach** [əˈprəʊtʃ] *v.* Come near or nearer to (someone or something) in distance or time. (接近)

🔹 **They improve / their ability / and level / of winning / modern wars / through reference / and learning.**
🔸 **（在）借鉴/和/学习中/提高/自己/打赢/现代战争的/能力/和/水平/。**

🔹 **In his / communication / with the reporter, / Hao Jingwen / mentioned / that one / of his / deepest realizations / is: / Pilots / must, of course, / practice / highly difficult maneuvers.**
🔸 **郝井文/在/与/记者的/交流中/，谈到/自己/最深的一个/体会/就是/：/飞行员/当然/要/练/高难动作/。**

🔹 **Flying / a fighter jet / to its limits / is / to better / master / actual combat skills.**
🔸 **把/战机/飞到/极限/是/为了/更好地/掌握/实战本领/。**

🔹 **However, / if highly difficult maneuvers / do not / stem from / actual combat, / no matter / how well / they are flown, / they are / just a "show."**
🔸 **但是/，如果/高难动作/不从/实战/出发/，飞得/再好/也是/一个/“秀”/。**

> 💡 **重点词汇解析**
>
> 1. **Stem from** *phrase* To originate or develop as the result of something. (起源于，来自)
>    - [拓展] 雅思写作分析原因时的地道短语。

🔹 **We must / resolutely / drive / "flower and fist" / techniques / that are not / suited / for actual combat / out of / the training ground.**
🔸 **我们/要/坚决/把/不切合/实战的/“花拳绣腿”/赶出/训练场/。**

# 模块一：翻译与全文概要

## 英文翻译（Translation）

> **The Atypical Story of a "Role Model for the Times"**
> *— Reflections and Inspirations from Brigade Commander Hao Jingwen of an Air Force Aviation Brigade on Combat Readiness*

Looking up at the sky, we see "Role Model for the Times" Brigade Commander Hao Jingwen at the helm of an advanced fighter jet — thunderous, imposing, and awe-inspiring. When he returns to the ground and we observe him at eye level, an entirely different picture emerges.

Though the reporter's time with Commander Hao was brief, he never felt unapproachable. In his flight suit, Hao exudes the dominance of a seasoned warrior; in civilian clothes, he is as unassuming as the neighbor next door. During interviews, one is reminded of Chairman Mao's words: *Doing one good deed is not difficult; what's difficult is doing good deeds for an entire lifetime.* The "good deeds" Hao performs are, naturally, all about preparing for war.

If Hao's flying skills are beyond the reach of ordinary people, many of the things he does are well within their capacity. Is the art of commanding troops and waging war profound? Absolutely. Military work is both a profession and a vocation. A pilot's calling is in the sky, but to become an "ace in the air," one must keep both feet firmly on the ground and do every seemingly simple task well.

Standing at the edge of the runway, watching Hao Jingwen take to the sky, one witnesses nothing short of "a thunderclap strike" and "a blade unsheathed." Over his 25-year flying career, Hao has achieved a number of remarkable feats, but what he has done far more consistently is an accumulation of seemingly ordinary, mundane tasks. Persisting in doing small, easy things well over a long period — even taking them to the extreme — may ultimately produce extraordinary, exceptional results. This is likely why Hao Jingwen became a "Role Model for the Times," and this is the reflection and inspiration he offers us.

**Train for the Worst, Strive for the Best**

After returning from leading a delegation at the "Aviadarts" International Military Games, Chief of Staff Zhao Demin noticed that Commander Hao had become increasingly restless. By all logic, winning an outstanding result in the competition should be a cause for celebration.

Why the disquiet? Zhao explained that Commander Hao had received a significant "shock" on the competition grounds. During the games, Hao observed foreign pilots who had transitioned directly from active combat to the competition field, executing tight-line attacks, high-angle continuous maneuvers, and landings without deploying drag chutes — all with exceptionally high real-combat value.

Deeply stirred, Hao immediately overhauled the training plan upon returning to base. In attack drills, he required pilots to pull up at boundary conditions, drastically compressing attack windows, continuously improving battlefield survivability, and enhancing lethality.

Everyone talks about combat-oriented training — but what does it truly mean? Hao's unit had not fought an actual war for decades. Yet this did not prevent them from constantly approximating and entering real combat through an "outbound exchange, inbound invitation" approach, and from raising their ability to win modern wars through learning and borrowing.

In conversations with reporters, Hao shared his most profound insight: pilots must certainly drill high-difficulty maneuvers, and pushing a fighter to its limits is about mastering real combat skills. But if difficult maneuvers are not grounded in real-combat scenarios, however impressive the flying, it is nothing but a "performance." We must resolutely drive ornamental, impractical "flowery fists and embroidered legs" out of the training field.

Earlier, media reported that a Russian warplane was shot down over Syria by opposition forces, and after ejecting, the pilot was tracked and surrounded by armed militants. After a brief firefight, the Russian pilot detonated the last grenade he carried and died heroically.

After reading this news, Hao was once again galvanized. He proposed strengthening two training subjects for pilots: swimming, and live-fire pistol and grenade practice.

Some pilots were puzzled: why does the Air Force need this? Hao replied gravely: an advanced fighter is a weapon, and the pistol on a pilot's belt is also a weapon. We talk every day about preparing for war, yet our minds are filled with thoughts of *not* fighting. On the battlefield, any situation can arise — it is a minefield of crises. How can you operate on far-ocean combat readiness missions if you can't swim? What good are you if you can't hit a target with a pistol or throw a live grenade when engaged in close-range ground combat with the enemy?

In interviews with numerous journalists, Hao stated without reservation: "I simply want to fight!"

At first listen, this may sound like a bold proclamation, mere words. But once you understand him deeply, you realize his mind is entirely occupied with the business of war.

On his first far-ocean flight mission, the day before departure, Hao had a candid conversation with his wife about the risks, possible consequences, and various contingency arrangements, preparing mentally for every scenario.

Why do this? "We're flying that far," Hao explained. "Who knows what situation might arise. Every flight is part of fighting and preparing to fight. You have to plan for the worst and strive for the best possible outcome."

**Commanding Troops Requires Both a Hard Core and a Warm Heart**

Deputy Chief of Staff Wang Dengdong, with over 2,000 flight hours, recalled his first impression of Hao Jingwen: "The Commander has a kind of 'dominance' about him — like Li Yunlong." (Note: Li Yunlong is a famously bold and unorthodox military commander character from a popular TV drama.)

Others described him as carrying a "killer instinct," a "tiger's ferocity," or being "full of warrior spirit." Whatever adjective one uses, Hao Jingwen is, by all accounts, a "hardcore" commander.

In the Korean War, Hao's heroic unit shot down 42 enemy aircraft and damaged 17, etching a glorious chapter in military history. When reviewing this history with reporters and discussing the secrets of victory on the Korean battlefield, Hao offered an objective analysis: in terms of weaponry, the two sides were evenly matched. In terms of flying experience, American pilots had thousands of flight hours, while PLAAF pilots averaged only a few dozen hours — clearly at a disadvantage. In combat, it was the courage to engage in aerial close combat, the fearless warrior spirit of "I'll ram you down even if it means my own destruction," that produced battlefield results that astonished the world.

Many years ago, even when serving as a squadron commander, Hao placed enormous importance on the inheritance of "red genes" — the fighting spirit and revolutionary traditions. He has always adhered to one old principle: flying skills can be taught and passed on, but forging the warrior spirit of daring to fight and determined to win is a long process of accumulated cultivation.

In the eyes of his officers and men, Commander Hao is a tough man who seizes every mission and competes for every top ranking. The word "concede defeat" does not exist in Hao Jingwen's vocabulary. "Soft men" cannot win battles; without a bit of warrior spirit, how can a pilot subdue the enemy?

This is why Hao constantly drills this concept into everyone: not finishing first is losing.

He says it and means it — Hao Jingwen's standards for pilots never waver. In major missions and competitions, the drive for first place goes without saying, but in seemingly trivial matters, Hao is equally uncompromising. Fan Zhigang, a pilot with over 3,000 flight hours who joined the service in the same cohort as Hao, remembers this clearly. In recreational basketball, for instance, Hao would stop the game and dress down anyone playing without full intensity.

Fan Zhigang once thought this excessive and advised Hao to lighten up on the small things. Hao refused. To him, the goal is to make "daring to compete, daring to be exacting, and daring to strive for first" habitual — a soldier's warrior spirit is cultivated drop by drop, in every small thing.

Spending enough time with Hao, however, his subordinates discover a warm, deeply human side that can be genuinely moving.

When the brigade's basketball team won the championship in a higher-level competition, Hao was overjoyed and insisted on welcoming the team back in person to congratulate and encourage them. The team was expected to return around 9 or 10 PM, but traffic delays pushed their arrival much later. Colleagues urged Hao to go to bed, given the late hour. "The team fought through every round, put in tremendous effort and sweat to win the championship — what's a few hours' less sleep for us?" Hao refused to leave and waited until the early hours of the morning. When the exhausted players stepped off the vehicle and saw Commander Hao and their leaders waiting to receive them personally, they were truly moved.

On the training ground, Hao's support goes beyond warmth — it instills confidence. "Golden Helmet" winner Yang Xin, in his first ground-attack penetration competition, made a blunder — hitting the wrong target and losing what should have been a "Golden Dart" win. Would there be a second chance? In some units, one might wait a long while. Hao gave Yang Xin another shot immediately. As fate would have it, the other two pilots who competed alongside Yang Xin both won "Golden Helmets," while Yang Xin again returned empty-handed.

When a third opportunity arose, Hao gave the slot to Yang Xin again. Someone warned him: if Yang Xin falters again, the team's honor and results will suffer.

Hao overruled the objections and held firm. His reasoning was simple: in everyday combat training, Yang Xin always ranked at the top. "We must trust our comrades and believe in their abilities. We cannot give up on someone because of one or two setbacks — that is not conducive to the growth of a pilot's combat and technical capabilities."

Yang Xin seized the third opportunity and brought home the "Golden Helmet."

In this episode, we see not only Hao Jingwen's hard-nosed side, but also the breadth of vision and depth of feeling of a leader who guides the whole flock. This also explains why one "aerial ace" has been able to "incubate" so many others.

**Drills Must Pursue Not Only First Place, But the Winning Formula**

Everyone says Hao Jingwen is impatient by nature, and his appearance betrays it too — deeply creased face, looking older than his years. In conversation, his sense of crisis and vigilance surfaces imperceptibly.

Hao's unit has produced a string of legendary "aerial aces" — Wang Hai, Sun Shenglu, Jiao Jingwen, and many others. What keeps Hao up at night is the worry: "I can't let this unit fall apart on my watch."

How to maintain the unit's combat effectiveness at a consistently high level, without cyclical ups and downs, has been an enduring subject of Hao's thought. *One person may run fast, but a group runs far.* Hao uses this plain-spoken wisdom to explain the principle.

Discussing this with reporters, Hao moved from the battlefield to the arena of sport. Why has no second or third Liu Xiang emerged since Liu Xiang's glory days? And why have China's diving and ping-pong teams become "Dream Teams"? One important reason is whether you have discovered the laws of talent development, whether you have found the formula for producing world champions. Find the formula, and you can operate a "production line" for world champions.

Over the years, Hao's unit has won the "Golden Helmet" 10 times and the "Golden Dart" 6 times, leading the entire Air Force, and has produced a large cohort of "aerial combat experts." Asked whether he had cracked the formula, Hao replied modestly, "I wouldn't dare say so." Then he offered a different affirmative answer: "We do have confidence in ourselves."

The unit had its own "Waterloo" moment in the past. Years ago, in a long-range ground-attack competition, other units' missiles flew straight into the mouth of the cave target — "a hundred paces, one arrow" precision — while their own missiles didn't even graze the edge of the cave entrance. Returning to base, to ensure no one ever forgot this painful lesson, they mounted a one-square-meter bronze relief on the main gate of the aircrew building. It depicted the scene of the rival unit's missile striking the mountain cave, with the inscription: *This is the target!*

Every day, pilots could see this bronze relief, a constant reminder to learn from humiliation and strive with renewed determination. But looking is not enough — solutions must be found. Hao led a team to form a "Dedicated Training Group for a Specific Weapons System" and launched a concerted effort to crack this hard nut.

Pilot Tang Shuchao, only 29 years old with fewer than 800 flight hours, shot to fame in the Air Force's penetration-and-strike assessment the previous year, becoming the youngest "Golden Dart" winner since the competition's inception.

Now heading the "Dedicated Training Group," Tang Shuchao talks about the secrets of penetration and the techniques of ground attack with even more animated expression than when describing winning the "Golden Dart." The key was found through a painstaking process. Tang walked reporters through one detail in the breakthrough: whether a strike hits accurately depends on flight speed, angle, wind speed, firing timing, and more. Under equal conditions, why did impact points differ? They considered every possible factor, even developed a precise set of calculations, yet accuracy remained elusive. Eventually, Hao had maintenance personnel retrieve the used projectiles for study. In theory, assembly-line production should yield no variation. But upon examination, subtle differences existed between different production batches. For instance, if the weight of individual projectiles varies, their kinetic energy naturally differs. By understanding and correcting for these variations, accuracy steadily improved.

The secret lies hidden within countless details. Now, whenever they enter a competition or assessment, the pilots step forward brimming with confidence.

Someone summarizing *The Thirty-Six Stratagems* noted that the core philosophy is calculation and strategy. Hao Jingwen is also a masterful calculator: winning one "first place" is only one; but discovering the formula for reaching "first place" means you can win N first places. With N first places, aerial combat experts will naturally emerge. With a unit forged in sound political character, military excellence, and a hard-charging combat style, you will have real odds and readiness when true war comes.

This is the winning formula of one "aerial ace" brigade commander.

**Targeting the Future: Use Your Own Brain, and Use External Brains**

Hao Jingwen's old unit has a motto: *To build a world-class elite air force.* After the brigade was established, Hao set the unit motto as: *To forge a smart, valiant, and invincible airborne force.*

Why emphasize "intelligence" (*zhì*)? Hao explained: although the brave prevail when two sides meet face-to-face, winning modern warfare requires more than courage alone — it requires smart victory.

Wanting to win wars naturally means learning to use one's brain. But spending time with Hao, one realizes the "intelligence" he envisions is not just IQ — it is artificial intelligence.

Hao told reporters: "To win future wars, we must seize the high ground of informatization, unmanned systems, and intelligent warfare."

Xing Zheng, with over 1,400 flight hours, is the brigade's "AI guru." For an "AI research project," Hao emptied the unit's budget reserves. Xing found the commander's generosity in this domain startling — and felt the weight of that trust.

Hao's passion for AI is not new. Even when he was a regimental commander, Hao had organized research into information warfare, led technical specialists to explore multiple new combat models, and mapped out the pathways and methods of informatized warfare.

Having once "tasted the first bite of the crab," Hao again organized efforts the previous year in collaboration with a research institute of the National University of Defense Technology, leveraging big data…

"Big data and cloud computing are not for show — they must be put to use. Today's computers don't just have powerful memory, they can do deep learning, which is enormously helpful for battlefield situational analysis and precision calculation. We have such a powerful 'external brain' — if we don't use it, there must be something wrong with our own brains." This is how Hao defines "intelligence" as he envisions it.

With the wings of intelligent technology, Hao's unit not only flies faster and higher, but stronger and better. In the various Air Force competitions and assessments of the past year or two, they have claimed no shortage of "first places." Yet Hao says: "The true first place in my heart is to win on the future battlefield."

"Fly higher to see farther; see farther to fight better." This is a phrase Hao often repeats. Soaring through the blue sky and dancing across the heavens in his fighter, the whole world lies beneath his wings. But after many conversations with Hao, one comes to understand: what soars even higher than his body is the *heart* that spends every day thinking about how to fight and win.

*(Reporter: Fan Jianghuai)*

### 人物

**郝井文（Hao Jingwen）**
中国人民解放军空军航空兵某旅旅长，空军特级飞行员，飞行时间超过2500小时。2017年被中央军委授予"时代楷模"荣誉称号。曾带队参加"航空飞镖"国际军事比赛，是中国空军歼击机首次飞越对马海峡编队的带队长机，长期致力于空军实战化训练改革与智能化作战研究。

**王海（Wang Hai，1926—2020）**
中国人民解放军空军上将，山东平度人。朝鲜战争期间任空军三师九团一大队大队长，带领大队参加空战80余次，击落击伤敌机29架，个人击落击伤美机9架，被授予"空军一级战斗英雄"。后升任中国人民解放军空军司令员（1985—1992年在任），1988年授上将军衔。其所在大队被誉为"王海大队"。

**孙生禄、焦景文**
均为该旅历史上涌现出的知名"空中王牌"飞行员，在文中作为该旅辉煌历史传统的象征性人物被提及，具体经历未作详述。

**刘翔（Liu Xiang）**
中国著名男子110米栏运动员，2004年雅典奥运会金牌得主，2006年世界田径锦标赛冠军，曾创男子110米栏世界纪录。文中以其退役后无人接班作为"缺乏人才培养规律"的对照案例。

### 机构/组织

**国防科技大学（National University of Defense Technology, NUDT）**
前身为1953年创建于哈尔滨的中国人民解放军军事工程学院（"哈军工"），陈赓大将任首任院长。1978年于湖南长沙重建为国防科技大学，现为中华人民共和国顶级军事高等学府，"双一流A类"、原"985工程"重点院校，下设多个理工科及军事技术研究院所，是中国国防科技自主创新的重要基地。文中提及与郝井文部队合作开展大数据攻关研究。

### 赛事/荣誉

**"航空飞镖"国际军事比赛（Aviadarts International Military Games）**
由俄罗斯国防部主办的国际性军事航空竞技赛事，2013年首次举办于俄罗斯利佩茨克飞行训练中心。中国自2014年起应邀参赛，2017年首次在中国境内（吉林长春、四平、双辽）承办该赛事。比赛设体能竞赛和飞行技能竞赛，分歼击机、强击机等多个机种小组，考察飞行员在接近实战条件下的作战技能。

**"金头盔"（Golden Helmet）**
中国人民解放军空军授予精英王牌飞行员的最高荣誉奖项，由空军每年组织的自由空战对抗竞赛考核评出，代表战斗机飞行员空战对抗能力的最高水平，是空军实战化训练"四大品牌"之一。

**"金飞镖"（Golden Dart）**
中国空军突防突击竞赛考核的最高荣誉称号，代表战机突防突击能力的最高水平。该考核以打击预定目标为主要任务，与地面防御部队展开实弹对抗，是空军实战化训练"四大品牌"之一。

### W — 写作高频词（Writing High-Frequency Words）

---

**W1. persevere**
- **音标**：/ˌpɜːrsɪˈvɪr/ (美式)
- **词性**：vi. 不及物动词
- **英文释义**：to continue trying to do or achieve something despite difficulties or lack of success (Longman)
- **中文释义**：坚持不懈，锲而不舍（即使面对困难也不放弃）
- **语域**：正式 / 书面 / 学术
- **同义词**：persist, perseverate, endure, press on; **反义词**：give up, quit, abandon
- **常见搭配**：persevere with sth. / persevere in doing sth. / persevere against all odds
- **拓展**：
  - 名词形式：**perseverance**（不可数名词）"坚持不懈的精神"，常用于写作和演讲
  - 固定搭配：**perseverance pays off**（坚持终有回报）
  - 注意区分：**persist**（中性，坚持/执拗均可）vs. **persevere**（褒义，带有克服困难的含义）
  - 剑桥释义补充：中文对应"锲而不舍，坚持不懈"（Cambridge Dictionary）
- **例句**：
  - **Perseverance** in the face of repeated failure is what ultimately **separates** elite athletes from the rest.
  - 面对一次次失败仍坚持不懈，正是精英运动员与普通人最终拉开差距的所在。

**W2. tenacious**
- **音标**：/təˈneɪʃəs/ (美式)
- **词性**：adj. 形容词
- **英文释义**：determined to do something and unwilling to stop trying even when the situation becomes difficult; also used of beliefs/ideas that continue to have influence for a long time (Longman)
- **中文释义**：坚韧不拔的；执着的；（信念/传统）根深蒂固的
- **语域**：正式 / 书面 / 新闻
- **同义词**：persistent, resolute, unyielding, dogged; **反义词**：irresolute, yielding, faint-hearted
- **常见搭配**：a tenacious fighter / tenacious spirit / tenacious grip / tenacious beliefs
- **拓展**：
  - 副词：**tenaciously**；名词：**tenacity** /təˈnæsəti/（不可数，"坚韧；执着"）
  - 词源：来自拉丁语 *tenax*，源于 *tenere*（to hold），本义"紧握不放"
  - 区分：**tenacious**（强调意志力的坚韧）vs. **stubborn**（含执拗、固执的负面意味）vs. **persistent**（较中性）
- **例句**：
  - The team's **tenacious** defense in the final quarter **secured** them an unlikely victory.
  - 球队在最后一节的顽强防守为他们赢得了一场出人意料的胜利。

**W3. hone**
- **音标**：/hoʊn/ (美式)
- **词性**：vt. 及物动词
- **英文释义**：① to make a knife, sword etc sharp; ② to make a skill, ability, or idea more effective or exact (Cambridge)
- **中文释义**：①磨砺（刀具等）；②精炼、磨练（技能/能力）
- **语域**：正式 / 书面 / 新闻 / 学术
- **同义词**：sharpen, refine, polish, perfect; **反义词**：blunt, dull
- **常见搭配**：**hone one's skills** / **hone one's instincts** / **finely honed**
- **拓展**：
  - 最常见用法为"hone + 技能类名词"，如：hone one's writing, hone one's craft, hone one's competitive edge
  - 注意：**hone** 偶被误用为 **home in on**（锁定目标），两者意义不同，注意区分
  - 形容词搭配：**finely honed** = 精心打磨的、精密的（常用于写作）
- **例句**：
  - Years of competitive drills had **honed** his reflexes to a **razor-sharp** edge.
  - 多年的对抗训练将他的反应能力磨练得如同刀锋般敏锐。

**W4. simulate / combat simulation**
- **音标**：/ˈsɪmjuleɪt/ (美式)
- **词性**：vt. 及物动词
- **英文释义**：to make or produce something that is not real but looks, sounds, or feels as though it is real; to pretend to have a feeling (Cambridge)
- **中文释义**：模拟；仿真；假装
- **语域**：学术 / 正式 / 军事 / 科技
- **同义词**：replicate, emulate, mimic, model; **反义词**：enact (reality)
- **常见搭配**：**simulate combat conditions** / **a simulated attack** / **simulation software**
- **拓展**：
  - 名词：**simulation**（可数/不可数均可）；形容词：**simulated**
  - 短语：**combat simulation**（作战模拟）；**flight simulation**（飞行模拟）
  - 学术写作中常见：*simulate real-world conditions*（模拟真实情境）
- **例句**：
  - The new training system can **simulate** a wide range of **adversarial scenarios**, allowing pilots to rehearse responses without real-world risk.
  - 新训练系统能够模拟多种对抗场景，使飞行员得以在无真实风险的情况下演练应对方法。

**W5. instil / instill**
- **音标**：/ɪnˈstɪl/ (美式)
- **词性**：vt. 及物动词
- **英文释义**：to gradually make someone have a feeling, attitude, or principle (Longman)
- **中文释义**：灌输；使逐渐形成（某种品质/观念/精神）
- **语域**：正式 / 书面 / 教育 / 军事
- **同义词**：inculcate, imbue, ingrain, implant; **反义词**：uproot, eradicate
- **常见搭配**：**instil discipline / values / courage / confidence in sb.**；**instil a sense of duty**
- **拓展**：
  - 英式拼法：**instil**（双写l：instilled）；美式拼法：**instill**
  - 区分：**instil**（逐渐、潜移默化地植入）vs. **impose**（强制施加）vs. **teach**（教授，较中性）
  - 常用结构：**instil sth. in/into sb.**
- **例句**：
  - Effective military leadership does not merely **command** compliance; it **instils** in every soldier the **intrinsic motivation** to excel.
  - 有效的军事领导力不仅仅是命令服从，更是在每一位士兵心中植入追求卓越的内在驱动。

**W6. forge**
- **音标**：/fɔːrdʒ/ (美式)
- **词性**：vt./vi. 动词；n. 名词
- **英文释义**：① vt. to develop a strong relationship, especially by working hard at it; ② vt. to make an illegal copy of something; ③ vt. to make something from iron/steel by heating and hammering; ④ vi. (forge ahead/forward) to move forward steadily (Longman)
- **中文释义**：①锻造（精神/关系/品格）；②伪造；③冶炼；④（forge ahead）奋力前进
- **语域**：正式 / 书面 / 学术（义①③④）；法律（义②）
- **同义词（义①）**：build, cultivate, develop; **（义②）**：counterfeit, fabricate, falsify
- **常见搭配**：**forge a bond/partnership/identity**；**forge ahead**；**forge a document**
- **拓展**：
  - 注意"一词多义"：本文语境中"铸就敢打必胜的战斗精神"对应义①/③（锻造/铸造品格）；法律语境中为"伪造文件"
  - 名词：**forgery**（伪造行为/伪造品）；**forger**（伪造者）
  - 成语用法：**forge one's own path**（开辟自己的道路）
- **例句**：
  - Decades of rigorous training under demanding conditions had **forged** this squadron into a **battle-hardened** elite unit.
  - 数十年苛刻条件下的严格训练将这支中队锻造成了一支久经沙场的精锐部队。

**W7. leverage**
- **音标**：/ˈlevərɪdʒ/ (美式)
- **词性**：n. 不可数名词；vt. 及物动词
- **英文释义**：① n. the power to influence people or events; ② n. the action or advantage of using a lever; ③ vt. to use something to maximum advantage (Cambridge Business English Dictionary)
- **中文释义**：①影响力；杠杆优势；②（动词）充分利用，发挥杠杆效应
- **语域**：商业 / 正式 / 学术；动词用法在学术和新闻写作中极为常见
- **同义词**：utilize, capitalize on, harness, exploit (vt.); **influence, clout** (n.)
- **常见搭配**：**leverage technology / data / expertise**；**leverage one's position**；**financial leverage**
- **拓展**：
  - 在金融领域，**leverage** 特指"财务杠杆"（以小博大的借贷投资方式）
  - 学术/商业写作中，**leverage** 作动词意为"充分借助/利用"（如：*leverage big data for competitive advantage*）
  - 区分：**leverage**（发挥杠杆效应，强调效率和优势放大）vs. **use**（普通用法）
- **例句**：
  - The brigade's decision to **leverage** big data analytics transformed its **targeting accuracy** from a persistent weakness into a competitive edge.
  - 该旅决定充分利用大数据分析，将长期以来的打击精度短板转化为竞争优势。

**W8. unflinching**
- **音标**：/ʌnˈflɪntʃɪŋ/ (美式)
- **词性**：adj. 形容词
- **英文释义**：not changing or becoming weaker, even in a very difficult or dangerous situation (Longman)
- **中文释义**：毫不退缩的；坚定不移的；不动摇的
- **语域**：正式 / 书面 / 新闻 / 文学
- **同义词**：steadfast, resolute, unwavering, dauntless; **反义词**：wavering, flinching, irresolute
- **常见搭配**：**unflinching courage / determination / commitment / gaze**；**unflinching dedication to duty**
- **拓展**：
  - 副词：**unflinchingly**；基础动词：**flinch**（退缩；因痛苦/惊吓而畏缩）
  - 写作亮点词：在人物刻画和议论文中替代"firm"或"brave"，语气更强
- **例句**：
  - Her **unflinching** commitment to realistic training, even under criticism, ultimately **paid dividends** on the competition floor.
  - 她对实战化训练毫不动摇的坚守，即便饱受批评，最终也在赛场上收获了回报。

## 4D. 金句积累

**金句一：**

> "做一件好事并不难，难的是一辈子都做好事。"
> ——毛泽东（文中引用）

**英译：**
It is not difficult to do one good thing; the difficulty lies in doing good things for an entire lifetime.

**写作应用**：适用于论述"持之以恒"与"初心不变"的议论文段落；也可用于讨论制度建设、人才培养的长期性与一贯性。

---

**金句二：**

> "独行快，众行远。"

**英译：**
One person may run fast, but a group runs far.
*(cf. the African proverb: "If you want to go fast, go alone; if you want to go far, go together.")*

**写作应用**：适用于讨论团队合作、组织建设、集体主义与可持续发展的写作场景；英文表述与非洲谚语高度契合，便于跨文化引用。

---

**金句三：**

> "飞得高才能看得远，看得远才能打得赢。"

**英译：**
Only by flying higher can you see farther; only by seeing farther can you fight and win.

**写作应用**：以飞行高度比喻战略眼光与格局，适用于论述"远见与战略思维"、"视野决定高度"以及"长远规划与即时行动之辩证关系"等主题的写作开篇或收尾。也可活用于非军事语境，如商业战略、教育政策等。
