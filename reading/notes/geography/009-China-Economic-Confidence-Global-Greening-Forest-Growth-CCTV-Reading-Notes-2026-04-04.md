# 模块一：翻译与全文概要

## 英文翻译

**"China's Economic Confidence | How Did China Become the World's Fastest and Largest Contributor to Global Greening?"**

Around the Qingming Festival, when grass and trees begin to sprout and life stirs anew, a meaningful trend is capturing growing public attention this spring: heading to the desert to plant trees, contributing to the fight against desertification.

Minqin County in Gansu Province, situated at the junction of the Badain Jaran and Tengger Deserts, was once a land ravaged by sandstorms. In recent years, Minqin launched a volunteer campaign — "Come to Minqin, Plant a Tree" — which drew enthusiastic responses nationwide. To date, more than 40,000 volunteers from across the country have planted over one million sand-tolerant plants such as *Haloxylon ammodendron* (saxaul) across 4,500 mu of desert. When this year's campaign kicked off, it sparked another wave of tree-planting enthusiasm. On the WeChat mini-program "Plant Trees in Minqin," single-day reservations during the Qingming holiday surpassed 4,000. Volunteers and Minqin residents are joining hands in this "green romance" to prevent the deserts from merging.

"A sound ecological environment is a shared benefit — and a shared responsibility." Minqin's greening efforts are a microcosm of China's decades-long afforestation practice. Since the launch of the "Three-North Shelterbelt Program" in 1978, generation after generation has carried the torch. After nearly half a century of effort, China has built up a substantial "green legacy" for future generations: forest area now reaches 3.614 billion mu, with a national forest coverage rate of 25.09%; artificial forest area stands at 1.386 billion mu, ranking first in the world; the total number of trees exceeds 142.6 billion, or about 100 trees per person. During the 14th Five-Year Plan period, China completed 549 million mu of land greening — as the world's fastest and largest contributor to greening, China accounts for one-quarter of the world's net increase in green cover.

China's vast territory encompasses enormously diverse climates and topographies — from the sweeping Gobi Desert with its lone columns of smoke, to the gully-ridden Loess Plateau where "growing a tree can be harder than raising a child." The answer lies in an adaptive approach: "match greenery to the land, match greenery to the water; plant trees where trees suit, plant grass where grass suits" — tailored strategies rooted in respect for natural laws.

Inner Mongolia uses drones to transport *Salix psammophila* (sand willow) and straw, with autonomous tree-planting robots capable of completing one mu of planting in just 19 minutes, and relies on an integrated sky-ground monitoring system for precision desertification control. The Talatan area in Qinghai has pioneered a "pasture-solar" model, combining photovoltaic development with grazing to achieve dual benefits. Along the Yangtze River Basin, a cross-regional "three-dimensional greening" initiative has seen provinces such as Hunan, Sichuan, and Anhui establish riparian buffer forests around rivers, lakes, and reservoirs, creating a layered, multifunctional ecological corridor. Shanghai, as a megacity, has adopted a "fill every gap with green" strategy to build 1,013 parks, earning the title "City of a Thousand Parks." Beijing's two rounds of million-mu afforestation have doubled the forest coverage of its plains, with pocket parks and urban forests making greenery visible at every turn.

Netizens have described China's greening as "kaleidoscopically green" and "wisely green." Combating sandstorms and planting trees requires not brute force, but the "Chinese wisdom" of meeting each challenge with a tailored solution. To lock in shifting sands, locals invented the "straw checkerboard" — dubbed "China's Magic Square" — embedding wheat straw in a grid pattern of one-meter squares to form a golden mesh. These simple-looking grids reduce near-surface wind speeds, stabilize drifting sand, and retain moisture. Within the grids, sand-adapted plants such as saxaul, *Tamarix* (red willow), *Calligonum*, and *Hedysarum scoparium* are cultivated, with green slowly spreading and raging sands gradually subdued. Today, agricultural tractors press down sand barriers, drones scatter grass seeds and tree seeds, nylon nets have replaced the more fragile straw checkerboards, and sensors buried in the sand monitor soil moisture and temperature — with drones for transport and inspection, satellite remote sensing, and AI-assisted decision-making all enhancing the precision and efficiency of greening efforts.

China has also explored multiple models including road-based desertification control, photovoltaic-plus-restoration, and industry-driven afforestation, realizing mutual reinforcement between ecology and industry, and between humanity and nature, boosting both economic and ecological value simultaneously. Jingning County in Gansu sees annual apple industry output exceeding 15 billion yuan; Fujian's forest-floor economy generates annual output surpassing 100 billion yuan; Qinghai's clean electricity export now covers 16 provinces. Improving quality, developing industry, and benefiting people — ordinary citizens enjoy both clear waters and green mountains while reaping real economic rewards. Through innovative public participation mechanisms and "those who restore, those who benefit" policies, people's enthusiasm for greening has been ignited — from the "Great Wall Girls' Sand-Fighting Platoon" to ordinary villagers, from corporate involvement to public-interest afforestation, everyone has become a guardian of green.

From the spectacle of photovoltaic blue and verdant green across the Tengger Desert, to green spreading through the gullies of the Loess Plateau; from forests and waterways intertwined in the Jiangnan region, to the view of green through every window in northern cities — the miracle of deserts turning into oases continues to unfold, and China's green territory steadily expands. As a new beginning takes root in the spring breeze, the defining color of Chinese modernization grows ever more radiant, and China's contributions to global ecological governance grow ever more significant.

**【巴丹吉林沙漠 / Badain Jaran Desert】**
中国第二大沙漠，位于内蒙古阿拉善盟西部，面积约4.92万平方公里。以高大沙山和神秘湖泊著称，最高沙山相对高度可达500米。是中国北方风沙源地之一，亦是三北防护林工程的重点治理区域。
The second-largest desert in China, located in the western Alxa League of Inner Mongolia, covering approximately 49,200 km². Known for towering dunes and mysterious lakes, it is one of northern China's primary sand sources and a key target of the Three-North Shelterbelt Program.

---

**【腾格里沙漠 / Tengger Desert】**
中国第四大沙漠，主体位于内蒙古阿拉善盟，面积约3.67万平方公里，跨甘肃、宁夏部分地区。曾因腾格里沙漠污染事件（2014年）引发全国关注。近年通过光伏治沙、草方格等手段取得显著治理成效。
The fourth-largest desert in China, mainly located in the Alxa League of Inner Mongolia, covering about 36,700 km². It drew national attention due to a pollution incident in 2014. In recent years, significant desertification control progress has been achieved through photovoltaic restoration and straw checkerboard techniques.

---

**【甘肃省民勤县 / Minqin County, Gansu Province】**
位于甘肃省河西走廊东端，地处巴丹吉林与腾格里两大沙漠夹击之间，三面环沙，历史上曾因过度农垦导致生态严重退化，被视为全国荒漠化最严重地区之一。近年成为社会力量参与治沙的典型示范县。
Located at the eastern end of the Hexi Corridor in Gansu Province, sandwiched between the Badain Jaran and Tengger Deserts on three sides. Historically one of the most severely desertified counties in China due to excessive land reclamation. In recent years, it has become a model county for citizen-led desertification control.

---

**【三北防护林工程 / Three-North Shelterbelt Program】**
1978年由中国国家林业局启动，规划建设期73年（至2050年），覆盖东北、华北、西北13个省区市，总面积约406万平方公里。是迄今世界上规模最大的人工生态防护林体系，被称为"绿色长城"。旨在防治风沙危害、水土流失及改善生态环境。
Launched in 1978 by China's State Forestry Administration, with a planned construction period of 73 years (through 2050), covering 13 provinces in northeast, north, and northwest China, spanning approximately 4.06 million km². The world's largest artificial ecological shelterbelt system, dubbed the "Green Great Wall," designed to control sandstorms, soil erosion, and ecological degradation.

---

**【梭梭 / Haloxylon ammodendron (Saxaul)】**
超旱生落叶小乔木，耐盐碱、抗干旱，是中国荒漠地区最重要的防风固沙植物之一。根系深达数米，可有效固定流沙，同时作为寄主植物与名贵中药材肉苁蓉共生，具有重要经济价值。
An extremely drought-resistant deciduous shrub/small tree, highly tolerant of saline-alkaline conditions. One of the most important sand-fixing plants in Chinese desert regions. Its deep root system effectively stabilizes shifting sand; it also serves as a host plant for *Cistanche*, a valuable traditional Chinese medicine, giving it significant economic value.

---

**【草方格沙障 / Straw Checkerboard Sand Barrier】**
20世纪50年代由中国工程师发明，被誉为"中国魔方"。将麦草或稻草半埋入沙中，形成约1米×1米的方格网，有效降低近地面风速、固定流沙、截留水分。已出口技术经验至中东、非洲等地区，获联合国环境规划署认可。
Invented by Chinese engineers in the 1950s and dubbed "China's Magic Square." Wheat or rice straw is half-buried in sand to form approximately 1m×1m grid cells, effectively reducing near-surface wind speeds, stabilizing drifting sand, and retaining moisture. The technique has been shared with the Middle East, Africa, and other regions, and has been recognized by UNEP.

---

**【"十四五"规划 / 14th Five-Year Plan (2021–2025)】**
中国国民经济和社会发展第十四个五年规划，规划期为2021至2025年，由国务院编制、全国人大审批通过。涵盖国土绿化、碳达峰、生态修复等重要生态文明建设目标，绿化目标为完成国土绿化5亿亩以上。

**【光伏治沙 / Photovoltaic-assisted Desertification Control】**

An innovative model combining photovoltaic power generation with desertification control. Solar panels installed in desert areas reduce evaporation and create a microenvironment beneath them for drought-resistant vegetation. The Talatan area in Qinghai serves as the representative case, having developed a sustainable "agrivoltaic" model integrating livestock grazing.

# 模块四：素材与语料库积累

## 4A. 重点词汇解析

以下 **W / R / T / S / L** 五类各自**单独编号**：**W** 为 ①–⑥，其余四类均为 ①–⑤（不与其它类混排）。

---

### W — 写作高频词

---

**① desertification**
- **音标**：/dɪˌzɜːrtɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/　**词性**：n.（不可数）
- **英文释义**（Cambridge）：*the process by which land changes into desert, for example because of too much farming or not enough rain.*
- **中文释义**：土地荒漠化；沙漠化
- **语域**：学术 / 书面 / 新闻 / 政策文件
- **同义词**：aridification, land degradation；**反义词**：revegetation, land restoration
- **拓展**：
  - 动词形式：*desertify*（较少用）；形容词：*desertified*
  - 常见搭配：*combat / prevent / reverse desertification*；*desertification control*；*desertification-prone areas*
  - 联合国相关机构：UNCCD（United Nations Convention to Combat Desertification，联合国防治荒漠化公约，1994年生效）
  - 区分：*desertification*（原非沙漠土地变为沙漠）vs. *aridification*（气候驱动的干旱化，更侧重自然过程）
- **例句**：
  > **Desertification** threatens nearly one-third of the Earth's land surface, jeopardizing food security and livelihoods for millions of people.
  > 土地荒漠化威胁着地球近三分之一的陆地面积，危及数百万人的粮食安全和生计。

---

**② afforestation**
- **音标**：/əˌfɔːrɪˈsteɪʃən/　**词性**：n.（不可数）
- **英文释义**（Cambridge）：*the action of planting trees on an area of land in order to make a forest.*
- **中文释义**：植树造林；（在原无森林之地）营造林地
- **语域**：学术 / 书面 / 环境政策
- **同义词**：tree planting, reforestation（有区别，见下）；**反义词**：deforestation, clear-cutting
- **拓展**：
  - 区分：**afforestation**（在历史上从未有过森林的土地上植树造林）vs. **reforestation**（在曾经有森林、后来被砍伐/破坏的土地上重新造林）——两者在政策文件中常混用，但学术语境有别。
  - 动词：*afforest*；常见搭配：*afforestation program / project / campaign*；*large-scale afforestation*
  - 气候议题中常与碳汇（*carbon sink*）联系讨论
- **例句**：
  > China's large-scale **afforestation** program has transformed millions of hectares of barren land into thriving green belts, contributing significantly to global carbon sequestration.
  > 中国大规模植树造林项目将数百万公顷的荒地变为郁郁葱葱的绿带，对全球碳汇作出了重要贡献。

---

**③ revegetation**
- **音标**：/ˌriːˌvedʒɪˈteɪʃən/　**词性**：n.（不可数）
- **英文释义**：*the process of replanting and rebuilding the soil of disturbed land.*
- **中文释义**：植被恢复；重新植被化
- **语域**：学术 / 生态工程 / 政策报告
- **同义词**：vegetation restoration, ecological rehabilitation；**反义词**：vegetation loss, land degradation
- **拓展**：
  - 动词：*revegetate*；形容词：*revegetated*
  - 常见搭配：*revegetation of degraded land*；*revegetation strategies*；*natural revegetation vs. artificial revegetation*
  - 在干旱半干旱地区的生态修复语境中极为常用
- **例句**：
  > Successful **revegetation** of the Loess Plateau has dramatically reduced soil erosion and improved water retention in the region's heavily degraded watersheds.
  > 黄土高原植被的成功恢复大幅减少了水土流失，改善了严重退化流域的蓄水能力。

---

**④ ecological corridor**
- **音标**：/ˌiːkəˈlɒdʒɪkəl kəˈrɪdər/　**词性**：n.（可数）
- **英文释义**：*a strip of natural habitat connecting wildlife populations separated by human activities or structures, thus enabling species migration and gene flow.*
- **中文释义**：生态廊道；生物通道
- **语域**：学术 / 生态规划 / 环境政策
- **同义词**：wildlife corridor, green corridor, habitat corridor
- **拓展**：
  - 复数：*ecological corridors*
  - 常见搭配：*establish / build / create an ecological corridor*；*riparian ecological corridor*（滨水生态廊道）
  - 在城市规划、国家公园体系建设等文件中高频出现
- **例句**：
  > The government's plan to establish an **ecological corridor** along the Yangtze River aims to reconnect fragmented habitats and restore biodiversity across the basin.
  > 政府沿长江建立生态廊道的计划旨在重新连接碎片化栖息地，恢复整个流域的生物多样性。

---

**⑤ carbon sequestration**
- **音标**：/ˈkɑːrbən ˌsiːkwɪˈstreɪʃən/　**词性**：n.（不可数）
- **英文释义**：*the process of capturing and storing atmospheric carbon dioxide, especially through biological or geological processes.*
- **中文释义**：碳封存；碳固定；碳汇
- **语域**：学术 / 气候政策 / 书面
- **同义词**：carbon capture, carbon sink function；**反义词**：carbon emission, carbon release
- **拓展**：
  - 区分：*carbon sequestration*（泛指固碳过程）vs. *carbon capture and storage（CCS）*（特指工业技术手段）vs. *carbon sink*（已实现固碳功能的生态系统）
  - 常见搭配：*forest carbon sequestration*；*enhance carbon sequestration capacity*；*nature-based carbon sequestration*
- **例句**：
  > Forests act as vital **carbon sequestration** reserves, absorbing roughly 2.6 billion tons of CO₂ annually and playing an indispensable role in mitigating climate change.
  > 森林是重要的碳封存储库，每年吸收约26亿吨二氧化碳，在减缓气候变化方面发挥着不可或缺的作用。

---

**⑥ biodiversity**
- **音标**：/ˌbaɪoʊdaɪˈvɜːrsɪti/ 　**词性**：n.（不可数）
- **英文释义**（Cambridge）：*the number and types of plants and animals that exist in a particular area or in the world generally, or the problem of protecting this.*
- **中文释义**：生物多样性
- **语域**：学术 / 环境政策 / 书面 / 新闻
- **同义词**：biological diversity, species diversity；**反义词**：monoculture（单一栽培）, species homogenization（物种均质化）
- **拓展**：
  - 三个层级：**genetic diversity**（基因多样性）/ **species diversity**（物种多样性）/ **ecosystem diversity**（生态系统多样性）
  - 常见搭配：*protect / preserve / maintain / threaten / undermine / restore biodiversity*；*biodiversity hotspot*（生物多样性热点区域）；*biodiversity loss*（生物多样性丧失）
  - 相关国际框架：*Convention on Biological Diversity（CBD，生物多样性公约，1992年）*；*Kunming-Montreal Global Biodiversity Framework*（昆明-蒙特利尔全球生物多样性框架，2022年通过）
- **例句**：
  > The restoration of native plant species along the river bank has significantly boosted local **biodiversity**, attracting dozens of bird and insect species that had not been recorded in the area for decades.
  > 沿河岸恢复本地植物种类显著提升了当地生物多样性，吸引了数十种鸟类和昆虫物种——这些物种已数十年未在该区域有过记录。

### R — 阅读高频词

---

**① shelterbelt**
- **音标**：/ˈʃɛltərˌbɛlt/ 　**词性**：n.（可数）
- **英文释义**（Longman）：*a row of trees or shrubs planted to protect crops or land from wind and erosion.*
- **中文释义**：防护林带；防风林
- **语域**：农业 / 林业 / 地理 / 政策文件
- **同义词**：windbreak, protective forest, green belt；**反义词**：open terrain（对照语境）
- **拓展**：
  - 常见搭配：*plant / establish a shelterbelt*；*shelterbelt system / network*；*the Three-North Shelterbelt Program*（三北防护林工程，1978年启动，预计2050年完工，覆盖13省区市）
  - 区分：**shelterbelt**（成带状、规模较大的防护林系统，常见于政策文件）vs. **windbreak**（通常指较小规模的田间防风障）
  - "Three-North Shelterbelt Forest Program"已于2025年累计完成建设任务约1.6亿亩，被称为"绿色长城（Green Great Wall）"
- **例句**：
  > Engineers designed an interlocking **shelterbelt** network across the region's vulnerable plains, dramatically cutting the frequency and severity of sandstorms that had plagued local farmers for generations.
  > 工程师在该地区脆弱的平原上设计了一套相互连接的防护林带网络，大幅降低了困扰当地农民数代人的沙尘暴频次与强度。

---

**② arid**
- **音标**：/ˈærɪd/ 　**词性**：adj.
- **英文释义**（Cambridge）：*very dry and without enough rain for plants; (figurative) lacking in interest or imagination.*
- **中文释义**：干旱的；贫瘠的；（引申义）枯燥乏味的
- **语域**：正式 / 学术 / 地理 / 书面；比喻义多见于文学评论
- **同义词**：parched, desiccated, barren；**反义词**：fertile, humid, lush, verdant
- **拓展**：
  - 名词：**aridity**（干旱性 / 干燥度）；副词：*aridly*（极少用）
  - 常见搭配：*arid region / zone / climate / land / landscape / conditions*；*semi-arid*（半干旱）；*hyper-arid*（极干旱）
  - **熟词僻义（S类重叠）**：*an arid debate*（索然无味的辩论）——用于比喻语境时意为"枯燥乏味的，毫无生气的"，常见于英语文学批评或时评写作
  - 地理分类标准（按年降水量）：*hyper-arid* < 25mm / *arid* 25–200mm / *semi-arid* 200–500mm
- **例句**：
  > Decades of overgrazing turned once-fertile grassland into **arid** wasteland, leaving the topsoil exposed to wind erosion and the local population vulnerable to food shortages.
  > 数十年的过度放牧将曾经肥沃的草地变成了干旱荒地，使表层土壤暴露于风蚀之中，当地居民面临粮食短缺的风险。

---

**③ photovoltaic (PV)**
- **音标**：/ˌfoʊtəʊvɒlˈteɪɪk/ 　**词性**：adj. / n.（复数 *photovoltaics* 指光伏系统/技术）
- **英文释义**：*relating to or using the conversion of light into electricity; (n.) a photovoltaic cell or system.*
- **中文释义**：光伏的；光电的；（名词复数）光伏设备/系统
- **语域**：科技 / 能源政策 / 学术 / 新闻
- **常用缩写**：**PV**（高频，在新闻报道和学术论文中几乎通用）
- **拓展**：
  - 常见搭配：*photovoltaic panel / cell / array / module / plant / power generation*；*photovoltaic-plus desertification control*（光伏+治沙，中国政策语境）；*rooftop photovoltaic system*（屋顶光伏）
  - 区分：**photovoltaic (PV)**（光伏，直接将光能转化为电能）vs. **solar thermal**（太阳能热力，将光能转化为热能再发电）vs. **concentrated solar power (CSP)**（聚光太阳能）
  - 青海塔拉滩"牧光互补"模式：光伏板下养羊，上方发电，开创"**agrivoltaics**（农光互补/牧光互补）"实践先例
- **例句**：
  > The **photovoltaic** installation across the desert acts as a dual-purpose project: the panels generate renewable electricity while their shade reduces surface temperature and moisture evaporation, enabling vegetation to re-establish beneath them.
  > 横贯沙漠的光伏装置承担着双重功能：光伏板发电的同时，其产生的阴影降低了地表温度和水分蒸发量，使植被得以在板下重新生长。

---

**④ remote sensing**
- **音标**：/rɪˈmoʊt ˈsɛnsɪŋ/ 　**词性**：n.（不可数，常作前置修饰语）
- **英文释义**：*the use of satellites or aircraft to obtain data and images of the Earth's surface, especially to monitor environmental changes.*
- **中文释义**：遥感；遥感监测技术
- **语域**：科技 / 地理信息 / 学术 / 新闻
- **相关词**：*GIS*（Geographic Information System，地理信息系统）；*LiDAR*（激光雷达，光探测与测距）；*satellite imagery*（卫星图像）
- **拓展**：
  - 常见搭配：*satellite remote sensing*；*remote sensing data / imagery / technology / monitoring*；*remote sensing-based assessment*
  - **"天空地一体化"监测体系**（sky-ground integrated monitoring system）：文中提及的中国治沙精准管理模式，整合卫星遥感、无人机巡检与地面传感器，是"数字治沙"的典型方案
  - 区分：**remote sensing**（被动/主动接收反射电磁波）vs. **in-situ monitoring**（原位/就地监测）
- **例句**：
  > By combining **remote sensing** data from satellites with ground-level soil moisture readings, land managers can detect early signs of vegetation degradation and intervene before irreversible damage occurs.
  > 通过将卫星遥感数据与地面土壤湿度读数相结合，土地管理者能够及早发现植被退化的迹象，在造成不可逆损害之前介入处置。

---

**⑤ encroach (on/upon)**
- **音标**：/ɪnˈkroʊtʃ/ 　**词性**：vi.（不及物动词，后接介词 *on* 或 *upon*）
- **英文释义**（Longman Phrasal Verbs Dictionary）：*① to gradually take more of someone's time, rights, or possessions than you should; ② (of a desert, sea, or city) to spread into or take over an area that was previously of a different kind.*
- **中文释义**：①（逐渐）侵占、蚕食（权利/时间/土地）；②（沙漠/城市等）向外扩张、侵入
- **语域**：书面 / 正式 / 法律 / 新闻（*upon* 比 *on* 更正式，多用于书面语）
- **同义词**：infringe (on), intrude (on), trespass, impinge；**反义词**：recede, retreat, withdraw
- **拓展**：
  - 名词：**encroachment**（侵占；蚕食）；形容词：*encroaching*
  - 常见搭配：*encroach on/upon someone's rights / territory / privacy / time*；*the desert is encroaching on farmland*（沙漠正在蚕食农田）——文章中"阻止沙漠合拢"即是防止两大沙漠向中间地带的 *encroachment*
  - 法律语境：*encroachment* 可指建筑物、构筑物越界侵占他人地产的行为（侵权/相邻关系纠纷）
  - 区分：**encroach on**（逐渐蚕食，强调过程的渐进性）vs. **invade**（突然入侵，强调行为的突发性）
- **例句**：
  > Without effective intervention, the expanding desert will continue to **encroach upon** agricultural land, threatening the food supply of millions living along the desert's margins.
  > 若无有效干预，不断扩张的沙漠将持续蚕食农业用地，威胁数百万生活在沙漠边缘地带居民的粮食供给。

### T — 翻译重要词

---

**① harness**
- **音标**：/ˈhɑːrnɪs/ 　**词性**：v.（及物） / n.（可数）
- **英文释义**（Collins）：
  - *v.* ① *to control and make use of a natural source of energy or power;* ② *to put a harness on a horse.*
  - *n.* ①*a set of leather straps for controlling a horse;* ② *a set of straps for attaching equipment to a person.*
- **中文释义**：
  - *v.* ①利用、驾驭（自然力量/资源/技术）；②给（马）套上挽具
  - *n.* ①（马的）挽具、缰绳；②（人的）安全带、背带
- **语域**：v.义①：正式 / 学术 / 书面 / 新闻（写作中极高频）；n.义①：日常 / 专业
- **同义词（v.义①）**：leverage, exploit, utilize, tap into；**反义词**：waste, squander
- **拓展**：
  - **Cambridge Collocations in Use (Advanced)**：*harness technology / energy / resources / potential / power*（高频搭配组合）
  - 翻译难点："充满智慧的绿"→ *wisely deployed greenery* 或 *intelligence-driven greening*；"科技手段赋能"→ *leveraging / harnessing technological tools*
  - 惯用表达：***in harness***（协同工作；同时进行）——*working in harness with local communities*（与当地社区协力合作）
  - 常见搭配：*harness solar / wind / water power*；*harness the power of AI*；*harness public enthusiasm*（调动公众热情）
- **例句**：
  > China's ability to **harness** both traditional ecological knowledge and cutting-edge technology has given it a decisive edge in the global race to restore degraded land.
  > 中国将传统生态知识与前沿科技相结合并加以综合运用的能力，使其在全球恢复退化土地的竞争中占据了决定性优势。

---

**② mitigate**
- **音标**：/ˈmɪtɪɡeɪt/ 　**词性**：v.（及物）
- **英文释义**（Cambridge）：*to make something less harmful, serious, or severe.*
- **中文释义**：减轻、缓和（负面影响）；降低（风险/损害）
- **语域**：正式 / 学术 / 法律 / 书面（高频出现于政策、法律、学术文本）
- **同义词**：alleviate, ameliorate, lessen, reduce, soften；**反义词**：aggravate, exacerbate, worsen, intensify
- **拓展**：
  - 名词：**mitigation**（减缓；缓和措施）；形容词：*mitigating*（减轻的）——法律语境中 ***mitigating circumstances / factors***（减轻处罚情节）极为重要
  - 常见搭配：*mitigate the impact / effects / risks / damage / consequences of...*；*climate change mitigation*（气候变化减缓，对应 *adaptation* 适应）
  - **三词辨析**：
    - **mitigate**：侧重减轻某事的负面严重程度（偏正式/法律/政策语境）
    - **alleviate**：侧重缓解痛苦或不适感（情感色彩稍强）
    - **ameliorate**：侧重使整体状况得到改善，语气更书面，强调"变好"而非单纯"减轻"
- **例句**：
  > Planting drought-resistant trees along the edge of the Gobi Desert has helped **mitigate** the destructive effects of seasonal sandstorms on nearby agricultural communities.
  > 沿戈壁沙漠边缘种植耐旱树木有助于减轻季节性沙尘暴对附近农业社区造成的破坏性影响。

**④ tailored**

**⑤ leverage**
- **音标**：/ˈlɛvərɪdʒ/ 　**词性**：n.（不可数）/ v.（及物）

   > 2026年3月12日，全国人大表决通过《生态环境法典》（主席令第七十号），将《防沙治沙法》《水土保持法》等整合其中，自2026年8月15日起施行，《环境保护法》等10部法律同时废止。法典第三编第六章专设"防沙治沙"节，是备考核心法条。（来源：全国人大官网 npc.gov.cn / 生态环境部官网 mee.gov.cn）

2. **《中华人民共和国森林法》（2019年修订）第6条 公众参与 森林保护义务**
   > 现行《森林法》（2020年7月1日施行）第六条规定"国家鼓励公民、法人和非法人组织等社会力量，以捐资、志愿服务等方式参与森林保护和生态修复活动"，是志愿治沙法律依据之一。

3. **"谁治理谁受益"原则 营利性治沙管理办法 国家林业局 2004年**
   > 《营利性治沙管理办法》（国家林业局，2004年施行）规定了企业参与治沙的权利与义务，"谁治理、谁受益"原则的法律渊源之一，涉及土地使用权、经营权分配等民事权利配置问题。

4. **中国环境公益诉讼 最高人民法院司法解释 2015年 生态环境修复责任**
   > 最高人民法院2015年《关于审理环境民事公益诉讼案件适用法律若干问题的解释》（法释〔2015〕1号）确立了生态环境损害赔偿与修复责任制度，与文中"谁破坏谁修复"逻辑呼应，是中国环境法重点考点。

5. **《中华人民共和国土地管理法》沙化土地使用权 承包经营权 流转**
   > 《土地管理法》（2019年修正）及《农村土地承包法》（2018年修正）规定了沙化土地的经营权流转规则，是理解"企业参与治沙、长期受益"模式的私法基础，涉及不动产物权与经营权分离问题。

6. **美国《清洁空气法》沙尘暴管理 State Implementation Plan (SIP) 环境联邦主义**
   > 美国《清洁空气法》（Clean Air Act, 42 U.S.C. § 7401 et seq.）要求各州制定州实施计划（SIP）应对颗粒物（PM2.5/PM10）污染，西南各州的沙尘防控计划是美国环境法中联邦与州权划分的典型案例，可与中国治沙法律体系形成比较法视角。

## 4D. 金句积累

---

**金句一**

> **原文**：良好生态人人共享，也需要合力共建。
>
> **英译**：A sound ecological environment is a shared benefit for all — and equally, a shared responsibility to build together.
>
> **赏析**：结构对称（共享 / 共建），言简意赅，适合作为环境议题写作的论点句或收尾句。英译采用破折号强化逻辑转折，*shared benefit / shared responsibility* 对仗工整。

---

**金句二**

> **原文**：以地定绿、以水定绿，宜树则树，宜草则草。
>
> **英译**：Let the land determine the greenery, let the water determine the greenery — plant trees where trees are suited, plant grass where grass belongs.
>
> **赏析**：四字句的整饬美感通过排比英译得以再现，体现"因地制宜"的哲学智慧。英译中 *let...determine* 的结构赋予原则以行动感，*where...suited / where...belongs* 的平行结构贴合汉语四字格的节律。适合用于论述定制化政策、反对"一刀切"的写作场景。

---

**金句三**

> **原文**：从腾格里沙漠的光伏蓝与草木绿相映成景，到黄土高原的沟壑间绿意铺展……荒漠变绿洲的奇迹持续上演，绿色版图在神州大地不断延伸。
>
> **英译**：From the photovoltaic blue and verdant green painting the Tengger Desert in complementary hues, to emerald spreading through the gullies of the Loess Plateau — the miracle of deserts transformed into oases continues to unfold, and China's green territory steadily expands across the land.
>
> **赏析**：原文以色彩意象（光伏蓝、草木绿）统领地理叙事，格局宏阔。英译以 *photovoltaic blue and verdant green painting...in complementary hues* 还原色彩张力，*continues to unfold* 呼应"奇迹持续上演"的动态感。此句适合作为生态议题作文的文学性结尾，兼具画面感与升华效果。
