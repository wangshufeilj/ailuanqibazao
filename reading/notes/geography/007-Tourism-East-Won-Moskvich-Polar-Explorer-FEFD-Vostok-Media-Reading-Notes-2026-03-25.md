### 文章背景与来源 | Background & Source
*   **来源 (Source):** Vostok Media (Восток-Медиа)
*   **题目 (Title):** Tourism - The East is Won: The "Moskvich-Polar Explorer" Has Conquered All Regions of the FEFD (Туризм - Восток взят: Москвич-Полярник покорил все регионы ДФО)
*   **作者 (Author):** Ksenia Murzina (Ксения Мурзина). 
*   **作者背景:** 驻远东地区的资深记者，长期关注俄罗斯远东联邦管区（DFO）的社会、旅游及基础设施发展。
*   **核心人物背景:** 
    *   **Aleksey Zhirukhin (Алексей Жирухин):** 俄罗斯知名极限旅行博主、探险家，擅长驾驶苏联老式汽车挑战极寒和无人区，其“莫斯科人-极地探险者”项目在俄语互联网拥有极高知名度。
    *   **Alexander Elikov (Александр Еликов):** 资深北极探险专家，多次参与极地汽车越野。

```text
【前情提要 | Article Structure & Summary】

Overall Structure:
本篇文章报道了探险团队驾驶一辆名为“莫斯科人-极地探险者”的苏联老旧汽车，首次完成环游俄罗斯远东联邦管区（DFO）全部11个地区的壮举。文章通过记录他们在最后四个地区的旅程，展示了远东地区的旅游潜力、严酷的地理环境以及人与人之间淳朴的互助精神。

Paragraph-Level Breakdown:
1. Feat Announcement (Para 1-3): 宣布探险成功，强调这是历史上首次单车跑遍远东所有地区，并介绍团队成员。
2. Exploring Uncharted Regions (Para 4-5): 重点描述犹太自治州（EAO）和阿穆尔州。提到比罗比詹的独特文化及对该地区旅游价值的重新评估。
3. Vostochny Cosmodrome Incident (Para 6-9): 讲述在东方航天发射场的惊险插曲——因燃油耗尽导致汽车在发射台附近熄火，展现了旅途的不可预测性。
4. Challenges on the BAM Route (Para 10-14): 放弃平坦高速，选择艰苦的贝尔卡尔-阿穆尔铁路（BAM）替代公路。记录了在零下35度极寒中抛锚、团队分头求援及通过电商购买零件的曲折过程。
5. Human Connection & Conclusion (Para 15-17): 描述在西伯利亚深处得到的当地人无私帮助。总结旅行感悟：人是旅途中最深刻的印象。目前车辆暂存伊尔库茨克，等待夏季开启克拉斯诺亚尔斯克的新旅程。

Intra-paragraph Logic (Example: BAM Challenges):
- Decision: Shift from standard highways to off-road BAM service tracks to prove accessibility.
- Conflict: Mechanical failure in extreme cold (-35°C) -> Strategic decision to wait and then split up for help.
- Resolution: Local mechanic's help -> Logistic hurdles (parts delivery) -> Creative solution (taking a train to pick up parts).
- Moral: Demonstrating that even a vintage Soviet car can facilitate "accessible tourism" through resilience.
```

**The East Is Conquered: "Moskvich-Polyarnik" Completes All Regions of the Russian Far East**

The crew of the legendary green Moskvich became the first in the history of Russia and the Soviet Union to travel through all regions of the Russian Far East in a single vehicle.

The Moskvich-Polyarnik has completed its journey across the Russian Far East. The road-trip team aboard a green Soviet-era automobile became the first in the history of Russia and the USSR to visit all regions of the Far East in one car.

"Since January 2024, we have been travelling across the Far East and have covered all 11 regions during this time. Before us, no vehicle had ever driven through absolutely all the regions of the Far East. We verified this information for a long time," says road traveller Alexei Zhirukhin.

**A Stop at the Cosmodrome**

In January, the Moskvich-Polyarnik team (Alexei Zhirukhin, AlexanderYelikov, Natalya Shipovskaya, and Dmitry Gostinshchikov) set out from Khabarovsk to conquer the four remaining Far Eastern regions not yet visited by their Moskvich: the Jewish Autonomous Oblast, Amur Oblast, Transbaikal Territory, and Buryatia. The Jewish Autonomous Oblast was first on the list. This region pleasantly surprised the travellers, who had already driven across all of Russia.

"The Jewish Autonomous Oblast is an underrated region from a tourism perspective. People usually driving to the Far East from across Russia just blaze through it. And yet what a colourful Birobidzhan it is, with its street names, shop signs, and Yiddish — and what hot springs there are in the Jewish Autonomous Oblast," Alexei Zhirukhin notes.

While the Jewish Autonomous Oblast came as a discovery for the Moskvich-Polyarnik team, Amur Oblast felt almost like home to Alexei Zhirukhin and Alexander Yellikov — they had visited the region several times before in other vehicles and as part of other projects.

"We come to Amur Oblast every year as part of various projects, returning to this region again and again, each time discovering something new. This year, for example, we took a plunge into an ice hole in the Amur River — with a view of China. But we came here in the Moskvich-Polyarnik for the first time. And of course we couldn't miss the Vostochny Cosmodrome. To be in such a place and not stop in — that would simply be a sin," the auto bloggers confess.

The green Moskvich's visit to the cosmodrome will be remembered for a long time — and here's why. Since the facility is a restricted zone, the team had to unload everything that had accumulated in the boot over three years, including a petrol canister kept for emergencies, since the car's fuel gauge periodically malfunctions.

"We had unloaded everything from the car but didn't know how much fuel was left in the tank. So we're pulling up to the Soyuz launch pad and literally 100 metres from it, the Moskvich stalls. The whole of Vostochny was in shock, because nothing like this had ever happened at the cosmodrome before. They offered to refuel us with rocket fuel, but we declined — we would have flown across all of Russia too quickly," laughs Alexei Zhirukhin.

Fuel for the Moskvich was eventually found, and the team set off again — though as usual, they refused to take easy routes, so instead of the Amur and Siberia highways, the travellers chose the BAM motor road.

**A Frosty Night**

"Our mission is to showcase accessible tourism — to show that even challenging routes that were always considered off-road can be driven in an old Moskvich. So we drove through the BAM route. And on the very first day the Moskvich broke down — it stalled in the night at minus 35 degrees. The distances this time were great, and it was apparently tired — it broke down 50 kilometres from the village where we planned to stay the night," the road travellers recount.

After inspecting the car and diagnosing the fault, it became clear that the Moskvich needed to be brought into a warm space for repairs — doing so in the frost was impossible. The team decided to spend the night in the car and wait for passing vehicles to ask for help.

"We always show from our own experience what incredible situations can arise on the road and how to deal with them. We always carry warm clothing and sleeping bags. So we got dressed, bundled up, and went to sleep. By midday we realised nobody would drive past, so we lit a fire, warmed ourselves up, and decided to split into two pairs — two stayed by the car while two went to look for help. A few hours later the guys came back and reported that they had met a technician who had come in a UAZ from the settlement of Yuktali to fix a breakdown. After resolving the fault, he promised to come and get us — and he kept his word," recalls Alexei Zhirukhin.

In Yuktali, the father of one of their subscribers from Tynda was already waiting for the team. He put the road travellers up, fed them, and warmed them up. It turned out that the Moskvich's distributor had fallen apart, and a replacement could not be found locally. A popular online marketplace came to the rescue — but there was no delivery to Yuktali, so the order had to be picked up in Krasnoyarsk, where the spare part was delivered faster than to Irkutsk or Blagoveshchensk.

"We set off to meet the distributor — we boarded a train and rode to Krasnoyarsk for two days. We fulfilled a dream — we travelled the BAM by train. Then we picked up the part and rode back for two days. We returned to Yuktali, fixed everything, and set off again," Alexei Zhirukhin continues.

**The Main Impression — The People**

After this, the team visited BAM settlements, saw the Charsky Sands, and drove across the Vitim Bridge — the most dangerous in the world — but near Severomuysk the car broke down again. This time, however, they didn't have to wait long for help — shift workers helped out and towed them to the nearest settlement. There the travellers spotted a garage with its light on, asked to warm up, and ended up receiving overnight lodging and the chance to repair the car.

"On the BAM they saved us practically everywhere. The next morning the locals helped us do the repairs and refused to take a single kopeck. We drove on, showed the Moskvich Lake Baikal in Severobaykalsk, and headed towards Irkutsk. And there in the middle of the route we broke down again, near an old village on the Lena River. Only eight people live there; one of them lent us tools to fix the Moskvich, fed us, and also categorically refused any money. Our main impression from all the Moskvich trips is the people. Thanks to this car, we have met an enormous number of people, seen an enormous number of settlements, and have looked at Russia in a different way," Alexei Zhirukhin notes.

The current journey of the Moskvich-Polyarnik concluded in Irkutsk, where the car will await the team until summer. When the warm season arrives, the auto bloggers plan to begin exploring Krasnoyarsk Krai. This is expected to take them a year.

### 地名与机构

**东方航天发射场（Космодром Восточный / Vostochny Cosmodrome）**
俄罗斯最新民用航天发射场，位于阿穆尔州西北部（北纬51°—52°）。2007年由总统普京签署建设令，2012年正式开工，2016年4月完成首次发射。建设及运营单位为俄罗斯国家航天集团（Роскосмос）。设有联盟号（Soyuz）及未来安加拉号（Angara）发射台，属于严格的军民两用管控区域。

**贝阿铁路（БАМ，Байкало-Амурская Магистраль / BAM, Baikal-Amur Mainline）**
全长约4,300公里的铁路干线，东起苏维埃港（Советская Гавань），西至泰舍特（Тайшет），途经外贝加尔、布里亚特、萨哈（雅库特）等地区。苏联于1974年启动大规模建设，1984年全线贯通（官方通车），被视为苏联最具代表性的建设工程之一。该铁路伴随有平行的公路线，即文中的"БАМ公路"（автодублер БАМа）。

**维季姆大桥（Витимский мост / Vitim Bridge）**
横跨外贝加尔边疆区与布里亚特共和国交界处维季姆河的公路桥，桥长570米，桥面距河面约15米，宽仅约4米（勉强容纳一辆汽车通行），建于1980年代末，原为临时性工程结构。路面为木质，多年未经系统维护，曾多次失火。2018年被法国《西部法兰西报》（Ouest-France）列入全球最危险道路榜单前十。

**恰尔斯基沙漠（Чарские пески / Charsky Sands）**
位于外贝加尔边疆区卡拉尔区（Каларский район）的沙丘地貌奇观，总面积约50平方公里，最高沙丘约80米。是西伯利亚极其罕见的"沙漠嵌入森林"自然景观，地处海拔约1,000米的山地，四周围绕针叶林和雪山，为俄罗斯著名地质奇观与旅游目的地。

**犹太自治州（Еврейская автономная область / Jewish Autonomous Oblast）**
俄罗斯远东联邦区成员，首府比罗比詹（Биробиджан）。1934年根据苏联民族政策设立，原旨在为犹太人提供一块"社会主义家园"用地。俄语与意第绪语（Yiddish）均为官方语言，但今日犹太族裔仅占当地人口极少比例。全州人口约15万，是俄罗斯人口最少的联邦主体之一，旅游业开发程度较低。

**意第绪语（Идиш / Yiddish）**
起源于中世纪欧洲的犹太日耳曼语言，以希伯来字母书写，词汇以德语为基础，融合大量希伯来语、斯拉夫语等成分。历史上为东欧阿什肯纳兹犹太人的主要日常语言。苏联建立犹太自治州时，意第绪语被确立为官方语言，比罗比詹街头至今仍保留双语标识。

# 模块四：素材与语料库积累

## 4A. 重点词汇解析

---

### W — 写作高频词

---

**1. expedition**
- **音标**：/ˌekspəˈdɪʃən/　**词性**：n.
- **英文释义**：A long and carefully organized journey, especially to a dangerous or unfamiliar place; also a short journey made for a particular purpose. (Longman)
- **中文释义**：远征；探险；（有特定目的的）旅行
- **语域**：书面/新闻/正式
- **同义词**：journey, trek, voyage, quest
- **反义词**：—
- **常见词组**：go on an expedition（参加探险）；launch/mount an expedition（发起/组织探险）；Arctic/scientific expedition（北极/科学探险）
- **拓展**：可数名词（countable）。注意区分：expedition 强调组织性与目的性，而 journey 是中性泛指；trek 多指徒步跋涉；voyage 多用于海上。expedition 还可指参与探险的"团队"本身（"the expedition" = the people）。
- **例句**：
  > The **research expedition** to the Antarctic lasted over six months, yielding unprecedented data on glacial retreat.
  > 这次前往南极的**科考探险**历时逾六个月，获取了关于冰川退缩的前所未有的数据。

---

**2. traverse**
- **音标**：/trəˈvɜːrs/ (v.)　/ˈtrævɜːrs/ (n.)　**词性**：v. / n.
- **英文释义**：(v.) To travel across, through, or over an area, especially a large or difficult one. (Cambridge)
- **中文释义**：穿越；横贯；通过（某地区）
- **语域**：书面/正式/地理学/登山
- **同义词**：cross, navigate, negotiate (a terrain)
- **反义词**：—
- **常见词组**：traverse a mountain range（横穿山脉）；traverse difficult terrain（穿越险峻地形）
- **拓展**：traverse 比 cross 更正式，且含有"经历重重阻碍"的隐含义。作名词时，指"横向移动"或登山中的"横切路线"。词性变化：traversal (n.)。
- **例句**：
  > It took the crew three weeks to **traverse** the desolate stretches of permafrost between the two outposts.
  > 团队用了三周时间**穿越**两个前哨站之间荒无人烟的永久冻土地带。

---

**3. arduous**
- **音标**：/ˈɑːrdjuəs/　**词性**：adj.
- **英文释义**：Involving a lot of strength and effort; very difficult and tiring. (Cambridge)
- **中文释义**：艰苦的；费力的；艰巨的
- **语域**：书面/正式
- **同义词**：gruelling, strenuous, taxing, laborious, demanding
- **反义词**：easy, effortless, undemanding
- **常见词组**：an arduous task/journey/climb（艰巨的任务/旅途/攀登）
- **拓展**：arduous 侧重于体力和精力的双重消耗，比 difficult 更有质感，常与 journey、task、undertaking 搭配，是写作中提升文本层次的好词。副词形式：arduously；名词形式：arduousness。
- **例句**：
  > The **arduous** trek along the unpaved BAM road tested both the vehicle's endurance and the crew's resolve.
  > 沿着未铺装的贝阿公路进行的**艰苦**跋涉，既考验了车辆的耐久性，也考验了团队的意志。

---

**4. resilience**
- **音标**：/rɪˈzɪliəns/　**词性**：n.
- **英文释义**：The ability to be happy, successful, or recover quickly after something difficult or unpleasant happens. (Cambridge)
- **中文释义**：韧性；恢复力；适应力
- **语域**：学术/正式/书面
- **同义词**：toughness, tenacity, durability, robustness
- **反义词**：fragility, vulnerability
- **常见词组**：show/demonstrate resilience（展现韧性）；emotional/psychological resilience（情感/心理韧性）；the resilience of the human spirit（人类精神的韧性）
- **拓展**：不可数名词（uncountable）。形容词：resilient；副词：resiliently。该词在生态学中也常用（ecosystem resilience，生态系统弹性），是当代学术写作中频率极高的词。
- **例句**：
  > The **resilience** of local communities along the BAM corridor, who selflessly assisted stranded travellers, speaks volumes about the social fabric of Russia's hinterland.
  > 贝阿沿线当地社区无私帮助受困旅行者所展现出的**韧性**，深刻揭示了俄罗斯内陆的社会凝聚力。

---

**5. venture**
- **音标**：/ˈventʃər/　**词性**：v. / n.
- **英文释义**：(v.) To go somewhere that could be dangerous or unpleasant; (n.) a new business activity that involves taking risks. (Longman)
- **中文释义**：（v.）冒险前往；斗胆尝试；（n.）冒险项目；（商业）新创企业
- **语域**：书面/正式（v.）；商业/新闻（n.）
- **同义词**：dare (v.), risk (v.), enterprise (n.), undertaking (n.)
- **反义词**：retreat, withdraw
- **常见词组**：venture into (somewhere)（冒险进入某处）；joint venture（合资企业）；venture capital（风险投资）；nothing ventured, nothing gained（不入虎穴，焉得虎子）
- **拓展**：动词 venture 含有"明知有风险仍鼓起勇气"的语气，比 go 更有文学感。名词用法在商业语境中极为常见。注意：venture capital（风险投资）是固定搭配。
- **例句**：
  > Few travellers dare to **venture** beyond the paved highway into the isolated timber roads of eastern Siberia.
  > 很少有旅行者敢于**冒险深入**西伯利亚东部那些与主干道相隔绝的林区土路。

---

**6. accessible**
- **音标**：/əkˈsesəbl/　**词性**：adj.
- **英文释义**：Easy to reach or get into; easy to understand or use. (Longman)
- **中文释义**：可进入的；可到达的；易于理解/使用的；亲民的
- **语域**：通用/新闻/学术
- **同义词**：reachable, attainable, approachable
- **反义词**：inaccessible, remote, unattainable
- **常见词组**：accessible tourism（平民旅游；无障碍旅游）；accessible to the public（向公众开放）；make something accessible（使……易于获取）
- **拓展**：名词：accessibility（可及性，在城市规划、无障碍设计、信息技术领域极常用）；副词：accessibly。注意 accessible tourism 在本文语境中指"让普通人也能实现的旅游"，而在国际旅游语境中也可指"无障碍旅游（为残障人士设计）"，需按语境判断。
- **例句**：
  > The project aims to prove that **accessible** travel is not a matter of budget or equipment, but of determination and local knowledge.
  > 该项目旨在证明，**平民化**旅行的实现与否，并不取决于预算或装备，而在于决心和对本地情况的了解。

---

**7. spontaneous**
- **音标**：/spɑːnˈteɪniəs/　**词性**：adj.
- **英文释义**：Done or happening naturally, without being planned or forced. (Longman)
- **中文释义**：自发的；出于本能的；不经刻意安排的；自然流露的
- **语域**：书面/正式/学术
- **同义词**：unplanned, instinctive, unprompted, impromptu
- **反义词**：premeditated, deliberate, calculated
- **常见词组**：spontaneous generosity（自发的慷慨）；spontaneous combustion（自燃）；spontaneous recovery（自然恢复）
- **拓展**：副词：spontaneously；名词：spontaneity /ˌspɑːntəˈniːəti/（自发性，注意发音）。该词在心理学（spontaneous remission）、物理学、写作描述中均有广泛用途。
- **例句**：
  > The **spontaneous** hospitality of strangers — offering food, shelter, and tools without expectation of reward — became the defining theme of the journey.
  > 陌生人**自发**的热情款待——提供食物、住所和工具，不求回报——成为这段旅途最鲜明的主题。

---

**8. undertaking**
- **音标**：/ˈʌndərˌteɪkɪŋ/　**词性**：n.
- **英文释义**：An important or difficult piece of work; a formal promise to do something. (Longman)
- **中文释义**：（艰巨的）事业；任务；（正式的）承诺；保证
- **语域**：正式/书面/法律
- **同义词**：endeavour, enterprise, venture, commitment (promise)
- **反义词**：—
- **常见词组**：a major/ambitious undertaking（重大/雄心勃勃的事业）；give an undertaking（作出承诺）；formal undertaking（正式保证）
- **拓展**：可数名词。注意双重含义：①"（困难的）任务/项目"（名词化的 undertake = to take on a task）；②英式法律语境中的"正式承诺/保证"，相当于 pledge。另注意 undertaker（n.）指"殡仪从业者"，是完全不同的含义！
- **例句**：
  > Driving a 1981 Soviet automobile across eleven Far Eastern regions in sub-zero temperatures is no small **undertaking**, even for seasoned travellers.
  > 驾驶一辆1981年的苏联老爷车穿越11个远东地区，在零下温度中跋涉，对于久经沙场的旅行者来说也绝非易事——这是一项重大的**挑战**。

---

### R — 阅读高频词

---

**9. remote**
- **音标**：/rɪˈmoʊt/　**词性**：adj. / n.
- **英文释义**：(adj.) Far from towns or other places where people live; not likely to happen; unfriendly and not interested in people. (Longman)
- **中文释义**：偏远的；遥远的；（可能性）极小的；疏远冷漠的
- **语域**：通用/新闻/地理
- **同义词**：isolated, secluded, distant, outlying
- **反义词**：accessible, central, nearby
- **常见词组**：remote area/village（偏远地区/村庄）；remote chance（极小的可能性）；remote control（遥控器，作名词）；remote from（与……格格不入）
- **拓展**：注意 remoteness（不可数名词）指"偏僻"。形容词 remote 有三个常见含义需分辨：①地理上的"偏远"；②可能性上的"极小"；③人际关系上的"疏离"。副词：remotely。
- **例句**：
  > In these **remote** settlements along the Lena River, residents still live largely off the land, with little connection to the urban economy.
  > 在勒拿河沿岸这些**偏远**定居点，居民依然主要靠土地为生，与城市经济几乎没有联系。

---

**10. regime**
- **音标**：/reɪˈʒiːm/　**词性**：n.
- **英文释义**：A system of rules, or a government that strictly controls a country; a particular system of organization or management. (Longman)
- **中文释义**：（政治上的）政权；体制；管理制度；（某种）规程
- **语域**：新闻/政治/正式
- **同义词**：government, administration, system, order
- **反义词**：—
- **常见词组**：security regime（安全管理制度）；regime change（政权更迭）；a strict regime（严格的管理制度）
- **拓展**：在文中"объект режимный"意为"管控设施/军事禁区"，对应英文为 restricted/security facility；此处 regime 用于形容词性修饰，是书面俄语法律行政词汇。英文 regime 在政治语境中可带有贬义，在管理/医学语境中（a fitness regime 健身方案）则为中性。
- **例句**：
  > Access to the cosmodrome is strictly regulated under a **security regime** that requires all personal belongings to be inspected before entry.
  > 进入航天发射场受到严格的**安全管理制度**约束，所有随身物品在入场前均须接受检查。

---

**11. subsist / subsistence**
- **音标**：/səbˈsɪst/（v.）；/səbˈsɪstəns/（n.）　**词性**：v. / n.
- **英文释义**：To stay alive using whatever limited food, money etc is available; the state of barely surviving. (Longman)
- **中文释义**：维持生计；（仅够）维持生存；生存状态
- **语域**：学术/正式/社会学
- **同义词**：survive, eke out a living, get by
- **反义词**：thrive, flourish
- **常见词组**：subsistence farming（自给农业）；subsistence level（最低生存水平）；subsist on（靠……维生）
- **拓展**：不可数名词（subsistence）。该词在经济学、人类学中常见，如 subsistence economy（自给自足经济）。
- **例句**：
  > The handful of residents in the riverside hamlet appeared to **subsist** on fishing and small-scale cultivation, entirely removed from the market economy.
  > 这个河畔小村的寥寥几户居民似乎完全靠捕鱼和小规模种植**维持生计**，与市场经济完全隔绝。

---

**12. stranded**
- **音标**：/ˈstrændɪd/　**词性**：adj. （past participle of strand, v.）
- **英文释义**：Left in a place you cannot get away from, usually because of a mechanical failure or bad weather. (Longman)
- **中文释义**：被困住的；进退两难的；搁浅的
- **语域**：新闻/通用
- **同义词**：stuck, marooned, isolated, immobilised
- **反义词**：mobile, free
- **常见词组**：be stranded (somewhere)（被困某处）；leave someone stranded（让某人进退两难）；stranded motorist（抛锚被困的司机）
- **拓展**：strand 作动词，其原义是"（船）搁浅"，引申为"使陷入困境"。过去式/过去分词均为 stranded（规则变化）。
- **例句**：
  > The crew found themselves **stranded** in a snowstorm fifty kilometres from the nearest habitation, with no phone signal and dwindling fuel.
  > 团队发现自己被困在暴风雪中，距最近的居民点有50公里，没有手机信号，燃料也所剩无几。

---

**13. malfunction**
- **音标**：/mælˈfʌŋkʃən/　**词性**：n. / v.
- **英文释义**：If a machine or part of the body malfunctions, it does not work properly; the failure of a machine or part to work correctly. (Longman)
- **中文释义**：（机器/器官）故障；失灵；运作不正常
- **语域**：技术/新闻/正式
- **同义词**：failure, breakdown, fault, glitch (informal)
- **反义词**：function, operate correctly
- **常见词组**：engine/system malfunction（发动机/系统故障）；due to a malfunction（由于故障）
- **拓展**：可数名词。作动词时：the gauge malfunctioned（仪表失灵）。注意与 breakdown 的区别：breakdown 更强调"完全停止工作"；malfunction 强调"工作异常但尚未完全停止"。
- **例句**：
  > A **malfunction** in the fuel gauge meant the team could never be entirely certain how much petrol remained in the tank.
  > 油量表的**故障**意味着团队永远无法确切知道油箱里还剩多少汽油。

---

**14. improvise**
- **音标**：/ˈɪmprəˌvaɪz/　**词性**：v.
- **英文释义**：To do something by using whatever is available, because you do not have the things you need; to make or do something without preparation. (Longman)
- **中文释义**：即兴创作；就地取材应急；临时凑合
- **语域**：通用/书面
- **同义词**：ad-lib, extemporise, make do, cobble together
- **反义词**：plan, prepare, rehearse
- **常见词组**：improvise a solution（临时想出解决方案）；improvise with available materials（利用现有材料凑合）
- **拓展**：名词：improvisation（即兴发挥）；形容词：improvised（临时的）。在爵士乐/戏剧语境中，improvise 指"即兴演奏/表演"，但在实用语境中指"临时应付"。
- **例句**：
  > Without a spare distributor in stock, the mechanics had to **improvise** with hand tools and parts salvaged from a decommissioned vehicle.
  > 由于没有备用分电器，修理工只能用手动工具和从一辆报废车上拆下的零件**临时凑合**。

---

**15. hospitality**
- **音标**：/ˌhɑːspɪˈtæləti/　**词性**：n.
- **英文释义**：Friendly and generous behaviour towards guests or people you have just met; the hotel, restaurant, and catering industry. (Longman)
- **中文释义**：热情款待；好客；（商业）餐旅服务业
- **语域**：通用/书面/商业
- **同义词**：generosity, warmth, cordiality, welcome
- **反义词**：hostility, inhospitality
- **常见词组**：show/offer hospitality（表示好客）；hospitality industry（餐旅业）；corporate hospitality（企业接待/公关招待）
- **拓展**：不可数名词。词根 hospit- 来自拉丁文，与 hospital（医院）、hotel（酒店）同源，均有"照顾客人"的含义。
- **例句**：
  > The **hospitality** extended by villagers who opened their homes and workshops to stranded strangers was, by the team's own account, the most memorable aspect of the journey.
  > 据团队自述，沿途村民打开家门和车间接纳素不相识的落难旅行者所表现出的**热情款待**，是这段旅程最难忘的部分。

---

**16. disclose / revelation**
- **音标**：/dɪˈskloʊz/（v.）；/ˌrevəˈleɪʃən/（n.）　**词性**：v. / n.
- **英文释义**：disclose: to give information to people, especially about something that was previously secret; revelation: a surprising and previously unknown fact that is made known to people. (Longman)
- **中文释义**：披露；透露；揭示（v.）；（令人惊喜/震惊的）新发现/启示（n.）
- **语域**：新闻/正式/书面
- **同义词**：reveal, uncover, unveil (v.)；discovery, surprise, eye-opener (n.)
- **反义词**：conceal, hide (v.)
- **常见词组**：a revelation to sb（对某人是一大启示/发现）；disclose information（披露信息）
- **拓展**：文中"Еврейская автономия стала открытием" 中的 открытие 即 revelation/discovery 的对应词，指"令人惊喜的发现"。disclosure（名词）在法律/金融语境中专指"强制披露/信息公示"。
- **例句**：
  > For many travellers rushing to Vladivostok, the Jewish Autonomous Oblast proves a quiet **revelation** — a region of unexpected cultural richness.
  > 对于许多匆匆赶往符拉迪沃斯托克的旅行者而言，犹太自治州往往是一个令人安静惊喜的**发现**——一个拥有出人意料的文化底蕴的地区。

### T — 翻译重要词

---

**17. break down / breakdown**
- **音标**：/breɪk daʊn/（v.）；/ˈbreɪkdaʊn/（n.）　**词性**：phrasal v. / n.
- **英文释义**：(v.) If a car or machine breaks down, it stops working; (n.) the failure of a vehicle or machine to work; the failure of a relationship, system, or discussion. (Longman)
- **中文释义**：（车辆/机器）抛锚；（关系/系统）崩溃；（n.）故障；失败；细目分析
- **语域**：通用（v.）；通用/书面/法律/统计（n.）
- **同义词**：stall, break (v.); failure, malfunction, collapse (n.)
- **常见词组**：have a breakdown（发生故障；精神崩溃）；nervous breakdown（神经崩溃）；breakdown of marriage（婚姻破裂）；a breakdown of figures（数据明细）
- **拓展**：breakdown 是高度多义词：①机械故障；②精神崩溃（nervous breakdown）；③关系破裂；④细目/分类统计。翻译时务必辨明语境。注意：break down 作短语动词时分开写；作名词时合写为 breakdown。
- **例句**：
  > After the third **breakdown** in two weeks, the team began to carry a more comprehensive set of spare parts, determined not to be caught out again.
  > 在两周内第三次**抛锚**之后，团队开始携带更为齐全的备用零件，决心不再被打个措手不及。

**18. come to the rescue**
- **音标**：/kʌm tə ðə ˈreskjuː/　**词性**：idiom / phrase
- **英文释义**：To help someone who is in a difficult situation. (Longman)
- **中文释义**：前来救援；伸出援手；及时帮助
- **语域**：通用/口语/书面均可
- **同义词**：save the day, bail someone out, come to someone's aid
- **反义词**：abandon, leave in the lurch
- **常见词组**：come to the rescue of sb/sth；rescue operation（救援行动）；rescue plan（救援方案）
- **拓展**：rescue 作名词/动词均常见。注意 come to the rescue 与 ride to the rescue（骑马来救援，更文学化/夸张）的区别。在经济新闻中常见 come to the rescue of failing banks（出手拯救濒临倒闭的银行）。
- **例句**：

**19. take a leap / plunge**

- **英文释义**：(take the plunge) To decide to do something important or risky after thinking about it for a long time. (Longman)

- **语域**：通用/口语/书面
- **同义词**：take the leap, bite the bullet, go for it
- **反义词**：hesitate, hold back
- **常见词组**：take the plunge（下定决心）；plunge into（投入；跌入）；plunge in temperatures（气温骤降）
- **拓展**：文中提到"искупались в проруби"（跳入冰窟/破冰冬泳），英文即 take a plunge in an ice hole，plunge 此处实指"跳入"。作为习语 take the plunge，则指"毅然踏出重要一步"。注意区分字面义与比喻义。

**20. odds**
- **音标**：/ɑːdz/　**词性**：n. (plural)
- **英文释义**：How likely it is that something will happen, often expressed as a number or ratio; the difficulties that make a good result seem very unlikely. (Longman)
- **中文释义**：（事情发生的）可能性；概率；不利条件；障碍
- **语域**：通用/书面/新闻
- **同义词**：chances, probability, likelihood; obstacles, difficulties
- **反义词**：—
- **常见词组**：against all odds（排除万难）；the odds are（very likely/unlikely）（很可能/不太可能）；beat the odds（战胜不利局面）；odds and ends（杂物）
- **拓展**：odds 通常为复数形式（永远不说 an odd）。against all odds 是写作/演讲中极具感染力的短语，意为"克服一切不利条件"。在赌博语境中，odds 指"赔率"（如 odds of 3 to 1）。
- **例句**：

3. **"中国道路交通安全法 机动车抛锚救援义务"** — 对照中国法律中关于道路抛锚后当事人义务、救援责任的规定（《道路交通安全法》第52条等）
4. **"Good Samaritan Law Russia China 见义勇为免责"** — 检索中俄两国关于紧急救助免责的法律比较（中国《民法典》第184条"好人法"）
5. **"中华人民共和国民法典 无因管理 紧急救助"** — 检索《民法典》第121条无因管理制度在路途紧急救助中的适用
6. **"网络内容创作者法律责任 平台规则 中国互联网法"** — 检索中国博主在涉及军事/敏感设施内容发布上的合规义务（《互联网信息服务管理办法》）

## 4D. 金句积累

---

**金句一**

> **«Наше главное впечатление во всех поездках на Москвиче — это люди. Благодаря этой машине мы познакомились с огромным количеством людей, посмотрели на Россию по-другому.»**

**英译（English）：**
> "Our chief impression from every Moskvich journey is the people. Thanks to this car, we have met an enormous number of people and have come to see Russia in an entirely different light."

**中译（Chinese）：**
> "在所有驾驶莫斯科人的旅途中，我们最深刻的印象始终是人。正是因为这辆车，我们结识了无数的人，以一种全然不同的眼光重新认识了俄罗斯。"

**【写作启示】** 此句将物质媒介（汽车）升华为情感与认知的桥梁，适用于"旅行的意义""工具与连接""文化认同"等论述类写作场景中，作为结尾点睛之笔。

---

**金句二**

> **«У нас же задача — показать доступный туризм, показать, что даже сложные маршруты, которые считались всегда внедорожными, можно проехать даже на стареньком Москвиче.»**

**英译（English）：**
> "Our mission is to showcase accessible tourism — to prove that even routes long considered impassable off-road tracks can be navigated in an ageing Soviet automobile."

**中译（Chinese）：**
> "我们的使命是展示平民旅游的可能——证明那些历来被认为属于越野车专属的艰难路线，哪怕一辆老旧的苏联小轿车也能驾驭。"

**【写作启示】** 该句以"使命宣言"的形式出现，逻辑结构为"总纲+破除偏见的具体证明"，适用于议论文中"颠覆刻板印象"类论点的引入，以及"精神大于物质"的主题论述。

---

**金句三**

> **«Еврейская автономия — недооцененный с точки зрения туризма регион. Обычно люди проскакивают его. А ведь какой колоритный Биробиджан, какие горячие источники!»**

**英译（English）：**
> "The Jewish Autonomous Oblast is a region grossly underestimated from a tourism perspective. People usually race straight through it. And yet — what a colourful, characterful Birobidzhan it is, and what extraordinary hot springs it harbours!"

**中译（Chinese）：**
> "犹太自治州是一个在旅游层面被严重低估的地区。人们通常过而不入。然而——多么色彩斑斓、别具一格的比罗比詹，多么出色的温泉资源！"

**【写作启示】** 此句运用"先抑后扬"的反转结构（先指出被忽视，再以感叹句揭示价值），是描写被遗忘的地方、被忽视的议题或被低估的事物时的经典写作范式，感染力强。
