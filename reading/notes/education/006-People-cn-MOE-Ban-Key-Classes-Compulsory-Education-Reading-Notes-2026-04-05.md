# 模块一：翻译与全文概要

## 英文翻译

**Ministry of Education: Compulsory Education Schools Strictly Prohibited from Establishing Key Classes, Experimental Classes, or Fast-and-Slow Track Classes**

The Ministry of Education's General Office recently issued a circular deploying the 2026 "Sunshine School Enrollment" special campaign for primary and secondary schools. The notice explicitly requires that compulsory education schools must strictly comply with the Compulsory Education Law, and are strictly prohibited from establishing—or establishing in disguised forms—key classes, experimental classes, or fast-and-slow track classes.

The notice prohibits all forms of irregular enrollment. Primary and secondary schools are forbidden from enrolling students in advance in violation of regulations, enrolling beyond planned quotas, or enrolling across administrative regions. Schools are also prohibited from conducting disguised early enrollment under the names of "intent registration," "pre-acceptance agreements," "guaranteed enrollment agreements," or "class placement guarantee agreements." Charging so-called "school-choice fees" or "intent deposits" during enrollment is strictly forbidden, as is linking enrollment with donations under the guise of "education support funds" or "educational foundations."

Senior high schools must fully comply with local enrollment requirements. Schools affiliated with ministries, provincial high schools, and high schools in prefecture-level cities are strictly prohibited from illegally cherry-picking top students from county areas. All bonus-point programs and their values must be strictly regulated. The policy of allocating enrollment quotas from quality high schools to educationally weaker junior high schools within the region must be properly implemented; cross-regional cherry-picking under the banner of "quota allocation" is strictly prohibited.

Regarding the regulation of specific types of enrollment, the notice requires that a provincial-level review and filing system be fully implemented for special enrollment types. Any locality or school is strictly prohibited from conducting special enrollment in the name of "early talent cultivation for innovative talents" during the compulsory education stage without provincial approval. Reform pilot programs for cultivating youth football talent must be strictly confined to the designated pilot scope. Schools offering minor-language specialization programs must reasonably define their enrollment scope and quotas, and must not conduct—or conduct in disguised form—academic subject examinations during the primary-to-junior high school transition.

The notice also calls for comprehensively advancing balanced class formation in compulsory education. This means promoting the equitable allocation of teaching staff and random assignment of students to classes, with full implementation of balanced class formation. Schools' class formation plans, processes, and results must be made publicly available. Once class formation results are announced and confirmed, they must not be arbitrarily changed.

The notice also mentions accelerating the interconnection and sharing of enrollment-related information, including household registration, property records, residence permits, social insurance, and student registration data. An "education enrollment one-stop" service guide must be formulated; online services must achieve "one-portal" handling for registration, review, and admission. Offline services must simplify form-filling and document submission to achieve "one-stop-shop" processing.

# 模块四：素材与语料库积累

## 4A. 重点词汇解析

---

### **W — 写作高频词**

---

**① prohibit**
- **音标/词性：** /proʊˈhɪbɪt/ v.
- **英文释义：** To officially forbid something by law or by an authoritative order. *(Longman)*
- **中文释义：** （以法律或权威命令）明令禁止
- **语域：** 正式；法律；新闻
- **同义词：** ban, forbid, bar, outlaw | **反义词：** permit, allow, authorize
- **拓展：**
  - 动词不规则变化：prohibit – prohibited – prohibited
  - 常见搭配：prohibit sb from doing sth；be strictly prohibited；explicitly prohibited
  - 派生词：prohibition (n. 不可数/可数均可)；prohibitive (adj. 表"高得令人望而却步的")；prohibitory (adj. 法律语域)
  - ⚠️ 区分：**forbid**（语气较重，常用于个人之间）vs **prohibit**（通常指官方/制度性禁止）vs **ban**（更口语化，常用于新闻标题）
- **例句：**
  *Schools are **strictly prohibited from** collecting so-called "school-choice fees" during the enrollment process.*
  义务教育阶段，学校在招生过程中被**严禁**收取所谓的"择校费"。

---

**② mandate**
- **音标/词性：** /ˈmændeɪt/ v./n.
- **英文释义：** (v.) To officially require something to be done; (n.) an official order or authority to do something. *(Cambridge)*
- **中文释义：** v. 强制要求；授权 | n. 授权；任务；指令
- **语域：** 正式；法律；新闻；政策文件
- **同义词：** require, order, direct, authorize | **反义词：** prohibit, forbid
- **拓展：**
  - 名词：a mandate (可数)；搭配：have a mandate to do sth；under the mandate of；fulfill one's mandate
  - 动词搭配：be mandated by law；be federally mandated
  - 考点：在美式英语中，mandate作动词较英式更常见
- **例句：**
  *The new regulation **mandates** that all schools publicly disclose their class formation process.*
  新规**强制要求**所有学校公开其编班程序。

---

**③ transparent**
- **音标/词性：** /trænsˈpærənt/ adj.
- **英文释义：** Open and not secretive, so that people can see what is happening and know what is going on. *(Longman)*
- **中文释义：** 透明的；公开的；不隐瞒的
- **语域：** 正式；书面；政策；新闻
- **同义词：** open, clear, accountable | **反义词：** opaque, secretive
- **拓展：**
  - 派生词：transparency (n. 不可数，"透明度")；transparently (adv.)
  - 搭配：transparent process / procedure；full transparency；call for greater transparency
  - ⚠️ 区分：**transparent**（侧重过程可见）vs **open**（更广泛，也可指性格）vs **accountable**（侧重问责）
- **例句：**
  *A **transparent** enrollment procedure helps prevent favoritism and reduces parental anxiety.*
  **公开透明的**招生程序有助于防止徇私舞弊，减轻家长的焦虑。

---

**④ equity**
- **音标/词性：** /ˈekwɪti/ n. 不可数（此义项）
- **英文释义：** A situation in which everyone is treated fairly and given equal opportunities. *(Cambridge)*
- **中文释义：** 公平；公正（尤指机会平等）
- **语域：** 正式；学术；政策；教育领域
- **同义词：** fairness, impartiality, justice | **反义词：** inequity, disparity, bias
- **拓展：**
  - ⚠️ 熟词多义：**equity** 在金融领域指"股权/净资产"（可数/不可数），与"公平"义完全不同，须注意语境区分。
  - 搭配：educational equity；promote equity；equity in access to education
  - 派生词：equitable (adj. "公平合理的")；inequity (n.)
- **例句：**
  *Randomized class assignment is a key mechanism for promoting **equity** in access to quality teaching resources.*
  随机编班是促进优质教学资源获取**公平性**的关键机制。

---

**⑤ allocate**
- **音标/词性：** /ˈæləkeɪt/ v.
- **英文释义：** To officially give money, time, or resources to a particular purpose. *(Longman)*
- **中文释义：** 分配；划拨；指定（资源/名额等）
- **语域：** 正式；书面；行政；经济
- **同义词：** assign, distribute, apportion | **反义词：** withhold, retain
- **拓展：**
  - 派生词：allocation (n. 可数/不可数)；allocable (adj.)
  - 搭配：allocate resources；allocate enrollment quotas；be allocated to
  - 考点：allocation of quotas（指标分配）是本文的核心概念，也是教育政策英译的常见表达
- **例句：**
  *The policy requires that a reasonable share of enrollment quotas from top high schools be **allocated** to educationally weaker schools.*
  政策要求将优质高中的部分招生名额**分配**给教育基础薄弱的学校。

---

**⑥ comply**
- **音标/词性：** /kəmˈplaɪ/ v.
- **英文释义：** To do what you are required to do or what someone asks you to do. *(Longman)*
- **中文释义：** 遵从；遵守；照章执行
- **语域：** 正式；法律；行政
- **同义词：** adhere to, abide by, conform to | **反义词：** violate, breach, defy
- **拓展：**
  - 固定搭配：comply with (laws/regulations/orders)——后接名词，不可直接接动词不定式
  - 派生词：compliance (n. 不可数，"合规")；non-compliance (n.)；compliant (adj.)
  - 考点：comply with = abide by = adhere to，三者均可互换，但comply with最为书面正式
- **例句：**
  *All secondary schools must **comply with** local enrollment regulations and refrain from recruiting students across district boundaries.*
  所有中学必须**遵守**当地招生规定，不得跨区招生。

---

**⑦ standardize**
- **音标/词性：** /ˈstændərdaɪz/ v.
- **英文释义：** To make things of the same type all have the same basic features. *(Cambridge)*
- **中文释义：** 使标准化；规范化
- **语域：** 正式；书面；行政；学术
- **同义词：** normalize, regulate, systematize | **反义词：** diversify, vary
- **拓展：**
  - 派生词：standardization (n. 不可数)；standardized (adj.，如"standardized test"标准化考试)
  - 搭配：standardize procedures；standardized enrollment；a standardized system
- **例句：**
  *Efforts to **standardize** bonus-point programs aim to prevent schools from using them as covert tools for selective admission.*
  **规范化**加分项目的举措旨在防止学校将其作为变相选拔录取的隐性工具。

---

**⑧ streamline**
- **音标/词性：** /ˈstriːmlaɪn/ v.
- **英文释义：** To make a system, organization, or process simpler and more efficient. *(Cambridge)*
- **中文释义：** 精简；优化；使更高效
- **语域：** 书面；商业；行政；新闻
- **同义词：** simplify, optimize, rationalize | **反义词：** complicate, bureaucratize
- **拓展：**
  - 派生词：streamlined (adj.)；搭配：streamline procedures；a streamlined process；streamline administration
  - 形象来源：原指"流线型设计"（减少阻力），引申为消除冗余、提高效率
- **例句：**
  *The "one-stop enrollment" initiative is designed to **streamline** administrative procedures and reduce the burden on families.*
  "入学一件事"举措旨在**精简**行政程序、减轻家庭负担。

### **R — 阅读高频词**

---

**① compulsory**
- **音标/词性：** /kəmˈpʌlsəri/ adj.
- **英文释义：** Required by law or by a rule; not optional. *(Longman)*
- **中文释义：** 强制性的；义务的；必须执行的
- **语域：** 正式；法律；教育政策
- **同义词：** mandatory, obligatory, required | **反义词：** optional, voluntary, elective
- **拓展：**
  - 核心搭配：compulsory education（义务教育）；compulsory subject（必修科目）；compulsory military service（义务兵役）
  - ⚠️ 区分：**compulsory**（法律或规定强制）vs **mandatory**（强调权威命令，更多用于行政/政策语境）vs **obligatory**（偏书面，强调道德或社会义务）
- **例句：**
  *Under China's **compulsory** education system, all children between the ages of six and fifteen are entitled to free schooling.*
  在中国的**义务**教育体制下，所有六至十五岁的儿童均有权享受免费教育。

---

**② disparity**
- **音标/词性：** /dɪˈspærɪti/ n. 可数/不可数均可
- **英文释义：** A difference between two or more things, especially an unfair one. *(Cambridge)*
- **中文释义：** 差距；悬殊；不平等
- **语域：** 正式；学术；新闻；社会政策
- **同义词：** gap, inequality, imbalance | **反义词：** parity, equality
- **拓展：**
  - 搭配：educational disparity；a disparity between X and Y；glaring/stark disparity；narrow/bridge/widen the disparity
  - 考点：disparity常与between搭配，强调两者之间的差异；与in搭配时强调某一领域内部差异（e.g., disparity in income）
- **例句：**
  *The policy aims to address deep-rooted **disparities** in educational resources between urban and rural schools.*
  该政策旨在解决城乡学校之间根深蒂固的教育资源**差距**。

---

**③ merit-based**
- **音标/词性：** /ˈmerɪt beɪst/ adj.（复合形容词）

- **中文释义：** 以能力/成绩为依据的；择优的
- **语域：** 正式；教育；政策；新闻
- **同义词：** ability-based, performance-based | **反义词：** need-based, quota-based
- **拓展：**

- **例句：**
  *Critics argue that rigidly **merit-based** admissions can entrench social inequalities rather than alleviate them.*
  批评者认为，严格以**成绩为标准**的招生制度可能固化社会不平等，而非缓解它。

### **T — 翻译重要词**

---

**① 义务教育 → Compulsory Education**
- **音标/词性：** phrase
- **英文释义：** Education that children are legally required to receive, typically covering primary and lower secondary stages.
- **中文释义：** 义务教育（中国指小学至初中九年阶段）
- **语域：** 法律；政策；教育
- **拓展：**
  - ⚠️ 翻译陷阱："义务"不译为"free"（免费），虽中国义务教育同时免费，但"compulsory"才是核心语义
  - 延伸：Higher education = 高等教育；vocational education = 职业教育；pre-school education = 学前教育
- **例句：**
  ***Compulsory education** in China spans nine years, covering primary school and junior high school.*
  中国的**义务教育**为期九年，涵盖小学和初中阶段。

---

**② 掐尖招生 → cherry-picking top students / skimming top-performing students**
- **音标/词性：** phrase
- **英文释义：** The practice of selectively recruiting only the highest-achieving students, often bypassing geographic or procedural restrictions.
- **中文释义：** 掐尖招生；择优录取（含违规跨区抢优生之义）
- **语域：** 新闻；教育政策；口语化书面语
- **拓展：**
  - cherry-pick (v.) 本义"精心挑选樱桃"，引申为"择优挑选"，含批评性色彩
  - 亦可译为：siphoning top talent；selective poaching of high-achieving students
- **例句：**
  *Elite urban schools have long been accused of **cherry-picking top students** from less affluent districts, exacerbating educational inequality.*
  城市精英学校长期被指责从较贫困地区**掐尖抢优生**，加剧了教育不平等。

---

**③ 均衡编班 → balanced/equitable class formation**
- **音标/词性：** phrase
- **英文释义：** A system in which students are randomly or systematically assigned to classes to ensure no class has a disproportionate concentration of high- or low-achieving students.
- **中文释义：** 均衡编班；平行分班
- **语域：** 教育政策；行政
- **拓展：**
  - 亦可译为：heterogeneous grouping；mixed-ability class formation；de-tracked class assignment
  - 对立概念：ability grouping / tracking（按能力分班），即本文禁止的行为
- **例句：**
  ***Balanced class formation**, which randomly assigns students regardless of test scores, is now a legal requirement under China's revised education policies.*
  **均衡编班**是指不以考试成绩为依据随机分配学生，现已成为中国修订后教育政策的法定要求。

---

**④ 择校费 → school-choice fee / school selection fee**
- **音标/词性：** phrase
- **英文释义：** An unofficial or semi-official payment required by schools from parents who wish to enroll their children in a school outside their designated district.
- **中文释义：** 择校费（家长为子女进入非对口学校而缴纳的费用）
- **语域：** 新闻；教育政策；口语
- **拓展：**
  - 西方语境近似说法：under-the-table donations；pay-to-play enrollment
  - 注意：此为中国特定语境词汇，翻译时加引号并附解释效果更佳
- **例句：**
  *The circular explicitly bans the collection of **school-choice fees**, which have long been condemned as a form of institutionalized educational inequality.*
