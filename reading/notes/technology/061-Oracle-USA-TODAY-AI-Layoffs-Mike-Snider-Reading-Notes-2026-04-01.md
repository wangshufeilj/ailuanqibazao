🔹 **Oracle / reportedly undergoing / round of layoffs / amid / push toward AI**  
🔸 据报道，**甲骨文公司**在**推进人工智能**之际，正在进行一轮**裁员**。

> 1. **Reportedly** (adv.): According to what many say; rumored. (据报道，据传。常用于引述未经官方证实的真实情况。)
> 2. **Undergo** (v.): To experience or be subjected to (something, typically something unpleasant). (经历，遭受。常接 layoffs, surgery, transformation 等词。)
> 3. **Layoff** (n.): A discharge of a cumulative number of employees for business reasons. (裁员。注意其动词形式为 lay off。)
> 4. **Amid** (prep.): In the middle of or during. (在……之中，在……期间。雅思写作中常用于交代大环境背景。)
> 5. **Push** (n.): A vigorous effort to achieve something. (努力，推进，大规模行动。)
>
> **注释：** **Oracle (甲骨文公司)**：全球最大的企业级软件公司之一，总部位于美国德克萨斯州奥斯汀。

🔹 **Oracle / has cut / an unspecified number of jobs / as the software giant / looks to reduce costs / and continue growing / its artificial intelligence business, / according to / former employees / and multiple reports.**  
🔸 据前员工及多方报道，这家**软件巨头**为了**降低成本**并继续发展其**人工智能业务**，已经削减了**数量不明**的工作岗位。

> 1. **Unspecified** (adj.): Not stated clearly or in detail. (未指明的，不详的。常用于描述未公开的具体数字。)
> 2. **Software giant** (n. phr.): A very large and powerful company in the software industry. (软件巨头。用于指代 Oracle，避免重复使用公司名。)
> 3. **Look to** (v. phr.): To plan or intend to do something. (打算，致力于。雅思阅读中常表达目的或愿景。)
> 4. **Artificial intelligence (AI)** (n. phr.): The theory and development of computer systems able to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence. (人工智能。)
> 5. **According to** (prep. phr.): As stated by or in. (根据。用于引述信息来源。)

🔹 **Numerous former employees / posted on LinkedIn / on Tuesday, March 31, / that Oracle fired them.**  
🔸 3月31日（周二），**众多前员工**在 **LinkedIn（领英）**上发布消息称，甲骨文公司辞退了他们。

> 1. **Numerous** (adj.): Great in number; many. (众多的，许多的。比 many 更正式，适合写作。)
> 2. **Post** (v.): To publish something in an online forum or social media. (发布，上传。互联网语境下的常用词。)
> 3. **LinkedIn**: A professional social networking site. (领英：全球知名的职场社交平台，常成为公司裁员消息的最早发酵地。)
> 4. **Fire** (v.): Dismiss (an employee) from a job. (开除，解雇。虽然 layoff 强调客观裁员，但员工常自称被 fire。)

🔹 **It's uncertain / how many workers / were affected, / but CNBC reported / that the company's layoffs / were in the thousands, / citing / persons familiar with the move.**  
🔸 目前尚不清楚有多少工人**受到影响**，但 **CNBC** 援引**知情人士**的话报道称，该公司的裁员人数以**千**计。

> 1. **Uncertain** (adj.): Not able to be relied on; not known or definite. (不确定的，未知的。)
> 2. **Affect** (v.): Have an effect on; make a difference to. (影响。在裁员背景下，affected workers 指被裁减的员工。)
> 3. **In the thousands** (phr.): A quantity between 2,000 and 9,999. (以千计，数以千计。)
> 4. **Cite** (v.): To mention something as proof for a theory or as a reason why something has happened. (引用，援引。学术写作和新闻报道的高频词。)
> 5. **Persons familiar with the move** (n. phr.): People who have inside knowledge of a specific company decision. (知情人士。新闻报道中保护消息来源的常用委婉语。)
>
> **注释：** **CNBC**：美国 NBC 环球集团旗下的消费者新闻与商业频道，是全球领先的财经新闻媒体。

🔹 **Those who posted / about the firings / held positions / in software engineering / and cybersecurity.**

> 4. **Over the last week** (phr.): During the period of the past seven days. (在过去的一周里。)
>
> **注释：** **Reddit**：美国社交新闻站点，类似于中国的贴吧或知乎，是员工匿名讨论公司内部事务的热门场所。

> 1. **Decline** (v.): Politely refuse (an invitation or offer). (拒绝。比 refuse 更委婉、正式。)
> 2. **Comment** (v./n.): A remark expressing an opinion or reaction. (评论。新闻中常用 decline to comment 表示“不予置评”。)
>
> **注释：** **USA TODAY**：美国发行量最大的日报之一，涵盖广泛的全国性及国际性新闻。

🔹 **As of March 31, / the company / had not filed / a recent Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification (WARN) Act notice, / a legally required warning / that employers / with 100 or more employees / must provide / 60 calendar-day advance notice / of planned closings and mass layoffs.**  
🔸 截至3月31日，该公司尚未提交近期的《**工人调整和再培训通知法案**》(WARN) 通告。根据法律要求，员工人数达到或超过100人的雇主在计划关闭工厂或进行**大规模裁员**前，必须提前60个日历日发出预警。

> 1. **As of** (prep. phr.): Up to the time of. (截至……。常用于说明数据的时效性。)
> 2. **File** (v.): To submit a legal document or application. (提交，备案。法律和商务语境下的常用动词。)
> 3. **Legally required** (adj. phr.): Compulsory according to the law. (法律要求的，法定的。)
> 4. **Advance notice** (n. phr.): Warning given before something happens. (预先通知，提前告知。)
> 5. **Mass layoff** (n. phr.): The termination of a large number of employees at once. (大规模裁员。)
>
> **注释：** **WARN Act**：美国联邦法律，旨在保护工人、其家人及社区，通过要求雇主在发生大规模裁员或工厂关闭前提供提前通知。
