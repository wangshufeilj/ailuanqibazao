> 🔻 **重点词汇解析：**
> 1.  **front-row seat** [ˌfrʌnt ˈroʊ siːt] (n. phrase): A position where you can see something very clearly. **第一排座位；亲身见证的机会**。
>     *   [搭配]: have a front-row seat to sth. (近距离观察某事)
> 2.  **clunky** [ˈklʌŋki] (adj.): Heavy and awkward in form or appearance; (of computer jargon) unattractive or difficult to use. **沉重的；笨拙的；（术语等）不雅观的**。
>     *   [同义词]: awkward, cumbersome.
> 3.  **jargon** [ˈdʒɑːrɡən] (n. [U]): Special words or expressions that are used by a particular profession or group and are difficult for others to understand. **行话；术语**。
>     *   [考点]: 雅思阅读中常指特定领域的生僻词汇。
> 4.  **superlative** [suːˈpɜːrlətɪv] (n. [C]/adj.): (n.) The form of an adjective or adverb that expresses the highest degree of a quality. (adj.) Of the highest quality or degree. **最高级（形式）；极好的**。

### A.G.I. (通用人工智能)

🔹 **For years, / companies including OpenAI, Google and Amazon / have said / their goal is to achieve A.G.I., / meaning artificial general intelligence, / a technology with humanlike cognition.**

🔸 **多年来，/ 包括 OpenAI、谷歌和亚马逊在内的公司 / 一直表示 / 他们的目标是实现 A.G.I.，/ 即通用人工智能，/ 一种具有类人认知能力的技术。**

> 🔻 **注释及重点词：**
> *   **OpenAI**: 位于旧金山的 AI 研究实验室，开发了 ChatGPT。
> *   **Amazon (亚马逊)**: 全球最大的电子商务和云计算公司。
> 1.  **cognition** [kɑːɡˈnɪʃn] (n. [U]): The mental action or process of acquiring knowledge and understanding through thought, experience, and the senses. **认知；感知**。
>     *   [形容词]: cognitive (认知的)。

---

🔹 **But for decades, / plain old “artificial intelligence” / has referred to technology / mimicking the human brain.**

🔸 **但在过去的几十年里，/ 平平无奇的“人工智能”一词 / 一直指代的是 / 模仿人类大脑的技术。**

> 🔻 **重点词汇解析：**
> 1.  **plain old** (idiom): Used to emphasize that something is basic and simple. **普普通通的；老掉牙的**。
> 2.  **mimic** [ˈmɪmɪk] (v.): To copy the way in which a person speaks or moves; to simulate. **模仿；模拟**。
>     *   [变化]: mimicked, mimicking (注意要加 k)。

---

🔹 **The elusive nature of A.G.I. / raises confusion / about whether / other tech products labeled “A.I.” / are artificially intelligent at all.**

🔸 **A.G.I. 难以定义的特性 / 引起了人们的困惑：/ 那些被贴上 “A.I.” 标签的 / 其他科技产品 / 是否真的具备人工智能。**

> 🔻 **重点词汇解析：**
> 1.  **elusive** [iˈluːsɪv] (adj.): Difficult to find, define, or achieve. **难以捉摸的；难以定义的**。
> 2.  **at all** (phrase): Used in negative sentences to add emphasis. **一点也不；到底**。

### RAG (检索增强生成)

🔹 **This acronym stands for / retrieval-augmented generation, / a technique / to improve the accuracy of chatbots.**

🔸 **这个首字母缩略词代表 / 检索增强生成，/ 这是一项 / 用于提高聊天机器人准确性的技术。**

> 🔻 **重点词汇解析：**
> 1.  **retrieval** [rɪˈtriːvl] (n. [U]): The process of finding and bringing back something. **检索；取回**。
>     *   [动词]: retrieve (找回)。
> 2.  **augmented** [ɔːɡˈmentɪd] (adj.): Made greater in size or value. **增强的；扩张的**。

---

🔹 **It involves / connecting a chatbot with external sources of information, / such as an encyclopedia, / a history book or a news article.**

🔸 **它涉及 / 将聊天机器人与外部信息源连接起来，/ 例如百科全书、/ 历史书或新闻文章。**

---

🔹 **It’s an off-putting acronym, / but think of it as a rag / that can clean up / occasionally messy answers / spewed by chatbots.**

# 模块四：素材与语料库积累

## 4A. 重点词汇解析

---

### 🔵 W — 写作高频词（Writing-Oriented）

---

**W1. jargon** /ˈdʒɑːrɡən/ *n.*

- **英文释义**：Words and expressions used in a particular profession or by a particular group of people, which are difficult for other people to understand; often used to show disapproval. *(Longman)*
- **中文释义**：（特定行业或群体使用的）行话；术语；黑话（常含贬义）
- **语域**：新闻 / 书面 / 批评性写作
- **词性说明**：不可数名词（uncountable）
- **同义词**：argot, lingo, cant, buzzword, terminology
- **反义词**：plain language, plain English, layman's terms
- **常见搭配**：
  - *technical / legal / medical / scientific jargon*（技术/法律/医学/科学行话）
  - *avoid / cut through / decode jargon*（避免/穿透/解码行话）
  - *industry jargon*（行业术语）
  - *laden with / full of jargon*（充斥着行话）
- **词源**：源自古法语 *jargoun*（约14世纪），本义为"鸟鸣"或含混不清的言语
- **拓展**：
  - *jargon* 与 *terminology* 的区别：*terminology* 中性，指某领域的专业术语体系；*jargon* 含贬义，强调晦涩难懂或滥用
  - *jargon-free writing*（无行话写作）是学术写作、新闻写作的重要原则
- **例句**：
  - The government report **was riddled with bureaucratic jargon** that ordinary citizens found completely impenetrable.
  - 这份政府报告充斥着官僚行话，普通市民根本无从理解。

---

**W2. superfluous** /suːˈpɜːrfluəs/ *adj.*

- **英文释义**：More than is needed or wanted; unnecessary. *(Longman)*
- **中文释义**：多余的；不必要的；过剩的
- **语域**：正式 / 书面 / 学术
- **同义词**：redundant, unnecessary, excessive, surplus, extraneous
- **反义词**：necessary, essential, indispensable
- **常见搭配**：*superfluous detail / decoration / words / adjectives*
- **词性变化**：
  - *superfluously*（副词）：in a superfluous manner
  - *superfluity*（名词）：*a superfluity of adjectives*（形容词的滥用）
- **词源**：来自拉丁语 *superfluus*（溢出的），*super*（超过）+ *fluere*（流）
- **拓展**：写作中常见搭配 *cut superfluous words*，是精简文风的标志性表达；GRE/GMAT 词汇高频词
- **例句**：
  - A good editor's job is to cut **superfluous adjectives** and let the facts speak for themselves.
  - 优秀编辑的职责是删去多余的形容词，让事实自己说话。

---

**W3. relentless** /rɪˈlentləs/ *adj.*

- **英文释义**：① Strict, cruel, or determined without ever stopping. ② Something bad that continues without stopping or getting less severe. *(Longman)*
- **中文释义**：①（人）严酷的；坚定不移的；不屈不挠的 ②（事物）持续不断的；无情的；毫不减弱的
- **语域**：书面 / 新闻 / 正式
- **同义词**：unrelenting, incessant, persistent, unyielding, ceaseless
- **反义词**：intermittent, lenient, sporadic
- **常见搭配**：*relentless pursuit / pressure / pace / competition / criticism*
- **词性变化**：*relentlessly*（副词）；*relentlessness*（名词，不可数）
- **拓展**：*relentless* 常用于描述竞争、进步、压力等抽象力量，在商业和科技报道中极为常见；区别于 *ruthless*（冷酷无情、不顾他人），*relentless* 更强调"不停歇"而非道德层面的残忍
- **例句**：
  - The **relentless pace** of technological change is making it harder for workers to keep their skills current.
  - 技术变革的步伐持续不止，令工人越来越难以维持技能的时效性。

---

**W4. culprit** /ˈkʌlprɪt/ *n.*

- **英文释义**：①（法律）犯罪者；罪犯 ②（非正式）导致问题或困难的原因 *(Longman)*
- **中文释义**：①罪犯；犯人 ②（引申）罪魁祸首；症结所在
- **语域**：新闻（引申义更常见）/ 法律（原义）
- **同义词**：offender, perpetrator（原义）; cause, reason, source（引申义）
- **常见搭配**：*the main / obvious / real culprit*；*identify the culprit*；*catch the culprit*
- **重要用法**：在非正式或新闻语境中，culprit 常指"造成某一问题的根本原因"，如文中 *"The obvious culprit is the artificial intelligence boom"*，此为熟词僻义，需特别注意
- **例句**：
  - Experts identified excessive screen time as a **major culprit** in the rising rates of adolescent anxiety.
  - 专家们认定过度使用屏幕是青少年焦虑率上升的主要诱因。

---

**W5. elusive** /ɪˈluːsɪv/ *adj.*

- **英文释义**：① Difficult to find or not often seen. ② Difficult to achieve. ③ Difficult to describe or understand. *(Longman)*
- **中文释义**：①难以找到的；难以捉摸的 ②难以实现的 ③难以描述或理解的
- **语域**：正式 / 书面 / 学术
- **同义词**：elusive ≈ *intangible*（无形难触碰）；*fugitive*（短暂飞逝）；*unattainable*（不可及）
- **词性变化**：*elusively*（副词）；*elusiveness*（名词，不可数）；动词来源：*elude*（逃避；难以实现）
- **常见搭配**：*an elusive goal / dream / quality / figure / answer*
- **拓展**：*elude* 是对应动词，常用于 "success has eluded him"（成功与他无缘）；不要与 *illusive*（幻觉的）混淆
- **例句**：
  - A definitive cure for Alzheimer's disease remains as **elusive** as ever despite decades of research.
  - 尽管数十年来研究从未停止，阿尔茨海默症的根治方法依然遥不可及。

---

**W6. upend** /ʌpˈend/ *v.*

- **英文释义**：To turn something upside down; (figurative) to completely change or overturn something such as a system, institution, or set of ideas. *(Cambridge)*
- **中文释义**：①（物理上）使颠倒；倒置 ②（引申）彻底颠覆；打乱；推翻
- **语域**：新闻 / 商业写作 / 书面（引申义使用频率极高）
- **同义词**：overturn, disrupt, revolutionize, subvert, transform（引申义）
- **常见搭配**：*upend the industry / market / order / power structure / traditional model*
- **词性变化**：过去式/过去分词均为 *upended*；形容词 *upended*
- **拓展**：在科技、商业、政治报道中，*upend* 是描述"颠覆性变革"的标志性动词，比 *change* 语气更强烈，比 *destroy* 更中性；文中 "the artificial intelligence boom that has **upended** the tech industry" 是典型用法
- **例句**：
  - The arrival of streaming platforms completely **upended** the traditional television broadcasting model.
  - 流媒体平台的兴起彻底颠覆了传统电视广播模式。

---

**W7. glossary** /ˈɡlɑːsəri/ *n.* (plural: *glossaries*)

- **英文释义**：A list of special words and explanations of their meanings, often at the end of a book. *(Longman)*
- **中文释义**：（术语）词汇表；词汇注释；小词典（常附于书末）
- **语域**：学术 / 出版 / 书面
- **同义词**：lexicon, vocabulary list, terminology guide
- **区别辨析**：*glossary*（某书/领域专用词汇注释表）vs. *dictionary*（综合性词典）vs. *lexicon*（语言或领域的完整词汇体系，更学术）
- **可数性**：可数名词，复数 *glossaries*
- **常见搭配**：*a glossary of terms / lingo / expressions / jargon*；文中 "birthing a fresh **glossary** of lingo"（催生出一批全新行话词汇）是绝佳写作例句
- **例句**：
  - The textbook includes a comprehensive **glossary of legal terms** at the back for easy reference.
  - 该教材末尾附有一份详尽的法律术语词汇表，便于读者随时查阅。

---

**W8. verbiage** /ˈvɜːrbiɪdʒ/ *n.*

- **英文释义**：Speech or writing that has many unnecessary words in it. *(Longman)*
- **中文释义**：（令人厌烦的）冗词赘语；废话连篇
- **语域**：正式 / 书面（常带批评语气）
- **同义词**：verbosity, wordiness, prolixity, padding
- **反义词**：concision, brevity, economy of language
- **词源**：来自法语 *verbier*（喋喋不休），源于拉丁语 *verbum*（词语）
- **不可数名词**
- **拓展**：*cut the verbiage*（删减废话）是编辑语境中的常用表达；*meaningless verbiage* 是词典给出的标准搭配
- **例句**：
  - The contract was so full of **legal verbiage** that it took a lawyer two hours to explain what it actually required.
  - 合同充斥着大量法律赘语，律师花了整整两小时才解释清楚其实质要求。

---

### 🟢 R — 阅读高频词（Reading-Oriented）

---

**R1. buzzword** /ˈbʌzwɜːrd/ *n.*

- **英文释义**：A word or phrase from one special area of knowledge that people suddenly think is very important and use a lot. *(Longman)*
- **中文释义**：流行词；时髦词；（某时期内风靡一时的）热词
- **语域**：非正式 / 新闻 / 商业
- **同义词**：catchphrase, trendy term, vogue word
- **常见搭配**：*tech / industry / management / political buzzword*；*the latest buzzword*；*buzzword-laden*（充满流行词的）
- **可数名词**
- **拓展**：*buzzword* 通常含轻微贬义，暗示该词被过度使用、内容空洞；与 *jargon* 区别：*buzzword* 强调"突然流行"的特征，*jargon* 更强调"圈内人专用、圈外人不懂"
- **例句**：
  - "Sustainability" has become such an overused **buzzword** in corporate communications that it has lost much of its original meaning.
  - "可持续性"在企业传播中已被过度使用，成了一个滥俗的流行词，原本的含义已大打折扣。

---

**R2. cognition** /kɑːɡˈnɪʃən/ *n.*

- **英文释义**：The process of knowing, understanding, and learning something. *(Longman)*
- **中文释义**：认知；认知过程；感知与理解能力
- **语域**：学术 / 正式 / 心理学/认知科学
- **不可数名词**
- **同义词**：thought, perception, understanding, mental processing
- **词族**：*cognitive*（adj. 认知的）；*cognitively*（adv.）；*cognizant*（adj. 知晓的，较正式）
- **重要搭配**：*humanlike cognition*（类人认知，文中原词）；*social / moral / language cognition*；*impair cognition*（损害认知能力）
- **拓展**：*cognitive science*（认知科学）是人工智能研究的重要交叉领域；AGI 的定义中核心要素即"humanlike cognition"
- **例句**：
  - Researchers are investigating how sleep deprivation affects **cognition**, particularly memory formation and problem-solving ability.
  - 研究人员正在探究睡眠剥夺如何影响认知功能，尤其是记忆形成与问题解决能力。

---

**R3. traction** /ˈtrækʃən/ *n.*

- **英文释义**：① The force that prevents a wheel sliding on a surface. ② Medical treatment that pulls a broken bone. ③ If a new idea gains traction, it becomes accepted by or popular with more and more people. *(Longman)*
- **中文释义**：①（轮胎）牵引力；抓地力 ②（医学）牵引术 ③（引申）认可度；影响力；势头（新兴高频用法）
- **语域**：新闻/商业（引申义）；医学（原义）
- **不可数名词**
- **重要搭配**：***gain / get traction***（获得认可/流行，最重要用法）；*lose traction*；*in traction
