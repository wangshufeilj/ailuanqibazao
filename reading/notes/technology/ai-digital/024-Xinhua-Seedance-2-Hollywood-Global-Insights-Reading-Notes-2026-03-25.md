After large language models focused on text generation became widely adopted, large models focused on generating video and images are seen as the next stage of AI development. **The Inexorable March of Technological Progress**

# 模块四：素材与语料库积累

## 4A. 重点词汇解析（完整40词）

---

### W — 写作高频词（8词）

---

**W-1. disrupt** /dɪˈsrʌpt/ *v.*

- **英文释义**：To prevent something from continuing in its usual way by causing problems; to fundamentally alter or undermine the traditional practices or structure of (an industry, system, etc.) (Cambridge / Longman)
- **中文释义**：扰乱、中断；（商业/科技语境）颠覆行业格局
- **语域**：新闻 / 商业 / 学术
- **同义词**：upend, overturn, unsettle, revolutionize | **反义词**：stabilize, consolidate
- **词性变化**：disruption *n.*（可数/不可数均可）| disruptive *adj.* | disruptor *n.*（可数，"颠覆者"）
- **拓展**："disruptive technology" / "disruptive innovation" 是科技写作核心搭配；注意区分 *disrupt*（系统性颠覆）与 *disturb*（一时性打扰，语气轻）；"creative disruption" 来自熊彼特经济学理论，常在高端写作中出现
- **例句**：
  > **Streaming platforms fundamentally disrupted** the traditional cable television business model, forcing legacy broadcasters to reinvent their distribution strategies.
  > 流媒体平台从根本上**颠覆了**传统有线电视的商业模式，迫使传统广播公司重新规划其发行策略。

---

**W-2. watershed** /ˈwɔːtərˌʃɛd/ *n.*

- **英文释义**：An event or period marking a turning point in a situation (Longman); a point at which something fundamentally changes (Cambridge)
- **中文释义**：（喻义）转折点、分水岭；（地理本义）分水岭、流域分界线
- **语域**：书面 / 新闻 / 正式
- **同义词**：turning point, inflection point, milestone, landmark
- **词性**：可数名词，单数居多；结构：*a watershed in/for sth.*
- **拓展**：英国英语中还特指电视播出时间分界线（"the watershed"，通常为晚9点），9点后可播出成人内容；写作中可替换 milestone 以提升表达质感
- **例句**：
  > The launch of GPT-4 proved to be a **watershed in** the history of natural language processing, setting off an unprecedented wave of commercial AI deployment.
  > GPT-4的发布被证明是自然语言处理史上的一个**分水岭**，引发了一波前所未有的商业AI部署浪潮。

---

**W-3. democratize** /dɪˈmɑːkrətaɪz/ *v.*

- **英文释义**：To make something available or possible for everyone, regardless of wealth or social standing; to introduce democratic principles (Longman / Cambridge)
- **中文释义**：使民主化；（科技/创意产业语境）使普惠化、降低门槛、让大众可及
- **语域**：学术 / 科技 / 书面
- **词性变化**：democratization *n.*（不可数，"普惠化/民主化进程"）| democratized *adj.*
- **拓展**：在AI和科技政策写作中，"democratize access to / democratize the tools of / democratize creativity" 是标志性三连搭配；注意与中文"民主化"的语境区分——英文技术语境中此词中性且偏褒义
- **例句**：
  > Low-cost generative AI tools are **democratizing** professional video production, enabling solo creators to compete with studio-backed productions for the first time.
  > 低成本生成式AI工具正在**普惠化**专业视频制作，使独立创作者首次得以与制片公司支持的作品相抗衡。

---

**W-4. proliferate** /prəˈlɪfərˌeɪt/ *v.*

- **英文释义**：To increase rapidly in number; to grow and reproduce quickly (Cambridge / Longman)
- **中文释义**：激增、大量涌现、迅速蔓延
- **语域**：书面 / 学术 / 新闻
- **同义词**：multiply, mushroom, burgeon, surge | **反义词**：dwindle, diminish, contract
- **词性变化**：proliferation *n.*（可数/不可数）| proliferating *adj.*
- **拓展**：经典搭配：*nuclear proliferation*（核扩散）/ *the proliferation of AI tools*（AI工具的大量涌现）；注意此词语气中性，不带正负评价，描述数量快速增加
- **例句**：
  > As open-source AI models **proliferate**, the barrier to building customized video generation tools has dropped dramatically.
  > 随着开源AI模型的**大量涌现**，构建定制化视频生成工具的门槛已大幅降低。

---

**W-5. safeguard** /ˈseɪfˌɡɑːrd/ *n./v.*

- **英文释义**：(n.) A law, rule, or measure intended to prevent harm or protect rights; (v.) To protect something from risk or damage (Cambridge / Longman)
- **中文释义**：（名）防护措施、保障机制；（动）保护、维护
- **语域**：法律 / 正式 / 新闻
- **同义词**：protection, precaution, security measure (n.); protect, shield, preserve (v.)
- **常见搭配**：*put safeguards in place* | *implement/introduce safeguards* | *safeguards against abuse* | *safeguard intellectual property*
- **拓展**：在法律和政策文件中极为高频；名词可数，常用复数 *safeguards*；作动词时后接名词宾语，如 *safeguard one's rights/interests*
- **例句**：
  > Policymakers called on AI developers to **implement robust safeguards** to prevent their models from generating content that reproduces copyrighted material verbatim.
  > 政策制定者呼吁AI开发者**实施有力的保障措施**，防止其模型逐字复制版权材料生成内容。

---

**W-6. incumbent** /ɪnˈkʌmbənt/ *adj./n.*

- **英文释义**：(adj.) Currently holding a position; (n.) The person currently holding a position; (business) An existing company with dominant market presence (Longman)
- **中文释义**：（形）在任的、现任的；（名）现任者；（商业语境）现有市场主导企业
- **语域**：正式 / 商业 / 新闻
- **熟词僻义**：商业/科技写作中，*incumbent firms/players/studios* 专指"被颠覆技术挑战的现有巨头"，与 *challengers/disruptors* 对立；另有固定结构：*it is incumbent on/upon sb. to do sth.*（某人有责任/义务做某事）
- **例句**：
  > **Incumbent** media conglomerates, whose revenue models depend on licensing fees and theatrical exclusivity, find themselves most exposed to AI-driven disruption.
  > **现有**媒体集团的收入模式依赖于授权费和院线独家播映权，因此在AI驱动的颠覆浪潮中处于最脆弱的位置。

---

**W-7. landscape** /ˈlændskeɪp/ *n.*（喻义）

- **英文释义**：(figurative) The general situation or overall state of affairs in a particular field or area of activity (Cambridge Business English)
- **中文释义**：（比喻）格局、态势、面貌；（本义）风景、景观
- **语域**：书面 / 商业 / 新闻 / 正式
- **常见搭配**：*the competitive landscape* | *the regulatory landscape* | *the technological landscape* | *reshape/transform the landscape* | *the media landscape*
- **拓展**：作为比喻名词，可数/不可数均见；在商业、科技、政策写作中几乎无处不在；警惕过度使用成为陈词滥调
- **例句**：
  > The **regulatory landscape** for generative AI remains fragmented, with the EU's AI Act, China's interim measures, and the absence of U.S. federal legislation creating a patchwork of obligations for global developers.
  > 生成式AI的**监管格局**依然碎片化——欧盟《AI法案》、中国暂行办法以及美国缺乏联邦立法，共同形成了对全球开发者杂乱拼凑的合规义务体系。

---

**W-8. seismic** /ˈsaɪzmɪk/ *adj.*

- **英文释义**：(literal) Relating to or caused by earthquakes; (figurative) Very great, serious, or important — used to describe major changes or disruptions (Longman)
- **中文释义**：（本义）地震的；（引申义）地震般的、剧烈的、影响深远的
- **语域**：新闻 / 书面 / 正式（引申义）
- **同义词**：profound, tectonic, momentous, far-reaching, sweeping
- **熟词僻义**：*seismic changes/shifts/impact* 是高端新闻写作中的经典比喻搭配，表达"震动性的、动摇根基的"变化，语气强烈
- **词源**：源自希腊语 *seismos*（震动、地震）
- **例句**：
  > The arrival of AI-generated video marks a **seismic shift** in the economics of content creation, comparable in scale to the transition from film to digital photography.
  > AI生成视频的到来标志着内容创作经济学的一次**震撼性转变**，其规模堪比从胶片向数字摄影的过渡。

### R — 阅读高频词（8词）

---

**R-1. infringe** /ɪnˈfrɪndʒ/ *v.*

- **英文释义**：To break a rule, law, or agreement; to limit or reduce someone's freedom or rights (Cambridge / Longman)
- **中文释义**：违反（规定/法律）；侵犯（权利/版权）
- **语域**：法律 / 正式 / 新闻
- **同义词**：violate, breach, contravene, transgress
- **词性变化**：infringement *n.*（可数/不可数均可）| infringing *adj.*
- **用法辨析**：*infringe a law/rule/patent/copyright*（直接接宾语）vs. *infringe on/upon sb.'s rights/privacy*（需介词）——两种结构均正确；名词 *copyright infringement*（版权侵权）/ *patent infringement*（专利侵权）是法律文书高频词
- **例句**：
  > The court ruled that training a generative AI on commercially released films without licensing agreements **infringes** the studios' exclusive reproduction rights.
  > 法院裁定，在未签订授权协议的情况下使用商业院线电影训练生成式AI，**侵犯了**制片公司的专有复制权。

---

**R-2. inflection point** /ɪnˈflɛkʃən pɔɪnt/ *n.*

- **英文释义**：A time of sudden, significant, or important change in an industry, company, or market (Cambridge); derived from calculus, where it denotes a point on a curve where the rate of change shifts direction
- **中文释义**：拐点、转折点（商业/科技语境）；拐点（数学本义）
- **语域**：商业 / 学术 / 新闻
- **同义词**：watershed, turning point, tipping point, pivot point
- **辨析**：*inflection point* 强调方向性变化（加速或减速的节点）；*tipping point* 强调临界状态（微小变化引发巨大连锁反应）；*watershed* 强调"之前/之后截然不同"；三者近义但侧重有别
- **例句**：
  > Industry analysts identified the release of Seedance 2.0 as a true **inflection point**, where AI video quality crossed the threshold required for professional deployment.
  > 行业分析师将Seedance 2.0的发布定性为真正的**拐点**——AI视频质量在这一节点跨越了专业应用所要求的门槛。

---

**R-3. intellectual property (IP)** /ˌɪntəˈlɛktʃuəl ˈprɒpərti/ *n.*

- **英文释义**：An idea, design, or creative work that a person or organization has invented or created and upon which they have legal rights; something that cannot legally be copied without the creator's permission (Longman Law topic)
- **中文释义**：知识产权（包括版权、专利权、商标权等无形资产权利）
- **语域**：法律 / 正式 / 商业
- **缩写**：IP（极为常见）
- **常见搭配**：*intellectual property rights (IPR)* | *IP protection* | *IP infringement* | *IP portfolio* | *IP licensing*
- **拓展**：不可数名词，无复数；中文法律术语"知识产权"对应英文 *intellectual property*；其下属分类：*copyright*（著作权）/ *patent*（专利）/ *trademark*（商标）/ *trade secret*（商业秘密）
- **例句**：
  > Hollywood studios have long viewed their **intellectual property portfolios**—spanning characters, storylines, and visual styles—as their most valuable strategic assets.
  > 好莱坞制片公司长期将其**知识产权组合**——涵盖角色、故事线和视觉风格——视为最重要的战略资产。

---

**R-4. entrenched** /ɪnˈtrɛntʃt/ *adj.*

- **英文释义**：(of an attitude, habit, or belief) Firmly established and unlikely to change; strongly fixed, often used to convey disapproval (Longman)
- **中文释义**：根深蒂固的、牢固确立的（常含负面含义，暗示难以撼动）
- **语域**：书面 / 新闻 / 学术
- **同义词**：deep-rooted, ingrained, embedded, calcified | **反义词**：fluid, malleable, flexible
- **拓展**：军事本义为"在壕沟中固守阵地"（entrenched troops）；比喻义在政治、经济、社会分析中极为高频：*entrenched interests*（固化利益）/ *entrenched attitudes*（根深蒂固的态度）/ *entrenched inequalities*（固化不平等）；注意 Longman 明确标注"often used to show disapproval"
- **例句**：
  > The **entrenched** resistance of major studios to AI licensing deals mirrors the reluctance of newspaper publishers to embrace digital distribution two decades ago.
  > 各大制片公司对AI授权协议**根深蒂固的**抵制，与二十年前报纸出版商不愿接受数字发行如出一辙。

---

**R-5. vested interest** /ˈvɛstɪd ˈɪntrəst/ *n.*

- **英文释义**：A strong personal reason for wanting something to happen because you will gain from it; an existing involvement in something that gives one a financial or personal stake in maintaining the status quo (Longman)
- **中文释义**：既得利益；对某事有切身利益（因此强烈希望维持现状或推动变化）
- **语域**：新闻 / 书面 / 正式
- **固定搭配**：*have a vested interest in sth./doing sth.*（在某事上有既得利益）
- **拓展**：常为可数名词，复数形式 *vested interests* 可泛指"既得利益群体"（集合概念）；注意区分 *interest*（利益/兴趣）在此为专有搭配，不可替换为 *benefit*
- **例句**：
  > Major studios have a **vested interest** in restricting AI training on their content libraries, since their valuation is tied directly to the exclusivity of those assets.
  > 各大制片公司在限制AI使用其内容库进行训练方面有**既得利益**，因为其估值直接取决于这些资产的专有性。

---

**R-6. threshold** /ˈθrɛʃhoʊld/ *n.*

- **英文释义**：① The floor or entrance of a room/building; ② The level at which something starts to happen or have an effect; ③ *on the threshold of sth.* = at the beginning of a new and important development (Longman)

### L — 地道表达（8词/词组）

**L-1. ahead of schedule** *adv. phrase*

- **英文释义**：Earlier than planned or expected (Cambridge)
- **中文释义**：提前、早于预期
- **语域**：书面 / 新闻 / 口语均可

**L-2. off guard / catch sb. off guard** *idiom*

- **英文释义**：To surprise someone by doing something they were not expecting or prepared for (Longman)
- **中文释义**：使某人措手不及、打了某人一个措手不及
- **语域**：口语 / 新闻 / 书面均可
- **对应原文**："Seedance 2.0的面世直接对'天空2'构成巨大挑战，甚至威胁其生存"——即 *caught OpenAI off guard*
- **例句**：
  > The rapid advancement of Chinese AI video tools **caught Hollywood's incumbent studios off guard**, forcing them into reactive legal postures rather than strategic adaptation.
  > 中国AI视频工具的快速进步**令好莱坞现有制片公司措手不及**，迫使它们采取被动应诉姿态，而非主动战略适应。

---

**L-3. lower the barrier(s) to entry** *set phrase*

- **英文释义**：To reduce the obstacles, costs, or requirements that prevent people from participating in a market, activity, or field (business/economics)
- **中文释义**：降低（市场）准入门槛；降低参与某领域的障碍
- **语域**：商业 / 书面 / 正式
- **对应原文**："大幅降低创作门槛"
- **拓展**：*barriers to entry* 源自经济学（Porter's Five Forces框架），是商业写作核心术语；相关表达：*raise barriers to entry*（提高门槛）/ *low-barrier platforms*（低门槛平台）
- **例句**：
  > Generative AI tools have dramatically **lowered the barriers to entry** for independent content creation, effectively democratizing a field once dominated by well-capitalized studios.
  > 生成式AI工具已大幅**降低了**独立内容创作的**准入门槛**，有效普惠了一个曾被资金雄厚的制片公司主导的领域。

---

**L-4. at stake** *adj. phrase*

- **英文释义**：If something is at stake, it is at risk of being lost or harmed if a plan fails or a situation develops badly (Longman / Cambridge)
- **中文释义**：处于危险之中、岌岌可危、利益攸关
- **语域**：书面 / 新闻 / 正式
- **对应原文**："素材库和版权是好莱坞影视巨头的立身之本"——即 their entire business model is *at stake*
- **常用结构**：*what is at stake* | *with so much at stake* | *jobs/reputations/billions of dollars are at stake*
- **例句**：
  > With billions of dollars in IP licensing revenue **at stake**, Hollywood studios have little choice but to pursue aggressive legal remedies against AI developers.
  > 由于数十亿美元的IP授权收入**岌岌可危**，好莱坞制片公司别无选择，只能对AI开发者采取强硬的法律救济手段。

---

**L-5. in the wake of** *prepositional phrase*

- **英文释义**：As a result of, or following closely after, an event (Longman / Cambridge)
- **中文释义**：在……之后；随……而来；作为……的后果
- **语域**：书面 / 新闻 / 正式
- **词源**：*wake* 本义为"船只驶过后留下的尾迹水流"，引申为"某事件之后的影响/结果"
- **近义表达**：*following*, *after*, *in the aftermath of*（aftermath 语气更强，常用于灾难性事件之后）
- **例句**：
  > **In the wake of** Disney's cease-and-desist letter, several other major studios began consulting their legal teams on similar actions against AI video companies.
  > **继**迪士尼发出停止侵权函**之后**，其他几家大型制片公司也开始就针对AI视频公司的类似行动咨询其法律团队。

---

**L-6. a far cry from** *idiom*

- **英文释义**：Something that is very different from something else (Longman)
- **中文释义**：与……相差甚远；和……判若云泥
- **语域**：书面 / 新闻 / 口语均可
- **结构**：*a far cry from sth./doing sth.*
- **对应主题**：原文提到早期"文本生成视频"模型与Seedance 2.0之间的巨大落差，即 *a far cry from earlier text-to-video attempts*
- **例句**：
  > The polished, cinema-grade footage generated by Seedance 2.0 is **a far cry from** the blurry, incoherent clips that early text-to-video models produced just two years ago.
  > Seedance 2.0生成的精致影院级画面，与两年前早期文本生成视频模型产出的模糊混乱短片**相差甚远**。

---

**L-7. tip the balance / scales** *idiom*

- **英文释义**：To be the decisive factor that causes a particular result; to shift the advantage from one side to another (Cambridge)
- **中文释义**：成为决定性因素；打破（力量）平衡；使天平倾斜
- **语域**：书面 / 新闻 / 正式
- **近义表达**：*tip the scales*, *swing the pendulum*, *be the deciding factor*
- **对应原文**：Seedance 2.0的出现对OpenAI Sora 2构成"威胁其生存"的压力，即有可能 *tip the balance* in the AI video generation market
- **例句**：
  > The cost advantage of AI-generated content could ultimately **tip the balance** against human-staffed productions in every segment of the entertainment industry except the very highest budget tier.
  > AI生成内容的成本优势，最终可能在娱乐行业的每个细分市场中**打破平衡**，使人工团队制作处于劣势——除了预算最高的顶级制作之外。

---

**L-8. move the goalposts** *idiom*

- **英文释义**：To change the rules or standards for something after it has already begun, making it harder for others to succeed (Cambridge)
- **中文释义**：随意更改规则或标准（使别人更难达标）；"移动球门柱"
- **语域**：非正式 / 新闻 / 商业
- **相关表达**：*shift the goalposts* / *change the rules of the game*（改变游戏规则，更正式）
- **对应原文**：好莱坞大公司在AI时代对"侵权"定义的扩张解释，实质上是在 *moving the goalposts* of copyright enforcement
- **例句**：
  > Critics accused Disney of **moving the goalposts** by applying traditional copyright standards to AI training, a process that bears little resemblance to conventional reproduction or public performance.
  > 批评人士指责迪士尼**随意更改规则标准**，将传统版权标准套用于AI训练——而这一过程与常规意义上的复制或公开表演几乎毫无相似之处。

## 4D. 金句积累

---

**金句一**

> **原文（中）：** 从历史角度来看，任何一场真正技术革命的到来，都会触及原有格局和利益分配，造就一个新时代。它摊薄一部分人利益，却让更多人受益，从而推动社会前行。

> **高质量英译：**
> Historically speaking, every genuine technological revolution disrupts existing power structures and the prevailing distribution of interests, ushering in a new era. It may dilute the gains of some, yet it ultimately benefits the many—and in doing so, it drives society forward.

> **适用场景：** 科技政策、AI治理、社会变革类写作开篇或结尾；可作为论述"技术进步的双面性"的权威引语。

---

**金句二**

> **原文（中）：** 未来已来，AI带来的冲击无可避免。

> **高质量英译：**
> The future is no longer on the horizon—it has arrived. The disruption wrought by artificial intelligence is not a contingency to be managed; it is an inevitability to be navigated.

> **适用场景：** 演讲、论文导言、商业报告；将原文两短句扩写为对仗英文结构，大幅提升表达张力。

---

**金句三**

> **原文（中）：** 如果对方起诉侵权，应起诉平台和工具的使用者，而不是平台和工具本身及其提供者。

> **高质量英译：**
> If infringement has occurred, the proper target of litigation is the user who wielded the tool—not the tool itself, nor the party that made it available.

> **适用场景：** 法律评论、AI版权争议、平台责任研究；精准呈现"平台工具中立性"法律抗辩逻辑，适合法律写作场景引用。
