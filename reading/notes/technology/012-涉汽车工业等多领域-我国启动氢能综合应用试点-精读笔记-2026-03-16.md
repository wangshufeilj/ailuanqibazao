### **专有名词与机构注释 | Notes on Proper Nouns**

1.  **Green Hydrogen (绿氢)**: 指通过风能、太阳能等可再生能源产生的电力电解水制取的氢气，整个生产过程零碳排放。
2.  **Fuel Cell (燃料电池)**: 一种将燃料（如氢气）的化学能直接转化为电能的化学装置。
3.  **Green Ammonia/Methanol (绿色氨/醇)**: 利用绿氢和氮气/二氧化碳合成的化学品，是未来航运、化工去碳的重要载体。
4.  **Hydrogen Metallurgy (氢冶金)**: 钢铁制造中的前沿技术，旨在用氢替代煤炭作为还原剂，大幅减少温室气体排放。
5.  **Ministry of Finance (财政部)**: 负责国家财政收支、税收政策等，本文中负责提供奖励资金。

# 模块一：翻译与全文概要

## 英文翻译 | English Translation

**China Launches Comprehensive Hydrogen Energy Application Pilot Program Spanning Automotive, Industrial and Other Sectors**

On March 16, 2026, China's Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT), Ministry of Finance (MOF), and National Development and Reform Commission (NDRC) jointly issued a notice to deploy a comprehensive hydrogen energy application pilot program. The initiative designates urban agglomerations as the primary pilot entities, aiming to drive down hydrogen costs through large-scale, multi-scenario application, propel high-quality development of the hydrogen energy industry, and accelerate the economy-wide green transition.

The pilot will use an open-competition ("Jie Bang Gua Shuai") mechanism to select urban agglomerations. Six tracks have been established: fuel cell vehicles, green ammonia and methanol, substitution of hydrogen-based chemical feedstocks, hydrogen metallurgy, hydrogen co-firing, and innovative application scenarios. The pilot runs for four years, with central government fiscal incentives capped at 1.6 billion yuan per urban agglomeration.

The program expands hydrogen applications beyond fuel cell vehicles into industry and transportation, building a "1 general fuel cell vehicle scenario + N industrial application scenarios + X innovative application scenarios" integrated ecosystem. Key focuses include:

- **Fuel cell vehicle track**: priority scaling of medium- and heavy-duty commercial vehicles, cold-chain logistics vehicles, etc.
- **Industrial tracks**: green ammonia/methanol, hydrogen-based chemical feedstock substitution, hydrogen metallurgy, and hydrogen co-firing.
- **Innovative tracks**: hydrogen applications in railway locomotives, vessels, and new-type energy storage.

By 2030, the program targets multi-sector large-scale hydrogen adoption in participating city clusters, with the average terminal hydrogen price falling below 25 yuan/kg—and down to approximately 15 yuan/kg in leading regions. The national fuel cell vehicle fleet is expected to double from 2025 levels, targeting 100,000 units. Concurrently, the pilot will accelerate iterative upgrades of fuel cells, electrolyzers, and other equipment, positioning hydrogen as a new economic growth engine.

An MIIT official noted that China's hydrogen industry has achieved an "0-to-1" breakthrough: as of end-2025, cumulative hydrogen fuel cell vehicle sales approached 40,000 units; the number of hydrogen refueling stations and refueling capacity both rank first globally; and green hydrogen production capacity stands at approximately 250,000 tonnes. This pilot is designed to overcome bottlenecks—limited application scenarios, high costs, and storage/transportation challenges—ushering the hydrogen industry into a new phase of large-scale development.

This article reports on a major policy initiative jointly launched by three Chinese ministries on March 16, 2026: a comprehensive pilot program for hydrogen energy applications. Anchored in the official policy notice, the piece outlines the program's top-level architecture—an open-competition mechanism for selecting participating urban agglomerations, six parallel application tracks, a four-year pilot duration, and central fiscal incentives of up to 1.6 billion yuan per cluster. Targets are explicitly quantified: terminal hydrogen prices to fall below 25 yuan/kg by 2030, and the national fuel cell vehicle fleet to double to 100,000 units. Contextually, the article situates the initiative against China's existing hydrogen industry achievements—global leadership in hydrogen refueling station capacity—while acknowledging structural bottlenecks in scenario diversity, pricing, and storage-transportation infrastructure. The policy's strategic significance lies in leveraging central fiscal resources to unlock multi-sector demand, breaking the classic "chicken-and-egg" cost-scale dilemma that has historically constrained hydrogen commercialization.
