# 雅思/考研英语精读笔记：印刷包装领域的数字化革命——激光模切技术解析

**基本信息 / Basic Information**
*   **来源 (Source):** *Publish* 杂志 (俄罗斯领先的印刷与出版行业媒体)
*   **题目 (Title):** Чёртова дюжина вопросов про лазерную высечку / A Baker's Dozen of Questions About Laser Die-cutting / 关于激光模切的13个核心问题
*   **作者 (Author):** Alexander Kharatyan (亚历山大·哈拉强，资深印刷技术评论员)
*   **核心人物 (Key Figure):** Mikhail Kuvshinov (米哈伊尔·库夫希诺夫，**Nissa Centrum** 公司发展总监，资深印刷设备专家)
*   **涉及机构 (Organizations):**
    *   **Nissa Centrum (НИССА Центрум):** 俄罗斯及独联体国家领先的印刷设备和耗材供应商。
    *   **Golden Laser (武汉金运激光):** 中国领先的激光设备制造商，在标签和包装激光模切领域具有全球影响力。
    *   **SEI Laser:** 意大利知名的工业激光系统制造商。

🇷🇺 **Чёртова дюжина вопросов про лазерную высечку**
🇬🇧 **A baker's dozen / of questions / about laser die-cutting**
🇨🇳 **关于激光模切的 / 13个（一打零一个）/ 核心问题**

> 🔹 **重点词汇/词组解析**
> *   **Baker's dozen** [ˈbeɪkəz ˈdʌzn] (n.): A group or set of thirteen.
>     *   [释义] 13个。源于旧时英国面包师为避免秤重不足受罚，在一打（12个）之外多给一个的习俗。在雅思阅读中常作为地道表达。
> *   **Die-cutting** [ˈdaɪ ˌkʌtɪŋ] (n.): The process of using a die to cut shapes out of paper, card, or other materials.
>     *   [释义] 模切。印刷后期处理的关键步骤。其中 "die" 作名词指“模具/冲模”。

🇷🇺 **За прошедшие 5–6 лет / многие предприятия / диверсифицировали свой бизнес, / начав развивать направление / малотиражной этикетки и упаковки.**
🇬🇧 **Over the past 5–6 years, / many enterprises / have diversified their businesses / by starting to develop / the niche of short-run labels and packaging.**
🇨🇳 **在过去的五到六年里，/ 许多企业 / 通过开始开发 / 短版标签和包装领域，/ 实现了业务的多元化。**

> 🔹 **重点词汇/词组解析**
> *   **Diversify** [daɪˈvɜːsɪfaɪ] (v.): To enlarge or vary the range of products or field of operation.
>     *   [释义] 使多元化。写作高频词，用于描述经济或业务结构调整。
> *   **Short-run** [ʃɔːt rʌn] (adj.): Produced in a small quantity.
>     *   [释义] 短版的、小批量的。印刷行业术语，对应长版 (long-run)。
> *   **Niche** [niːʃ] (n.): A specialized segment of the market for a particular kind of product or service.
>     *   [释义] 利基市场、缝隙领域。指针对性强的特定市场。

🇷🇺 **В её основе — / программно-управляемый лазерный луч (или лучи), / режущий, / а также надрезающий (не на полную глубину), / перфорирующий, / гравирующий или маркирующий обрабатываемый материал / с требуемой в производстве точностью и скоростью.**

> 🔹 **重点词汇/词组解析**

>     *   [释义] 打孔。
> *   **Engrave** [ɪnˈɡreɪv] (v.): To cut or carve a text or design on the surface of a hard object.
>     *   [释义] 雕刻。
> *   **Precision** [prɪˈsɪʒn] (n.): The quality, condition, or fact of being exact and accurate.
>     *   [释义] 精确度。在工程和科技类文章中频繁出现。

# 模块一：翻译与全文概要

## 英文翻译

**"The Devil's Dozen Questions About Laser Die Cutting"**

Over the past five to six years, many enterprises have diversified their businesses by developing short-run label and packaging operations. But as this segment grows, they require higher-productivity equipment not only for printing but also for cutting. Roll-fed laser die cutting is one such solution. Mikhail Kuvshinov, Director of Business Development at NISSA Centrum, answers a series of obvious yet important questions about the capabilities of this type of equipment.

**What is laser die cutting?**
At its core, it is a software-controlled laser beam (or beams) that cuts, scores (partially through), perforates, engraves, or marks the processed material with the precision and speed required in production. It also includes auxiliary structures such as the frame, material transport mechanism, etc. Both roll-fed and flatbed systems, as well as hybrid configurations, have become widespread.

**For what applications is it used, and how is it used in the printing industry?**
Modern systems can cut almost anything — rails, armour plate, bus floor mats, abrasive grinding discs from rolls (a material that would destroy any mechanical cutter within hours, while the beam never wears out) — but different applications require different systems. In the printing and textile industries, roll-fed laser die cutters for labels and flatbed systems for a wide range of tasks — from folding carton to textiles — are widely used.

**How does roll-fed laser die cutting differ from mechanical plotter cutting and standard rotary die cutting?**
Traditional die cutting requires the manufacture of cutting dies, typically from steel sheet. This takes time and money. Laser die cutting, like plotter cutting, is built on digital technology that requires no tooling changeover between runs. But a properly configured laser die cutter is much faster than a plotter — characteristically ten times or more.

**What limitations does laser die cutting have?**
This depends on its type. For a roll-fed label system, the key factors are speed and material compatibility. Based on NISSA Centrum's experience, systems with two 300 W lasers provide speeds suitable for industrial operation alongside digital printing machines — what I consider the gold standard for solving any task in label production. The Golden Laser LC350 2×300 W demonstrates speeds of over 80 m/min on real, if simple, jobs — and this is the speed of web movement, not the beam itself. For very heavy materials (e.g., grinding discs), significantly higher-power units are needed. Golden Laser offers up to 800 W per head for the LC350. In NISSA Centrum's practice, the highest configuration has been 2×600 W.

Basic designs cannot work with reflective or transparent materials. For advanced systems like the one mentioned above, this is not an issue. Any laser system generates toxic fumes when cutting PVC, imposing serious requirements on filtration and air extraction. I would generally be cautious about the idea of laser-cutting PVC. Monolayer polyethylene (PE) films — as opposed to composites and laminates incorporating PE — require testing. There is a nuance: if PE melts rather than vaporises, the cut edge may re-seal itself. Score cutting (not cutting all the way through) on single-material PE is also problematic.

**Has the issue of melted edges in laser cutting still not been resolved?**
A melted edge typically results from insufficient laser power and/or poor focus. A weak laser cuts more slowly, so heat spreads beyond the cut zone. A poorly focused laser — including in the Z-axis depth — heats not only the desired cut area but the surrounding material as well. A powerful and well-focused laser resolves this problem in the vast majority of cases.

**What about productivity?**
It depends primarily on the die-cut shape and laser power, as well as on the system design. It would be naive to expect serious output from a single 100–200 W head. As I noted, in most label production scenarios, the optimal solution is the Golden Laser LC350 2×300 W with throughput of around 80 m/min.

I should clarify that such speeds are only achievable with a galvo (galvanometer) design. In this design, two galvanometers control mirrors that deflect the laser beam, delivering enormous beam-spot velocities. The alternative gantry design moves the laser head mechanically, like a cutting blade — speeds are orders of magnitude lower, but it can cut far more complex materials, including large-diameter steel pipes.

**Which enterprises need roll-fed laser die cutting?**
First and foremost, digital print shops producing label products with short and very short turnaround times, since no dies need to be made. If the label shape is one of several standard sizes, a library of dies accumulates quickly and a laser is unlikely to be necessary. But if the shape constantly changes — the extreme case being sticker packs with continuously varying shapes — then it is the opposite.

Laser die cutting also benefits print shops that have a roll-fed cutting plotter and have maxed out its capabilities. Yes, you can add a second or third plotter, but a laser can increase throughput by an order of magnitude.

**Is laser die cutting expensive compared to a roll-fed cutting plotter?**
"Expensive" and "cheap" are concepts that belong to consumer goods. In business, they are replaced by the concept of return on investment. The cheapest equipment on the market may prove — and usually does prove — unprofitable, while equipment costing several times more may recoup its cost quickly and generate profit for its owner. Laser die cutters with the right specifications pay off quickly.

**How often does the laser head need to be replaced or reconditioned, and what else requires periodic replacement?**
It depends on the head. Cheap Chinese options have service lives of just a few thousand hours — six months to a year under industrial conditions. Golden Laser uses high-quality laser heads as standard. In many years, not one of them has come close to half its rated service life among our clients.

There are no consumable components like plotter blades in the laser die cutters we work with (I cannot speak for others). Consumable parts appear when the system includes additional mechanical operations — for example, lane slitting, where the blades are a wear item.

**What about warranty?**
The warranty period for such equipment is at least one year, but this is a matter of negotiation, as is price. The warranty on a roll-fed laser die cutter, like any other industrial system, covers proper operation under specified conditions of use and maintenance, as well as our engineers' working time and all necessary materials.

**Who manufactures laser die cutters?**
The undisputed leader in China is Golden Laser. It also produces OEM solutions for Western companies for sale under their own brands. There are a few other companies in the world — for example, SEI Laser — but their equipment is not accessible to us. A number of Chinese companies make lower-tier label systems: with only one head, typically with a short service life, rated at 50–100 W, rarely up to 200 W.

**What should one look for when choosing a model?**
The most important factor is matching the die-cutting speed to the speed of the printing equipment — they must correspond. And one should not trust brochures; only tests with one's own files and materials. Changing the material can reduce speed very significantly, which is behind my recommendation of 2×300 W.

Second — the ability to work with the full range of materials used in production, including reflective and transparent ones. Assess this based on results from your own tests.

Third — spot size and the ability to focus on the Z-axis. All of this should also be assessed through real tests.

Built-in redundancy is extremely useful: a dual-head model, in the event of a serious failure, continues to operate at half capacity. This happens occasionally in practice.

The ability to read QR codes on the material and switch from job to job accordingly is also important.

And finally, what one must absolutely pay attention to: the availability of documentation. If there is none — proper documentation, that is — it means the service team does not know the machine.

**What about the software, and is it difficult to train an operator?**
The software situation is mixed: some systems have excellent software, others are only rudimentary. Its capabilities need to be assessed in practice against actual production requirements.

Training an operator takes roughly as long as for a digital printing machine — possibly somewhat less.

*（适用场景：科技评测写作、商业决策建议、实证研究导言）*

> **Оригинал：** Если документации нет, причём вменяемой, то значит, что сервис не знает машину.

> **English：** If there is no documentation — proper documentation, that is — it means the service team does not know the machine.

> **中文：** 若没有文档——像样的文档——则意味着售后团队根本不了解这台机器。
