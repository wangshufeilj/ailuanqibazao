## 英文译文

**News**
**Three Gas Boiler Houses to Begin Construction in Blagoveshchensk This Year**
*24.03.2026*

Construction of three gas boiler houses is set to begin in the capital of the Amur Oblast this year. Their combined capacity will amount to 250 gigacalories per hour. These facilities will replace two aging coal-fired boiler houses and create conditions for the development of residential construction along the embankments of the Zeya and Amur rivers.

This was reported by the press service of the Amur Oblast government. The progress of the project was discussed at a meeting chaired by Governor Vasily Orlov. One of the new gas heat sources is planned for Block 607 — within the boundaries of Krasnoarmeyskaya, Zheleznodorozhnaya, Amurskaya, and Nagornaya streets. The project is currently undergoing state review, and a construction permit is expected in May.

In parallel, documentation is being prepared for two additional facilities. In Block 101, bounded by Lenin, Naberezhnaya, Amurskaya, and Pervomayskaya streets, a boiler house will be built to replace two worn-out coal units. Furthermore, to provide heating for new residential blocks, another boiler house is planned for Block 252 — in the area of Pushkin and Konnaya streets.

Governor Vasily Orlov emphasized that all three modern heat supply facilities must be commissioned for the 2027–2028 heating season, stressing that delays are unacceptable. He noted that the project opens significant prospects: the transition to gas will reduce the ecological burden by eliminating soot emissions and marks a step toward renewing urban infrastructure by replacing outdated coal boiler houses with modern, energy-efficient complexes.

### W — 写作高频词

---

**1. commission**
- **音标**：/kəˈmɪʃən/ *v./n.*
- **英文释义**：*(v.)* to formally request that someone produce a piece of work, or to formally place a piece of equipment into active service; *(n.)* an instruction or contract to produce something, or the act of putting equipment into service
- **中文释义**：（动词）正式委托、投入使用/服役；（名词）委托、佣金、正式启用
- **语域**：正式/新闻/工程/商业
- **同义词**：authorize, mandate, put into service, inaugurate
- **反义词**：decommission（停用）
- **拓展**：
  - 本文语境中"commissioned for the heating season"即"在采暖季前投入使用"，是基础设施报道的核心搭配。
  - 名词"commission"在商法中指"佣金"（可数/不可数均有）；在工程语境指"竣工验收/正式启用"。
  - 词性变化：commission (n./v.) → commissioner (n. 专员) → commissioned (adj.)
  - 常见搭配：*commission a study / commission an inquiry / be commissioned into service*
- **例句**：
  > The new wastewater treatment plant was formally **commissioned** by the municipal government ahead of the scheduled deadline.
  > 新建污水处理厂由市政府提前正式**竣工投用**。

---

**2. obsolete**
- **音标**：/ˈɑːbsəˌliːt/ *adj.*
- **英文释义**：no longer useful or in use, because something newer or better has been invented
- **中文释义**：过时的、废弃的、已淘汰的
- **语域**：书面/学术/新闻/技术
- **同义词**：outdated, outmoded, antiquated, superseded, archaic
- **反义词**：state-of-the-art, cutting-edge, up-to-date
- **拓展**：
  - 常用搭配：*render sth obsolete*（使某物过时）；*become obsolete*（变得过时）
  - 来自拉丁语 *obsolescere*（逐渐废弃）
  - 注意与 *outdated*（过时的，强调时间维度）、*outmoded*（过时的，强调风格/方式）区分：*obsolete* 语气更强，常指彻底不再使用。
- **例句**：
  > Rapid advances in renewable energy technology have **rendered** many traditional coal-fired heating systems **obsolete** within just a decade.
  > 可再生能源技术的迅猛发展，使许多传统燃煤供暖系统在短短十年内便**彻底淘汰**。

---

**3. infrastructure**
- **音标**：/ˈɪnfrəˌstrʌktʃər/ *n.*
- **英文释义**：the basic systems and structures a country or organization needs to function properly, e.g., roads, power supplies, water systems
- **中文释义**：基础设施；基础架构
- **语域**：学术/新闻/政府文件/经济
- **同义词**：utilities, public works, facilities, framework
- **拓展**：
  - 可数/不可数均可，但多用作不可数：*invest in infrastructure*; 可数：*a piece of critical infrastructure*
  - 形容词：*infrastructural*；常与 *aging/decaying/crumbling/modern/energy* 搭配
  - AWL（学术词汇表）高频词，写作必备
  - 常见搭配：*urban infrastructure / energy infrastructure / critical infrastructure / infrastructure investment*
- **例句**：
  > The city's **aging urban infrastructure**, including decades-old pipelines and outdated heating plants, is in urgent need of a comprehensive overhaul.
  > 该市**老化的城市基础设施**，包括数十年前铺设的管道和陈旧的供热厂，亟待全面改造升级。

---

**4. ecological**
- **音标**：/ˌiːkəˈlɑːdʒɪkəl/ *adj.*
- **英文释义**：relating to the relationship between living things and their environment; concerned with protecting the environment
- **中文释义**：生态的；生态学的；与环境保护相关的
- **语域**：学术/新闻/正式
- **同义词**：environmental, green, eco-friendly（后者偏口语）
- **拓展**：
  - 副词：*ecologically*；名词：*ecology*；科学家：*ecologist*
  - 搭配：*ecological burden / ecological footprint / ecological disaster / ecological balance*
  - 注意：*ecological* 侧重生态系统关系；*environmental* 更泛指环境问题，两者有交叉但不完全同义。
- **例句**：
  > The transition from coal to natural gas is expected to significantly reduce the **ecological burden** on the surrounding communities by eliminating soot and particulate emissions.
  > 从燃煤转向天然气，预计将通过消除煤烟和颗粒物排放，大幅降低周边社区的**生态负荷**。

---

**5. emission**
- **音标**：/ɪˈmɪʃən/ *n.*
- **英文释义**：a gas or other substance released into the air, especially as a pollutant; the act of sending out light, heat, gas, etc.
- **中文释义**：排放（物）；散发
- **语域**：新闻/学术/政策/环境
- **同义词**：discharge, release, output, effluent（液体排放）
- **反义词**：absorption, capture
- **拓展**：
  - 通常用复数：*carbon emissions / greenhouse gas emissions / soot emissions*
  - 可数（指具体的一次排放行为或排放物）；作"排放总量"时不可数
  - 搭配：*cut/reduce/control/limit emissions*；*emission standards / emission trading*
  - 区分：*emission*（排放物本身）vs. *pollution*（污染，结果导向）
- **例句**：
  > Switching to gas-fired boilers is projected to **cut soot emissions** by nearly 90%, dramatically improving air quality in residential areas.
  > 改用燃气锅炉预计将**削减煤烟排放量**近90%，大幅改善居民区空气质量。

**6. overhaul**
- **音标**：/ˈoʊvərˌhɔːl/ *n./v.*
- **英文释义**：*(v.)* to examine a machine, system, or organization carefully and make changes to improve or repair it; *(n.)* the act of doing this
- **中文释义**：全面检修；彻底改造；大修
- **语域**：新闻/工程/政策
- **同义词**：revamp, renovate, refurbish, rebuild, reform（系统性改造）
- **拓展**：
  - 可用于机械维修（*engine overhaul*）或制度改革（*policy overhaul*），语义较宽泛
  - 名词可数：*a complete overhaul / a major overhaul*
  - 搭配：*undergo an overhaul / carry out an overhaul*
- **例句**：
  > The district's decades-old coal heating network is undergoing a **complete overhaul**, with modern gas infrastructure set to replace all legacy facilities by 2028.
  > 该地区已有数十年历史的燃煤供热管网正在进行**全面改造**，现代化燃气基础设施将于2028年前取代所有旧设施。

**7. phase out**
- **音标**：/ˌfeɪz ˈaʊt/ *phrasal v.*
- **英文释义**：to gradually stop using or producing something over a period of time
- **中文释义**：逐步淘汰；分阶段停用
- **语域**：政策/新闻/商业
- **同义词**：discontinue, eliminate gradually, wind down, retire
- **反义词**：phase in（逐步引入）
- **拓展**：
  - 常见于能源、工业、政策语境
  - 名词形式：*phase-out*（名词需加连字符）
  - 搭配：*phase out coal / phase out subsidies / a coal phase-out plan*
- **例句**：
  > The municipal government announced a concrete timetable to **phase out** all remaining coal-fired boilers within the city limits by 2030.
  > 市政府宣布了一个具体时间表，要求在2030年前**逐步淘汰**市区内所有剩余的燃煤锅炉。

**8. residential**
- **音标**：/ˌrezɪˈdenʃəl/ *adj.*
- **英文释义**：relating to or consisting of houses or apartments where people live; used as or relating to a place of residence
- **中文释义**：住宅的；居民区的；居住性质的
- **语域**：新闻/法律/规划
- **同义词**：housing, domestic, neighborhood-level
- **反义词**：commercial, industrial
- **拓展**：
  - 搭配：*residential area / residential development / residential construction / residential buildings*
  - 注意与 *domicile*（法律用语，住所）的区别
  - 名词：*residence*（居所）；*resident*（居民）
- **例句**：

**10. gigacalorie (Gcal)**
- **音标**：/ˌɡɪɡəˈkæləri/ *n.*
- **英文释义**：a unit of energy equal to one billion calories, commonly used to measure heat output in district heating systems, especially in Russia and post-Soviet states
- **中文释义**：吉卡（十亿卡路里），是苏联及后苏联国家常用的区域供热计量单位
- **语域**：工程/能源/俄语语境
- **拓展**：
  - 1 Gcal = 10⁹ cal ≈ 4.187 GJ（吉焦）
  - 俄语中标准写法为 "Гкал/ч"（吉卡/小时），与英语文献中 "Gcal/h" 对应
  - 该单位在西方国家新闻报道中较少见，阅读俄语或中亚能源新闻时须掌握
- **例句**：
  > District heating systems in Russia typically measure output in **gigacalories per hour**, a legacy metric from the Soviet-era centralized planning system.
  > 俄罗斯区域供热系统通常以**吉卡/小时**计量产出，这是苏联时代集中计划体制留下的计量遗产。

**13. heat source**
- **音标**：/hiːt sɔːrs/ *n.*
- **英文释义**：any facility or installation that generates and supplies thermal energy for heating purposes, such as a boiler house, power plant, or geothermal well

- **语域**：工程/能源/城市规划

**14. district heating**
- **音标**：/ˈdɪstrɪkt ˈhiːtɪŋ/ *n.*
- **英文释义**：a system by which heat generated in a centralized location is distributed through a network of insulated pipes to multiple buildings in a neighborhood or city
- **中文释义**：区域集中供热（系统）
- **语域**：工程/能源/城市规划
- **同义词**：centralized heating, community heating
- **拓展**：
  - 俄语为"централизованное теплоснабжение"，是俄罗斯、中国北方城市的主要供暖模式
  - 与分散式供暖（decentralized/individual heating）相对
  - 搭配：*district heating network / district heating system / district heating operator*
- **例句**：
  > Unlike individual gas furnaces, **district heating** systems allow a single centralized facility to serve entire neighborhoods, improving energy efficiency and reducing per-household costs.
  > 与分户式燃气炉不同，**区域集中供热**系统允许一个中心设施为整个社区供热，从而提高能效、降低每户成本。

**15. soot**
- **音标**：/sʊt/ *n.*
- **英文释义**：black powder produced by the incomplete burning of coal, wood, or other fuels; a major component of air pollution from solid fuel combustion
- **中文释义**：煤烟；烟尘；炭黑（不完全燃烧产生的黑色颗粒物）
- **语域**：技术/新闻/环境
- **同义词**：soot particles, carbon black, particulate matter (PM), smog
- **拓展**：
  - 不可数名词
  - *soot* 是细颗粒物（PM2.5）的重要来源，是燃煤供热最突出的空气污染物
  - 搭配：*soot emissions / soot deposits / coated in soot*
- **例句**：
  > Coal-fired boiler houses are major contributors to local **soot** pollution, and their replacement with gas-fired alternatives is expected to produce measurable improvements in air quality.
  > 燃煤锅炉房是当地**煤烟**污染的主要来源，用燃气设施取而代之预计将带来可量化的空气质量改善。

  - 本文中煤改气带来的多重联动效益（生态、居住开发、城市更新）正体现了 *ripple effect*
  - *knock-on effect* 偏英式，*ripple effect* 更国际通用
  - 可用于写作：*The gas transition will have a ripple effect on the city's development agenda.*
- **例句**：
  > The replacement of coal boiler houses is expected to have a significant **ripple effect**, stimulating residential development, improving public health outcomes, and boosting property values along the riverfront.
  > 燃煤锅炉房的替换预计将产生显著的**连锁效应**：拉动住宅开发、改善公众健康状况，并提升滨河地带的房产价值。

**39. earmark / designate for**
- **音标**：/ˈɪərˌmɑːrk/ *v.*
- **英文释义**：to officially decide that something will be used for a particular purpose
- **中文释义**：专门指定（用途）；划拨；指定（某物/款项）用于某事
- **语域**：新闻/政策/行政/财政
- **同义词**：allocate, designate, set aside, reserve
- **说明**：
  - 本文中各街区选址的规划安排即有 *earmark* 之意
  - 搭配：*earmark funds for / earmark land for development / earmarked budget*
- **例句**：
  > Three specific city blocks have been **earmarked** for the construction of the new gas heating facilities, each chosen for its strategic proximity to existing residential clusters.
  > 三个特定街区已被**指定**用于建设新燃气供热设施，每处均因毗邻现有居民聚居区而被策略性选中。

**40. pressing / urgent need**
- **音标**：/ˈpresɪŋ/ *adj.*
- **英文释义**：requiring immediate attention; urgent and important
- **中文释义**：紧迫的；亟需的；迫切的
- **语域**：新闻/学术/政策
- **同义词**：urgent, critical, imperative, compelling
- **说明**：
  - 用于书面写作时，*pressing* 比 *urgent* 更正式、更地道
  - 搭配：*a pressing need for / a pressing concern / address pressing issues*
- **例句**：
  > The **pressing** need to replace aging coal boiler houses has become one of the most urgent infrastructure priorities for the regional government heading into the 2026 construction season.
  > 更换老旧燃煤锅炉房的**迫切**需求，已成为地区政府进入2026年建设季最优先的基础设施事项之一。
