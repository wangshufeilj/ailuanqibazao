# 模块一：翻译与全文概要

## 英文翻译（Translation）

> **Original Source:** CCTV News / 央视新闻, April 3, 2026

**"15th Five-Year Plan Vision | Flying Over Hills, Diving Into Orchards: Smart Agricultural Equipment Opens New Space for Agricultural Development"**

The 15th Five-Year Plan Outline proposes strengthening agricultural technology and equipment support. Since the beginning of the year, China's agricultural machinery has been accelerating its advance into previously uncovered territories.

Inside the headquarters of one drone company, new models for this year are undergoing intensive testing: wind tunnel tests verify stable flight capability, computer upgrades enhance the model's reasoning ability, and darkroom environments test obstacle-recognition capacity.

What is being done here is essentially "correcting the eyesight" of drones. Previously they could only distinguish outlines of objects 10 centimeters in size; in the future, they will need to identify objects smaller than 1 centimeter. The shift from "blurry vision" to "eagle-eyed precision" is intended to open up more operational markets for drones.

**Zheng Liqiang, Algorithm Engineer at XAG's (极飞科技) Decision-Making System Department:**
*"We are working to expand into mountainous application scenarios. The common obstacles there are more than double those of flatlands—power lines running through the mountains, uneven terrain, and numerous small obstacles. We must therefore enhance the computing power of the equipment and improve its 'vision.'"*

Flying over hills is precisely aimed at the new opportunities brought by the 15th Five-Year Plan. Over the next five years, China's comprehensive mechanization rate for crop cultivation, planting, and harvesting will rise to above 80%—an increase of 3.3 percentage points compared to the end of the 14th Five-Year Plan. The area with the greatest room for improvement is the over 600 million mu of hilly and mountainous regions.

At a lychee orchard in Zengcheng, Guangzhou, uneven terrain and varying crop heights once made uniform fertilization the biggest challenge. This spring, intelligent drones that have learned terrain-following flight can navigate freely along the contours of fruit trees and spray evenly. Improved operational efficiency and quality have brought new opportunities to more people. Lychee grower Liu Guangzhen has transformed himself into a drone operator and, within just a few days, has already received orders to service 2,000 mu of lychee orchards.

**Liu Guangzhen, New-Type Farmer, Zengcheng District, Guangzhou:**
*"The functions of drones are becoming increasingly powerful—this is also a new opportunity for us. The demand and market for pesticide application and cargo lifting are enormous. We are planning to expand our service workforce and build a better service brand."*

New opportunities come not only from the air. On the ground, a "robot dog" for patrolling lychee orchards, developed by research scientists, is also undergoing intensive testing. Because the dense canopy of fruit trees makes it difficult for traditional machinery to enter, and because field inspection on foot is time-consuming and laborious for farmers, the developers have integrated modules for soil nutrient detection, pest and disease identification, and crop growth monitoring onto the agile and compact robot dog, sending it into orchards to conduct daily patrols and automatically generate inspection reports.

**Feng Xiaochuan, Deputy Director of Industry Services and Development, Guangdong Academy of Modern Agricultural Equipment:**
*"Currently we can identify over 20 mainstream cash crops in hilly and mountainous regions, with corresponding identification of over 1,300 pest and disease types, at an accuracy rate of over 90%."*

---

**The New Opportunities Brought by a 3.3 Percentage-Point Increase**

During the 15th Five-Year Plan period, China's comprehensive mechanization rate for crop cultivation, planting, and harvesting will rise from 76.7% at the end of the 14th Five-Year Plan to over 80%. What new opportunities does this 3.3 percentage-point increase bring?

Data maps generated from the position information of hundreds of thousands of agricultural drones nationwide reveal vast blank areas over hilly and mountainous regions. With technological breakthroughs, these areas will see many more agricultural drones during the 15th Five-Year Plan period—the fleet size is expected to double compared to the end of the 14th Five-Year Plan, reaching approximately 200,000 units. Application scenarios will expand from single spraying operations to a full range of functions including field patrol monitoring, seeding and fertilizer broadcasting, and cargo lifting for agricultural inputs and produce. Calculations estimate this will generate a service market worth approximately 10 billion yuan and directly attract approximately 300,000 new-type farmers into this new track.

Pulling the view from sky to ground, constantly innovating small-scale farm machinery is also charging up the hills. During the 15th Five-Year Plan, over 800,000 small hillside farm machines are expected to take on various tasks. They have mastered the full repertoire of skills—slope climbing, body-swiveling, and high-precision positioning—and will improve operational efficiency by over 30% in the 600+ million mu of hilly and mountainous farmland.

Opening the 15th Five-Year Plan Outline, the blueprint for agricultural and rural modernization is becoming ever clearer. Assisted by artificial intelligence, the breeding cycle for key varieties will be shortened by 20%, and a number of breakthrough varieties will accelerate commercialization; in smart farms, satellites, multi-spectral drones, and environmental sensors are building an integrated sky-land monitoring network, providing real-time sensing of crop growth, while various intelligent farm machines work in orderly division under AI big model scheduling, helping China's food production capacity reach a new milestone of approximately 1.45 trillion jin.

---

**When Equipment Learns to "Think"—Agriculture Enters a New Era of Intelligent Development**

In the inaugural year of the 15th Five-Year Plan, China is deeply implementing an action plan for new agricultural technologies, products, and application scenarios, and is accelerating the maturation and commercialization of scientific and technological achievements. What new development spaces does this open for agriculture?

Since the beginning of the year, innovative achievements in China's agricultural science and technology have been emerging rapidly:

- The world's largest smart aquaculture vessel series, "Guoxin No. 1," has achieved "three-vessel synergy," producing 13,000 tons of premium marine fish annually.
- The first intelligent breeding robot, "Ji'er," achieved intelligent tomato pollination in a greenhouse, establishing a new "seed-machine collaborative" breeding paradigm.
- South China's first "Fuxi Farm Demonstration Zone" was established in Guangdong, driving smart transformation of 50,000 mu of surrounding farmland.
- China's first tractor with body-leveling capability completed trials; once mass-produced, it will be able to work on slopes above 15° and perform horizontal operations.

**Ding Xiaobing, Deputy General Manager of the Agricultural Machinery Division, Chongqing Shineray Agricultural Machinery Co., Ltd.:**
*"This is a breakthrough from '0 to 1'—an original innovation. When operating on hilly slopes above 15°, its body-leveling posture greatly enhances overall stability and safety, and will provide strong equipment support for moderately large-scale contiguous operations on hilly slopes."*

From plains to hills, from land to sea, from staple grain varieties to cash crops, China's agricultural production is moving into a new phase of intelligent development, with more and more smart equipment being rapidly deployed on the front lines of production. Inside all these "thinking" devices, there is a common "sensory nerve"—sensors. At the National Smart Agricultural Technology and Equipment Quality Inspection and Testing Center, one after another new agricultural sensor products are lining up to pass inspection. After passing tests in extreme environments, they will be integrated into various types of intelligent farm machinery. Since the beginning of this year, the center has already received 105 orders for new product testing—double the figure from last year.

**Li Wenlong, Head of the Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Affairs' Smart Agricultural Technology and Equipment Quality Inspection and Testing Center:**
*"The products on these shelves are those submitted for testing here, from 25 enterprises across 10 provinces, mainly including agricultural machine driving assistance systems and agricultural machine positioning and monitoring terminals. In recent years, China's smart agricultural technology and equipment sector has developed extremely rapidly, spawning a number of start-up companies with an ever-increasing range of product models."*

## 中英文对照概要（Bilingual Summary）

**[EN]** China's **15th Five-Year Plan (2026–2030)** has set a clear mandate: elevate the country's comprehensive agricultural mechanization rate to above **80%**, up 3.3 percentage points from the 76.7% recorded at the close of the 14th Plan. The core battleground for this push is the nation's over **600 million mu of hilly and mountainous farmland**—terrain that has historically resisted conventional mechanization due to complex topography, dense obstacles, and limited accessibility.

This report documents a nationwide mobilization of **smart agricultural equipment** at precisely this frontier. Agricultural drone manufacturers such as **XAG (极飞科技)** are upgrading their machines' visual acuity from 10-centimeter resolution to sub-centimeter precision, enabling obstacle avoidance in rugged mountain terrain and terrain-following spray operations in orchards. Concurrently, a four-legged **robot dog** equipped with soil nutrient sensors and AI-driven pest-identification modules—capable of recognizing 1,300+ pest and disease types at 90%+ accuracy—is being field-tested in lychee orchards, automating what was once laborious manual inspection.

The economic implications are substantial: the hillside drone fleet is projected to **double** to ~200,000 units, generating an estimated **¥10 billion** service market and drawing ~**300,000 new-type farmers** into the sector. Over 800,000 small-scale hillside machines will improve operational efficiency by over 30% in hilly areas. The plan also targets a 20% reduction in breeding cycles through AI assistance and a national grain production capacity milestone of **1.45 trillion jin**.

The broader vision is one of full-stack agricultural intelligence: satellites, multi-spectral drones, and environmental sensors forming a sky-land monitoring network, all coordinated by AI large models—transforming farmers from "firefighters" reacting to crises into "field doctors" delivering preemptive interventions.

更宏观的图景，是"天空地一体化"的智慧农业体系：卫星、多光谱无人机、环境传感器构成实时感知网络，AI大模型统一调度各类智能农机，农民角色从"救火队员"转型为"稻田医生"——这是装备学会"思考"之后，中国农业迈入的新阶段。
