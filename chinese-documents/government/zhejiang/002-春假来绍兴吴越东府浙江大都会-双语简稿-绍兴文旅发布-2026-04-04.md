🔹 **"Spring Break" Comes to Shaoxing | Following CCTV's Interview into the Eastern Capital of Wu-Yue, Exploring the Past and Present of 'Zhejiang's Great Metropolis'**  
🔸 **「春假」来绍兴｜跟随央视访谈走进吴越东府，探寻「浙江大都会」的前世今生**

🔹 **"Before the King of Wu-Yue, Shaoxing was the true great metropolis of Zhejiang."** Recently, **CCTV's Lu Jian Interview** walked into **Qianwang Temple**, where Chen Guangsheng, Director-General of the Zhejiang Provincial Department of Culture, Radio, Film and Tourism, made this resounding assertion while revealing the backstory of the TV drama **Taiping Nian (Years of Peace)**, prompting countless viewers to re-examine this millennium-old city.  
🔸 **「吴越王之前，绍兴才是浙江真正的大都会。」** 近日，**央视《鲁健访谈》**走进**钱王祠**。浙江省文化广电和旅游厅党组书记、厅长陈广胜在揭秘电视剧**《太平年》**幕后故事时，发出了这一掷地有声的断言，引发无数观众重新审视这座千年古城。

🔹 In the interview, Director Chen also noted that the **Wu-Yue Kingdom's** three generations and five kings "served one term after another," consistently upholding the national policy of **"protecting the realm and keeping the people safe"**—preserving 72 years of peace across the Two Zhe region amid the turbulence of the Five Dynasties period. **Shaoxing, as the "Eastern Capital" standing alongside Hangzhou**, is a crucial witness to this chapter of history.  
🔸 访谈中，陈厅长提到，**吴越国**三代五王「接力式」地践行**「保境安民」**的国策，在五代乱世中守护了两浙之地72年的和平。**绍兴作为与杭州并立的「东府」**，正是这段历史的重要见证者。

🔹 Shaoxing's legend did not fade with the Wu-Yue King Qian Liu's cultivation of Hangzhou. On the contrary, throughout the 72 years of the Wu-Yue Kingdom's existence, Shaoxing served as the **"Eastern Capital" co-equal with Hangzhou** and remained a strategically vital southeastern stronghold relied upon by the Qian family.  
🔸 绍兴的传奇，并未因吴越王钱镠对杭州的营建而褪色。相反，在吴越国存在的72年间，绍兴始终是**与杭州平级的「东府」**，是钱氏家族倚重的东南战略重镇。

🔹 The genesis of this status traces back to 895 AD, when **Dong Chang proclaimed himself emperor in Yuezhou**. Qian Liu received an imperial edict from the Tang dynasty to suppress the rebellion, whereupon **Emperor Zhaozong of Tang bestowed upon him the gold-inscribed iron certificate (丹书铁券)**. After this war, Qian Liu consolidated his rule over the Two Zhe and laid the foundation for establishing the Wu-Yue Kingdom. Subsequently, Qian Liu established **Hangzhou as the Western Capital and Yuezhou as the Eastern Capital**.  
🔸 这一地位的确立可追溯至公元895年，当时**董昌在越州称帝**。钱镠奉唐廷之命讨伐平乱，**唐昭宗赐其「金书铁券」**。此战后，钱镠巩固了两浙统治，奠定了建立吴越国的基石。随后，钱镠确立了**西府杭州、东府越州**的双城格局。

🔹 As the Eastern Capital, Shaoxing not only served as the administrative center of eastern Zhejiang, but also assumed vital functions in **grain transport, commerce, and military defense**. Qian Liu and his successors vigorously developed water conservancy, promoting Yuezhou's prosperity as the **"foremost prefecture of the southeast."**  
🔸 作为东府，绍兴不仅是浙东的行政中心，还承担着**漕运、商贸与国防**的重要职能。钱氏父子大兴水利，促进了越州作为**「东南第一州」**的繁荣与稳定。

🔹 Today, the relics of that historical period remain traceable. Let us walk into Shaoxing, into the epic of the Wu-Yue Kingdom's **"protection of the realm, keeping the people safe, and peaceful surrender of territory to the Song."**  
🔸 如今，那段历史的痕迹依然清晰可寻。让我们走进绍兴，走进吴越国**「保境安民、纳土归宋」**的史诗。

> **"Spring Break" Comes to Shaoxing | Following CCTV's Interview into the Eastern Capital of Wu-Yue, Exploring the Past and Present of "Zhejiang's Great Metropolis"**

*Source: Official WeChat account of Shaoxing Culture and Tourism*
*Published: April 4, 2026*

"Before the King of Wu-Yue, Shaoxing was the true great metropolis of Zhejiang." Recently, CCTV's *Lu Jian Interview* walked into Qianwang Temple, where Chen Guangsheng, Director-General of the Zhejiang Provincial Department of Culture, Radio, Film and Tourism, made this resounding assertion while revealing the backstory of the TV drama *Taiping Nian* (*Years of Peace*), prompting countless viewers to re-examine this millennium-old city.

In the interview, Director Chen also noted that the Wu-Yue Kingdom's three generations and five kings "served one term after another," consistently upholding the national policy of "protecting the realm and keeping the people safe, while maintaining good relations with China proper"—preserving 72 years of peace across the Two Zhe region amid the turbulence of the Five Dynasties period. Shaoxing, as the "Eastern Capital" standing alongside Hangzhou, is a crucial witness to this chapter of history.

Shaoxing's legend did not fade with the Wu-Yue King Qian Liu's cultivation of Hangzhou. On the contrary, throughout the 72 years of the Wu-Yue Kingdom's existence, Shaoxing served as the "Eastern Capital" co-equal with Hangzhou and remained a strategically vital southeastern stronghold relied upon by the Qian family.

The genesis of this status traces back to 895 AD, when Dong Chang proclaimed himself emperor in Yuezhou. Qian Liu received an imperial edict from the Tang dynasty to suppress the rebellion, pacifying it the following year, whereupon Emperor Zhaozong of Tang bestowed upon him the Iron Coupon of the Dan Book. After this war, Qian Liu consolidated his rule over the Two Zhe and laid the foundation for establishing the Wu-Yue Kingdom. Subsequently, Qian Liu established Hangzhou as the Western Capital and Yuezhou as the Eastern Capital—two capitals standing side by side.

As the Eastern Capital, Shaoxing not only served as the administrative center of eastern Zhejiang, but also assumed vital functions in grain transport, commerce, and military defense. Qian Liu and his successors vigorously developed water conservancy, promoted agriculture and sericulture, and expanded overseas trade, maintaining Yuezhou's prosperity and stability as the "foremost prefecture of the southeast" amid the turbulent era.

Today, the relics of that historical period remain traceable. Let us walk into Shaoxing, into the epic of the Wu-Yue Kingdom's "protection of the realm, keeping the people safe, and peaceful surrender of territory to the Song."

**Fushan Park**
Atop Fushan Hill stands the King Goujian Terrace, built in commemoration of King Goujian of Yue. During the Wu-Yue Kingdom period, Qian Liu repaired and expanded it, making it part of the Eastern Capital's administrative seat, bearing witness to the Wu-Yue kings' continuation and glorification of the ancient Yue cultural lineage.

The Feiyi Tower stands amid the hills with its upturned eaves; ascending it commands a panoramic view of Shaoxing's entire cityscape.

📍 Address: No. 270, Fushan Hengjie, Fushan Sub-district, Yuecheng District, Shaoxing

**Jishan Park**
On the eastern slope of Jishan Hill lies an inconspicuous cliff inscription that is the only Wu-Yue Kingdom relic in Yuecheng with dual verification from both documentary records and physical artifacts. The weathered characters on the rock face chronicle the turmoil of the Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms period.

Dong Chang, having usurped the throne and proclaimed himself emperor, used Shaoxing as his base and had a living shrine built here with stone carvings in self-glorification. It was precisely this rebellion that triggered the historical turning point of Qian Liu receiving the imperial edict to suppress and pacify the Two Zhe. After more than a thousand years of wind and rain erosion, the characters remain legible.

In 1963, it was designated as a Shaoxing Municipal Cultural Relic Protection Site, silently narrating the stark contrast between the "Dong Chang Rebellion" and Qian Liu's "steadfast loyalty to the throne."

Ascending the Wenbi Pagoda affords a bird's-eye view of the ancient city's crisscrossing streets and alleys, allowing one to both envision the elegant scholarly pursuits of Wang Xizhi during his hermitage and savor this cultural city's millennium of literary heritage amid the daily bustle.

📍 Address: No. 133, Shijiachiyan, Yuecheng District, Shaoxing

**The Sage of Calligraphy's Hometown**
The adjacent Sage of Calligraphy's Hometown features the Tisan Bridge standing quietly—an ordinary stone arch bridge, yet one condensing a millennium of ink fragrance through the celebrated tale of Wang Xizhi inscribing a fan for an elderly woman.

In spring, one can experience here the elegant pleasure of inserting flowers into one's hair and play the role of a "fairy of flowers"; or stroll along the bluestone path and enter the Wang Xizhi Exhibition Hall to sense the daily life of the Sage of Calligraphy; or step into the immersive VR Experience Center on the second floor of the visitor center for an appointment with a realm where reality and illusion intertwine.

📍 Address: No. 62, Jishan Street, Yuecheng District, Shaoxing

**Bai Wang Bridge (Bridge of Bowing to the King)**
On the south side of Lu Xun West Road in Yuecheng District, an ancient stone arch bridge spans the waterway, named "Bai Wang Bridge." The *Jiatai Kuaiji Gazetteer* records: "When the Martial and Majestic King of Wu-Yue pacified Dong Chang, the people of the prefecture bowed in salutation here." The people, grateful to Qian Liu for suppressing the rebellion and protecting the region, spontaneously came here to pay their respects, giving the bridge its name.

As a nationally designated key cultural relic protection unit, Bai Wang Bridge is a precious extant example of an early five-fold-edge stone arch bridge. Today on the bridge, elderly people pass slowly carrying vegetable baskets, children frolic on the stone steps—millennia of reverence, dissolved into the fabric of everyday life.

📍 Address: Intersection of Lu Xun West Road and Fushan Straight Street, Yuecheng District, Shaoxing

**Qianwang Temple Former Site**
The place name "Qianwang Temple Former Site," carrying this historical memory, silently narrates Qian Liu's accomplishments in the Yue region even though the original temple no longer exists.

After Qian Liu's death, his son Qian Yuanguan built Zhejiang's earliest Qianwang Temple in the Eastern Capital of Yuezhou—the "Temple of the Martial and Majestic King"—and the likenesses of the four subsequent kings were successively installed therein. The temple survived through the Song, Yuan, Ming, and Qing dynasties; its ruins are located at the current site of the Yuecheng District Government, and the place name "Qianwang Temple Former Site" is retained to this day.

📍 Address: From Jiefang South Road in the west to Xinjian South Road in the east, Yuecheng District, Shaoxing

**Shier Pusu Inn (12 Pusu)**
Hidden within the Sage of Calligraphy's Hometown scenic area, this Jiangnan courtyard-style boutique hotel is an ideal residence for unlocking Shaoxing's ancient city nightlife and an unparalleled choice for an immersive experience of Jiangnan elegance.

The wooden beams, green tile roofs, and courtyard wells of the old building are fully preserved while modern refinement is cleverly incorporated—ancient charm and contemporary delight complement each other perfectly here.

Not only does it offer comfortable accommodation, but it also integrates a café, reading room, hanfu experience, and exclusive photography services, letting you explore all the flavors of Jiangnan's leisurely pace.

📍 Address: No. 19, Sage of Calligraphy's Hometown, Yuecheng District, Shaoxing
☎️ Tel: 17369628520

**Keyan Scenic Area**
Within the Keyan Scenic Area, Jianhu Lake and Luzhen together witness the Wu-Yue Kingdom's millennium of "water conservancy construction and cultural heritage transmission."

Jianhu Lake, as the lifeblood of Yuezhou, underwent systematic dredging and reinforcement during the Wu-Yue period. The managed Jianhu Lake freed Yuezhou from "the worry of floods and drought" while providing "the benefit of fish and rice," creating the prosperous tableau of the "800-li Jianhu."

Luzhen, built beside the water with its white walls, gray roof tiles, old streets and deep alleys, carries the rich Jiangnan water-town ambiance that traces a direct lineage from the layout of Yuezhou city during the Wu-Yue period.

The millennium's cultural lineage continues here. The classic scenes of *Shè Xì (Village Opera)* from Lu Xun's pen now come alive in Luzhen Grand Theater as an immersive live-action film performance. Classic characters from Lu Xun's novels—Xianglin's Wife, Ah Q, Kong Yiji—"walk off the page" and perform on a stage interwoven with sound and light, re-enacting the urban life of Shaoxing a hundred years ago.

📍 Address: No. 558, Keyan Avenue, Keqiao District, Shaoxing
⌚️ Hours: 8:00–16:30 (last entry 16:00); Night Luzhen open until 21:00
🎫 Tickets: Adults ¥95, Children/Elderly ¥55 (includes Jianhu + Luzhen + painted boat)

**Keqiao Ancient Town**
Keqiao Ancient Town, the millennium-old heart of Shaoxing's water transport, sees the Wu-Yue legacy flow quietly within it. In those days, Shaoxing was the "Eastern Capital"; Keqiao, leveraging the Eastern Zhejiang Canal, saw boats come and go and merchants gather, bearing the flourishing grain-transport of the Wu-Yue Kingdom and reflecting the prosperity under Qian Liu's governance of "water conservancy development and agricultural promotion."

Today the ancient town still rests against the water. The Eastern Zhejiang Canal flows through the town; "three bridges and four waterways" reflect each other; bluestone paths are polished bright by time; black-canopied boats glide lightly, shattering the reflections across the river. Between white walls and grey tiles is Jiangnan at its most authentic.

During the spring holiday, Keqiao Ancient Town is kneading spring into the bluestone paths—markets, exhibitions, intangible cultural heritage study tours... the whole town full of poetic charm, waiting for you to explore.

📍 Address: No. 1, Yucai Road, Keqiao District, Shaoxing

**Anchang Ancient Town**
Anchang Ancient Town—its very name conceals the beacon fires and aspirations of the Wu-Yue Kingdom. In the third year of Tang Qianning (896 AD), after Qian Liu pacified the Dong Chang rebellion, he renamed this place from "Changle" to "Anchang," meaning "the stabilization of the southeast and the peace and well-being of the people."

During the Wu-Yue Kingdom period, Anchang relied on waterways to form its market, with rice and cotton trade passing through; it was an important water-town settlement in eastern Zhejiang, witnessing the flourishing remnants of Shaoxing as the "Eastern Capital."

### 人物 Persons

**钱镠 (Qian Liu, 852–932)**
五代十国时期吴越国开国国王，字具美，浙江临安人。公元895年奉唐廷之诏讨伐越州叛将董昌，平乱后获赐"丹书铁券"（铁券免死金牌）。907年后梁开国后受封吴越王，923年正式建国称"吴越国王"，建都杭州（西府），以越州（今绍兴）为东府。奉行"保境安民、善事中国"国策，积极发展水利、农业与海外贸易，使两浙在乱世中维持72年相对太平。其后人钱俶于978年纳土归宋，史称"不战而统一"。

*Qian Liu (852–932): Founding king of the Wu-Yue Kingdom during the Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms period, born in Lin'an, Zhejiang. He received an imperial edict to suppress the rebel Dong Chang in Yuezhou in 895 AD, was awarded the Iron Coupon upon victory, and formally established the Wu-Yue Kingdom in 923. He designated Hangzhou as the Western Capital and Yuezhou (modern Shaoxing) as the Eastern Capital, pursuing a policy of "protecting the realm while maintaining good relations with the central court." His descendant Qian Chu peacefully ceded the kingdom to the Song in 978 AD.*

---

**董昌 (Dong Chang, ?–896)**
唐末浙东将领，原为钱镠旧部，后任越州刺史。公元895年（乾宁二年）在越州僭越称帝，国号"大越罗平"，在蕺山等地建生祠刻碑自颂。唐廷诏令钱镠讨伐，896年兵败，董昌被擒后溺水而死（一说被杀）。其叛乱客观上成为钱镠兼并两浙、奠定吴越国基础的历史契机。

*Dong Chang (?–896): A late Tang dynasty military commander in eastern Zhejiang who served under Qian Liu before becoming prefect of Yuezhou. He declared himself emperor in 895 AD, had a living shrine built on Jishan Hill, and was suppressed and killed in 896 after Qian Liu received imperial orders to pacify the rebellion.*

---

**王羲之 (Wang Xizhi, 303–361 AD)**
东晋著名书法家，被尊称为"书圣"，祖籍山东琅琊，曾任会稽（今绍兴）内史，长期在绍兴生活创作。其代表作《兰亭序》相传作于绍兴兰亭，被誉为"天下第一行书"。书圣故里景区即以其在绍兴的生活遗迹为核心建设，题扇桥典故出自其为贫苦老妪题扇使其售出的故事。

*Wang Xizhi (303–361 AD): Celebrated Eastern Jin dynasty calligrapher revered as the "Sage of Calligraphy." He served as Administrator of Kuaiji (present-day Shaoxing) and lived there for many years. His masterpiece "Preface to the Orchid Pavilion Poems" (Lantingxu), composed in Shaoxing, is regarded as the greatest work of running script in Chinese history.*

---

**鲁迅 (Lu Xun, 1881–1936)**
中国现代文学奠基人，原名周树人，浙江绍兴人。作品多以绍兴水乡为背景，《社戏》《祝福》《孔乙己》《阿Q正传》等均涉及绍兴特定地名与人文风俗。柯岩风景区内的鲁镇即以其小说中虚构地名"鲁镇"为主题打造，将祥林嫂、阿Q、孔乙己等人物形象融入实景演艺。

*Lu Xun (1881–1936): A foundational figure of modern Chinese literature, born in Shaoxing, Zhejiang. Many of his most celebrated works are set against the backdrop of Shaoxing's water-town landscape, including "Village Opera," "The New Year's Sacrifice," and "The True Story of Ah Q." The "Luzhen" theme park in the Keyan Scenic Area is named after a fictional town recurring in his fiction.*

- **常见义**：顾客；光顾者
- **熟词僻义**：赞助人；（文化/艺术的）保护者；（历史）庇护主
- **英文释义（僻义）**：a person or organization that supports an activity or organization, especially by giving money; a famous or important person who supports an organization
- **中文释义（僻义）**：赞助人；倡导者；（历史语境）庇护主；恩主
- **语域**：正式 / 历史 / 艺术 / 文化
- **拓展**：
  - 相关词：patronage /ˈpeɪtrənɪdʒ/（n. 赞助；庇护；光顾）; patronize（v. 光顾；赞助；对人居高临下）
  - 历史语境：中国古代帝王对文人的"庇护/支持"可译为 **act as patron to scholars**
  - 注意：**patronize** 有两义——"光顾"和"以恩赐态度对待人"，后者含贬义
- **例句**：
  - As both a military commander and a cultural **patron**, Qian Liu repaired ancient monuments and promoted scholarship, ensuring that the Wu-Yue Eastern Capital retained its intellectual prestige.
  - 作为军事统帅与文化**庇护者**，钱镠既修缮古迹，又提倡学问，确保吴越东府保持着其学术声望。

**29. canvas** /ˈkænvəs/ *n.*

- **常见义**：帆布；画布
- **熟词僻义**：（比喻）宽广的叙事/创作背景；大舞台
- **英文释义（僻义）**：a situation or set of conditions that provides a background for something
- **中文释义（僻义）**：（比喻）大背景；画卷；舞台
- **语域**：文学 / 书面 / 新闻
- **拓展**：
  - 常见比喻用法："against a broader canvas of…"（在……更宏观的背景下）; "paint on a broad canvas"（从宏观视野描绘）
  - 文旅写作中，城市历史常被比喻为 **a rich canvas of history**（一幅丰富的历史画卷）
- **例句**：
  - Shaoxing offers visitors a **canvas** of layered civilizations, on which the brush strokes of the Wu-Yue Kingdom, the Song dynasty, and twentieth-century literary giants are all distinctly visible.
  - 绍兴为游客呈现出一幅层叠文明交织的**历史画卷**，吴越国、宋朝与二十世纪文学巨匠的笔触在其上清晰可辨。

**30. testament** /ˈtestəmənt/ *n.*

- **常见义**：遗嘱；《圣经》新约/旧约
- **熟词僻义**：（引申）有力的证明；见证
- **英文释义（僻义）**：proof or evidence that something exists or is true
- **中文释义（僻义）**：……的有力证明；见证；佐证
- **语域**：书面 / 正式 / 新闻
- **拓展**：
  - 固定表达：**a testament to**（……的有力证明）——写作中极为常用，可替代 "a proof of" 或 "evidence of"
  - 辨析：**testament to** 多用于正面事物；**testimony** 更中性；**legacy** 强调留存下来的东西
- **例句**：
  - That Anchang Ancient Town still bears the name given by Qian Liu in 896 is a quiet but enduring **testament** to the lasting impact of Wu-Yue governance on the region.
  - 安昌古镇至今仍沿用896年钱镠所赐之名，是吴越国治理对该地区深远影响的一份静默而**长久的见证**。

**31. a stone's throw (from)** *idiom*

- **英文释义**：a very short distance away
- **中文释义**：近在咫尺；一步之遥
- **语域**：口语 / 非正式 / 新闻（轻松语气）
- **拓展**：
  - 常见句式：be within a stone's throw of…; just a stone's throw from…
  - 用于描述地理位置的接近，比 "very close to" 更生动、更口语化
- **例句**：
  - The Wang Xizhi Exhibition Hall is **a stone's throw from** Tisan Bridge, making it easy to combine both into a single afternoon's cultural walk through the Sage of Calligraphy's Hometown.
  - 王羲之陈列馆与题扇桥**近在咫尺**，游客可将二者轻松纳入书圣故里的半日文化漫步之中。

**32. stand the test of time** *idiom*

- **英文释义**：to prove durable or successful over a long period; to remain valuable or relevant despite the passage of time
- **中文释义**：经受住时间的考验；历久弥新；长久不衰
- **语域**：书面 / 正式 / 新闻
- **拓展**：
  - 类似表达：**withstand the test of time**; **endure the ravages of time**（更文学化，含时间摧残之意）
  - 写作万能句型，可套用于历史文化、艺术、制度等多种语境
- **例句**：
  - Few ancient structures in China have **stood the test of time** as remarkably as Bai Wang Bridge, which has witnessed over a thousand years of daily life in Shaoxing.
  - 中国鲜有古建筑能像拜王桥那样如此出色地**经受住时间的考验**，历经千余年，见证绍兴市井百态。

**33. bring to life** *phrasal verb*

- **英文释义**：to make something more real, vivid, or exciting; to give life to a character, story, or scene
- **中文释义**：使（某物）栩栩如生；赋予生命；将……演绎/呈现出来
- **语域**：口语 / 非正式 / 新闻 / 文旅推广
- **拓展**：
  - 近义短语：**breathe life into**（赋予活力）; **come alive**（变得生动活泼）; **bring…to the fore**（使……凸显）
  - 文旅文本中极为高频，常用于描述表演、展览、影视改编将历史/文学形象"活化"
- **例句**：
  - The immersive drama at Luzhen Grand Theater **brings to life** the grey-robed figures of Lu Xun's fiction, transforming words on a page into a visceral encounter with early twentieth-century Shaoxing.
  - 鲁镇大剧院的沉浸式演出将鲁迅笔下身着灰衫的人物**栩栩如生地呈现**，让书页上的文字化为对二十世纪初绍兴的真实触碰。

**34. off the beaten track / path** *idiom*

- **英文释义**：in or to a place that is not well known or often visited; away from popular tourist areas
- **中文释义**：偏僻之处；鲜为人知的地方；远离旅游热点的地方
- **语域**：口语 / 非正式 / 旅游文本
- **拓展**：
  - 两种形式均可：**off the beaten track** / **off the beaten path**（美式更常用 path）
  - 反义表达：**on the tourist trail**; **a well-trodden destination**
  - 文旅语境中常用于推荐小众目的地
- **例句**：
  - For travelers who prefer to venture **off the beaten track**, the cliff inscriptions on Jishan Hill offer a quietly extraordinary encounter with a chapter of history that most tourists overlook.
  - 对于偏爱**探访冷僻之地**的旅行者而言，蕺山上的摩崖题记是一次与大多数游客所忽视的历史章节的静默而不凡的邂逅。

**35. living and breathing** *adj. phrase*

- **英文释义**：used to emphasize that something is real, active, and alive, rather than historical or theoretical
- **中文释义**：鲜活的；有生命力的；活生生的
- **语域**：口语 / 非正式 / 新闻
- **拓展**：
  - 类似表达：**a living legacy**（活的遗产）; **a living museum**（活态博物馆）
  - 与文章中"没有围墙的博物馆"（a museum without walls）形成互文
- **例句**：

  - 安昌古镇是一处**鲜活生动**的江南历史切片，日常生活的节奏依然与吴越统治下水乡聚落的脉动相呼应。

**36. layer upon layer** *phrase*

- **英文释义**：used to describe something built up gradually with many overlapping levels, strata, or elements
- **中文释义**：层层叠叠；叠加积累
- **语域**：书面 / 文学 / 正式
- **拓展**：
  - 近义表达：**strata upon strata of…**（更学术）; **accumulated over centuries**
  - 常用于描述城市、历史文化、意义的多层次积累
- **例句**：
  - What makes Shaoxing exceptional is not any single monument but the **layer upon layer** of history that every street, bridge, and waterway quietly carries.
  - 绍兴的非凡之处并非某一座单独的古迹，而是每一条街道、每一座桥梁、每一条水道所悄然承载的**层层叠叠**的历史厚度。

**37. stand as a testament to** *phrase*

**38. leave one's mark** *idiom*

- **语域**：新闻 / 书面 / 正式
- **同义表达**：make a lasting impact; leave an indelible impression

**39. a window into** *phrase*

- **中文释义**：了解……的窗口；窥见……的一扇窗

## 4D. 金句积累

---

**金句一**

> "没有吴越王之前，浙江真正的大都市是绍兴。"
> ——陈广胜，浙江省文化广电和旅游厅厅长

**高质量英文翻译：**
> "Before the advent of the Wu-Yue kings, Shaoxing was the true great metropolis of Zhejiang."

**写作场景提示：** 此句为"推翻成见型"论断，可套用结构：*"Before the [widely recognized figure/event], [lesser-known subject] was the true [superlative role]."* 适用于历史重新定位类议论文开篇。

---

**金句二**

> "三代五王'一任接着一任干'，将'保境安民、善事中国'的国策贯穿始终，让两浙之地在五代乱世中保持了七十二年的太平。"

**高质量英文翻译：**
> "Across three generations and five kings, each ruler carried the torch of his predecessor, sustaining an unwavering national policy of 'protecting the realm and keeping the people safe, while maintaining good relations with the central court'—and in so doing, preserved seventy-two years of peace for the Two Zhe region amid the tumult of the Five Dynasties period."

**写作场景提示：** "carry the torch of one's predecessor"（传承前人事业）与"sustaining an unwavering policy"（坚守一贯方针）是新闻与历史写作中的高频组合，可用于描述政策连续性。

---

**金句三**

> "千年敬意，融入了寻常烟火。"

**高质量英文翻译：**
> "A reverence spanning a thousand years has been quietly woven into the fabric of everyday life."

**写作场景提示：** 此句以极简笔墨完成"宏大时间尺度"与"日常生活细节"的对接，是文旅与文化评论写作中"以小见大"技法的典范。核心结构：*"[Abstract quality] has been woven into the fabric of [concrete domain]."* 可广泛套用。
